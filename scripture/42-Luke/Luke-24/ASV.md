# Luke 24 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** But on the first day of the week, at early dawn, they came unto the tomb, bringing the spices which they had prepared.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And they found the stone rolled away from the tomb.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And they entered in, and found not the body of the Lord Jesus.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And it came to pass, while they were perplexed thereabout, behold, two men stood by them in dazzling apparel:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** and as they were affrighted and bowed down their faces to the earth, they said unto them, Why seek ye the living among the dead?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He is not here, but is risen: remember how he spake unto you when he was yet in Galilee,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** saying that the Son of man must be delivered up into the hands of sinful men, and be crucified, and the third day rise again.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And they remembered his words,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** and returned from the tomb, and told all these things to the eleven, and to all the rest.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Now they were Mary Magdalene, and Joanna, and Mary the mother of James: and the other women with them told these things unto the apostles.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And these words appeared in their sight as idle talk; and they disbelieved them.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** But Peter arose, and ran unto the tomb; and stooping and looking in, he seeth the linen cloths by themselves; and he departed to his home, wondering at that which was come to pass.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And behold, two of them were going that very day to a village named Emmaus, which was threescore furlongs from Jerusalem.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And they communed with each other of all these things which had happened.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And it came to pass, while they communed and questioned together, that Jesus himself drew near, and went with them.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But their eyes were holden that they should not know him.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And he said unto them, What communications are these that ye have one with another, as ye walk? And they stood still, looking sad.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And one of them, named Cleopas, answering said unto him, Dost thou alone sojourn in Jerusalem and not know the things which are come to pass there in these days?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And he said unto them, What things? And they said unto him, The things concerning Jesus the Nazarene, who was a prophet mighty in deed and word before God and all the people:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** and how the chief priests and our rulers delivered him up to be condemned to death, and crucified him.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** But we hoped that it was he who should redeem Israel. Yea and besides all this, it is now the third day since these things came to pass.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Moreover certain women of our company amazed us, having been early at the tomb;

<a id="v23"></a>**23** and when they found not his body, they came, saying, that they had also seen a vision of angels, who said that he was alive.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And certain of them that were with us went to the tomb, and found it even so as the women had said: but him they saw not.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And he said unto them, O foolish men, and slow of heart to believe in all that the prophets have spoken!

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Behooved it not the Christ to suffer these things, and to enter into his glory?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And beginning from Moses and from all the prophets, he interpreted to them in all the scriptures the things concerning himself.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And they drew nigh unto the village, whither they were going: and he made as though he would go further.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And they constrained him, saying, Abide with us; for it is toward evening, and the day is now far spent. And he went in to abide with them.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And it came to pass, when he had sat down with them to meat, he took the bread and blessed; and breaking it he gave to them.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And their eyes were opened, and they knew him; and he vanished out of their sight.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And they said one to another, Was not our heart burning within us, while he spake to us in the way, while he opened to us the scriptures?

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And they rose up that very hour, and returned to Jerusalem, and found the eleven gathered together, and them that were with them,

<a id="v34"></a>**34** saying, The Lord is risen indeed, and hath appeared to Simon.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And they rehearsed the things that happened in the way, and how he was known of them in the breaking of the bread.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And as they spake these things, he himself stood in the midst of them, and saith unto them, Peace be unto you.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** But they were terrified and affrighted, and supposed that they beheld a spirit.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And he said unto them, Why are ye troubled? and wherefore do questionings arise in your heart?

<a id="v39"></a>**39** See my hands and my feet, that it is I myself: handle me, and see; for a spirit hath not flesh and bones, as ye behold me having.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** And when he had said this, he showed them his hands and his feet.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** And while they still disbelieved for joy, and wondered, he said unto them, Have ye here anything to eat?

<a id="v42"></a>**42** And they gave him a piece of a broiled fish.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** And he took it, and ate before them.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** And he said unto them, These are my words which I spake unto you, while I was yet with you, that all things must needs be fulfilled, which are written in the law of Moses, and the prophets, and the psalms, concerning me.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** Then opened he their mind, that they might understand the scriptures;

<a id="v46"></a>**46** and he said unto them, Thus it is written, that the Christ should suffer, and rise again from the dead the third day;

<a id="v47"></a>**47** and that repentance and remission of sins should be preached in his name unto all the nations, beginning from Jerusalem.

<a id="v48"></a>**48** Ye are witnesses of these things.

<a id="v49"></a>**49** And behold, I send forth the promise of my Father upon you: but tarry ye in the city, until ye be clothed with power from on high.

<a id="v50"></a>**50** And he led them out untilthey were over against Bethany: and he lifted up his hands, and blessed them.

<a id="v51"></a>**51** And it came to pass, while he blessed them, he parted from them, and was carried up into heaven.

<a id="v52"></a>**52** And they worshipped him, and returned to Jerusalem with great joy:

<a id="v53"></a>**53** and were continually in the temple, blessing God.
