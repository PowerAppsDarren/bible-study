# Luke 24 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Now upon the first day of the week, very early in the morning, they came unto the sepulchre, bringing the spices which they had prepared, and certain others with them.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And they found the stone rolled away from the sepulchre.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And they entered in, and found not the body of the Lord Jesus.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And it came to pass, as they were much perplexed thereabout, behold, two men stood by them in shining garments:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And as they were afraid, and bowed down their faces to the earth, they said unto them, Why seek ye the living among the dead?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He is not here, but is risen: remember how he spake unto you when he was yet in Galilee,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Saying, The Son of man must be delivered into the hands of sinful men, and be crucified, and the third day rise again.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And they remembered his words,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And returned from the sepulchre, and told all these things unto the eleven, and to all the rest.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** It was Mary Magdalene, and Joanna, and Mary the mother of James, and other women that were with them, which told these things unto the apostles.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And their words seemed to them as idle tales, and they believed them not.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Then arose Peter, and ran unto the sepulchre; and stooping down, he beheld the linen clothes laid by themselves, and departed, wondering in himself at that which was come to pass.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And, behold, two of them went that same day to a village called Emmaus, which was from Jerusalem about threescore furlongs.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And they talked together of all these things which had happened.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And it came to pass, that, while they communed together and reasoned, Jesus himself drew near, and went with them.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But their eyes were holden that they should not know him.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And he said unto them, What manner of communications are these that ye have one to another, as ye walk, and are sad?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And the one of them, whose name was Cleopas, answering said unto him, Art thou only a stranger in Jerusalem, and hast not known the things which are come to pass there in these days?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And he said unto them, What things? And they said unto him, Concerning Jesus of Nazareth, which was a prophet mighty in deed and word before God and all the people:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And how the chief priests and our rulers delivered him to be condemned to death, and have crucified him.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** But we trusted that it had been he which should have redeemed Israel: and beside all this, to day is the third day since these things were done.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Yea, and certain women also of our company made us astonished, which were early at the sepulchre;

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And when they found not his body, they came, saying, that they had also seen a vision of angels, which said that he was alive.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And certain of them which were with us went to the sepulchre, and found it even so as the women had said: but him they saw not.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Then he said unto them, O fools, and slow of heart to believe all that the prophets have spoken:

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Ought not Christ to have suffered these things, and to enter into his glory?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And beginning at Moses and all the prophets, he expounded unto them in all the scriptures the things concerning himself.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And they drew nigh unto the village, whither they went: and he made as though he would have gone further.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** But they constrained him, saying, Abide with us: for it is toward evening, and the day is far spent. And he went in to tarry with them.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And it came to pass, as he sat at meat with them, he took bread, and blessed it, and brake, and gave to them.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And their eyes were opened, and they knew him; and he vanished out of their sight.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And they said one to another, Did not our heart burn within us, while he talked with us by the way, and while he opened to us the scriptures?

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And they rose up the same hour, and returned to Jerusalem, and found the eleven gathered together, and them that were with them,

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Saying, The Lord is risen indeed, and hath appeared to Simon.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And they told what things were done in the way, and how he was known of them in breaking of bread.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And as they thus spake, Jesus himself stood in the midst of them, and saith unto them, Peace be unto you.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** But they were terrified and affrighted, and supposed that they had seen a spirit.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And he said unto them, Why are ye troubled? and why do thoughts arise in your hearts?

<a id="v39"></a>**39** Behold my hands and my feet, that it is I myself: handle me, and see; for a spirit hath not flesh and bones, as ye see me have.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** And when he had thus spoken, he shewed them his hands and his feet.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** And while they yet believed not for joy, and wondered, he said unto them, Have ye here any meat?

<a id="v42"></a>**42** And they gave him a piece of a broiled fish, and of an honeycomb.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** And he took it, and did eat before them.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** And he said unto them, These are the words which I spake unto you, while I was yet with you, that all things must be fulfilled, which were written in the law of Moses, and in the prophets, and in the psalms, concerning me.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** Then opened he their understanding, that they might understand the scriptures,

<a id="v46"></a>**46** And said unto them, Thus it is written, and thus it behoved Christ to suffer, and to rise from the dead the third day:

<a id="v47"></a>**47** And that repentance and remission of sins should be preached in his name among all nations, beginning at Jerusalem.

<a id="v48"></a>**48** And ye are witnesses of these things.

<a id="v49"></a>**49** And, behold, I send the promise of my Father upon you: but tarry ye in the city of Jerusalem, until ye be endued with power from on high.

<a id="v50"></a>**50** And he led them out as far as to Bethany, and he lifted up his hands, and blessed them.

<a id="v51"></a>**51** And it came to pass, while he blessed them, he was parted from them, and carried up into heaven.

<a id="v52"></a>**52** And they worshipped him, and returned to Jerusalem with great joy:

<a id="v53"></a>**53** And were continually in the temple, praising and blessing God. Amen.
