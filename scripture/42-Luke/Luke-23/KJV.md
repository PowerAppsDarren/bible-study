# Luke 23 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And the whole multitude of them arose, and led him unto Pilate.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And they began to accuse him, saying, We found this fellow perverting the nation, and forbidding to give tribute to Cesar, saying that he himself is Christ a King.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And Pilate asked him, saying, Art thou the King of the Jews? And he answered him and said, Thou sayest it.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Then said Pilate to the chief priests and to the people, I find no fault in this man.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And they were the more fierce, saying, He stirreth up the people, teaching throughout all Jewry, beginning from Galilee to this place.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** When Pilate heard of Galilee, he asked whether the man were a Galilean.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And as soon as he knew that he belonged unto Herod’s jurisdiction, he sent him to Herod, who himself also was at Jerusalem at that time.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And when Herod saw Jesus, he was exceeding glad: for he was desirous to see him of a long season, because he had heard many things of him; and he hoped to have seen some miracle done by him.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Then he questioned with him in many words; but he answered him nothing.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And the chief priests and scribes stood and vehemently accused him.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And Herod with his men of war set him at nought, and mocked him, and arrayed him in a gorgeous robe, and sent him again to Pilate.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And the same day Pilate and Herod were made friends together: for before they were at enmity between themselves.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And Pilate, when he had called together the chief priests and the rulers and the people,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Said unto them, Ye have brought this man unto me, as one that perverteth the people: and, behold, I, having examined him before you, have found no fault in this man touching those things whereof ye accuse him:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** No, nor yet Herod: for I sent you to him; and, lo, nothing worthy of death is done unto him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I will therefore chastise him, and release him.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** (For of necessity he must release one unto them at the feast.)

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And they cried out all at once, saying, Away with this man, and release unto us Barabbas:

<a id="v19"></a>**19** (Who for a certain sedition made in the city, and for murder, was cast into prison.)

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Pilate therefore, willing to release Jesus, spake again to them.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** But they cried, saying, Crucify him, crucify him.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And he said unto them the third time, Why, what evil hath he done? I have found no cause of death in him: I will therefore chastise him, and let him go.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And they were instant with loud voices, requiring that he might be crucified. And the voices of them and of the chief priests prevailed.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And Pilate gave sentence that it should be as they required.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And he released unto them him that for sedition and murder was cast into prison, whom they had desired; but he delivered Jesus to their will.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And as they led him away, they laid hold upon one Simon, a Cyrenian, coming out of the country, and on him they laid the cross, that he might bear it after Jesus.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And there followed him a great company of people, and of women, which also bewailed and lamented him.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** But Jesus turning unto them said, Daughters of Jerusalem, weep not for me, but weep for yourselves, and for your children.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For, behold, the days are coming, in the which they shall say, Blessed are the barren, and the wombs that never bare, and the paps which never gave suck.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Then shall they begin to say to the mountains, Fall on us; and to the hills, Cover us.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** For if they do these things in a green tree, what shall be done in the dry?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And there were also two other, malefactors, led with him to be put to death.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And when they were come to the place, which is called Calvary, there they crucified him, and the malefactors, one on the right hand, and the other on the left.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Then said Jesus, Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do. And they parted his raiment, and cast lots.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And the people stood beholding. And the rulers also with them derided him, saying, He saved others; let him save himself, if he be Christ, the chosen of God.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And the soldiers also mocked him, coming to him, and offering him vinegar,

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And saying, If thou be the king of the Jews, save thyself.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And a superscription also was written over him in letters of Greek, and Latin, and Hebrew, THIS IS THE KING OF THE JEWS.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And one of the malefactors which were hanged railed on him, saying, If thou be Christ, save thyself and us.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** But the other answering rebuked him, saying, Dost not thou fear God, seeing thou art in the same condemnation?

<a id="v41"></a>**41** And we indeed justly; for we receive the due reward of our deeds: but this man hath done nothing amiss.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** And he said unto Jesus, Lord, remember me when thou comest into thy kingdom.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** And Jesus said unto him, Verily I say unto thee, To day shalt thou be with me in paradise.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** And it was about the sixth hour, and there was a darkness over all the earth until the ninth hour.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** And the sun was darkened, and the veil of the temple was rent in the midst.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** And when Jesus had cried with a loud voice, he said, Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit: and having said thus, he gave up the ghost.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** Now when the centurion saw what was done, he glorified God, saying, Certainly this was a righteous man.

<a id="v48"></a>**48** And all the people that came together to that sight, beholding the things which were done, smote their breasts, and returned.

<a id="v49"></a>**49** And all his acquaintance, and the women that followed him from Galilee, stood afar off, beholding these things.

<a id="v50"></a>**50** And, behold, there was a man named Joseph, a counsellor; and he was a good man, and a just:

<a id="v51"></a>**51** (The same had not consented to the counsel and deed of them;) he was of Arimathea, a city of the Jews: who also himself waited for the kingdom of God.

<a id="v52"></a>**52** This man went unto Pilate, and begged the body of Jesus.

<a id="v53"></a>**53** And he took it down, and wrapped it in linen, and laid it in a sepulchre that was hewn in stone, wherein never man before was laid.

<a id="v54"></a>**54** And that day was the preparation, and the sabbath drew on.

<a id="v55"></a>**55** And the women also, which came with him from Galilee, followed after, and beheld the sepulchre, and how his body was laid.

<a id="v56"></a>**56** And they returned, and prepared spices and ointments; and rested the sabbath day according to the commandment.
