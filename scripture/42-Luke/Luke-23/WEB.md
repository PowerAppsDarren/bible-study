# Luke 23 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The whole company of them rose up and brought him before Pilate.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** They began to accuse him, saying, “We found this man perverting the nation, forbidding paying taxes to Caesar, and saying that he himself is Christ, a king.”

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Pilate asked him, “Are you the King of the Jews?”   He answered him, “So you say.”

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Pilate said to the chief priests and the multitudes, “I find no basis for a charge against this man.”

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But they insisted, saying, “He stirs up the people, teaching throughout all Judea, beginning from Galilee even to this place.”

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But when Pilate heard Galilee mentioned, he asked if the man was a Galilean.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** When he found out that he was in Herod’s jurisdiction, he sent him to Herod, who was also in Jerusalem during those days.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Now when Herod saw Jesus, he was exceedingly glad, for he had wanted to see him for a long time, because he had heard many things about him. He hoped to see some miracle done by him.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He questioned him with many words, but he gave no answers.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The chief priests and the scribes stood, vehemently accusing him.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Herod with his soldiers humiliated him and mocked him. Dressing him in luxurious clothing, they sent him back to Pilate.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Herod and Pilate became friends with each other that very day, for before that they were enemies with each other.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Pilate called together the chief priests and the rulers and the people,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** and said to them, “You brought this man to me as one that perverts the people, and see, I have examined him before you, and found no basis for a charge against this man concerning those things of which you accuse him.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Neither has Herod, for I sent you to him, and see, nothing worthy of death has been done by him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I will therefore chastise him and release him.”

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Now he had to release one prisoner to them at the feast.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But they all cried out together, saying, “Away with this man! Release to us Barabbas!”—

<a id="v19"></a>**19** one who was thrown into prison for a certain revolt in the city, and for murder.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Then Pilate spoke to them again, wanting to release Jesus,

<a id="v21"></a>**21** but they shouted, saying, “Crucify! Crucify him!”

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He said to them the third time, “Why? What evil has this man done? I have found no capital crime in him. I will therefore chastise him and release him.”

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But they were urgent with loud voices, asking that he might be crucified. Their voices and the voices of the chief priests prevailed.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Pilate decreed that what they asked for should be done.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** He released him who had been thrown into prison for insurrection and murder, for whom they asked, but he delivered Jesus up to their will.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** When they led him away, they grabbed one Simon of Cyrene, coming from the country, and laid on him the cross, to carry it after Jesus.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** A great multitude of the people followed him, including women who also mourned and lamented him.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** But Jesus, turning to them, said, “Daughters of Jerusalem, don’t weep for me, but weep for yourselves and for your children.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For behold, the days are coming in which they will say, ‘Blessed are the barren, the wombs that never bore, and the breasts that never nursed.’

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Then they will begin to tell the mountains, ‘Fall on us!’ and tell the hills, ‘Cover us.’

<a id="v31"></a>**31** For if they do these things in the green tree, what will be done in the dry?”

<a id="v32"></a>**32** There were also others, two criminals, led with him to be put to death.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** When they came to the place that is called The Skull, they crucified him there with the criminals, one on the right and the other on the left.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Jesus said, “Father, forgive them, for they don’t know what they are doing.”   Dividing his garments among them, they cast lots.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** The people stood watching. The rulers with them also scoffed at him, saying, “He saved others. Let him save himself, if this is the Christ of God, his chosen one!”

<a id="v36"></a>**36** The soldiers also mocked him, coming to him and offering him vinegar,

<a id="v37"></a>**37** and saying, “If you are the King of the Jews, save yourself!”

<a id="v38"></a>**38** An inscription was also written over him in letters of Greek, Latin, and Hebrew: “THIS IS THE KING OF THE JEWS.”

<a id="v39"></a>**39** One of the criminals who was hanged insulted him, saying, “If you are the Christ, save yourself and us!”

<a id="v40"></a>**40** But the other answered, and rebuking him said, “Don’t you even fear God, seeing you are under the same condemnation?

<a id="v41"></a>**41** And we indeed justly, for we receive the due reward for our deeds, but this man has done nothing wrong.”

<a id="v42"></a>**42** He said to Jesus, “Lord, remember me when you come into your Kingdom.”

<a id="v43"></a>**43** Jesus said to him, “Assuredly I tell you, today you will be with me in Paradise.”

<a id="v44"></a>**44** It was now about the sixth hour, and darkness came over the whole land until the ninth hour.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** The sun was darkened, and the veil of the temple was torn in two.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** Jesus, crying with a loud voice, said, “Father, into your hands I commit my spirit!” Having said this, he breathed his last.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** When the centurion saw what was done, he glorified God, saying, “Certainly this was a righteous man.”

<a id="v48"></a>**48** All the multitudes that came together to see this, when they saw the things that were done, returned home beating their breasts.

<a id="v49"></a>**49** All his acquaintances, and the women who followed with him from Galilee, stood at a distance, watching these things.

<a id="v50"></a>**50** Behold, a man named Joseph, who was a member of the council, a good and righteous man

<a id="v51"></a>**51** (he had not consented to their counsel and deed), from Arimathaea, a city of the Jews, who was also waiting for the Kingdom of God:

<a id="v52"></a>**52** this man went to Pilate, and asked for Jesus’ body.

<a id="v53"></a>**53** He took it down, and wrapped it in a linen cloth, and laid him in a tomb that was cut in stone, where no one had ever been laid.

<a id="v54"></a>**54** It was the day of the Preparation, and the Sabbath was drawing near.

<a id="v55"></a>**55** The women, who had come with him out of Galilee, followed after, and saw the tomb, and how his body was laid.

<a id="v56"></a>**56** They returned, and prepared spices and ointments. On the Sabbath they rested according to the commandment.
