# Luke 23 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And the whole company of them rose up, and brought him before Pilate.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And they began to accuse him, saying, We found this man perverting our nation, and forbidding to give tribute to Cæsar, and saying that he himself is Christ a king.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And Pilate asked him, saying, Art thou the King of the Jews? And he answered him and said, Thou sayest.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And Pilate said unto the chief priests and the multitudes, I find no fault in this man.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But they were the more urgent, saying, He stirreth up the people, teaching throughout all Judæa, and beginning from Galilee even unto this place.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But when Pilate heard it, he asked whether the man were a Galilæan.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And when he knew that he was of Herod’s jurisdiction, he sent him unto Herod, who himself also was at Jerusalem in these days.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Now when Herod saw Jesus, he was exceeding glad: for he was of a long time desirous to see him, because he had heard concerning him; and he hoped to see some miracle done by him.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And he questioned him in many words; but he answered him nothing.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And the chief priests and the scribes stood, vehemently accusing him.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And Herod with his soldiers set him at nought, and mocked him, and arraying him in gorgeous apparel sent him back to Pilate.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And Herod and Pilate became friends with each other that very day: for before they were at enmity between themselves.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And Pilate called together the chief priests and the rulers and the people,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** and said unto them, Ye brought unto me this man, as one that perverteth the people: and behold, I, having examined him before you, found no fault in this man touching those things whereof ye accuse him:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** no, nor yet Herod: for he sent him back unto us; and behold, nothing worthy of death hath been done by him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I will therefore chastise him, and release him.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But they cried out all together, saying, Away with this man, and release unto us Barabbas:—

<a id="v19"></a>**19** one who for a certain insurrection made in the city, and for murder, was cast into prison.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And Pilate spake unto them again, desiring to release Jesus;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** but they shouted, saying, Crucify, crucify him.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And he said unto them the third time, Why, what evil hath this man done? I have found no cause of death in him: I will therefore chastise him and release him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But they were urgent with loud voices, asking that he might be crucified. And their voices prevailed.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And Pilate gave sentence that what they asked for should be done.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And he released him that for insurrection and murder had been cast into prison, whom they asked for; but Jesus he delivered up to their will.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And when they led him away, they laid hold upon one Simon of Cyrene, coming from the country, and laid on him the cross, to bear it after Jesus.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And there followed him a great multitude of the people, and of women who bewailed and lamented him.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** But Jesus turning unto them said, Daughters of Jerusalem, weep not for me, but weep for yourselves, and for your children.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For behold, the days are coming, in which they shall say, Blessed are the barren, and the wombs that never bare, and the breasts that never gave suck.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Then shall they begin to say to the mountains, Fall on us; and to the hills, Cover us.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** For if they do these things in the green tree, what shall be done in the dry?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And there were also two others, malefactors, led with him to be put to death.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And when they came unto the place which is called The skull, there they crucified him, and the malefactors, one on the right hand and the other on the left.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** And Jesus said, Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do. And parting his garments among them, they cast lots.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And the people stood beholding. And the rulers also scoffed at him, saying, He saved others; let him save himself, if this is the Christ of God, his chosen.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And the soldiers also mocked him, coming to him, offering him vinegar,

<a id="v37"></a>**37** and saying, If thou art the King of the Jews, save thyself.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And there was also a superscription over him, THIS IS THE KING OF THE JEWS.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And one of the malefactors that were hanged railed on him, saying, Art not thou the Christ? save thyself and us.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** But the other answered, and rebuking him said, Dost thou not even fear God, seeing thou art in the same condemnation?

<a id="v41"></a>**41** And we indeed justly; for we receive the due reward of our deeds: but this man hath done nothing amiss.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** And he said, Jesus, remember me when thou comest in thy kingdom.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** And he said unto him, Verily I say unto thee, To-day shalt thou be with me in Paradise.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** And it was now about the sixth hour, and a darkness came over the whole land until the ninth hour,

<a id="v45"></a>**45** the sun’s light failing: and the veil of the temple was rent in the midst.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** And Jesus, crying with a loud voice, said, Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit: and having said this, he gave up the ghost.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** And when the centurion saw what was done, he glorified God, saying, Certainly this was a righteous man.

<a id="v48"></a>**48** And all the multitudes that came together to this sight, when they beheld the things that were done, returned smiting their breasts.

<a id="v49"></a>**49** And all his acquaintance, and the women that followed with him from Galilee, stood afar off, seeing these things.

<a id="v50"></a>**50** And behold, a man named Joseph, who was a councillor, a good and righteous man

<a id="v51"></a>**51** (he had not consented to their counsel and deed), a man of Arimathæa, a city of the Jews, who was looking for the kingdom of God:

<a id="v52"></a>**52** this man went to Pilate, and asked for the body of Jesus.

<a id="v53"></a>**53** And he took it down, and wrapped it in a linen cloth, and laid him in a tomb that was hewn in stone, where never man had yet lain.

<a id="v54"></a>**54** And it was the day of the Preparation, and the sabbath drew on.

<a id="v55"></a>**55** And the women, who had come with him out of Galilee, followed after, and beheld the tomb, and how his body was laid.

<a id="v56"></a>**56** And they returned, and prepared spices and ointments. And on the sabbath they rested according to the commandment.
