# Habakkuk 1 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The burden which Habakkuk the prophet did see.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** O Lord, how long shall I cry, and thou wilt not hear! even cry out unto thee of violence, and thou wilt not save!

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Why dost thou shew me iniquity, and cause me to behold grievance? for spoiling and violence are before me: and there are that raise up strife and contention.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Therefore the law is slacked, and judgment doth never go forth: for the wicked doth compass about the righteous; therefore wrong judgment proceedeth.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Behold ye among the heathen, and regard, and wonder marvellously: for I will work a work in your days, which ye will not believe, though it be told you.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For, lo, I raise up the Chaldeans, that bitter and hasty nation, which shall march through the breadth of the land, to possess the dwellingplaces that are not theirs.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** They are terrible and dreadful: their judgment and their dignity shall proceed of themselves.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Their horses also are swifter than the leopards, and are more fierce than the evening wolves: and their horsemen shall spread themselves, and their horsemen shall come from far; they shall fly as the eagle that hasteth to eat.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** They shall come all for violence: their faces shall sup up as the east wind, and they shall gather the captivity as the sand.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And they shall scoff at the kings, and the princes shall be a scorn unto them: they shall deride every strong hold; for they shall heap dust, and take it.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Then shall his mind change, and he shall pass over, and offend, imputing this his power unto his god.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Art thou not from everlasting, O Lord my God, mine Holy One? we shall not die. O Lord, thou hast ordained them for judgment; and, O mighty God, thou hast established them for correction.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Thou art of purer eyes than to behold evil, and canst not look on iniquity: wherefore lookest thou upon them that deal treacherously, and holdest thy tongue when the wicked devoureth the man that is more righteous than he?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And makest men as the fishes of the sea, as the creeping things, that have no ruler over them?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** They take up all of them with the angle, they catch them in their net, and gather them in their drag: therefore they rejoice and are glad.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Therefore they sacrifice unto their net, and burn incense unto their drag; because by them their portion is fat, and their meat plenteous.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Shall they therefore empty their net, and not spare continually to slay the nations?
