# Proverbs 1 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The wise sayings of Solomon, the son of David, king of Israel.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** To have knowledge of wise teaching; to be clear about the words of reason:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** To be trained in the ways of wisdom, in righteousness and judging truly and straight behaviour:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** To make the simple-minded sharp, and to give the young man knowledge, and serious purpose:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** (The wise man, hearing, will get greater learning, and the acts of the man of good sense will be wisely guided:)

<a id="v6"></a>**6** To get the sense of wise sayings and secrets, and of the words of the wise and their dark sayings.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The fear of the Lord is the start of knowledge: but the foolish have no use for wisdom and teaching.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** My son, give ear to the training of your father, and do not give up the teaching of your mother:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For they will be a crown of grace for your head, and chain-ornaments about your neck.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** My son, if sinners would take you out of the right way, do not go with them.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** If they say, Come with us; let us make designs against the good, waiting secretly for the upright, without cause;

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Let us overcome them living, like the underworld, and in their strength, as those who go down to death;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Goods of great price will be ours, our houses will be full of wealth;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Take your chance with us, and we will all have one money-bag:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** My son, do not go with them; keep your feet from their ways:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For their feet are running after evil, and they are quick to take a man's life.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Truly, to no purpose is the net stretched out before the eyes of the bird:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And they are secretly waiting for their blood and making ready destruction for themselves.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Such is the fate of everyone who goes in search of profit; it takes away the life of its owners.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Wisdom is crying out in the street; her voice is loud in the open places;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Her words are sounding in the meeting-places, and in the doorways of the town:

<a id="v22"></a>**22** How long, you simple ones, will foolish things be dear to you? and pride a delight to the haters of authority? how long will the foolish go on hating knowledge?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Be turned again by my sharp words: see, I will send the flow of my spirit on you, and make my words clear to you.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Because your ears were shut to my voice; no one gave attention to my out-stretched hand;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** You were not controlled by my guiding, and would have nothing to do with my sharp words:

<a id="v26"></a>**26** So in the day of your trouble I will be laughing; I will make sport of your fear;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** When your fear comes on you like a storm, and your trouble like a rushing wind; when pain and sorrow come on you.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Then I will give no answer to their cries; searching for me early, they will not see me:

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For they were haters of knowledge, and did not give their hearts to the fear of the Lord:

<a id="v30"></a>**30** They had no desire for my teaching, and my words of protest were as nothing to them.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** So the fruit of their way will be their food, and with the designs of their hearts they will be made full.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For the turning back of the simple from teaching will be the cause of their death, and the peace of the foolish will be their destruction.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** But whoever gives ear to me will take his rest safely, living in peace without fear of evil.
