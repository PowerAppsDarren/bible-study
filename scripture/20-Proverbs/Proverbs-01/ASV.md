# Proverbs 1 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The proverbs of Solomon the son of David, king of Israel:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** To know wisdom and instruction; To discern the words of understanding;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** To receive instruction in wise dealing, In righteousness and justice and equity;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** To give prudence to the simple, To the young man knowledge and discretion:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** That the wise man may hear, and increase in learning; And that the man of understanding may attain unto sound counsels:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** To understand a proverb, and a figure, The words of the wise, and their dark sayings.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The fear of Jehovah is the beginning of knowledge; Butthe foolish despise wisdom and instruction.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** My son, hear the instruction of thy father, And forsake not the law of thy mother:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For they shall be a chaplet of grace unto thy head, And chains about thy neck.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** My son, if sinners entice thee, Consent thou not.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** If they say, Come with us, Let us lay wait for blood; Let us lurk privily for the innocent without cause;

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Let us swallow them up alive as Sheol, And whole, as those that go down into the pit;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** We shall find all precious substance; We shall fill our houses with spoil;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Thou shalt cast thy lot among us; We will all have one purse:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** My son, walk not thou in the way with them; Refrain thy foot from their path:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For their feet run to evil, And they make haste to shed blood.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For in vain is the net spread  In the sight of any bird:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And these lay wait for their own blood; They lurk privily for their own lives.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** So are the ways of every one that is greedy of gain; It taketh away the life of the owners thereof.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Wisdom crieth aloud in the street; She uttereth her voice in the broad places;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** She crieth in the chief place of concourse; At the entrance of the gates, In the city, she uttereth her words:

<a id="v22"></a>**22** How long, ye simple ones, will ye love simplicity? And scoffers delight them in scoffing, And fools hate knowledge?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Turn you at my reproof: Behold, I will pour out my spirit upon you; I will make known my words unto you.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Because I have called, and ye have refused; I have stretched out my hand, and no man hath regarded;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** But ye have set at nought all my counsel, And would none of my reproof:

<a id="v26"></a>**26** I also will laugh inthe day ofyour calamity; I will mock when your fear cometh;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** When your fear cometh as a storm, And your calamity cometh on as a whirlwind; When distress and anguish come upon you.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Then will they call upon me, but I will not answer; They will seek me diligently, but they shall not find me:

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For that they hated knowledge, And did not choose the fear of Jehovah,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** They would none of my counsel, They despised all my reproof.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Therefore shall they eat of the fruit of their own way, And be filled with their own devices.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For the backsliding of the simple shall slay them, And the careless ease of fools shall destroy them.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** But whoso hearkeneth unto me shall dwell securely, And shall be quiet without fear of evil.
