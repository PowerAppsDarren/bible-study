# Proverbs 1 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The proverbs of Solomon, the son of David, king of Israel:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** to know wisdom and instruction; to discern the words of understanding;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** to receive instruction in wise dealing, in righteousness, justice, and equity;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** to give prudence to the simple, knowledge and discretion to the young man:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** that the wise man may hear, and increase in learning; that the man of understanding may attain to sound counsel:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** to understand a proverb, and parables, the words and riddles of the wise.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The fear of Yahweh is the beginning of knowledge; but the foolish despise wisdom and instruction.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** My son, listen to your father’s instruction, and don’t forsake your mother’s teaching:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** for they will be a garland to grace your head, and chains around your neck.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** My son, if sinners entice you, don’t consent.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** If they say, “Come with us, Let’s lay in wait for blood; let’s lurk secretly for the innocent without cause;

<a id="v12"></a>**12** let’s swallow them up alive like Sheol, and whole, like those who go down into the pit.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** We’ll find all valuable wealth. We’ll fill our houses with plunder.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** You shall cast your lot among us. We’ll all have one purse.”

<a id="v15"></a>**15** My son, don’t walk on the path with them. Keep your foot from their path,

<a id="v16"></a>**16** for their feet run to evil. They hurry to shed blood.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For in vain is the net spread in the sight of any bird:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** but these lay wait for their own blood. They lurk secretly for their own lives.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** So are the ways of everyone who is greedy for gain. It takes away the life of its owners.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Wisdom calls aloud in the street. She utters her voice in the public squares.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** She calls at the head of noisy places. At the entrance of the city gates, she utters her words:

<a id="v22"></a>**22** “How long, you simple ones, will you love simplicity? How long will mockers delight themselves in mockery, and fools hate knowledge?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Turn at my reproof. Behold, I will pour out my spirit on you. I will make known my words to you.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Because I have called, and you have refused; I have stretched out my hand, and no one has paid attention;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** but you have ignored all my counsel, and wanted none of my reproof;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** I also will laugh at your disaster. I will mock when calamity overtakes you;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** when calamity overtakes you like a storm, when your disaster comes on like a whirlwind; when distress and anguish come on you.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Then will they call on me, but I will not answer. They will seek me diligently, but they will not find me;

<a id="v29"></a>**29** because they hated knowledge, and didn’t choose the fear of Yahweh.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** They wanted none of my counsel. They despised all my reproof.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Therefore they will eat of the fruit of their own way, and be filled with their own schemes.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For the backsliding of the simple will kill them. The careless ease of fools will destroy them.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** But whoever listens to me will dwell securely, and will be at ease, without fear of harm.”
