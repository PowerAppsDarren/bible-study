# Proverbs 29 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** He, that being often reproved hardeneth his neck, shall suddenly be destroyed, and that without remedy.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** When the righteous are in authority, the people rejoice: but when the wicked beareth rule, the people mourn.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Whoso loveth wisdom rejoiceth his father: but he that keepeth company with harlots spendeth his substance.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The king by judgment establisheth the land: but he that receiveth gifts overthroweth it.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A man that flattereth his neighbour spreadeth a net for his feet.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** In the transgression of an evil man there is a snare: but the righteous doth sing and rejoice.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The righteous considereth the cause of the poor: but the wicked regardeth not to know it.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Scornful men bring a city into a snare: but wise men turn away wrath.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** If a wise man contendeth with a foolish man, whether he rage or laugh, there is no rest.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The bloodthirsty hate the upright: but the just seek his soul.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** A fool uttereth all his mind: but a wise man keepeth it in till afterwards.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** If a ruler hearken to lies, all his servants are wicked.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The poor and the deceitful man meet together: the Lord lighteneth both their eyes.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The king that faithfully judgeth the poor, his throne shall be established for ever.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The rod and reproof give wisdom: but a child left to himself bringeth his mother to shame.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** When the wicked are multiplied, transgression increaseth: but the righteous shall see their fall.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Correct thy son, and he shall give thee rest; yea, he shall give delight unto thy soul.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Where there is no vision, the people perish: but he that keepeth the law, happy is he.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A servant will not be corrected by words: for though he understand he will not answer.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Seest thou a man that is hasty in his words? there is more hope of a fool than of him.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He that delicately bringeth up his servant from a child shall have him become his son at the length.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** An angry man stirreth up strife, and a furious man aboundeth in transgression.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** A man’s pride shall bring him low: but honour shall uphold the humble in spirit.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Whoso is partner with a thief hateth his own soul: he heareth cursing, and bewrayeth it not.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The fear of man bringeth a snare: but whoso putteth his trust in the Lord shall be safe.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Many seek the ruler’s favour; but every man’s judgment cometh from the Lord.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** An unjust man is an abomination to the just: and he that is upright in the way is abomination to the wicked.
