# Proverbs 13 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** A wise son listens to his father’s instruction, but a scoffer doesn’t listen to rebuke.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** By the fruit of his lips, a man enjoys good things; but the unfaithful crave violence.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** He who guards his mouth guards his soul. One who opens wide his lips comes to ruin.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The soul of the sluggard desires, and has nothing, but the desire of the diligent shall be fully satisfied.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A righteous man hates lies, but a wicked man brings shame and disgrace.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Righteousness guards the way of integrity, but wickedness overthrows the sinner.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** There are some who pretend to be rich, yet have nothing. There are some who pretend to be poor, yet have great wealth.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The ransom of a man’s life is his riches, but the poor hear no threats.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The light of the righteous shines brightly, but the lamp of the wicked is snuffed out.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Pride only breeds quarrels, but with ones who take advice is wisdom.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Wealth gained dishonestly dwindles away, but he who gathers by hand makes it grow.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Hope deferred makes the heart sick, but when longing is fulfilled, it is a tree of life.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Whoever despises instruction will pay for it, but he who respects a command will be rewarded.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The teaching of the wise is a spring of life, to turn from the snares of death.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Good understanding wins favor; but the way of the unfaithful is hard.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Every prudent man acts from knowledge, but a fool exposes folly.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** A wicked messenger falls into trouble, but a trustworthy envoy gains healing.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Poverty and shame come to him who refuses discipline, but he who heeds correction shall be honored.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Longing fulfilled is sweet to the soul, but fools detest turning from evil.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** One who walks with wise men grows wise, but a companion of fools suffers harm.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Misfortune pursues sinners, but prosperity rewards the righteous.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** A good man leaves an inheritance to his children’s children, but the wealth of the sinner is stored for the righteous.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** An abundance of food is in poor people’s fields, but injustice sweeps it away.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** One who spares the rod hates his son, but one who loves him is careful to discipline him.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The righteous one eats to the satisfying of his soul, but the belly of the wicked goes hungry.
