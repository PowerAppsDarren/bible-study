# Proverbs 16 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The designs of the heart are man's, but the answer of the tongue comes from the Lord.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** All a man's ways are clean to himself; but the Lord puts men's spirits into his scales.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Put your works into the hands of the Lord, and your purposes will be made certain.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The Lord has made everything for his purpose, even the sinner for the day of evil.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Everyone who has pride in his heart is disgusting to the Lord: he will certainly not go free from punishment.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** By mercy and good faith evil-doing is taken away: and by the fear of the Lord men are turned away from evil.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** When a man's ways are pleasing to the Lord, he makes even his haters be at peace with him.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Better is a little with righteousness, than great wealth with wrongdoing.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** A man may make designs for his way, but the Lord is the guide of his steps.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Decision is in the lips of the king: his mouth will not go wrong in judging.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** True measures and scales are the Lord's: all the weights of the bag are his work.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Evil-doing is disgusting to kings: for the seat of the ruler is based on righteousness.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Lips of righteousness are the delight of kings; and he who says what is upright is dear to him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The wrath of the king is like those who give news of death, but a wise man will put peace in place of it.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** In the light of the king's face there is life; and his approval is like a cloud of spring rain.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** How much better it is to get wisdom than gold! and to get knowledge is more to be desired than silver.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The highway of the upright is to be turned away from evil: he who takes care of his way will keep his soul.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Pride goes before destruction, and a stiff spirit before a fall.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Better it is to have a gentle spirit with the poor, than to take part in the rewards of war with men of pride.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He who gives attention to the law of right will get good; and whoever puts his faith in the Lord is happy.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The wise-hearted will be named men of good sense: and by pleasing words learning is increased.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Wisdom is a fountain of life to him who has it; but the punishment of the foolish is their foolish behaviour.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The heart of the wise man is the teacher of his mouth, and gives increased learning to his lips.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Pleasing words are like honey, sweet to the soul and new life to the bones.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** There is a way which seems straight before a man, but its end is the ways of death.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** The desire of the working man is working for him, for his need of food is driving him on.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** A good-for-nothing man is a designer of evil, and in his lips there is a burning fire.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A man of twisted purposes is a cause of fighting everywhere: and he who says evil secretly makes trouble between friends.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** A violent man puts desire of evil into his neighbour's mind, and makes him go in a way which is not good.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** He whose eyes are shut is a man of twisted purposes, and he who keeps his lips shut tight makes evil come about.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The grey head is a crown of glory, if it is seen in the way of righteousness.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He who is slow to be angry is better than a man of war, and he who has control over his spirit than he who takes a town.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** A thing may be put to the decision of chance, but it comes about through the Lord.
