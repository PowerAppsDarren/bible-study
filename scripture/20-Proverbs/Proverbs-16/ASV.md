# Proverbs 16 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The plans of the heart belong to man; But the answer of the tongue is from Jehovah.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** All the ways of a man are clean in his own eyes; But Jehovah weigheth the spirits.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Commit thy works unto Jehovah, And thy purposes shall be established.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Jehovah hath made everything for its own end; Yea, even the wicked for the day of evil.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Every one that is proud in heart is an abomination to Jehovah: Thoughhandjoinin hand, he shall not be unpunished.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** By mercy and truth iniquity is atoned for; And by the fear of Jehovah men depart from evil.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** When a man’s ways please Jehovah, He maketh even his enemies to be at peace with him.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Better is a little, with righteousness, Than great revenues with injustice.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** A man’s heart deviseth his way; But Jehovah directeth his steps.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** A divine sentence is in the lips of the king; His mouth shall not transgress in judgment.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** A just balance and scales are Jehovah’s; All the weights of the bag are his work.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** It is an abomination to kings to commit wickedness; For the throne is established by righteousness.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Righteous lips are the delight of kings; And they love him that speaketh right.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The wrath of a king isasmessengers of death; But a wise man will pacify it.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** In the light of the king’s countenance is life; And his favor is as a cloud of the latter rain.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** How much better is it to get wisdom than gold! Yea, to get understanding is rather to be chosen than silver.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The highway of the upright is to depart from evil: He that keepeth his way preserveth his soul.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Pridegoethbefore destruction, And a haughty spirit before a fall.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Better it is to be of a lowly spirit with the poor, Than to divide the spoil with the proud.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He that giveth heed unto the word shall find good; And whoso trusteth in Jehovah, happy is he.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The wise in heart shall be called prudent; And the sweetness of the lips increaseth learning.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Understanding is a well-spring of life unto him that hath it; But the correction of fools istheirfolly.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The heart of the wise instructeth his mouth, And addeth learning to his lips.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Pleasant words areas a honeycomb, Sweet to the soul, and health to the bones.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** There is a way which seemeth right unto a man, But the end thereof are the ways of death.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** The appetite of the laboring man laboreth for him; For his mouth urgeth him thereto.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** A worthless man deviseth mischief; And in his lips there is as a scorching fire.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A perverse man scattereth abroad strife; And a whisperer separateth chief friends.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** A man of violence enticeth his neighbor, And leadeth him in a way that is not good.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** He that shutteth his eyes, it is to devise perverse things: He that compresseth his lips bringeth evil to pass.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The hoary head is a crown of glory; It shall be found in the way of righteousness.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He that is slow to anger is better than the mighty; And he that ruleth his spirit, than he that taketh a city.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The lot is cast into the lap; But the whole disposing thereof is of Jehovah.
