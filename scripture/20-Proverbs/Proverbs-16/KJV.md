# Proverbs 16 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The preparations of the heart in man, and the answer of the tongue, is from the Lord.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** All the ways of a man are clean in his own eyes; but the Lord weigheth the spirits.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Commit thy works unto the Lord, and thy thoughts shall be established.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The Lord hath made all things for himself: yea, even the wicked for the day of evil.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Every one that is proud in heart is an abomination to the Lord: though hand join in hand, he shall not be unpunished.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** By mercy and truth iniquity is purged: and by the fear of the Lord men depart from evil.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** When a man’s ways please the Lord, he maketh even his enemies to be at peace with him.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Better is a little with righteousness than great revenues without right.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** A man’s heart deviseth his way: but the Lord directeth his steps.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** A divine sentence is in the lips of the king: his mouth transgresseth not in judgment.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** A just weight and balance are the Lord’s: all the weights of the bag are his work.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** It is an abomination to kings to commit wickedness: for the throne is established by righteousness.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Righteous lips are the delight of kings; and they love him that speaketh right.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The wrath of a king is as messengers of death: but a wise man will pacify it.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** In the light of the king’s countenance is life; and his favour is as a cloud of the latter rain.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** How much better is it to get wisdom than gold! and to get understanding rather to be chosen than silver!

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The highway of the upright is to depart from evil: he that keepeth his way preserveth his soul.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Pride goeth before destruction, and an haughty spirit before a fall.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Better it is to be of an humble spirit with the lowly, than to divide the spoil with the proud.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He that handleth a matter wisely shall find good: and whoso trusteth in the Lord, happy is he.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The wise in heart shall be called prudent: and the sweetness of the lips increaseth learning.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Understanding is a wellspring of life unto him that hath it: but the instruction of fools is folly.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The heart of the wise teacheth his mouth, and addeth learning to his lips.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Pleasant words are as an honeycomb, sweet to the soul, and health to the bones.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** There is a way that seemeth right unto a man, but the end thereof are the ways of death.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He that laboureth laboureth for himself; for his mouth craveth it of him.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** An ungodly man diggeth up evil: and in his lips there is as a burning fire.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A froward man soweth strife: and a whisperer separateth chief friends.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** A violent man enticeth his neighbour, and leadeth him into the way that is not good.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** He shutteth his eyes to devise froward things: moving his lips he bringeth evil to pass.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The hoary head is a crown of glory, if it be found in the way of righteousness.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He that is slow to anger is better than the mighty; and he that ruleth his spirit than he that taketh a city.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The lot is cast into the lap; but the whole disposing thereof is of the Lord.
