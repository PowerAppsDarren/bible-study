# Proverbs 3 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My son, forget not my law; but let thine heart keep my commandments:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For length of days, and long life, and peace, shall they add to thee.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Let not mercy and truth forsake thee: bind them about thy neck; write them upon the table of thine heart:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** So shalt thou find favour and good understanding in the sight of God and man.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Trust in the Lord with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** In all thy ways acknowledge him, and he shall direct thy paths.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Be not wise in thine own eyes: fear the Lord, and depart from evil.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** It shall be health to thy navel, and marrow to thy bones.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Honour the Lord with thy substance, and with the firstfruits of all thine increase:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** So shall thy barns be filled with plenty, and thy presses shall burst out with new wine.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** My son, despise not the chastening of the Lord; neither be weary of his correction:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For whom the Lord loveth he correcteth; even as a father the son in whom he delighteth.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Happy is the man that findeth wisdom, and the man that getteth understanding.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For the merchandise of it is better than the merchandise of silver, and the gain thereof than fine gold.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** She is more precious than rubies: and all the things thou canst desire are not to be compared unto her.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Length of days is in her right hand; and in her left hand riches and honour.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Her ways are ways of pleasantness, and all her paths are peace.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** She is a tree of life to them that lay hold upon her: and happy is every one that retaineth her.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The Lord by wisdom hath founded the earth; by understanding hath he established the heavens.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** By his knowledge the depths are broken up, and the clouds drop down the dew.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** My son, let not them depart from thine eyes: keep sound wisdom and discretion:

<a id="v22"></a>**22** So shall they be life unto thy soul, and grace to thy neck.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Then shalt thou walk in thy way safely, and thy foot shall not stumble.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** When thou liest down, thou shalt not be afraid: yea, thou shalt lie down, and thy sleep shall be sweet.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Be not afraid of sudden fear, neither of the desolation of the wicked, when it cometh.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For the Lord shall be thy confidence, and shall keep thy foot from being taken.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Withhold not good from them to whom it is due, when it is in the power of thine hand to do it.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Say not unto thy neighbour, Go, and come again, and to morrow I will give; when thou hast it by thee.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Devise not evil against thy neighbour, seeing he dwelleth securely by thee.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Strive not with a man without cause, if he have done thee no harm.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Envy thou not the oppressor, and choose none of his ways.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For the froward is abomination to the Lord: but his secret is with the righteous.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The curse of the Lord is in the house of the wicked: but he blesseth the habitation of the just.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Surely he scorneth the scorners: but he giveth grace unto the lowly.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** The wise shall inherit glory: but shame shall be the promotion of fools.
