# Proverbs 3 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My son, keep my teaching in your memory, and my rules in your heart:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For they will give you increase of days, years of life, and peace.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Let not mercy and good faith go from you; let them be hanging round your neck, recorded on your heart;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** So you will have grace and a good name in the eyes of God and men.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Put all your hope in God, not looking to your reason for support.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** In all your ways give ear to him, and he will make straight your footsteps.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Put no high value on your wisdom: let the fear of the Lord be before you, and keep yourself from evil:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** This will give strength to your flesh, and new life to your bones.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Give honour to the Lord with your wealth, and with the first-fruits of all your increase:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** So your store-houses will be full of grain, and your vessels overflowing with new wine.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** My son, do not make your heart hard against the Lord's teaching; do not be made angry by his training:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For to those who are dear to him the Lord says sharp words, and makes the son in whom he has delight undergo pain.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Happy is the man who makes discovery of wisdom, and he who gets knowledge.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For trading in it is better than trading in silver, and its profit greater than bright gold.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** She is of more value than jewels, and nothing for which you may have a desire is fair in comparison with her.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Long life is in her right hand, and in her left are wealth and honour.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Her ways are ways of delight, and all her goings are peace.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** She is a tree of life to all who take her in their hands, and happy is everyone who keeps her.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The Lord by wisdom put in position the bases of the earth; by reason he put the heavens in their place.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** By his knowledge the deep was parted, and dew came dropping from the skies.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** My son, keep good sense, and do not let wise purpose go from your eyes.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** So they will be life for your soul, and grace for your neck.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Then you will go safely on your way, and your feet will have no cause for slipping.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** When you take your rest you will have no fear, and on your bed sleep will be sweet to you.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Have no fear of sudden danger, or of the storm which will come on evil-doers:

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For the Lord will be your hope, and will keep your foot from being taken in the net.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Do not keep back good from those who have a right to it, when it is in the power of your hand to do it.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Say not to your neighbour, Go, and come again, and tomorrow I will give; when you have it by you at the time.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Do not make evil designs against your neighbour, when he is living with you without fear.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Do not take up a cause at law against a man for nothing, if he has done you no wrong.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Have no envy of the violent man, or take any of his ways as an example.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For the wrong-hearted man is hated by the Lord, but he is a friend to the upright.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The curse of the Lord is on the house of the evil-doer, but his blessing is on the tent of the upright.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** He makes sport of the men of pride, but he gives grace to the gentle-hearted.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** The wise will have glory for their heritage, but shame will be the reward of the foolish.
