# Proverbs 5 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My son, attend unto my wisdom, and bow thine ear to my understanding:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** That thou mayest regard discretion, and that thy lips may keep knowledge.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For the lips of a strange woman drop as an honeycomb, and her mouth is smoother than oil:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But her end is bitter as wormwood, sharp as a twoedged sword.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Her feet go down to death; her steps take hold on hell.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Lest thou shouldest ponder the path of life, her ways are moveable, that thou canst not know them.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Hear me now therefore, O ye children, and depart not from the words of my mouth.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Remove thy way far from her, and come not nigh the door of her house:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Lest thou give thine honour unto others, and thy years unto the cruel:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Lest strangers be filled with thy wealth; and thy labours be in the house of a stranger;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And thou mourn at the last, when thy flesh and thy body are consumed,

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And say, How have I hated instruction, and my heart despised reproof;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And have not obeyed the voice of my teachers, nor inclined mine ear to them that instructed me!

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I was almost in all evil in the midst of the congregation and assembly.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Drink waters out of thine own cistern, and running waters out of thine own well.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Let thy fountains be dispersed abroad, and rivers of waters in the streets.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Let them be only thine own, and not strangers’ with thee.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Let thy fountain be blessed: and rejoice with the wife of thy youth.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Let her be as the loving hind and pleasant roe; let her breasts satisfy thee at all times; and be thou ravished always with her love.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And why wilt thou, my son, be ravished with a strange woman, and embrace the bosom of a stranger?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For the ways of man are before the eyes of the Lord, and he pondereth all his goings.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** His own iniquities shall take the wicked himself, and he shall be holden with the cords of his sins.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He shall die without instruction; and in the greatness of his folly he shall go astray.
