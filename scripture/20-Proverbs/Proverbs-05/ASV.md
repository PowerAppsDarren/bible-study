# Proverbs 5 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My son, attend unto my wisdom; Incline thine ear to my understanding:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** That thou mayest preserve discretion, And that thy lips may keep knowledge.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For the lips of a strange woman drop honey, And her mouth is smoother than oil:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But in the end she is bitter as wormwood, Sharp as a two-edged sword.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Her feet go down to death; Her steps take hold on Sheol;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** So that she findeth not the level path of life: Her ways are unstable, andshe knoweth it not.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Now therefore, mysons, hearken unto me, And depart not from the words of my mouth.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Remove thy way far from her, And come not nigh the door of her house;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Lest thou give thine honor unto others, And thy years unto the cruel;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Lest strangers be filled with thy strength, And thy labors be in the house of an alien,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And thou mourn at thy latter end, When thy flesh and thy body are consumed,

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And say, How have I hated instruction, And my heart despised reproof;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Neither have I obeyed the voice of my teachers, Nor inclined mine ear to them that instructed me!

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I was well-nigh in all evil In the midst of the assembly and congregation.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Drink waters out of thine own cistern, And running waters out of thine own well.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Should thy springs be dispersed abroad, And streams of water in the streets?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Let them be for thyself alone, And not for strangers with thee.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Let thy fountain be blessed; And rejoice in the wife of thy youth.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Asa loving hind and a pleasant doe, Let her breasts satisfy thee at all times; And be thou ravished always with her love.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** For why shouldest thou, my son, be ravished with a strange woman, And embrace the bosom of a foreigner?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For the ways of man are before the eyes of Jehovah; And he maketh level all his paths.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** His own iniquities shall take the wicked, And he shall be holden with the cords of his sin.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He shall die for lack of instruction; And in the greatness of his folly he shall go astray.
