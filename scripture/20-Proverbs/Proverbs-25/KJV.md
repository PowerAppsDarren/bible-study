# Proverbs 25 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** These are also proverbs of Solomon, which the men of Hezekiah king of Judah copied out.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** It is the glory of God to conceal a thing: but the honour of kings is to search out a matter.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The heaven for height, and the earth for depth, and the heart of kings is unsearchable.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Take away the dross from the silver, and there shall come forth a vessel for the finer.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Take away the wicked from before the king, and his throne shall be established in righteousness.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Put not forth thyself in the presence of the king, and stand not in the place of great men:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For better it is that it be said unto thee, Come up hither; than that thou shouldest be put lower in the presence of the prince whom thine eyes have seen.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Go not forth hastily to strive, lest thou know not what to do in the end thereof, when thy neighbour hath put thee to shame.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Debate thy cause with thy neighbour himself; and discover not a secret to another:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Lest he that heareth it put thee to shame, and thine infamy turn not away.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** A word fitly spoken is like apples of gold in pictures of silver.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** As an earring of gold, and an ornament of fine gold, so is a wise reprover upon an obedient ear.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** As the cold of snow in the time of harvest, so is a faithful messenger to them that send him: for he refresheth the soul of his masters.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Whoso boasteth himself of a false gift is like clouds and wind without rain.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** By long forbearing is a prince persuaded, and a soft tongue breaketh the bone.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Hast thou found honey? eat so much as is sufficient for thee, lest thou be filled therewith, and vomit it.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Withdraw thy foot from thy neighbour’s house; lest he be weary of thee, and so hate thee.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** A man that beareth false witness against his neighbour is a maul, and a sword, and a sharp arrow.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Confidence in an unfaithful man in time of trouble is like a broken tooth, and a foot out of joint.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** As he that taketh away a garment in cold weather, and as vinegar upon nitre, so is he that singeth songs to an heavy heart.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** If thine enemy be hungry, give him bread to eat; and if he be thirsty, give him water to drink:

<a id="v22"></a>**22** For thou shalt heap coals of fire upon his head, and the Lord shall reward thee.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The north wind driveth away rain: so doth an angry countenance a backbiting tongue.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** It is better to dwell in the corner of the housetop, than with a brawling woman and in a wide house.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** As cold waters to a thirsty soul, so is good news from a far country.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** A righteous man falling down before the wicked is as a troubled fountain, and a corrupt spring.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** It is not good to eat much honey: so for men to search their own glory is not glory.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** He that hath no rule over his own spirit is like a city that is broken down, and without walls.
