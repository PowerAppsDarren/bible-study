# Proverbs 17 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Better is a dry morsel, and quietness therewith, than an house full of sacrifices with strife.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** A wise servant shall have rule over a son that causeth shame, and shall have part of the inheritance among the brethren.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The fining pot is for silver, and the furnace for gold: but the Lord trieth the hearts.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** A wicked doer giveth heed to false lips; and a liar giveth ear to a naughty tongue.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Whoso mocketh the poor reproacheth his Maker: and he that is glad at calamities shall not be unpunished.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Children’s children are the crown of old men; and the glory of children are their fathers.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Excellent speech becometh not a fool: much less do lying lips a prince.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** A gift is as a precious stone in the eyes of him that hath it: whithersoever it turneth, it prospereth.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He that covereth a transgression seeketh love; but he that repeateth a matter separateth very friends.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** A reproof entereth more into a wise man than an hundred stripes into a fool.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** An evil man seeketh only rebellion: therefore a cruel messenger shall be sent against him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Let a bear robbed of her whelps meet a man, rather than a fool in his folly.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Whoso rewardeth evil for good, evil shall not depart from his house.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The beginning of strife is as when one letteth out water: therefore leave off contention, before it be meddled with.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He that justifieth the wicked, and he that condemneth the just, even they both are abomination to the Lord.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Wherefore is there a price in the hand of a fool to get wisdom, seeing he hath no heart to it?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** A friend loveth at all times, and a brother is born for adversity.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** A man void of understanding striketh hands, and becometh surety in the presence of his friend.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He loveth transgression that loveth strife: and he that exalteth his gate seeketh destruction.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He that hath a froward heart findeth no good: and he that hath a perverse tongue falleth into mischief.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He that begetteth a fool doeth it to his sorrow: and the father of a fool hath no joy.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** A merry heart doeth good like a medicine: but a broken spirit drieth the bones.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** A wicked man taketh a gift out of the bosom to pervert the ways of judgment.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Wisdom is before him that hath understanding; but the eyes of a fool are in the ends of the earth.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** A foolish son is a grief to his father, and bitterness to her that bare him.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Also to punish the just is not good, nor to strike princes for equity.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He that hath knowledge spareth his words: and a man of understanding is of an excellent spirit.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Even a fool, when he holdeth his peace, is counted wise: and he that shutteth his lips is esteemed a man of understanding.
