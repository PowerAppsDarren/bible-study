# Proverbs 17 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Better a bit of dry bread in peace, than a house full of feasting and violent behaviour.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** A servant who does wisely will have rule over a son causing shame, and will have his part in the heritage among brothers.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The heating-pot is for silver and the oven-fire for gold, but the Lord is the tester of hearts.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** A wrongdoer gives attention to evil lips, and a man of deceit gives ear to a damaging tongue.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Whoever makes sport of the poor puts shame on his Maker; and he who is glad because of trouble will not go free from punishment.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Children's children are the crown of old men, and the glory of children is their fathers.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Fair words are not to be looked for from a foolish man, much less are false lips in a ruler.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** An offering of money is like a stone of great price in the eyes of him who has it: wherever he goes, he does well.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He who keeps a sin covered is looking for love; but he who keeps on talking of a thing makes division between friends.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** A word of protest goes deeper into one who has sense than a hundred blows into a foolish man.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** An uncontrolled man is only looking for trouble, so a cruel servant will be sent against him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** It is better to come face to face with a bear whose young ones have been taken away than with a foolish man acting foolishly.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** If anyone gives back evil for good, evil will never go away from his house.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The start of fighting is like the letting out of water: so give up before it comes to blows.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He who gives a decision for the evil-doer and he who gives a decision against the upright, are equally disgusting to the Lord.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** How will money in the hand of the foolish get him wisdom, seeing that he has no sense?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** A friend is loving at all times, and becomes a brother in times of trouble.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** A man without sense gives his hand in an agreement, and makes himself responsible before his neighbour.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The lover of fighting is a lover of sin: he who makes high his doorway is looking for destruction.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Nothing good comes to him whose heart is fixed on evil purposes: and he who has an evil tongue will come to trouble.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He who has an unwise son gets sorrow for himself, and the father of a foolish son has no joy.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** A glad heart makes a healthy body, but a crushed spirit makes the bones dry.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** A sinner takes an offering out of his robe, to get a decision for himself in a cause.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Wisdom is before the face of him who has sense; but the eyes of the foolish are on the ends of the earth.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** A foolish son is a grief to his father, and bitter pain to her who gave him birth.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** To give punishment to the upright is not good, or to give blows to the noble for their righteousness.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He who has knowledge says little: and he who has a calm spirit is a man of good sense.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Even the foolish man, when he keeps quiet, is taken to be wise: when his lips are shut he is credited with good sense.
