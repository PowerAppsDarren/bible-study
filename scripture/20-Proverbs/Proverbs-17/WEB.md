# Proverbs 17 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Better is a dry morsel with quietness, than a house full of feasting with strife.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** A servant who deals wisely will rule over a son who causes shame, and shall have a part in the inheritance among the brothers.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The refining pot is for silver, and the furnace for gold, but Yahweh tests the hearts.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** An evildoer heeds wicked lips. A liar gives ear to a mischievous tongue.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Whoever mocks the poor reproaches his Maker. He who is glad at calamity shall not be unpunished.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Children’s children are the crown of old men; the glory of children are their parents.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Arrogant speech isn’t fitting for a fool, much less do lying lips fit a prince.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** A bribe is a precious stone in the eyes of him who gives it; wherever he turns, he prospers.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He who covers an offense promotes love; but he who repeats a matter separates best friends.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** A rebuke enters deeper into one who has understanding than a hundred lashes into a fool.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** An evil man seeks only rebellion; therefore a cruel messenger shall be sent against him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Let a bear robbed of her cubs meet a man, rather than a fool in his folly.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Whoever rewards evil for good, evil shall not depart from his house.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The beginning of strife is like breaching a dam, therefore stop contention before quarreling breaks out.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He who justifies the wicked, and he who condemns the righteous, both of them alike are an abomination to Yahweh.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Why is there money in the hand of a fool to buy wisdom, since he has no understanding?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** A friend loves at all times; and a brother is born for adversity.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** A man void of understanding strikes hands, and becomes collateral in the presence of his neighbor.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He who loves disobedience loves strife. One who builds a high gate seeks destruction.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** One who has a perverse heart doesn’t find prosperity, and one who has a deceitful tongue falls into trouble.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He who becomes the father of a fool grieves. The father of a fool has no joy.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** A cheerful heart makes good medicine, but a crushed spirit dries up the bones.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** A wicked man receives a bribe in secret, to pervert the ways of justice.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Wisdom is before the face of one who has understanding, but the eyes of a fool wander to the ends of the earth.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** A foolish son brings grief to his father, and bitterness to her who bore him.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Also to punish the righteous is not good, nor to flog officials for their integrity.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He who spares his words has knowledge. He who is even tempered is a man of understanding.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Even a fool, when he keeps silent, is counted wise. When he shuts his lips, he is thought to be discerning.
