# Proverbs 17 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Better is a dry morsel, and quietness therewith, Than a house full of feasting with strife.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** A servant that dealeth wisely shall have rule over a son that causeth shame, And shall have part in the inheritance among the brethren.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The refining pot is for silver, and the furnace for gold; But Jehovah trieth the hearts.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** An evil-doer giveth heed to wicked lips; Anda liar giveth ear to a mischievous tongue.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Whoso mocketh the poor reproacheth his Maker; Andhe that is glad at calamity shall not be unpunished.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Children’s children are the crown of old men; And the glory of children are their fathers.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Excellent speech becometh not a fool; Much less do lying lips a prince.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** A bribe isasa precious stone in the eyes of him that hath it; Whithersoever it turneth, it prospereth.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He that covereth a transgression seeketh love; But he that harpeth on a matter separateth chief friends.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** A rebuke entereth deeper into one that hath understanding Than a hundred stripes into a fool.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** An evil man seeketh only rebellion; Therefore a cruel messenger shall be sent against him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Let a bear robbed of her whelps meet a man, Rather than a fool in his folly.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Whoso rewardeth evil for good, Evil shall not depart from his house.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The beginning of strife isas when one letteth out water: Therefore leave off contention, before there is quarrelling.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He that justifieth the wicked, and he that condemneth the righteous, Both of them alike are an abomination to Jehovah.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Wherefore is there a price in the hand of a fool to buy wisdom, Seeing he hath no understanding?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** A friend loveth at all times; And a brother is born for adversity.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** A man void of understanding striketh hands, And becometh surety in the presence of his neighbor.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He loveth transgression that loveth strife: He that raiseth high his gate seeketh destruction.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He that hath a wayward heart findeth no good; And he that hath a perverse tongue falleth into mischief.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He that begetteth a fool doeth it to his sorrow; And the father of a fool hath no joy.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** A cheerful heart is a good medicine; But a broken spirit drieth up the bones.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** A wicked man receiveth a bribe out of the bosom, To pervert the ways of justice.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Wisdom is before the face of him that hath understanding; But the eyes of a fool are in the ends of the earth.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** A foolish son is a grief to his father, And bitterness to her that bare him.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Also to punish the righteous is not good, Norto smite the noble fortheiruprightness.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He that spareth his words hath knowledge; And he that is of a cool spirit is a man of understanding.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Even a fool, when he holdeth his peace, is counted wise; When he shutteth his lips, he is esteemed as prudent.
