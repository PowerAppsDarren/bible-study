# Proverbs 22 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** A good name is rather to be chosen than great riches, and loving favour rather than silver and gold.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The rich and poor meet together: the Lord is the maker of them all.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A prudent man foreseeth the evil, and hideth himself: but the simple pass on, and are punished.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** By humility and the fear of the Lord are riches, and honour, and life.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Thorns and snares are in the way of the froward: he that doth keep his soul shall be far from them.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Train up a child in the way he should go: and when he is old, he will not depart from it.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The rich ruleth over the poor, and the borrower is servant to the lender.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He that soweth iniquity shall reap vanity: and the rod of his anger shall fail.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He that hath a bountiful eye shall be blessed; for he giveth of his bread to the poor.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Cast out the scorner, and contention shall go out; yea, strife and reproach shall cease.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He that loveth pureness of heart, for the grace of his lips the king shall be his friend.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The eyes of the Lord preserve knowledge, and he overthroweth the words of the transgressor.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The slothful man saith, There is a lion without, I shall be slain in the streets.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The mouth of strange women is a deep pit: he that is abhorred of the Lord shall fall therein.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Foolishness is bound in the heart of a child; but the rod of correction shall drive it far from him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He that oppresseth the poor to increase his riches, and he that giveth to the rich, shall surely come to want.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Bow down thine ear, and hear the words of the wise, and apply thine heart unto my knowledge.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For it is a pleasant thing if thou keep them within thee; they shall withal be fitted in thy lips.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** That thy trust may be in the Lord, I have made known to thee this day, even to thee.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Have not I written to thee excellent things in counsels and knowledge,

<a id="v21"></a>**21** That I might make thee know the certainty of the words of truth; that thou mightest answer the words of truth to them that send unto thee?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Rob not the poor, because he is poor: neither oppress the afflicted in the gate:

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For the Lord will plead their cause, and spoil the soul of those that spoiled them.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Make no friendship with an angry man; and with a furious man thou shalt not go:

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Lest thou learn his ways, and get a snare to thy soul.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Be not thou one of them that strike hands, or of them that are sureties for debts.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** If thou hast nothing to pay, why should he take away thy bed from under thee?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Remove not the ancient landmark, which thy fathers have set.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Seest thou a man diligent in his business? he shall stand before kings; he shall not stand before mean men.
