# Proverbs 22 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** A good name is more desirable than great riches, and loving favor is better than silver and gold.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The rich and the poor have this in common: Yahweh is the maker of them all.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A prudent man sees danger, and hides himself; but the simple pass on, and suffer for it.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The result of humility and the fear of Yahweh is wealth, honor, and life.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Thorns and snares are in the path of the wicked: whoever guards his soul stays from them.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Train up a child in the way he should go, and when he is old he will not depart from it.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The rich rule over the poor. The borrower is servant to the lender.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He who sows wickedness reaps trouble, and the rod of his fury will be destroyed.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He who has a generous eye will be blessed; for he shares his food with the poor.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Drive out the mocker, and strife will go out; yes, quarrels and insults will stop.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He who loves purity of heart and speaks gracefully is the king’s friend.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The eyes of Yahweh watch over knowledge; but he frustrates the words of the unfaithful.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The sluggard says, “There is a lion outside! I will be killed in the streets!”

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The mouth of an adulteress is a deep pit: he who is under Yahweh’s wrath will fall into it.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Folly is bound up in the heart of a child: the rod of discipline drives it far from him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Whoever oppresses the poor for his own increase and whoever gives to the rich, both come to poverty.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Turn your ear, and listen to the words of the wise. Apply your heart to my teaching.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For it is a pleasant thing if you keep them within you, if all of them are ready on your lips.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** That your trust may be in Yahweh, I teach you today, even you.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Haven’t I written to you thirty excellent things of counsel and knowledge,

<a id="v21"></a>**21** To teach you truth, reliable words, to give sound answers to the ones who sent you?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Don’t exploit the poor, because he is poor; and don’t crush the needy in court;

<a id="v23"></a>**23** for Yahweh will plead their case, and plunder the life of those who plunder them.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Don’t befriend a hot-tempered man, and don’t associate with one who harbors anger:

<a id="v25"></a>**25** lest you learn his ways, and ensnare your soul.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Don’t you be one of those who strike hands, of those who are collateral for debts.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** If you don’t have means to pay, why should he take away your bed from under you?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Don’t move the ancient boundary stone, which your fathers have set up.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Do you see a man skilled in his work? He will serve kings. He won’t serve obscure men.
