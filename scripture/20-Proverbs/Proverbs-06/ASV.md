# Proverbs 6 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My son, if thou art become surety for thy neighbor, If thou hast stricken thy hands for a stranger;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Thou art snared with the words of thy mouth, Thou art taken with the words of thy mouth.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Do this now, my son, and deliver thyself, Seeing thou art come into the hand of thy neighbor: Go, humble thyself, and importune thy neighbor;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Give not sleep to thine eyes, Nor slumber to thine eyelids;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Deliver thyself as a roe from the hand of the hunter, And as a bird from the hand of the fowler.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Go to the ant, thou sluggard; Consider her ways, and be wise:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Which having no chief, Overseer, or ruler,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Provideth her bread in the summer, And gathereth her food in the harvest.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** How long wilt thou sleep, O sluggard? When wilt thou arise out of thy sleep?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Yeta little sleep, a little slumber, A little folding of the hands to sleep:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** So shall thy poverty come as a robber, And thy want as an armed man.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** A worthless person, a man of iniquity, Is he that walketh with a perverse mouth;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** That winketh with his eyes, that speaketh with his feet, That maketh signs with his fingers;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** In whose heart is perverseness, Who deviseth evil continually, Who soweth discord.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Therefore shall his calamity come suddenly; On a sudden shall he be broken, and that without remedy.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** There are six things which Jehovah hateth; Yea, seven which are an abomination unto him:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Haughty eyes, a lying tongue, And hands that shed innocent blood;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** A heart that deviseth wicked purposes, Feet that are swift in running to mischief,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A false witness that uttereth lies, And he that soweth discord among brethren.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** My son, keep the commandment of thy father, And forsake not the law of thy mother:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Bind them continually upon thy heart; Tie them about thy neck.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** When thou walkest, it shall lead thee; When thou sleepest, it shall watch over thee; And when thou awakest, it shall talk with thee.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For the commandment is a lamp; and the law is light; And reproofs of instruction are the way of life:

<a id="v24"></a>**24** To keep thee from the evil woman, From the flattery of the foreigner’s tongue.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Lust not after her beauty in thy heart; Neither let her take thee with her eyelids.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For on account of a harlota man is broughtto a piece of bread; And the adulteress hunteth for the precious life.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Can a man take fire in his bosom, And his clothes not be burned?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Or can one walk upon hot coals, And his feet not be scorched?

<a id="v29"></a>**29** So he that goeth in to his neighbor’s wife; Whosoever toucheth her shall not be unpunished.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Men do not despise a thief, if he steal To satisfy himself when he is hungry:

<a id="v31"></a>**31** But if he be found, he shall restore sevenfold; He shall give all the substance of his house.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He that committeth adultery with a woman is void of understanding: He doeth it who would destroy his own soul.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Wounds and dishonor shall he get; And his reproach shall not be wiped away.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** For jealousy is the rage of a man; And he will not spare in the day of vengeance.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** He will not regard any ransom; Neither will he rest content, though thou givest many gifts.
