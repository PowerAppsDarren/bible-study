# Proverbs 6 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My son, if you have made yourself responsible for your neighbour, or given your word for another,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** You are taken as in a net by the words of your mouth, the sayings of your lips have overcome you.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Do this, my son, and make yourself free, because you have come into the power of your neighbour; go without waiting, and make a strong request to your neighbour.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Give no sleep to your eyes, or rest to them;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Make yourself free, like the roe from the hand of the archer, and the bird from him who puts a net for her.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Go to the ant, you hater of work; give thought to her ways and be wise:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Having no chief, overseer, or ruler,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** She gets her meat in the summer, storing up food at the time of the grain-cutting.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** How long will you be sleeping, O hater of work? when will you get up from your sleep?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** A little sleep, a little rest, a little folding of the hands in sleep:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Then loss will come on you like an outlaw, and your need like an armed man

<a id="v12"></a>**12** A good-for-nothing man is an evil-doer; he goes on his way causing trouble with false words;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Making signs with his eyes, rubbing with his feet, and giving news with his fingers;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** His mind is ever designing evil: he lets loose violent acts.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** For this cause his downfall will be sudden; quickly he will be broken, and there will be no help for him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Six things are hated by the Lord; seven things are disgusting to him:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Eyes of pride, a false tongue, hands which take life without cause;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** A heart full of evil designs, feet which are quick in running after sin;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A false witness, breathing out untrue words, and one who lets loose violent acts among brothers.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** My son, keep the rule of your father, and have in memory the teaching of your mother:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Keep them ever folded in your heart, and have them hanging round your neck.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** In your walking, it will be your guide; when you are sleeping, it will keep watch over you; when you are awake, it will have talk with you.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For the rule is a light, and the teaching a shining light; and the guiding words of training are the way of life.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** They will keep you from the evil woman, from the smooth tongue of the strange woman.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Let not your heart's desire go after her fair body; let not her eyes take you prisoner.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For a loose woman is looking for a cake of bread, but another man's wife goes after one's very life.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** May a man take fire to his breast without burning his clothing?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Or may one go on lighted coals, and his feet not be burned?

<a id="v29"></a>**29** So it is with him who goes in to his neighbour's wife; he who has anything to do with her will not go free from punishment.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Men do not have a low opinion of a thief who takes food when he is in need of it:

<a id="v31"></a>**31** But if he is taken in the act he will have to give back seven times as much, giving up all his property which is in his house.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He who takes another man's wife is without all sense: he who does it is the cause of destruction to his soul.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Wounds will be his and loss of honour, and his shame may not be washed away.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** For bitter is the wrath of an angry husband; in the day of punishment he will have no mercy.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** He will not take any payment; and he will not make peace with you though your money offerings are increased.
