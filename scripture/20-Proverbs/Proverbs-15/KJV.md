# Proverbs 15 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** A soft answer turneth away wrath: but grievous words stir up anger.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The tongue of the wise useth knowledge aright: but the mouth of fools poureth out foolishness.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The eyes of the Lord are in every place, beholding the evil and the good.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** A wholesome tongue is a tree of life: but perverseness therein is a breach in the spirit.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A fool despiseth his father’s instruction: but he that regardeth reproof is prudent.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** In the house of the righteous is much treasure: but in the revenues of the wicked is trouble.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The lips of the wise disperse knowledge: but the heart of the foolish doeth not so.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The sacrifice of the wicked is an abomination to the Lord: but the prayer of the upright is his delight.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The way of the wicked is an abomination unto the Lord: but he loveth him that followeth after righteousness.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Correction is grievous unto him that forsaketh the way: and he that hateth reproof shall die.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Hell and destruction are before the Lord: how much more then the hearts of the children of men?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** A scorner loveth not one that reproveth him: neither will he go unto the wise.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** A merry heart maketh a cheerful countenance: but by sorrow of the heart the spirit is broken.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The heart of him that hath understanding seeketh knowledge: but the mouth of fools feedeth on foolishness.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** All the days of the afflicted are evil: but he that is of a merry heart hath a continual feast.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Better is little with the fear of the Lord than great treasure and trouble therewith.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Better is a dinner of herbs where love is, than a stalled ox and hatred therewith.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** A wrathful man stirreth up strife: but he that is slow to anger appeaseth strife.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The way of the slothful man is as an hedge of thorns: but the way of the righteous is made plain.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** A wise son maketh a glad father: but a foolish man despiseth his mother.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Folly is joy to him that is destitute of wisdom: but a man of understanding walketh uprightly.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Without counsel purposes are disappointed: but in the multitude of counsellors they are established.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** A man hath joy by the answer of his mouth: and a word spoken in due season, how good is it!

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The way of life is above to the wise, that he may depart from hell beneath.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The Lord will destroy the house of the proud: but he will establish the border of the widow.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** The thoughts of the wicked are an abomination to the Lord: but the words of the pure are pleasant words.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He that is greedy of gain troubleth his own house; but he that hateth gifts shall live.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The heart of the righteous studieth to answer: but the mouth of the wicked poureth out evil things.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** The Lord is far from the wicked: but he heareth the prayer of the righteous.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** The light of the eyes rejoiceth the heart: and a good report maketh the bones fat.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The ear that heareth the reproof of life abideth among the wise.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He that refuseth instruction despiseth his own soul: but he that heareth reproof getteth understanding.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The fear of the Lord is the instruction of wisdom; and before honour is humility.
