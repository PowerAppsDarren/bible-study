# Proverbs 15 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** A gentle answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The tongue of the wise commends knowledge, but the mouth of fools gush out folly.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Yahweh’s eyes are everywhere, keeping watch on the evil and the good.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** A gentle tongue is a tree of life, but deceit in it crushes the spirit.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A fool despises his father’s correction, but he who heeds reproof shows prudence.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** In the house of the righteous is much treasure, but the income of the wicked brings trouble.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The lips of the wise spread knowledge; not so with the heart of fools.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The sacrifice made by the wicked is an abomination to Yahweh, but the prayer of the upright is his delight.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The way of the wicked is an abomination to Yahweh, but he loves him who follows after righteousness.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** There is stern discipline for one who forsakes the way: whoever hates reproof shall die.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Sheol and Abaddon are before Yahweh— how much more then the hearts of the children of men!

<a id="v12"></a>**12** A scoffer doesn’t love to be reproved; he will not go to the wise.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** A glad heart makes a cheerful face; but an aching heart breaks the spirit.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The heart of one who has understanding seeks knowledge, but the mouths of fools feed on folly.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** All the days of the afflicted are wretched, but one who has a cheerful heart enjoys a continual feast.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Better is little, with the fear of Yahweh, than great treasure with trouble.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Better is a dinner of herbs, where love is, than a fattened calf with hatred.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** A wrathful man stirs up contention, but one who is slow to anger appeases strife.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The way of the sluggard is like a thorn patch, but the path of the upright is a highway.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** A wise son makes a father glad, but a foolish man despises his mother.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Folly is joy to one who is void of wisdom, but a man of understanding keeps his way straight.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Where there is no counsel, plans fail; but in a multitude of counselors they are established.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Joy comes to a man with the reply of his mouth. How good is a word at the right time!

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The path of life leads upward for the wise, to keep him from going downward to Sheol.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Yahweh will uproot the house of the proud, but he will keep the widow’s borders intact.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Yahweh detests the thoughts of the wicked, but the thoughts of the pure are pleasing.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He who is greedy for gain troubles his own house, but he who hates bribes will live.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The heart of the righteous weighs answers, but the mouth of the wicked gushes out evil.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Yahweh is far from the wicked, but he hears the prayer of the righteous.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** The light of the eyes rejoices the heart. Good news gives health to the bones.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The ear that listens to reproof lives, and will be at home among the wise.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He who refuses correction despises his own soul, but he who listens to reproof gets understanding.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The fear of Yahweh teaches wisdom. Before honor is humility.
