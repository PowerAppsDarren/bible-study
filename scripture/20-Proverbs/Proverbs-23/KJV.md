# Proverbs 23 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** When thou sittest to eat with a ruler, consider diligently what is before thee:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And put a knife to thy throat, if thou be a man given to appetite.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Be not desirous of his dainties: for they are deceitful meat.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Labour not to be rich: cease from thine own wisdom.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Wilt thou set thine eyes upon that which is not? for riches certainly make themselves wings; they fly away as an eagle toward heaven.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Eat thou not the bread of him that hath an evil eye, neither desire thou his dainty meats:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For as he thinketh in his heart, so is he: Eat and drink, saith he to thee; but his heart is not with thee.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The morsel which thou hast eaten shalt thou vomit up, and lose thy sweet words.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Speak not in the ears of a fool: for he will despise the wisdom of thy words.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Remove not the old landmark; and enter not into the fields of the fatherless:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For their redeemer is mighty; he shall plead their cause with thee.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Apply thine heart unto instruction, and thine ears to the words of knowledge.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Withhold not correction from the child: for if thou beatest him with the rod, he shall not die.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Thou shalt beat him with the rod, and shalt deliver his soul from hell.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** My son, if thine heart be wise, my heart shall rejoice, even mine.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Yea, my reins shall rejoice, when thy lips speak right things.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Let not thine heart envy sinners: but be thou in the fear of the Lord all the day long.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For surely there is an end; and thine expectation shall not be cut off.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Hear thou, my son, and be wise, and guide thine heart in the way.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Be not among winebibbers; among riotous eaters of flesh:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For the drunkard and the glutton shall come to poverty: and drowsiness shall clothe a man with rags.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Hearken unto thy father that begat thee, and despise not thy mother when she is old.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Buy the truth, and sell it not; also wisdom, and instruction, and understanding.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The father of the righteous shall greatly rejoice: and he that begetteth a wise child shall have joy of him.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Thy father and thy mother shall be glad, and she that bare thee shall rejoice.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** My son, give me thine heart, and let thine eyes observe my ways.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** For a whore is a deep ditch; and a strange woman is a narrow pit.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** She also lieth in wait as for a prey, and increaseth the transgressors among men.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Who hath woe? who hath sorrow? who hath contentions? who hath babbling? who hath wounds without cause? who hath redness of eyes?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** They that tarry long at the wine; they that go to seek mixed wine.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Look not thou upon the wine when it is red, when it giveth his colour in the cup, when it moveth itself aright.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** At the last it biteth like a serpent, and stingeth like an adder.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Thine eyes shall behold strange women, and thine heart shall utter perverse things.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Yea, thou shalt be as he that lieth down in the midst of the sea, or as he that lieth upon the top of a mast.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** They have stricken me, shalt thou say, and I was not sick; they have beaten me, and I felt it not: when shall I awake? I will seek it yet again.
