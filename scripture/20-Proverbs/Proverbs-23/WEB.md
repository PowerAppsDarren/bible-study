# Proverbs 23 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** When you sit to eat with a ruler, consider diligently what is before you;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** put a knife to your throat, if you are a man given to appetite.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Don’t be desirous of his dainties, since they are deceitful food.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Don’t weary yourself to be rich. In your wisdom, show restraint.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Why do you set your eyes on that which is not? For it certainly sprouts wings like an eagle and flies in the sky.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Don’t eat the food of him who has a stingy eye, and don’t crave his delicacies:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** for as he thinks about the cost, so he is. “Eat and drink!” he says to you, but his heart is not with you.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The morsel which you have eaten you shall vomit up, and lose your good words.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Don’t speak in the ears of a fool, for he will despise the wisdom of your words.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Don’t move the ancient boundary stone. Don’t encroach on the fields of the fatherless:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** for their Defender is strong. He will plead their case against you.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Apply your heart to instruction, and your ears to the words of knowledge.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Don’t withhold correction from a child. If you punish him with the rod, he will not die.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Punish him with the rod, and save his soul from Sheol.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** My son, if your heart is wise, then my heart will be glad, even mine:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** yes, my heart will rejoice, when your lips speak what is right.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Don’t let your heart envy sinners; but rather fear Yahweh all the day long.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Indeed surely there is a future hope, and your hope will not be cut off.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Listen, my son, and be wise, and keep your heart on the right path!

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Don’t be among ones drinking too much wine, or those who gorge themselves on meat:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** for the drunkard and the glutton shall become poor; and drowsiness clothes them in rags.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Listen to your father who gave you life, and don’t despise your mother when she is old.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Buy the truth, and don’t sell it. Get wisdom, discipline, and understanding.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The father of the righteous has great joy. Whoever fathers a wise child delights in him.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Let your father and your mother be glad! Let her who bore you rejoice!

<a id="v26"></a>**26** My son, give me your heart; and let your eyes keep in my ways.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** For a prostitute is a deep pit; and a wayward wife is a narrow well.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Yes, she lies in wait like a robber, and increases the unfaithful among men.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Who has woe? Who has sorrow? Who has strife? Who has complaints? Who has needless bruises? Who has bloodshot eyes?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Those who stay long at the wine; those who go to seek out mixed wine.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Don’t look at the wine when it is red, when it sparkles in the cup, when it goes down smoothly.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** In the end, it bites like a snake, and poisons like a viper.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Your eyes will see strange things, and your mind will imagine confusing things.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Yes, you will be as he who lies down in the midst of the sea, or as he who lies on top of the rigging:

<a id="v35"></a>**35** “They hit me, and I was not hurt! They beat me, and I don’t feel it! When will I wake up? I can do it again. I can find another.”
