# Proverbs 23 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** When thou sittest to eat with a ruler, Consider diligently him that is before thee;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And put a knife to thy throat, If thou be a man given to appetite.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Be not desirous of his dainties; Seeing they are deceitful food.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Weary not thyself to be rich; Cease from thine own wisdom.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Wilt thou set thine eyes upon that which is not? Forrichescertainly make themselves wings, Like an eagle that flieth toward heaven.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Eat thou not the bread of him that hath an evil eye, Neither desire thou his dainties:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For as he thinketh within himself, so is he: Eat and drink, saith he to thee; But his heart is not with thee.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The morsel which thou hast eaten shalt thou vomit up, And lose thy sweet words.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Speak not in the hearing of a fool; For he will despise the wisdom of thy words.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Remove not the ancient landmark; And enter not into the fields of the fatherless:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For their Redeemer is strong; He will plead their cause against thee.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Apply thy heart unto instruction, And thine ears to the words of knowledge.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Withhold not correction from the child; Forif thou beat him with the rod, he will not die.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Thou shalt beat him with the rod, And shalt deliver his soul from Sheol.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** My son, if thy heart be wise, My heart will be glad, even mine:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Yea, my heart will rejoice, When thy lips speak right things.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Let not thy heart envy sinners; Butbe thouin the fear of Jehovah all the day long:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For surely there is a reward; And thy hope shall not be cut off.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Hear thou, my son, and be wise, And guide thy heart in the way.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Be not among winebibbers, Among gluttonous eaters of flesh:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For the drunkard and the glutton shall come to poverty; And drowsiness will clothea manwith rags.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Hearken unto thy father that begat thee, And despise not thy mother when she is old.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Buy the truth, and sell it not; Yea, wisdom, and instruction, and understanding.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The father of the righteous will greatly rejoice; And he that begetteth a wise child will have joy of him.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Let thy father and thy mother be glad, And let her that bare thee rejoice.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** My son, give me thy heart; And let thine eyes delight in my ways.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** For a harlot is a deep ditch; And a foreign woman is a narrow pit.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Yea, she lieth in wait as a robber, And increaseth the treacherous among men.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Who hath woe? who hath sorrow? who hath contentions? Who hath complaining? who hath wounds without cause? Who hath redness of eyes?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** They that tarry long at the wine; They that go to seek out mixed wine.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Look not thou upon the wine when it is red, When it sparkleth in the cup, When it goeth down smoothly:

<a id="v32"></a>**32** At the last it biteth like a serpent, And stingeth like an adder.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Thine eyes shall behold strange things, And thy heart shall utter perverse things.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Yea, thou shalt be as he that lieth down in the midst of the sea, Or as he that lieth upon the top of a mast.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** They have stricken me, shalt thou say, and I was not hurt; They have beaten me, and I felt it not: When shall I awake? I will seek it yet again.
