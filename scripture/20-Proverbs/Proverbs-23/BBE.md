# Proverbs 23 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** When you take your seat at the feast with a ruler, give thought with care to what is before you;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And put a knife to your throat, if you have a strong desire for food.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Have no desire for his delicate food, for it is the bread of deceit.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Take no care to get wealth; let there be an end to your desire for money.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Are your eyes lifted up to it? it is gone: for wealth takes to itself wings, like an eagle in flight up to heaven.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Do not take the food of him who has an evil eye, or have any desire for his delicate meat:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For as the thoughts of his heart are, so is he: Take food and drink, he says to you; but his heart is not with you.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The food which you have taken will come up again, and your pleasing words will be wasted.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Say nothing in the hearing of a foolish man, for he will put no value on the wisdom of your words.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Do not let the landmark of the widow be moved, and do not go into the fields of those who have no father;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For their saviour is strong, and he will take up their cause against you.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Give your heart to teaching, and your ears to the words of knowledge.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Do not keep back training from the child: for even if you give him blows with the rod, it will not be death to him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Give him blows with the rod, and keep his soul safe from the underworld.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** My son, if your heart becomes wise, I, even I, will be glad in heart;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And my thoughts in me will be full of joy when your lips say right things.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Have no envy of sinners in your heart, but keep in the fear of the Lord all through the day;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For without doubt there is a future, and your hope will not be cut off.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Give ear, my son, and be wise, guiding your heart in the right way.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Do not be among those who give themselves to wine-drinking, or among those who make themselves full with meat:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For those who take delight in drink and feasting will come to be in need; and through love of sleep a man will be poorly clothed.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Give ear to your father whose child you are, and do not keep honour from your mother when she is old.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Get for yourself that which is true, and do not let it go for money; get wisdom and teaching and good sense.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The father of the upright man will be glad, and he who has a wise child will have joy because of him.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Let your father and your mother be glad, let her who gave you birth have joy.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** My son, give me your heart, and let your eyes take delight in my ways.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** For a loose woman is a deep hollow, and a strange woman is a narrow water-hole.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Yes, she is waiting secretly like a beast for its food, and deceit by her is increased among men.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Who says, Oh! who says, Ah! who has violent arguments, who has grief, who has wounds without cause, whose eyes are dark?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Those who are seated late over the wine: those who go looking for mixed wine.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Keep your eyes from looking on the wine when it is red, when its colour is bright in the cup, when it goes smoothly down:

<a id="v32"></a>**32** In the end, its bite is like that of a snake, its wound like the wound of a poison-snake.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Your eyes will see strange things, and you will say twisted things.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Yes, you will be like him who takes his rest on the sea, or on the top of a sail-support.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** They have overcome me, you will say, and I have no pain; they gave me blows without my feeling them: when will I be awake from my wine? I will go after it again.
