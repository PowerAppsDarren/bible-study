# Proverbs 20 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wine is a mocker, strong drink is raging: and whosoever is deceived thereby is not wise.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The fear of a king is as the roaring of a lion: whoso provoketh him to anger sinneth against his own soul.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** It is an honour for a man to cease from strife: but every fool will be meddling.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The sluggard will not plow by reason of the cold; therefore shall he beg in harvest, and have nothing.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Counsel in the heart of man is like deep water; but a man of understanding will draw it out.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Most men will proclaim every one his own goodness: but a faithful man who can find?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The just man walketh in his integrity: his children are blessed after him.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** A king that sitteth in the throne of judgment scattereth away all evil with his eyes.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Who can say, I have made my heart clean, I am pure from my sin?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Divers weights, and divers measures, both of them are alike abomination to the Lord.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Even a child is known by his doings, whether his work be pure, and whether it be right.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The hearing ear, and the seeing eye, the Lord hath made even both of them.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Love not sleep, lest thou come to poverty; open thine eyes, and thou shalt be satisfied with bread.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** It is naught, it is naught, saith the buyer: but when he is gone his way, then he boasteth.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** There is gold, and a multitude of rubies: but the lips of knowledge are a precious jewel.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Take his garment that is surety for a stranger: and take a pledge of him for a strange woman.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Bread of deceit is sweet to a man; but afterwards his mouth shall be filled with gravel.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Every purpose is established by counsel: and with good advice make war.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He that goeth about as a talebearer revealeth secrets: therefore meddle not with him that flattereth with his lips.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Whoso curseth his father or his mother, his lamp shall be put out in obscure darkness.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** An inheritance may be gotten hastily at the beginning; but the end thereof shall not be blessed.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Say not thou, I will recompense evil; but wait on the Lord, and he shall save thee.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Divers weights are an abomination unto the Lord; and a false balance is not good.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Man’s goings are of the Lord; how can a man then understand his own way?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** It is a snare to the man who devoureth that which is holy, and after vows to make enquiry.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** A wise king scattereth the wicked, and bringeth the wheel over them.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The spirit of man is the candle of the Lord, searching all the inward parts of the belly.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Mercy and truth preserve the king: and his throne is upholden by mercy.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** The glory of young men is their strength: and the beauty of old men is the gray head.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** The blueness of a wound cleanseth away evil: so do stripes the inward parts of the belly.
