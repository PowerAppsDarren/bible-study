# Proverbs 20 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wine makes men foolish, and strong drink makes men come to blows; and whoever comes into error through these is not wise.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The wrath of a king is like the loud cry of a lion: he who makes him angry does wrong against himself.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** It is an honour for a man to keep from fighting, but the foolish are ever at war.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The hater of work will not do his ploughing because of the winter; so at the time of grain-cutting he will be requesting food and will get nothing.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The purpose in the heart of a man is like deep water, but a man of good sense will get it out.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Most men make no secret of their kind acts: but where is a man of good faith to be seen?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** An upright man goes on in his righteousness: happy are his children after him!

<a id="v8"></a>**8** A king on the seat of judging puts to flight all evil with his eyes.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Who is able to say, I have made my heart clean, I am free from my sin?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Unequal weights and unequal measures, they are all disgusting to the Lord.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Even a child may be judged by his doings, if his work is free from sin and if it is right.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The hearing ear and the seeing eye are equally the Lord's work.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Do not be a lover of sleep, or you will become poor: keep your eyes open, and you will have bread enough.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** A poor thing, a poor thing, says he who is giving money for goods: but when he has gone on his way, then he makes clear his pride in what he has got.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** There is gold and a store of corals: but the lips of knowledge are a jewel of great price.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Take a man's clothing if he makes himself responsible for a strange man, and get an undertaking from him who gives his word for strange men.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Bread of deceit is sweet to a man; but after, his mouth will be full of sand.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Every purpose is put into effect by wise help: and by wise guiding make war.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He who goes about talking of the business of others gives away secrets: so have nothing to do with him whose lips are open wide.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** If anyone puts a curse on his father or his mother, his light will be put out in the blackest night.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** A heritage may be got quickly at first, but the end of it will not be a blessing.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Do not say, I will give punishment for evil: go on waiting for the Lord, and he will be your saviour.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Unequal weights are disgusting to the Lord, and false scales are not good.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** A man's steps are of the Lord; how then may a man have knowledge of his way?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** It is a danger to a man to say without thought, It is holy, and, after taking his oaths, to be questioning if it is necessary to keep them.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** A wise king puts evil-doers to flight, and makes their evil-doing come back on them.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The Lord keeps watch over the spirit of man, searching all the deepest parts of the body.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Mercy and good faith keep the king safe, and the seat of his power is based on upright acts.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** The glory of young men is their strength, and the honour of old men is their grey hairs.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** By the wounds of the rod evil is taken away, and blows make clean the deepest parts of the body.
