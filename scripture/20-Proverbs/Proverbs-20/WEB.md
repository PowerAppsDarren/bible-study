# Proverbs 20 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wine is a mocker, and beer is a brawler. Whoever is led astray by them is not wise.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The terror of a king is like the roaring of a lion. He who provokes him to anger forfeits his own life.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** It is an honor for a man to keep aloof from strife; but every fool will be quarreling.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The sluggard will not plow by reason of the winter; therefore he shall beg in harvest, and have nothing.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Counsel in the heart of man is like deep water; but a man of understanding will draw it out.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Many men claim to be men of unfailing love, but who can find a faithful man?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** A righteous man walks in integrity. Blessed are his children after him.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** A king who sits on the throne of judgment scatters away all evil with his eyes.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Who can say, “I have made my heart pure. I am clean and without sin?”

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Differing weights and differing measures, both of them alike are an abomination to Yahweh.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Even a child makes himself known by his doings, whether his work is pure, and whether it is right.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The hearing ear, and the seeing eye, Yahweh has made even both of them.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Don’t love sleep, lest you come to poverty. Open your eyes, and you shall be satisfied with bread.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** “It’s no good, it’s no good,” says the buyer; but when he is gone his way, then he boasts.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** There is gold and abundance of rubies; but the lips of knowledge are a rare jewel.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Take the garment of one who puts up collateral for a stranger; and hold him in pledge for a wayward woman.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Fraudulent food is sweet to a man, but afterwards his mouth is filled with gravel.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Plans are established by advice; by wise guidance you wage war!

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He who goes about as a tale-bearer reveals secrets; therefore don’t keep company with him who opens wide his lips.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Whoever curses his father or his mother, his lamp shall be put out in blackness of darkness.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** An inheritance quickly gained at the beginning, won’t be blessed in the end.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Don’t say, “I will pay back evil.” Wait for Yahweh, and he will save you.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Yahweh detests differing weights, and dishonest scales are not pleasing.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** A man’s steps are from Yahweh; how then can man understand his way?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** It is a snare to a man to make a rash dedication, then later to consider his vows.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** A wise king winnows out the wicked, and drives the threshing wheel over them.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The spirit of man is Yahweh’s lamp, searching all his innermost parts.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Love and faithfulness keep the king safe. His throne is sustained by love.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** The glory of young men is their strength. The splendor of old men is their gray hair.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Wounding blows cleanse away evil, and beatings purge the innermost parts.
