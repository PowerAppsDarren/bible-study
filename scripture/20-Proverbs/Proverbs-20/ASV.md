# Proverbs 20 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wine is a mocker, strong drink a brawler; And whosoever erreth thereby is not wise.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The terror of a king is as the roaring of a lion: He that provoketh him to anger sinneth against his own life.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** It is an honor for a man to keep aloof from strife; But every fool will be quarrelling.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The sluggard will not plow by reason of the winter; Therefore he shall beg in harvest, and have nothing.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Counsel in the heart of man islikedeep water; But a man of understanding will draw it out.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Most men will proclaim every one his own kindness; But a faithful man who can find?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** A righteous man that walketh in his integrity, Blessed are his children after him.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** A king that sitteth on the throne of judgment Scattereth away all evil with his eyes.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Who can say, I have made my heart clean, I am pure from my sin?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Diverse weights, and diverse measures, Both of them alike are an abomination to Jehovah.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Even a child maketh himself known by his doings, Whether his work be pure, and whether it be right.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The hearing ear, and the seeing eye, Jehovah hath made even both of them.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Love not sleep, lest thou come to poverty; Open thine eyes, and thou shalt be satisfied with bread.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** It is bad, it is bad, saith the buyer; But when he is gone his way, then he boasteth.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** There is gold, and abundance of rubies; But the lips of knowledge are a precious jewel.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Take his garment that is surety for a stranger; And hold him in pledge that is surety for foreigners.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Bread of falsehood is sweet to a man; But afterwards his mouth shall be filled with gravel.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Every purpose is established by counsel; And by wise guidance make thou war.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He that goeth about as a tale-bearer revealeth secrets; Therefore company not with him that openeth wide his lips.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Whoso curseth his father or his mother, His lamp shall be put out in blackness of darkness.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** An inheritancemay be gotten hastily at the beginning; But the end thereof shall not be blessed.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Say not thou, I will recompense evil: Wait for Jehovah, and he will save thee.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Diverse weights are an abomination to Jehovah; And a false balance is not good.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** A man’s goings are of Jehovah; How then can man understand his way?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** It is a snare to a man rashly to say, It is holy, And after vows to make inquiry.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** A wise king winnoweth the wicked, And bringeth the threshing-wheel over them.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The spirit of man is the lamp of Jehovah, Searching all his innermost parts.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Kindness and truth preserve the king; And his throne is upholden by kindness.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** The glory of young men is their strength; And the beauty of old men is the hoary head.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Stripes that wound cleanse away evil; And strokesreachthe innermost parts.
