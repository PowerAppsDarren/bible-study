# Proverbs 28 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The wicked flee when no man pursueth: but the righteous are bold as a lion.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For the transgression of a land many are the princes thereof: but by a man of understanding and knowledge the state thereof shall be prolonged.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A poor man that oppresseth the poor is like a sweeping rain which leaveth no food.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** They that forsake the law praise the wicked: but such as keep the law contend with them.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Evil men understand not judgment: but they that seek the Lord understand all things.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Better is the poor that walketh in his uprightness, than he that is perverse in his ways, though he be rich.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Whoso keepeth the law is a wise son: but he that is a companion of riotous men shameth his father.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He that by usury and unjust gain increaseth his substance, he shall gather it for him that will pity the poor.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He that turneth away his ear from hearing the law, even his prayer shall be abomination.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Whoso causeth the righteous to go astray in an evil way, he shall fall himself into his own pit: but the upright shall have good things in possession.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The rich man is wise in his own conceit; but the poor that hath understanding searcheth him out.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** When righteous men do rejoice, there is great glory: but when the wicked rise, a man is hidden.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He that covereth his sins shall not prosper: but whoso confesseth and forsaketh them shall have mercy.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Happy is the man that feareth alway: but he that hardeneth his heart shall fall into mischief.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** As a roaring lion, and a ranging bear; so is a wicked ruler over the poor people.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The prince that wanteth understanding is also a great oppressor: but he that hateth covetousness shall prolong his days.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** A man that doeth violence to the blood of any person shall flee to the pit; let no man stay him.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Whoso walketh uprightly shall be saved: but he that is perverse in his ways shall fall at once.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He that tilleth his land shall have plenty of bread: but he that followeth after vain persons shall have poverty enough.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** A faithful man shall abound with blessings: but he that maketh haste to be rich shall not be innocent.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** To have respect of persons is not good: for for a piece of bread that man will transgress.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He that hasteth to be rich hath an evil eye, and considereth not that poverty shall come upon him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He that rebuketh a man afterwards shall find more favour than he that flattereth with the tongue.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Whoso robbeth his father or his mother, and saith, It is no transgression; the same is the companion of a destroyer.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** He that is of a proud heart stirreth up strife: but he that putteth his trust in the Lord shall be made fat.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He that trusteth in his own heart is a fool: but whoso walketh wisely, he shall be delivered.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He that giveth unto the poor shall not lack: but he that hideth his eyes shall have many a curse.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** When the wicked rise, men hide themselves: but when they perish, the righteous increase.
