# Proverbs 28 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The wicked flee when no one pursues; but the righteous are as bold as a lion.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** In rebellion, a land has many rulers, but order is maintained by a man of understanding and knowledge.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A needy man who oppresses the poor is like a driving rain which leaves no crops.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Those who forsake the law praise the wicked; but those who keep the law contend with them.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Evil men don’t understand justice; but those who seek Yahweh understand it fully.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Better is the poor who walks in his integrity, than he who is perverse in his ways, and he is rich.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Whoever keeps the law is a wise son; but he who is a companion of gluttons shames his father.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He who increases his wealth by excessive interest gathers it for one who has pity on the poor.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He who turns away his ear from hearing the law, even his prayer is an abomination.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Whoever causes the upright to go astray in an evil way, he will fall into his own trap; but the blameless will inherit good.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The rich man is wise in his own eyes; but the poor who has understanding sees through him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** When the righteous triumph, there is great glory; but when the wicked rise, men hide themselves.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He who conceals his sins doesn’t prosper, but whoever confesses and renounces them finds mercy.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Blessed is the man who always fears; but one who hardens his heart falls into trouble.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** As a roaring lion or a charging bear, so is a wicked ruler over helpless people.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** A tyrannical ruler lacks judgment. One who hates ill-gotten gain will have long days.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** A man who is tormented by life blood will be a fugitive until death; no one will support him.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Whoever walks blamelessly is kept safe; but one with perverse ways will fall suddenly.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** One who works his land will have an abundance of food; but one who chases fantasies will have his fill of poverty.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** A faithful man is rich with blessings; but one who is eager to be rich will not go unpunished.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** To show partiality is not good; yet a man will do wrong for a piece of bread.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** A stingy man hurries after riches, and doesn’t know that poverty waits for him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** One who rebukes a man will afterward find more favor than one who flatters with the tongue.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Whoever robs his father or his mother, and says, “It’s not wrong.” He is a partner with a destroyer.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** One who is greedy stirs up strife; but one who trusts in Yahweh will prosper.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** One who trusts in himself is a fool; but one who walks in wisdom is kept safe.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** One who gives to the poor has no lack; but one who closes his eyes will have many curses.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** When the wicked rise, men hide themselves; but when they perish, the righteous thrive.
