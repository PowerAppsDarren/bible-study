# Proverbs 28 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The evil man goes running away when no man is after him, but the upright are without fear, like the lion.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Because of the sin of the land, its troubles are increased; but by a man of wisdom and knowledge they will be put out like a fire.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A man of wealth who is cruel to the poor is like a violent rain causing destruction of food.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Those who have no respect for the law give praise to the evil-doer; but such as keep the law are against him.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Evil men have no knowledge of what is right; but those who go after the Lord have knowledge of all things.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Better is the poor man whose ways are upright, than the man of wealth whose ways are not straight.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He who keeps the law is a wise son, but he who keeps company with feasters puts shame on his father.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He who makes his wealth greater by taking interest, only gets it together for him who has pity on the poor.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** As for the man whose ear is turned away from hearing the law, even his prayer is disgusting.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Anyone causing the upright to go wandering in an evil way, will himself go down into the hole he has made; but the upright will have good things for their heritage.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The man of wealth seems to himself to be wise, but the poor man who has sense has a low opinion of him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** When the upright do well, there is great glory; but when evil-doers are lifted up, men do not let themselves be seen.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He who keeps his sins secret will not do well; but one who is open about them, and gives them up, will get mercy.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Happy is the man in whom is the fear of the Lord at all times; but he whose heart is hard will come into trouble.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Like a loud-voiced lion and a wandering bear, is an evil ruler over a poor people.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The prince who has no sense is a cruel ruler; but he who has no desire to get profit for himself will have long life.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** One who has been the cause of a man's death will go in flight to the underworld: let no man give him help.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He whose ways are upright will be safe, but sudden will be the fall of him whose ways are twisted.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** By ploughing his land a man will have bread in full measure; but he who goes after good-for-nothing persons will be poor enough.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** A man of good faith will have great blessing, but one attempting to get wealth quickly will not go free from punishment.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** It is not good to have respect for a man's position: for a man will do wrong for a bit of bread.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He who is ever desiring wealth goes running after money, and does not see that need will come on him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He who says words of protest to a man will later have more approval than one who says smooth words with his tongue.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He who takes from his father or his mother what is theirs by right, and says, It is no sin; is the same as a taker of life.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** He who is ever desiring profit is a cause of fighting; but he who puts his faith in the Lord will be made fat.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He whose faith is in himself is foolish; but everyone walking wisely will be kept safe.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He who gives to the poor will never be in need, but great curses will be on him who gives no attention to them.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** When evil-doers are lifted up, men take cover; but when destruction overtakes them, the upright are increased.
