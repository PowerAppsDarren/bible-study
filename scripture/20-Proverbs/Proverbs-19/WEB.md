# Proverbs 19 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Better is the poor who walks in his integrity than he who is perverse in his lips and is a fool.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** It isn’t good to have zeal without knowledge; nor being hasty with one’s feet and missing the way.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The foolishness of man subverts his way; his heart rages against Yahweh.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Wealth adds many friends, but the poor is separated from his friend.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A false witness shall not be unpunished. He who pours out lies shall not go free.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Many will entreat the favor of a ruler, and everyone is a friend to a man who gives gifts.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** All the relatives of the poor shun him: how much more do his friends avoid him! He pursues them with pleas, but they are gone.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He who gets wisdom loves his own soul. He who keeps understanding shall find good.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** A false witness shall not be unpunished. He who utters lies shall perish.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Delicate living is not appropriate for a fool, much less for a servant to have rule over princes.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The discretion of a man makes him slow to anger. It is his glory to overlook an offense.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The king’s wrath is like the roaring of a lion, but his favor is like dew on the grass.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** A foolish son is the calamity of his father. A wife’s quarrels are a continual dripping.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** House and riches are an inheritance from fathers, but a prudent wife is from Yahweh.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Slothfulness casts into a deep sleep. The idle soul shall suffer hunger.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He who keeps the commandment keeps his soul, but he who is contemptuous in his ways shall die.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He who has pity on the poor lends to Yahweh; he will reward him.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Discipline your son, for there is hope; don’t be a willing party to his death.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A hot-tempered man must pay the penalty, for if you rescue him, you must do it again.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Listen to counsel and receive instruction, that you may be wise in your latter end.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** There are many plans in a man’s heart, but Yahweh’s counsel will prevail.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** That which makes a man to be desired is his kindness. A poor man is better than a liar.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The fear of Yahweh leads to life, then contentment; he rests and will not be touched by trouble.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The sluggard buries his hand in the dish; he will not so much as bring it to his mouth again.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Flog a scoffer, and the simple will learn prudence; rebuke one who has understanding, and he will gain knowledge.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He who robs his father and drives away his mother, is a son who causes shame and brings reproach.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** If you stop listening to instruction, my son, you will stray from the words of knowledge.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A corrupt witness mocks justice, and the mouth of the wicked gulps down iniquity.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Penalties are prepared for scoffers, and beatings for the backs of fools.
