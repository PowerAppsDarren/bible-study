# Proverbs 19 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Better is the poor that walketh in his integrity Than he that is perverse in his lips and is a fool.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Also, that the soul be without knowledge is not good; And he that hasteth with his feet sinneth.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The foolishness of man subverteth his way; And his heart fretteth against Jehovah.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Wealth addeth many friends; But the poor is separated from his friend.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A false witness shall not be unpunished; And he that uttereth lies shall not escape.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Many will entreat the favor of the liberal man; And every man is a friend to him that giveth gifts.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** All the brethren of the poor do hate him: How much more do his friends go far from him! He pursueth them with words, but they are gone.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He that getteth wisdom loveth his own soul: He that keepeth understanding shall find good.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** A false witness shall not be unpunished; And he that uttereth lies shall perish.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Delicate living is not seemly for a fool; Much less for a servant to have rule over princes.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The discretion of a man maketh him slow to anger; And it is his glory to pass over a transgression.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The king’s wrath is as the roaring of a lion; But his favor is as dew upon the grass.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** A foolish son is the calamity of his father; And the contentions of a wife are a continual dropping.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** House and riches are an inheritance from fathers; But a prudent wife is from Jehovah.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Slothfulness casteth into a deep sleep; And the idle soul shall suffer hunger.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He that keepeth the commandment keepeth his soul; But he that is careless of his ways shall die.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He that hath pity upon the poor lendeth unto Jehovah, And his good deed will he pay him again.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Chasten thy son, seeing there is hope; And set not thy heart on his destruction.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A man of great wrath shall bear the penalty; For if thou deliver him, thou must do it yet again.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Hear counsel, and receive instruction, That thou mayest be wise in thy latter end.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** There are many devices in a man’s heart; But the counsel of Jehovah, that shall stand.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** That which maketh a man to be desired is his kindness; And a poor man is better than a liar.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The fear of Jehovahtendethto life; And hethat hath itshall abide satisfied; He shall not be visited with evil.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The sluggard burieth his hand in the dish, And will not so much as bring it to his mouth again.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Smite a scoffer, and the simple will learn prudence; And reprove one that hath understanding, and he will understand knowledge.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He that doeth violence to his father, and chaseth away his mother, Is a son that causeth shame and bringeth reproach.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Cease, my son, to hear instruction Onlyto err from the words of knowledge.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A worthless witness mocketh at justice; And the mouth of the wicked swalloweth iniquity.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Judgments are prepared for scoffers, And stripes for the back of fools.
