# Proverbs 19 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Better is the poor that walketh in his integrity, than he that is perverse in his lips, and is a fool.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Also, that the soul be without knowledge, it is not good; and he that hasteth with his feet sinneth.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The foolishness of man perverteth his way: and his heart fretteth against the Lord.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Wealth maketh many friends; but the poor is separated from his neighbour.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A false witness shall not be unpunished, and he that speaketh lies shall not escape.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Many will intreat the favour of the prince: and every man is a friend to him that giveth gifts.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** All the brethren of the poor do hate him: how much more do his friends go far from him? he pursueth them with words, yet they are wanting to him.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He that getteth wisdom loveth his own soul: he that keepeth understanding shall find good.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** A false witness shall not be unpunished, and he that speaketh lies shall perish.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Delight is not seemly for a fool; much less for a servant to have rule over princes.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The discretion of a man deferreth his anger; and it is his glory to pass over a transgression.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The king’s wrath is as the roaring of a lion; but his favour is as dew upon the grass.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** A foolish son is the calamity of his father: and the contentions of a wife are a continual dropping.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** House and riches are the inheritance of fathers: and a prudent wife is from the Lord.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Slothfulness casteth into a deep sleep; and an idle soul shall suffer hunger.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He that keepeth the commandment keepeth his own soul; but he that despiseth his ways shall die.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He that hath pity upon the poor lendeth unto the Lord; and that which he hath given will he pay him again.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Chasten thy son while there is hope, and let not thy soul spare for his crying.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A man of great wrath shall suffer punishment: for if thou deliver him, yet thou must do it again.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Hear counsel, and receive instruction, that thou mayest be wise in thy latter end.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** There are many devices in a man’s heart; nevertheless the counsel of the Lord, that shall stand.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The desire of a man is his kindness: and a poor man is better than a liar.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The fear of the Lord tendeth to life: and he that hath it shall abide satisfied; he shall not be visited with evil.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** A slothful man hideth his hand in his bosom, and will not so much as bring it to his mouth again.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Smite a scorner, and the simple will beware: and reprove one that hath understanding, and he will understand knowledge.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He that wasteth his father, and chaseth away his mother, is a son that causeth shame, and bringeth reproach.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Cease, my son, to hear the instruction that causeth to err from the words of knowledge.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** An ungodly witness scorneth judgment: and the mouth of the wicked devoureth iniquity.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Judgments are prepared for scorners, and stripes for the back of fools.
