# Proverbs 14 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wisdom is building her house, but the foolish woman is pulling it down with her hands.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** He who goes on his way in righteousness has before him the fear of the Lord; but he whose ways are twisted gives him no honour.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** In the mouth of the foolish man is a rod for his back, but the lips of the wise will keep them safe.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Where there are no oxen, their food-place is clean; but much increase comes through the strength of the ox.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A true witness does not say what is false, but a false witness is breathing out deceit.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The hater of authority, searching for wisdom, does not get it; but knowledge comes readily to the open-minded man.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Go away from the foolish man, for you will not see the lips of knowledge.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The wisdom of the man of good sense makes his way clear; but the unwise behaviour of the foolish is deceit.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** In the tents of those hating authority there is error, but in the house of the upright man there is grace.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** No one has knowledge of a man's grief but himself; and a strange person has no part in his joy.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The house of the sinner will be overturned, but the tent of the upright man will do well.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** There is a way which seems straight before a man, but its end is the ways of death.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Even while laughing the heart may be sad; and after joy comes sorrow.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He whose heart is turned away will have the reward of his ways in full measure; but a good man will have the reward of his doings.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The simple man has faith in every word, but the man of good sense gives thought to his footsteps.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The wise man, fearing, keeps himself from evil; but the foolish man goes on in his pride, with no thought of danger.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He who is quickly angry will do what is foolish, but the man of good sense will have quiet.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Foolish behaviour is the heritage of the simple, but men of good sense are crowned with knowledge.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The knees of the evil are bent before the good; and sinners go down in the dust at the doors of the upright.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The poor man is hated even by his neighbour, but the man of wealth has numbers of friends.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He who has no respect for his neighbour is a sinner, but he who has pity for the poor is happy.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Will not the designers of evil come into error? But mercy and good faith are for the designers of good.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** In all hard work there is profit, but talk only makes a man poor.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Their wisdom is a crown to the wise, but their foolish behaviour is round the head of the unwise.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** A true witness is the saviour of lives; but he who says false things is a cause of deceit.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For him in whose heart is the fear of the Lord there is strong hope: and his children will have a safe place.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The fear of the Lord is a fountain of life, by which one may be turned from the nets of death.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A king's glory is in the number of his people: and for need of people a ruler may come to destruction.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** He who is slow to be angry has great good sense; but he whose spirit is over-quick gives support to what is foolish.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** A quiet mind is the life of the body, but envy is a disease in the bones.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** He who is hard on the poor puts shame on his Maker; but he who has mercy on those who are in need gives him honour.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** The sinner is overturned in his evil-doing, but the upright man has hope in his righteousness.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Wisdom has her resting-place in the mind of the wise, but she is not seen among the foolish.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** By righteousness a nation is lifted up, but sin is a cause of shame to the peoples.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** The king has pleasure in a servant who does wisely, but his wrath is against him who is a cause of shame.
