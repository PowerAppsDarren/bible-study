# Proverbs 14 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Every wise woman buildeth her house: but the foolish plucketh it down with her hands.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** He that walketh in his uprightness feareth the Lord: but he that is perverse in his ways despiseth him.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** In the mouth of the foolish is a rod of pride: but the lips of the wise shall preserve them.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Where no oxen are, the crib is clean: but much increase is by the strength of the ox.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A faithful witness will not lie: but a false witness will utter lies.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** A scorner seeketh wisdom, and findeth it not: but knowledge is easy unto him that understandeth.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Go from the presence of a foolish man, when thou perceivest not in him the lips of knowledge.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The wisdom of the prudent is to understand his way: but the folly of fools is deceit.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Fools make a mock at sin: but among the righteous there is favour.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The heart knoweth his own bitterness; and a stranger doth not intermeddle with his joy.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The house of the wicked shall be overthrown: but the tabernacle of the upright shall flourish.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** There is a way which seemeth right unto a man, but the end thereof are the ways of death.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Even in laughter the heart is sorrowful; and the end of that mirth is heaviness.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The backslider in heart shall be filled with his own ways: and a good man shall be satisfied from himself.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The simple believeth every word: but the prudent man looketh well to his going.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** A wise man feareth, and departeth from evil: but the fool rageth, and is confident.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He that is soon angry dealeth foolishly: and a man of wicked devices is hated.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The simple inherit folly: but the prudent are crowned with knowledge.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The evil bow before the good; and the wicked at the gates of the righteous.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The poor is hated even of his own neighbour: but the rich hath many friends.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He that despiseth his neighbour sinneth: but he that hath mercy on the poor, happy is he.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Do they not err that devise evil? but mercy and truth shall be to them that devise good.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** In all labour there is profit: but the talk of the lips tendeth only to penury.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The crown of the wise is their riches: but the foolishness of fools is folly.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** A true witness delivereth souls: but a deceitful witness speaketh lies.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** In the fear of the Lord is strong confidence: and his children shall have a place of refuge.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The fear of the Lord is a fountain of life, to depart from the snares of death.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** In the multitude of people is the king’s honour: but in the want of people is the destruction of the prince.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** He that is slow to wrath is of great understanding: but he that is hasty of spirit exalteth folly.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** A sound heart is the life of the flesh: but envy the rottenness of the bones.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** He that oppresseth the poor reproacheth his Maker: but he that honoureth him hath mercy on the poor.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** The wicked is driven away in his wickedness: but the righteous hath hope in his death.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Wisdom resteth in the heart of him that hath understanding: but that which is in the midst of fools is made known.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Righteousness exalteth a nation: but sin is a reproach to any people.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** The king’s favour is toward a wise servant: but his wrath is against him that causeth shame.
