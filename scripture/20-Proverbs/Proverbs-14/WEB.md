# Proverbs 14 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Every wise woman builds her house, but the foolish one tears it down with her own hands.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** He who walks in his uprightness fears Yahweh, but he who is perverse in his ways despises him.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The fool’s talk brings a rod to his back, but the lips of the wise protect them.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Where no oxen are, the crib is clean, but much increase is by the strength of the ox.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A truthful witness will not lie, but a false witness pours out lies.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** A scoffer seeks wisdom, and doesn’t find it, but knowledge comes easily to a discerning person.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Stay away from a foolish man, for you won’t find knowledge on his lips.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The wisdom of the prudent is to think about his way, but the folly of fools is deceit.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Fools mock at making atonement for sins, but among the upright there is good will.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The heart knows its own bitterness and joy; he will not share these with a stranger.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The house of the wicked will be overthrown, but the tent of the upright will flourish.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** There is a way which seems right to a man, but in the end it leads to death.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Even in laughter the heart may be sorrowful, and mirth may end in heaviness.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The unfaithful will be repaid for his own ways; likewise a good man will be rewarded for his ways.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** A simple man believes everything, but the prudent man carefully considers his ways.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** A wise man fears, and shuns evil, but the fool is hot headed and reckless.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He who is quick to become angry will commit folly, and a crafty man is hated.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The simple inherit folly, but the prudent are crowned with knowledge.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The evil bow down before the good, and the wicked at the gates of the righteous.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The poor person is shunned even by his own neighbor, but the rich person has many friends.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He who despises his neighbor sins, but blessed is he who has pity on the poor.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Don’t they go astray who plot evil? But love and faithfulness belong to those who plan good.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** In all hard work there is profit, but the talk of the lips leads only to poverty.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The crown of the wise is their riches, but the folly of fools crowns them with folly.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** A truthful witness saves souls, but a false witness is deceitful.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** In the fear of Yahweh is a secure fortress, and he will be a refuge for his children.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The fear of Yahweh is a fountain of life, turning people from the snares of death.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** In the multitude of people is the king’s glory, but in the lack of people is the destruction of the prince.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** He who is slow to anger has great understanding, but he who has a quick temper displays folly.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** The life of the body is a heart at peace, but envy rots the bones.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** He who oppresses the poor shows contempt for his Maker, but he who is kind to the needy honors him.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** The wicked is brought down in his calamity, but in death, the righteous has a refuge.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Wisdom rests in the heart of one who has understanding, and is even made known in the inward part of fools.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Righteousness exalts a nation, but sin is a disgrace to any people.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** The king’s favor is toward a servant who deals wisely, but his wrath is toward one who causes shame.
