# Proverbs 31 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The words of Lemuel, king of Massa: the teaching which he had from his mother.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** What am I to say to you, O Lemuel, my oldest son? and what, O son of my body? and what, O son of my oaths?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Do not give your strength to women, or your ways to that which is the destruction of kings.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** It is not for kings, O Lemuel, it is not for kings to take wine, or for rulers to say, Where is strong drink?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For fear that through drinking they may come to have no respect for the law, wrongly judging the cause of those who are in trouble.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Give strong drink to him who is near to destruction, and wine to him whose soul is bitter:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Let him have drink, and his need will go from his mind, and the memory of his trouble will be gone.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Let your mouth be open for those who have no voice, in the cause of those who are ready for death.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Let your mouth be open, judging rightly, and give right decisions in the cause of the poor and those in need.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Who may make discovery of a woman of virtue? For her price is much higher than jewels.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The heart of her husband has faith in her, and he will have profit in full measure.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** She does him good and not evil all the days of her life.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** She gets wool and linen, working at the business of her hands.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** She is like the trading-ships, getting food from far away.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** She gets up while it is still night, and gives meat to her family, and their food to her servant-girls.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** After looking at a field with care, she gets it for a price, planting a vine-garden with the profit of her work.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** She puts a band of strength round her, and makes her arms strong.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** She sees that her marketing is of profit to her: her light does not go out by night.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** She puts her hands to the cloth-working rod, and her fingers take the wheel.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Her hands are stretched out to the poor; yes, she is open-handed to those who are in need.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** She has no fear of the snow for her family, for all those in her house are clothed in red.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** She makes for herself cushions of needlework; her clothing is fair linen and purple.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Her husband is a man of note in the public place, when he takes his seat among the responsible men of the land.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** She makes linen robes and gets a price for them, and traders take her cloth bands for a price.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Strength and self-respect are her clothing; she is facing the future with a smile.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Her mouth is open to give out wisdom, and the law of mercy is on her tongue.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** She gives attention to the ways of her family, she does not take her food without working for it.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Her children get up and give her honour, and her husband gives her praise, saying,

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Unnumbered women have done well, but you are better than all of them.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Fair looks are a deceit, and a beautiful form is of no value; but a woman who has the fear of the Lord is to be praised.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Give her credit for what her hands have made: let her be praised by her works in the public place.
