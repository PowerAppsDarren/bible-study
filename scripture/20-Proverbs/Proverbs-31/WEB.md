# Proverbs 31 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The words of king Lemuel; the oracle which his mother taught him.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Oh, my son! Oh, son of my womb! Oh, son of my vows!

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Don’t give your strength to women, nor your ways to that which destroys kings.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** It is not for kings, Lemuel; it is not for kings to drink wine; nor for princes to say, ‘Where is strong drink?’

<a id="v5"></a>**5** lest they drink, and forget the law, and pervert the justice due to anyone who is afflicted.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Give strong drink to him who is ready to perish; and wine to the bitter in soul:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Let him drink, and forget his poverty, and remember his misery no more.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Open your mouth for the mute, in the cause of all who are left desolate.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Open your mouth, judge righteously, and serve justice to the poor and needy.”

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Who can find a worthy woman? For her price is far above rubies.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The heart of her husband trusts in her. He shall have no lack of gain.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** She does him good, and not harm, all the days of her life.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** She seeks wool and flax, and works eagerly with her hands.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** She is like the merchant ships. She brings her bread from afar.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** She rises also while it is yet night, gives food to her household, and portions for her servant girls.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** She considers a field, and buys it. With the fruit of her hands, she plants a vineyard.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** She arms her waist with strength, and makes her arms strong.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** She perceives that her merchandise is profitable. Her lamp doesn’t go out by night.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** She lays her hands to the distaff, and her hands hold the spindle.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** She opens her arms to the poor; yes, she extends her hands to the needy.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** She is not afraid of the snow for her household; for all her household are clothed with scarlet.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** She makes for herself carpets of tapestry. Her clothing is fine linen and purple.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Her husband is respected in the gates, when he sits among the elders of the land.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** She makes linen garments and sells them, and delivers sashes to the merchant.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Strength and dignity are her clothing. She laughs at the time to come.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** She opens her mouth with wisdom. Faithful instruction is on her tongue.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** She looks well to the ways of her household, and doesn’t eat the bread of idleness.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Her children rise up and call her blessed. Her husband also praises her:

<a id="v29"></a>**29** “Many women do noble things, but you excel them all.”

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Charm is deceitful, and beauty is vain; but a woman who fears Yahweh, she shall be praised.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Give her of the fruit of her hands! Let her works praise her in the gates!
