# Proverbs 31 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The words of king Lemuel; the oracle which his mother taught him.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** What, my son? and what, O son of my womb? And what, O son of my vows?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Give not thy strength unto women, Nor thy ways to that which destroyeth kings.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** It is not for kings, O Lemuel, it is not for kings to drink wine; Nor for princes to say, Where is strong drink?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Lest they drink, and forget the law, And pervert the justice due to any that is afflicted.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Give strong drink unto him that is ready to perish, And wine unto the bitter in soul:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Let him drink, and forget his poverty, And remember his misery no more.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Open thy mouth for the dumb, In the cause of all such as are left desolate.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Open thy mouth, judge righteously, And minister justice to the poor and needy.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** A worthy woman who can find? For her price is far above rubies.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The heart of her husband trusteth in her, And he shall have no lack of gain.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** She doeth him good and not evil All the days of her life.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** She seeketh wool and flax, And worketh willingly with her hands.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** She is like the merchant-ships; She bringeth her bread from afar.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** She riseth also while it is yet night, And giveth food to her household, And their task to her maidens.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** She considereth a field, and buyeth it; With the fruit of her hands she planteth a vineyard.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** She girdeth her loins with strength, And maketh strong her arms.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** She perceiveth that her merchandise is profitable; Her lamp goeth not out by night.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** She layeth her hands to the distaff, And her hands hold the spindle.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** She stretcheth out her hand to the poor; Yea, she reacheth forth her hands to the needy.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** She is not afraid of the snow for her household; For all her household are clothed with scarlet.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** She maketh for herself carpets of tapestry; Her clothing is fine linen and purple.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Her husband is known in the gates, When he sitteth among the elders of the land.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** She maketh linen garments and selleth them, And delivereth girdles unto the merchant.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Strength and dignity are her clothing; And she laugheth at the time to come.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** She openeth her mouth with wisdom; And the law of kindness is on her tongue.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** She looketh well to the ways of her household, And eateth not the bread of idleness.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Her children rise up, and call her blessed; Her husbandalso, and he praiseth her, saying:

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Many daughters have done worthily, But thou excellest them all.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Grace is deceitful, and beauty is vain; Buta woman that feareth Jehovah, she shall be praised.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Give her of the fruit of her hands; And let her works praise her in the gates.
