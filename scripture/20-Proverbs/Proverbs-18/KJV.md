# Proverbs 18 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Through desire a man, having separated himself, seeketh and intermeddleth with all wisdom.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** A fool hath no delight in understanding, but that his heart may discover itself.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** When the wicked cometh, then cometh also contempt, and with ignominy reproach.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The words of a man’s mouth are as deep waters, and the wellspring of wisdom as a flowing brook.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** It is not good to accept the person of the wicked, to overthrow the righteous in judgment.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** A fool’s lips enter into contention, and his mouth calleth for strokes.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** A fool’s mouth is his destruction, and his lips are the snare of his soul.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The words of a talebearer are as wounds, and they go down into the innermost parts of the belly.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He also that is slothful in his work is brother to him that is a great waster.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The name of the Lord is a strong tower: the righteous runneth into it, and is safe.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The rich man’s wealth is his strong city, and as an high wall in his own conceit.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Before destruction the heart of man is haughty, and before honour is humility.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He that answereth a matter before he heareth it, it is folly and shame unto him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The spirit of a man will sustain his infirmity; but a wounded spirit who can bear?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The heart of the prudent getteth knowledge; and the ear of the wise seeketh knowledge.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** A man’s gift maketh room for him, and bringeth him before great men.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He that is first in his own cause seemeth just; but his neighbour cometh and searcheth him.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The lot causeth contentions to cease, and parteth between the mighty.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A brother offended is harder to be won than a strong city: and their contentions are like the bars of a castle.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** A man’s belly shall be satisfied with the fruit of his mouth; and with the increase of his lips shall he be filled.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Death and life are in the power of the tongue: and they that love it shall eat the fruit thereof.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Whoso findeth a wife findeth a good thing, and obtaineth favour of the Lord.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The poor useth intreaties; but the rich answereth roughly.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** A man that hath friends must shew himself friendly: and there is a friend that sticketh closer than a brother.
