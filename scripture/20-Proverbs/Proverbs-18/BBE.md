# Proverbs 18 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** He who keeps himself separate for his private purpose goes against all good sense.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** A foolish man has no pleasure in good sense, but only to let what is in his heart come to light.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** When the evil-doer comes, a low opinion comes with him, and with the loss of honour comes shame.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The words of a man's mouth are like deep waters: the fountain of wisdom is like a flowing stream.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** To have respect for the person of the evil-doer is not good, or to give a wrong decision against the upright.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** A foolish man's lips are a cause of fighting, and his mouth makes him open to blows.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The mouth of a foolish man is his destruction, and his lips are a net for his soul.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The words of one who says evil of his neighbour secretly are like sweet food, and go down into the inner parts of the stomach.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He who does not give his mind to his work is brother to him who makes destruction.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The name of the Lord is a strong tower: the upright man running into it is safe.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The property of a man of wealth is his strong town, and it is as a high wall in the thoughts of his heart.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Before destruction the heart of man is full of pride, and before honour goes a gentle spirit.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** To give an answer before hearing is a foolish thing and a cause of shame.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The spirit of a man will be his support when he is ill; but how may a broken spirit be lifted up?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The heart of the man of good sense gets knowledge; the ear of the wise is searching for knowledge.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** A man's offering makes room for him, letting him come before great men.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The man who first puts his cause before the judge seems to be in the right; but then his neighbour comes and puts his cause in its true light.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The decision of chance puts an end to argument, parting the strong.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A brother wounded is like a strong town, and violent acts are like a locked tower.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** With the fruit of a man's mouth his stomach will be full; the produce of his lips will be his in full measure.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Death and life are in the power of the tongue; and those to whom it is dear will have its fruit for their food.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Whoever gets a wife gets a good thing, and has the approval of the Lord.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The poor man makes requests for grace, but the man of wealth gives a rough answer.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** There are friends who may be a man's destruction, but there is a lover who keeps nearer than a brother.
