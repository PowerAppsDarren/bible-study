# Proverbs 18 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** He that separateth himself seeketh his own desire, And rageth against all sound wisdom.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** A fool hath no delight in understanding, But only that his heart may reveal itself.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** When the wicked cometh, there cometh also contempt, And with ignominy cometh reproach.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The words of a man’s mouth areasdeep waters; The wellspring of wisdom is as a flowing brook.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** To respect the person of the wicked is not good, Norto turn aside the righteous in judgment.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** A fool’s lips enter into contention, And his mouth calleth for stripes.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** A fool’s mouth is his destruction, And his lips are the snare of his soul.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The words of a whisperer are as dainty morsels, And they go down into the innermost parts.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He also that is slack in his work  Is brother to him that is a destroyer.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The name of Jehovah is a strong tower; The righteous runneth into it, and is safe.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The rich man’s wealth is his strong city, And as a high wall in his own imagination.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Before destruction the heart of man is haughty; And before honorgoethhumility.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He that giveth answer before he heareth, It is folly and shame unto him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The spirit of a man will sustain his infirmity; But a broken spirit who can bear?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The heart of the prudent getteth knowledge; And the ear of the wise seeketh knowledge.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** A man’s gift maketh room for him, And bringeth him before great men.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He that pleadeth his cause first seemeth just; But his neighbor cometh and searcheth him out.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The lot causeth contentions to cease, And parteth between the mighty.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A brother offendedis harder to be won than a strong city; Andsuchcontentions are like the bars of a castle.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** A man’s belly shall be filled with the fruit of his mouth; With the increase of his lips shall he be satisfied.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Death and life are in the power of the tongue; And they that love it shall eat the fruit thereof.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Whoso findeth a wife findeth a good thing, And obtaineth favor of Jehovah.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The poor useth entreaties; But the rich answereth roughly.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He that maketh many friends doeth it to his own destruction; But there is a friend that sticketh closer than a brother.
