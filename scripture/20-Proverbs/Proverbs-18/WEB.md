# Proverbs 18 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** An unfriendly man pursues selfishness, and defies all sound judgment.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** A fool has no delight in understanding, but only in revealing his own opinion.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** When wickedness comes, contempt also comes, and with shame comes disgrace.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The words of a man’s mouth are like deep waters. The fountain of wisdom is like a flowing brook.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** To be partial to the faces of the wicked is not good, nor to deprive the innocent of justice.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** A fool’s lips come into strife, and his mouth invites beatings.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** A fool’s mouth is his destruction, and his lips are a snare to his soul.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The words of a gossip are like dainty morsels: they go down into a person’s innermost parts.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** One who is slack in his work is brother to him who is a master of destruction.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Yahweh’s name is a strong tower: the righteous run to him, and are safe.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The rich man’s wealth is his strong city, like an unscalable wall in his own imagination.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Before destruction the heart of man is proud, but before honor is humility.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He who gives answer before he hears, that is folly and shame to him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** A man’s spirit will sustain him in sickness, but a crushed spirit, who can bear?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The heart of the discerning gets knowledge. The ear of the wise seeks knowledge.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** A man’s gift makes room for him, and brings him before great men.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He who pleads his cause first seems right; until another comes and questions him.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The lot settles disputes, and keeps strong ones apart.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A brother offended is more difficult than a fortified city; and disputes are like the bars of a castle.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** A man’s stomach is filled with the fruit of his mouth. With the harvest of his lips he is satisfied.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Death and life are in the power of the tongue; those who love it will eat its fruit.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Whoever finds a wife finds a good thing, and obtains favor of Yahweh.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The poor plead for mercy, but the rich answer harshly.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** A man of many companions may be ruined, but there is a friend who sticks closer than a brother.
