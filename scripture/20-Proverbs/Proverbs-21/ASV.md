# Proverbs 21 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The king’s heart is in the hand of Jehovah as the watercourses: He turneth it whithersoever he will.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Every way of a man is right in his own eyes; But Jehovah weigheth the hearts.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** To do righteousness and justice  Is more acceptable to Jehovah than sacrifice.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** A high look, and a proud heart, Eventhe lamp of the wicked, is sin.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The thoughts of the diligent tend only to plenteousness; But every one that is hastyhastethonly to want.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The getting of treasures by a lying tongue Is a vapor driven to and fro by them that seek death.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The violence of the wicked shall sweep them away, Because they refuse to do justice.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The way of him that is laden with guilt is exceeding crooked; But as for the pure, his work is right.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** It is better to dwell in the corner of the housetop, Than with a contentious woman in a wide house.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The soul of the wicked desireth evil: His neighbor findeth no favor in his eyes.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** When the scoffer is punished, the simple is made wise; And when the wise is instructed, he receiveth knowledge.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The righteous man considereth the house of the wicked, Howthe wicked are overthrown to their ruin.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Whoso stoppeth his ears at the cry of the poor, He also shall cry, but shall not be heard.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** A gift in secret pacifieth anger; And a present in the bosom, strong wrath.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** It is joy to the righteous to do justice; But it is a destruction to the workers of iniquity.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The man that wandereth out of the way of understanding Shall rest in the assembly of the dead.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He that loveth pleasure shall be a poor man: He that loveth wine and oil shall not be rich.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The wicked is a ransom for the righteous; And the treacherouscomethin the stead of the upright.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** It is better to dwell in a desert land, Than with a contentious and fretful woman.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** There is precious treasure and oil in the dwelling of the wise; But a foolish man swalloweth it up.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He that followeth after righteousness and kindness Findeth life, righteousness, and honor.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** A wise man scaleth the city of the mighty, And bringeth down the strength of the confidence thereof.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Whoso keepeth his mouth and his tongue Keepeth his soul from troubles.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The proud and haughty man, scoffer is his name; He worketh in the arrogance of pride.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The desire of the sluggard killeth him; For his hands refuse to labor.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** There is that coveteth greedily all the day long; But the righteous giveth and withholdeth not.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The sacrifice of the wicked is an abomination; How much more, when he bringeth it with a wicked mind!

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A false witness shall perish; But the man that heareth shall speak so as to endure.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** A wicked man hardeneth his face; But as for the upright, he establisheth his ways.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** There is no wisdom nor understanding  Nor counsel against Jehovah.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The horse is prepared against the day of battle; But victory is of Jehovah.
