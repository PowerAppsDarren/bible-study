# Proverbs 21 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The king’s heart is in the hand of the Lord, as the rivers of water: he turneth it whithersoever he will.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Every way of a man is right in his own eyes: but the Lord pondereth the hearts.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** To do justice and judgment is more acceptable to the Lord than sacrifice.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** An high look, and a proud heart, and the plowing of the wicked, is sin.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The thoughts of the diligent tend only to plenteousness; but of every one that is hasty only to want.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The getting of treasures by a lying tongue is a vanity tossed to and fro of them that seek death.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The robbery of the wicked shall destroy them; because they refuse to do judgment.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The way of man is froward and strange: but as for the pure, his work is right.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** It is better to dwell in a corner of the housetop, than with a brawling woman in a wide house.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The soul of the wicked desireth evil: his neighbour findeth no favour in his eyes.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** When the scorner is punished, the simple is made wise: and when the wise is instructed, he receiveth knowledge.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The righteous man wisely considereth the house of the wicked: but God overthroweth the wicked for their wickedness.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Whoso stoppeth his ears at the cry of the poor, he also shall cry himself, but shall not be heard.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** A gift in secret pacifieth anger: and a reward in the bosom strong wrath.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** It is joy to the just to do judgment: but destruction shall be to the workers of iniquity.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The man that wandereth out of the way of understanding shall remain in the congregation of the dead.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He that loveth pleasure shall be a poor man: he that loveth wine and oil shall not be rich.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The wicked shall be a ransom for the righteous, and the transgressor for the upright.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** It is better to dwell in the wilderness, than with a contentious and an angry woman.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** There is treasure to be desired and oil in the dwelling of the wise; but a foolish man spendeth it up.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He that followeth after righteousness and mercy findeth life, righteousness, and honour.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** A wise man scaleth the city of the mighty, and casteth down the strength of the confidence thereof.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Whoso keepeth his mouth and his tongue keepeth his soul from troubles.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Proud and haughty scorner is his name, who dealeth in proud wrath.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The desire of the slothful killeth him; for his hands refuse to labour.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He coveteth greedily all the day long: but the righteous giveth and spareth not.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The sacrifice of the wicked is abomination: how much more, when he bringeth it with a wicked mind?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A false witness shall perish: but the man that heareth speaketh constantly.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** A wicked man hardeneth his face: but as for the upright, he directeth his way.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** There is no wisdom nor understanding nor counsel against the Lord.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The horse is prepared against the day of battle: but safety is of the Lord.
