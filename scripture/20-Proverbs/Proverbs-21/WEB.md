# Proverbs 21 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The king’s heart is in Yahweh’s hand like the watercourses. He turns it wherever he desires.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Every way of a man is right in his own eyes, but Yahweh weighs the hearts.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** To do righteousness and justice is more acceptable to Yahweh than sacrifice.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** A high look, and a proud heart, the lamp of the wicked, is sin.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The plans of the diligent surely lead to profit; and everyone who is hasty surely rushes to poverty.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Getting treasures by a lying tongue is a fleeting vapor for those who seek death.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The violence of the wicked will drive them away, because they refuse to do what is right.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The way of the guilty is devious, but the conduct of the innocent is upright.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** It is better to dwell in the corner of the housetop, than to share a house with a contentious woman.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The soul of the wicked desires evil; his neighbor finds no mercy in his eyes.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** When the mocker is punished, the simple gains wisdom. When the wise is instructed, he receives knowledge.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The Righteous One considers the house of the wicked, and brings the wicked to ruin.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Whoever stops his ears at the cry of the poor, he will also cry out, but shall not be heard.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** A gift in secret pacifies anger; and a bribe in the cloak, strong wrath.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** It is joy to the righteous to do justice; but it is a destruction to the workers of iniquity.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The man who wanders out of the way of understanding shall rest in the assembly of the departed spirits.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He who loves pleasure shall be a poor man. He who loves wine and oil shall not be rich.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The wicked is a ransom for the righteous; the treacherous for the upright.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** It is better to dwell in a desert land, than with a contentious and fretful woman.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** There is precious treasure and oil in the dwelling of the wise; but a foolish man swallows it up.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He who follows after righteousness and kindness finds life, righteousness, and honor.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** A wise man scales the city of the mighty, and brings down the strength of its confidence.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Whoever guards his mouth and his tongue keeps his soul from troubles.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The proud and haughty man, “scoffer” is his name; he works in the arrogance of pride.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The desire of the sluggard kills him, for his hands refuse to labor.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** There are those who covet greedily all day long; but the righteous give and don’t withhold.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The sacrifice of the wicked is an abomination: how much more, when he brings it with a wicked mind!

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A false witness will perish, and a man who listens speaks to eternity.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** A wicked man hardens his face; but as for the upright, he establishes his ways.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** There is no wisdom nor understanding nor counsel against Yahweh.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The horse is prepared for the day of battle; but victory is with Yahweh.
