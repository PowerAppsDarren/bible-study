# Proverbs 9 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wisdom has made her house, putting up her seven pillars.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** She has put her fat beasts to death; her wine is mixed, her table is ready.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** She has sent out her women-servants; her voice goes out to the highest places of the town, saying,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Whoever is simple, let him come in here; and to him who has no sense, she says:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Come, take of my bread, and of my wine which is mixed.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Give up the simple ones and have life, and go in the way of knowledge.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He who gives teaching to a man of pride gets shame for himself; he who says sharp words to a sinner gets a bad name.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Do not say sharp words to a man of pride, or he will have hate for you; make them clear to a wise man, and you will be dear to him.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Give teaching to a wise man, and he will become wiser; give training to an upright man, and his learning will be increased.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The fear of the Lord is the start of wisdom, and the knowledge of the Holy One gives a wise mind

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For by me your days will be increased, and the years of your life will be long.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** If you are wise, you are wise for yourself; if your heart is full of pride, you only will have the pain of it.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The foolish woman is full of noise; she has no sense at all.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Seated at the door of her house, in the high places of the town,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Crying out to those who go by, going straight on their way, she says:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Whoever is simple, let him come in here: and to him who is without sense, she says:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Drink taken without right is sweet, and food in secret is pleasing.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But he does not see that the dead are there, that her guests are in the deep places of the underworld.
