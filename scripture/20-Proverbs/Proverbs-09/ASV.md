# Proverbs 9 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wisdom hath builded her house; She hath hewn out her seven pillars:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** She hath killed her beasts; she hath mingled her wine; She hath also furnished her table:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** She hath sent forth her maidens; She crieth upon the highest places of the city:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Whoso is simple, let him turn in hither: As for him that is void of understanding, she saith to him,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Come, eat ye of my bread, And drink of the wine which I have mingled.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Leave off, ye simple ones, and live; And walk in the way of understanding.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He that correcteth a scoffer getteth to himself reviling; And he that reproveth a wicked man getteth himself a blot.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Reprove not a scoffer, lest he hate thee: Reprove a wise man, and he will love thee.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Giveinstructionto a wise man, and he will be yet wiser: Teach a righteous man, and he will increase in learning.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The fear of Jehovah is the beginning of wisdom; And the knowledge of the Holy One is understanding.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For by me thy days shall be multiplied, And the years of thy life shall be increased.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** If thou art wise, thou art wise for thyself; And if thou scoffest, thou alone shalt bear it.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The foolish woman is clamorous; She is simple, and knoweth nothing.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And she sitteth at the door of her house, On a seat in the high places of the city,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** To call to them that pass by, Who go right on their ways:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Whoso is simple, let him turn in hither; And as for him that is void of understanding, she saith to him,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Stolen waters are sweet, And breadeatenin secret is pleasant.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But he knoweth not that the dead are there; That her guests are in the depths of Sheol.
