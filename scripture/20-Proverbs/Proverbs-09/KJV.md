# Proverbs 9 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wisdom hath builded her house, she hath hewn out her seven pillars:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** She hath killed her beasts; she hath mingled her wine; she hath also furnished her table.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** She hath sent forth her maidens: she crieth upon the highest places of the city,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Whoso is simple, let him turn in hither: as for him that wanteth understanding, she saith to him,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Come, eat of my bread, and drink of the wine which I have mingled.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Forsake the foolish, and live; and go in the way of understanding.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He that reproveth a scorner getteth to himself shame: and he that rebuketh a wicked man getteth himself a blot.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Reprove not a scorner, lest he hate thee: rebuke a wise man, and he will love thee.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Give instruction to a wise man, and he will be yet wiser: teach a just man, and he will increase in learning.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom: and the knowledge of the holy is understanding.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For by me thy days shall be multiplied, and the years of thy life shall be increased.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** If thou be wise, thou shalt be wise for thyself: but if thou scornest, thou alone shalt bear it.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** A foolish woman is clamorous: she is simple, and knoweth nothing.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For she sitteth at the door of her house, on a seat in the high places of the city,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** To call passengers who go right on their ways:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Whoso is simple, let him turn in hither: and as for him that wanteth understanding, she saith to him,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Stolen waters are sweet, and bread eaten in secret is pleasant.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But he knoweth not that the dead are there; and that her guests are in the depths of hell.
