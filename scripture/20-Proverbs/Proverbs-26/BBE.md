# Proverbs 26 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Like snow in summer and rain when the grain is being cut, so honour is not natural for the foolish.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As the sparrow in her wandering and the swallow in her flight, so the curse does not come without a cause.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A whip for the horse, a mouth-bit for the ass, and a rod for the back of the foolish.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Do not give to the foolish man a foolish answer, or you will be like him.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Give a foolish man a foolish answer, or he will seem wise to himself.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He who sends news by the hand of a foolish man is cutting off his feet and drinking in damage.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The legs of one who has no power of walking are hanging loose; so is a wise saying in the mouth of the foolish.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Giving honour to a foolish man is like attempting to keep a stone fixed in a cord.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Like a thorn which goes up into the hand of a man overcome by drink, so is a wise saying in the mouth of a foolish man.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Like an archer wounding all who go by, is a foolish man overcome by drink.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Like a dog going back to the food which he has not been able to keep down, is the foolish man doing his foolish acts over again.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Have you seen a man who seems to himself to be wise? There is more hope for the foolish than for him.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The hater of work says, There is a lion in the way; a lion is in the streets.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** A door is turned on its pillar, and the hater of work on his bed.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The hater of work puts his hand deep into the basin: lifting it again to his mouth is a weariness to him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The hater of work seems to himself wiser than seven men who are able to give an answer with good sense.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He who gets mixed up in a fight which is not his business, is like one who takes a dog by the ears while it is going by.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** As one who is off his head sends about flaming sticks and arrows of death,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** So is the man who gets the better of his neighbour by deceit, and says, Am I not doing so in sport?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Without wood, the fire goes out; and where there is no secret talk, argument is ended.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Like breath on coals and wood on fire, so a man given to argument gets a fight started.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The words of one who says evil of his neighbour secretly are like sweet food, they go down into the inner parts of the stomach.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Smooth lips and an evil heart are like a vessel of earth plated with silver waste.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** With his lips the hater makes things seem what they are not, but deceit is stored up inside him;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** When he says fair words, have no belief in him; for in his heart are seven evils:

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Though his hate is covered with deceit, his sin will be seen openly before the meeting of the people.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He who makes a hole in the earth will himself go falling into it: and on him by whom a stone is rolled the stone will come back again.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A false tongue has hate for those who have clean hearts, and a smooth mouth is a cause of falling.
