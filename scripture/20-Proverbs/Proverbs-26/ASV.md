# Proverbs 26 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** As snow in summer, and as rain in harvest, So honor is not seemly for a fool.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As the sparrow in her wandering, as the swallow in her flying, So the curse that is causeless alighteth not.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A whip for the horse, a bridle for the ass, And a rod for the back of fools.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Answer not a fool according to his folly, Lest thou also be like unto him.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Answer a fool according to his folly, Lest he be wise in his own conceit.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He that sendeth a message by the hand of a fool Cutteth off his own feet, and drinketh in damage.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The legs of the lame hang loose; So is a parable in the mouth of fools.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** As one that bindeth a stone in a sling, So is he that giveth honor to a fool.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Asa thorn that goeth up into the hand of a drunkard, So is a parable in the mouth of fools.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** As an archer that woundeth all, So is he that hireth a fool and he that hireth them that pass by.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** As a dog that returneth to his vomit, So isa fool that repeateth his folly.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Seest thou a man wise in his own conceit? There is more hope of a fool than of him.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The sluggard saith, There is a lion in the way; A lion is in the streets.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Asthe door turneth upon its hinges, So doth the sluggard upon his bed.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The sluggard burieth his hand in the dish; It wearieth him to bring it again to his mouth.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The sluggard is wiser in his own conceit Than seven men that can render a reason.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He that passeth by, and vexeth himself with strife belonging not to him, Islikeone that taketh a dog by the ears.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** As a madman who casteth firebrands, Arrows, and death,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** So is the man that deceiveth his neighbor, And saith, Am not I in sport?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** For lack of wood the fire goeth out; And where there is no whisperer, contention ceaseth.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Ascoals are to hot embers, and wood to fire, So is a contentious man to inflame strife.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The words of a whisperer are as dainty morsels, And they go down into the innermost parts.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Fervent lips and a wicked heart  Arelikean earthen vessel overlaid with silver dross.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He that hateth dissembleth with his lips; But he layeth up deceit within him:

<a id="v25"></a>**25** When he speaketh fair, believe him not; For there are seven abominations in his heart:

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Though hishatred cover itself with guile, His wickedness shall be openly showed before the assembly.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Whoso diggeth a pit shall fall therein; And he that rolleth a stone, it shall return upon him.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A lying tongue hateth those whom it hath wounded; And a flattering mouth worketh ruin.
