# Proverbs 26 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** As snow in summer, and as rain in harvest, so honour is not seemly for a fool.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As the bird by wandering, as the swallow by flying, so the curse causeless shall not come.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A whip for the horse, a bridle for the ass, and a rod for the fool’s back.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Answer not a fool according to his folly, lest thou also be like unto him.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Answer a fool according to his folly, lest he be wise in his own conceit.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He that sendeth a message by the hand of a fool cutteth off the feet, and drinketh damage.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The legs of the lame are not equal: so is a parable in the mouth of fools.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** As he that bindeth a stone in a sling, so is he that giveth honour to a fool.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** As a thorn goeth up into the hand of a drunkard, so is a parable in the mouth of fools.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The great God that formed all things both rewardeth the fool, and rewardeth transgressors.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** As a dog returneth to his vomit, so a fool returneth to his folly.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Seest thou a man wise in his own conceit? there is more hope of a fool than of him.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The slothful man saith, There is a lion in the way; a lion is in the streets.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** As the door turneth upon his hinges, so doth the slothful upon his bed.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The slothful hideth his hand in his bosom; it grieveth him to bring it again to his mouth.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The sluggard is wiser in his own conceit than seven men that can render a reason.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He that passeth by, and meddleth with strife belonging not to him, is like one that taketh a dog by the ears.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** As a mad man who casteth firebrands, arrows, and death,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** So is the man that deceiveth his neighbour, and saith, Am not I in sport?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Where no wood is, there the fire goeth out: so where there is no talebearer, the strife ceaseth.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** As coals are to burning coals, and wood to fire; so is a contentious man to kindle strife.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The words of a talebearer are as wounds, and they go down into the innermost parts of the belly.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Burning lips and a wicked heart are like a potsherd covered with silver dross.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He that hateth dissembleth with his lips, and layeth up deceit within him;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** When he speaketh fair, believe him not: for there are seven abominations in his heart.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Whose hatred is covered by deceit, his wickedness shall be shewed before the whole congregation.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Whoso diggeth a pit shall fall therein: and he that rolleth a stone, it will return upon him.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A lying tongue hateth those that are afflicted by it; and a flattering mouth worketh ruin.
