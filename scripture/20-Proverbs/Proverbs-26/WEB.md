# Proverbs 26 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Like snow in summer, and as rain in harvest, so honor is not fitting for a fool.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Like a fluttering sparrow, like a darting swallow, so the undeserved curse doesn’t come to rest.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A whip is for the horse, a bridle for the donkey, and a rod for the back of fools!

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Don’t answer a fool according to his folly, lest you also be like him.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Answer a fool according to his folly, lest he be wise in his own eyes.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** One who sends a message by the hand of a fool is cutting off feet and drinking violence.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Like the legs of the lame that hang loose: so is a parable in the mouth of fools.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** As one who binds a stone in a sling, so is he who gives honor to a fool.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Like a thorn bush that goes into the hand of a drunkard, so is a parable in the mouth of fools.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** As an archer who wounds all, so is he who hires a fool or he who hires those who pass by.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** As a dog that returns to his vomit, so is a fool who repeats his folly.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Do you see a man wise in his own eyes? There is more hope for a fool than for him.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The sluggard says, “There is a lion in the road! A fierce lion roams the streets!”

<a id="v14"></a>**14** As the door turns on its hinges, so does the sluggard on his bed.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The sluggard buries his hand in the dish. He is too lazy to bring it back to his mouth.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The sluggard is wiser in his own eyes than seven men who answer with discretion.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Like one who grabs a dog’s ears is one who passes by and meddles in a quarrel not his own.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Like a madman who shoots torches, arrows, and death,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** is the man who deceives his neighbor and says, “Am I not joking?”

<a id="v20"></a>**20** For lack of wood a fire goes out. Without gossip, a quarrel dies down.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** As coals are to hot embers, and wood to fire, so is a contentious man to kindling strife.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The words of a whisperer are as dainty morsels, they go down into the innermost parts.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Like silver dross on an earthen vessel are the lips of a fervent one with an evil heart.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** A malicious man disguises himself with his lips, but he harbors evil in his heart.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** When his speech is charming, don’t believe him; for there are seven abominations in his heart.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** His malice may be concealed by deception, but his wickedness will be exposed in the assembly.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Whoever digs a pit shall fall into it. Whoever rolls a stone, it will come back on him.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** A lying tongue hates those it hurts; and a flattering mouth works ruin.
