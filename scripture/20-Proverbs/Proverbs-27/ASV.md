# Proverbs 27 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Boast not thyself of to-morrow; For thou knowest not what a day may bring forth.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Let another man praise thee, and not thine own mouth; A stranger, and not thine own lips.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A stone is heavy, and the sand weighty; But a fool’s vexation is heavier than they both.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Wrath is cruel, and anger is overwhelming; But who is able to stand before jealousy?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Better is open rebuke  Than love that is hidden.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Faithful are the wounds of a friend; But the kisses of an enemy are profuse.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The full soul loatheth a honeycomb; But to the hungry soul every bitter thing is sweet.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** As a bird that wandereth from her nest, So is a man that wandereth from his place.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Oil and perfume rejoice the heart; So doth the sweetness of a man’s friend that cometh of hearty counsel.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Thine own friend, and thy father’s friend, forsake not; And go not to thy brother’s house in the day of thy calamity: Better is a neighbor that is near than a brother far off.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** My son, be wise, and make my heart glad, That I may answer him that reproacheth me.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** A prudent man seeth the evil, and hideth himself; Butthe simple pass on, and suffer for it.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Take his garment that is surety for a stranger; And hold him in pledge that is surety for a foreign woman.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He that blesseth his friend with a loud voice, rising early in the morning, It shall be counted a curse to him.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** A continual dropping in a very rainy day And a contentious woman are alike:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He that would restrain her restraineth the wind; And his right hand encountereth oil.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Iron sharpeneth iron; So a man sharpeneth the countenance of his friend.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Whoso keepeth the fig-tree shall eat the fruit thereof; And he that regardeth his master shall be honored.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** As in water faceanswerethto face, So the heart of man to man.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Sheol and Abaddon are never satisfied; And the eyes of man are never satisfied.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The refining pot is for silver, and the furnace for gold; And a man istriedby his praise.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Though thou shouldest bray a fool in a mortar with a pestle along with bruised grain, Yet will not his foolishness depart from him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Be thou diligent to know the state of thy flocks, And look well to thy herds:

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For riches are not for ever; And doth the crown endure unto all generations?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The hay is carried, and the tender grass showeth itself, And the herbs of the mountains are gathered in.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** The lambs are for thy clothing, And the goats are the price of the field;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Andthere will begoats’ milk enough for thy food, for the food of thy household, And maintenance for thy maidens.
