# Proverbs 27 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Boast not thyself of to morrow; for thou knowest not what a day may bring forth.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Let another man praise thee, and not thine own mouth; a stranger, and not thine own lips.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A stone is heavy, and the sand weighty; but a fool’s wrath is heavier than them both.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Wrath is cruel, and anger is outrageous; but who is able to stand before envy?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Open rebuke is better than secret love.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Faithful are the wounds of a friend; but the kisses of an enemy are deceitful.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The full soul loatheth an honeycomb; but to the hungry soul every bitter thing is sweet.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** As a bird that wandereth from her nest, so is a man that wandereth from his place.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Ointment and perfume rejoice the heart: so doth the sweetness of a man’s friend by hearty counsel.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Thine own friend, and thy father’s friend, forsake not; neither go into thy brother’s house in the day of thy calamity: for better is a neighbour that is near than a brother far off.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** My son, be wise, and make my heart glad, that I may answer him that reproacheth me.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** A prudent man foreseeth the evil, and hideth himself; but the simple pass on, and are punished.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Take his garment that is surety for a stranger, and take a pledge of him for a strange woman.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He that blesseth his friend with a loud voice, rising early in the morning, it shall be counted a curse to him.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** A continual dropping in a very rainy day and a contentious woman are alike.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Whosoever hideth her hideth the wind, and the ointment of his right hand, which bewrayeth itself.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Iron sharpeneth iron; so a man sharpeneth the countenance of his friend.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Whoso keepeth the fig tree shall eat the fruit thereof: so he that waiteth on his master shall be honoured.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** As in water face answereth to face, so the heart of man to man.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Hell and destruction are never full; so the eyes of man are never satisfied.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** As the fining pot for silver, and the furnace for gold; so is a man to his praise.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Though thou shouldest bray a fool in a mortar among wheat with a pestle, yet will not his foolishness depart from him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Be thou diligent to know the state of thy flocks, and look well to thy herds.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For riches are not for ever: and doth the crown endure to every generation?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The hay appeareth, and the tender grass sheweth itself, and herbs of the mountains are gathered.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** The lambs are for thy clothing, and the goats are the price of the field.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And thou shalt have goats’ milk enough for thy food, for the food of thy household, and for the maintenance for thy maidens.
