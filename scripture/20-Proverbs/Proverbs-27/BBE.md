# Proverbs 27 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Do not make a noise about tomorrow, for you are not certain what a day's outcome may be.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Let another man give you praise, and not your mouth; one who is strange to you, and not your lips.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** A stone has great weight, and sand is crushing; but the wrath of the foolish is of greater weight than these.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Wrath is cruel, and angry feeling an overflowing stream; but who does not give way before envy?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Better is open protest than love kept secret.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The wounds of a friend are given in good faith, but the kisses of a hater are false.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The full man has no use for honey, but to the man in need of food every bitter thing is sweet.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Like a bird wandering from the place of her eggs is a man wandering from his station.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Oil and perfume make glad the heart, and the wise suggestion of a friend is sweet to the soul.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Do not give up your friend and your father's friend; and do not go into your brother's house in the day of your trouble: better is a neighbour who is near than a brother far off.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** My son, be wise and make my heart glad, so that I may give back an answer to him who puts me to shame.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The sharp man sees the evil and takes cover: the simple go straight on and get into trouble.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Take a man's clothing if he makes himself responsible for a strange man, and get an undertaking from him who gives his word for strange men.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He who gives a blessing to his friend with a loud voice, getting up early in the morning, will have it put to his account as a curse.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Like an unending dropping on a day of rain is a bitter-tongued woman.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He who keeps secret the secret of his friend, will get himself a name for good faith.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Iron makes iron sharp; so a man makes sharp his friend.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Whoever keeps a fig-tree will have its fruit; and the servant waiting on his master will be honoured.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Like face looking at face in water, so are the hearts of men to one another.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The underworld and Abaddon are never full, and the eyes of man have never enough.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The heating-pot is for silver and the oven-fire for gold, and a man is measured by what he is praised for.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Even if a foolish man is crushed with a hammer in a vessel among crushed grain, still his foolish ways will not go from him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Take care to have knowledge about the condition of your flocks, looking well after your herds;

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For wealth is not for ever, and money does not go on for all generations.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The grass comes up and the young grass is seen, and the mountain plants are got in.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** The lambs are for your clothing, and the he-goats make the value of a field:

<a id="v27"></a>**27** There will be goats' milk enough for your food, and for the support of your servant-girls.
