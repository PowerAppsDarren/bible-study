# Proverbs 7 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My son, keep my words, and lay up my commandments with thee.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Keep my commandments, and live; and my law as the apple of thine eye.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Bind them upon thy fingers, write them upon the table of thine heart.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Say unto wisdom, Thou art my sister; and call understanding thy kinswoman:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** That they may keep thee from the strange woman, from the stranger which flattereth with her words.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For at the window of my house I looked through my casement,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And beheld among the simple ones, I discerned among the youths, a young man void of understanding,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Passing through the street near her corner; and he went the way to her house,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** In the twilight, in the evening, in the black and dark night:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And, behold, there met him a woman with the attire of an harlot, and subtil of heart.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** (She is loud and stubborn; her feet abide not in her house:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now is she without, now in the streets, and lieth in wait at every corner.)

<a id="v13"></a>**13** So she caught him, and kissed him, and with an impudent face said unto him,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I have peace offerings with me; this day have I payed my vows.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Therefore came I forth to meet thee, diligently to seek thy face, and I have found thee.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I have decked my bed with coverings of tapestry, with carved works, with fine linen of Egypt.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** I have perfumed my bed with myrrh, aloes, and cinnamon.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Come, let us take our fill of love until the morning: let us solace ourselves with loves.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For the goodman is not at home, he is gone a long journey:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He hath taken a bag of money with him, and will come home at the day appointed.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** With her much fair speech she caused him to yield, with the flattering of her lips she forced him.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He goeth after her straightway, as an ox goeth to the slaughter, or as a fool to the correction of the stocks;

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Till a dart strike through his liver; as a bird hasteth to the snare, and knoweth not that it is for his life.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Hearken unto me now therefore, O ye children, and attend to the words of my mouth.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Let not thine heart decline to her ways, go not astray in her paths.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For she hath cast down many wounded: yea, many strong men have been slain by her.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Her house is the way to hell, going down to the chambers of death.
