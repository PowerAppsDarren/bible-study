# Proverbs 7 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My son, keep my words, And lay up my commandments with thee.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Keep my commandments and live; And my law as the apple of thine eye.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Bind them upon thy fingers; Write them upon the tablet of thy heart.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Say unto wisdom, Thou art my sister; And call understandingthy kinswoman:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** That they may keep thee from the strange woman, From the foreigner that flattereth with her words.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For at the window of my house  I looked forth through my lattice;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And I beheld among the simple ones, I discerned among the youths, A young man void of understanding,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Passing through the street near her corner; And he went the way to her house,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** In the twilight, in the evening of the day, In the middle of the night and in the darkness.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And, behold, there met him a woman With the attire of a harlot, and wily of heart.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** (She is clamorous and wilful; Her feet abide not in her house:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now she is in the streets, now in the broad places, And lieth in wait at every corner.)

<a id="v13"></a>**13** So she caught him, and kissed him, Andwith an impudent face she said unto him:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Sacrifices of peace-offerings are with me; This day have I paid my vows.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Therefore came I forth to meet thee, Diligently to seek thy face, and I have found thee.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I have spread my couch with carpets of tapestry, With striped cloths of the yarn of Egypt.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** I have perfumed my bed With myrrh, aloes, and cinnamon.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Come, let us take our fill of love until the morning; Let us solace ourselves with loves.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For the man is not at home; He is gone a long journey:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He hath taken a bag of money with him; He will come home at the full moon.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** With her much fair speech she causeth him to yield; With the flattering of her lips she forceth him along.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He goeth after her straightway, As an ox goeth to the slaughter, Or asone infetters to the correction of the fool;

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Till an arrow strike through his liver; As a bird hasteth to the snare, And knoweth not that it is for his life.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Now therefore, mysons, hearken unto me, And attend to the words of my mouth.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Let not thy heart decline to her ways; Go not astray in her paths.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For she hath cast down many wounded: Yea, all her slain are a mighty host.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Her house is the way to Sheol, Going down to the chambers of death.
