# Proverbs 7 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My son, keep my words. Lay up my commandments within you.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Keep my commandments and live! Guard my teaching as the apple of your eye.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Bind them on your fingers. Write them on the tablet of your heart.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Tell wisdom, “You are my sister.” Call understanding your relative,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** that they may keep you from the strange woman, from the foreigner who flatters with her words.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For at the window of my house, I looked out through my lattice.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** I saw among the simple ones. I discerned among the youths a young man void of understanding,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** passing through the street near her corner, he went the way to her house,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** in the twilight, in the evening of the day, in the middle of the night and in the darkness.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Behold, there a woman met him with the attire of a prostitute, and with crafty intent.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** She is loud and defiant. Her feet don’t stay in her house.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now she is in the streets, now in the squares, and lurking at every corner.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** So she caught him, and kissed him. With an impudent face she said to him:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** “Sacrifices of peace offerings are with me. This day I have paid my vows.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Therefore I came out to meet you, to diligently seek your face, and I have found you.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I have spread my couch with carpets of tapestry, with striped cloths of the yarn of Egypt.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** I have perfumed my bed with myrrh, aloes, and cinnamon.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Come, let’s take our fill of loving until the morning. Let’s solace ourselves with loving.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For my husband isn’t at home. He has gone on a long journey.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He has taken a bag of money with him. He will come home at the full moon.”

<a id="v21"></a>**21** With persuasive words, she led him astray. With the flattering of her lips, she seduced him.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He followed her immediately, as an ox goes to the slaughter, as a fool stepping into a noose.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Until an arrow strikes through his liver, as a bird hurries to the snare, and doesn’t know that it will cost his life.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Now therefore, sons, listen to me. Pay attention to the words of my mouth.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Don’t let your heart turn to her ways. Don’t go astray in her paths,

<a id="v26"></a>**26** for she has thrown down many wounded. Yes, all her slain are a mighty army.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Her house is the way to Sheol, going down to the rooms of death.
