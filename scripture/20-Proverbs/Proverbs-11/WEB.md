# Proverbs 11 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** A false balance is an abomination to Yahweh, but accurate weights are his delight.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** When pride comes, then comes shame, but with humility comes wisdom.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The integrity of the upright shall guide them, but the perverseness of the treacherous shall destroy them.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Riches don’t profit in the day of wrath, but righteousness delivers from death.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The righteousness of the blameless will direct his way, but the wicked shall fall by his own wickedness.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The righteousness of the upright shall deliver them, but the unfaithful will be trapped by evil desires.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** When a wicked man dies, hope perishes, and expectation of power comes to nothing.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** A righteous person is delivered out of trouble, and the wicked takes his place.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** With his mouth the godless man destroys his neighbor, but the righteous will be delivered through knowledge.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** When it goes well with the righteous, the city rejoices. When the wicked perish, there is shouting.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** By the blessing of the upright, the city is exalted, but it is overthrown by the mouth of the wicked.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** One who despises his neighbor is void of wisdom, but a man of understanding holds his peace.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** One who brings gossip betrays a confidence, but one who is of a trustworthy spirit is one who keeps a secret.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Where there is no wise guidance, the nation falls, but in the multitude of counselors there is victory.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He who is collateral for a stranger will suffer for it, but he who refuses pledges of collateral is secure.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** A gracious woman obtains honor, but violent men obtain riches.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The merciful man does good to his own soul, but he who is cruel troubles his own flesh.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Wicked people earn deceitful wages, but one who sows righteousness reaps a sure reward.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He who is truly righteous gets life. He who pursues evil gets death.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Those who are perverse in heart are an abomination to Yahweh, but those whose ways are blameless are his delight.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Most certainly, the evil man will not be unpunished, but the seed of the righteous will be delivered.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Like a gold ring in a pig’s snout, is a beautiful woman who lacks discretion.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The desire of the righteous is only good. The expectation of the wicked is wrath.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** There is one who scatters, and increases yet more. There is one who withholds more than is appropriate, but gains poverty.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The liberal soul shall be made fat. He who waters shall be watered also himself.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** People curse someone who withholds grain, but blessing will be on the head of him who sells it.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He who diligently seeks good seeks favor, but he who searches after evil, it shall come to him.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** He who trusts in his riches will fall, but the righteous shall flourish as the green leaf.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** He who troubles his own house shall inherit the wind. The foolish shall be servant to the wise of heart.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** The fruit of the righteous is a tree of life. He who is wise wins souls.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Behold, the righteous shall be repaid in the earth; how much more the wicked and the sinner!
