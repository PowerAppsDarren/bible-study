# 2 Peter 1 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Simon Peter, a servant and an apostle of Jesus Christ, to them that have obtained like precious faith with us through the righteousness of God and our Saviour Jesus Christ:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Grace and peace be multiplied unto you through the knowledge of God, and of Jesus our Lord,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** According as his divine power hath given unto us all things that pertain unto life and godliness, through the knowledge of him that hath called us to glory and virtue:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Whereby are given unto us exceeding great and precious promises: that by these ye might be partakers of the divine nature, having escaped the corruption that is in the world through lust.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And beside this, giving all diligence, add to your faith virtue; and to virtue knowledge;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And to knowledge temperance; and to temperance patience; and to patience godliness;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And to godliness brotherly kindness; and to brotherly kindness charity.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For if these things be in you, and abound, they make you that ye shall neither be barren nor unfruitful in the knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But he that lacketh these things is blind, and cannot see afar off, and hath forgotten that he was purged from his old sins.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Wherefore the rather, brethren, give diligence to make your calling and election sure: for if ye do these things, ye shall never fall:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For so an entrance shall be ministered unto you abundantly into the everlasting kingdom of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Wherefore I will not be negligent to put you always in remembrance of these things, though ye know them, and be established in the present truth.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Yea, I think it meet, as long as I am in this tabernacle, to stir you up by putting you in remembrance;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Knowing that shortly I must put off this my tabernacle, even as our Lord Jesus Christ hath shewed me.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Moreover I will endeavour that ye may be able after my decease to have these things always in remembrance.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For we have not followed cunningly devised fables, when we made known unto you the power and coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, but were eyewitnesses of his majesty.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For he received from God the Father honour and glory, when there came such a voice to him from the excellent glory, This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And this voice which came from heaven we heard, when we were with him in the holy mount.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** We have also a more sure word of prophecy; whereunto ye do well that ye take heed, as unto a light that shineth in a dark place, until the day dawn, and the day star arise in your hearts:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Knowing this first, that no prophecy of the scripture is of any private interpretation.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For the prophecy came not in old time by the will of man: but holy men of God spake as they were moved by the Holy Ghost.
