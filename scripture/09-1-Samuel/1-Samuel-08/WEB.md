# 1 Samuel 8 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** When Samuel was old, he made his sons judges over Israel.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Now the name of his firstborn was Joel; and the name of his second, Abijah: they were judges in Beersheba.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** His sons didn’t walk in his ways, but turned aside after lucre, and took bribes, and perverted justice.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Then all the elders of Israel gathered themselves together, and came to Samuel to Ramah;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** and they said to him, “Behold, you are old, and your sons don’t walk in your ways: now make us a king to judge us like all the nations.”

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But the thing displeased Samuel, when they said, “Give us a king to judge us.”   Samuel prayed to Yahweh.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Yahweh said to Samuel, “Listen to the voice of the people in all that they tell you; for they have not rejected you, but they have rejected me, that I should not be king over them.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** According to all the works which they have done since the day that I brought them up out of Egypt even to this day, in that they have forsaken me, and served other gods, so do they also to you.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Now therefore listen to their voice: however you shall protest solemnly to them, and shall show them the way of the king who shall reign over them.”

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Samuel told all Yahweh’s words to the people who asked of him a king.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He said, “This will be the way of the king who shall reign over you: he will take your sons, and appoint them to him, for his chariots, and to be his horsemen; and they shall run before his chariots;

<a id="v12"></a>**12** and he will appoint them to him for captains of thousands, and captains of fifties; and he will assign some to plow his ground, and to reap his harvest, and to make his instruments of war, and the instruments of his chariots.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He will take your daughters to be perfumers, and to be cooks, and to be bakers.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He will take your fields, and your vineyards, and your olive groves, even their best, and give them to his servants.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He will take the tenth of your seed, and of your vineyards, and give to his officers, and to his servants.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He will take your male servants, and your female servants, and your best young men, and your donkeys, and put them to his work.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He will take the tenth of your flocks: and you shall be his servants.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** You shall cry out in that day because of your king whom you shall have chosen you; and Yahweh will not answer you in that day.”

<a id="v19"></a>**19** But the people refused to listen to the voice of Samuel; and they said, “No; but we will have a king over us,

<a id="v20"></a>**20** that we also may be like all the nations, and that our king may judge us, and go out before us, and fight our battles.”

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Samuel heard all the words of the people, and he rehearsed them in the ears of Yahweh.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Yahweh said to Samuel, “Listen to their voice, and make them a king.”   Samuel said to the men of Israel, “Every man go to his city.”
