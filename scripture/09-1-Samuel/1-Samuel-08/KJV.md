# 1 Samuel 8 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And it came to pass, when Samuel was old, that he made his sons judges over Israel.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Now the name of his firstborn was Joel; and the name of his second, Abiah: they were judges in Beer–sheba.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And his sons walked not in his ways, but turned aside after lucre, and took bribes, and perverted judgment.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Then all the elders of Israel gathered themselves together, and came to Samuel unto Ramah,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And said unto him, Behold, thou art old, and thy sons walk not in thy ways: now make us a king to judge us like all the nations.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But the thing displeased Samuel, when they said, Give us a king to judge us. And Samuel prayed unto the Lord.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And the Lord said unto Samuel, Hearken unto the voice of the people in all that they say unto thee: for they have not rejected thee, but they have rejected me, that I should not reign over them.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** According to all the works which they have done since the day that I brought them up out of Egypt even unto this day, wherewith they have forsaken me, and served other gods, so do they also unto thee.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Now therefore hearken unto their voice: howbeit yet protest solemnly unto them, and shew them the manner of the king that shall reign over them.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And Samuel told all the words of the Lord unto the people that asked of him a king.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And he said, This will be the manner of the king that shall reign over you: He will take your sons, and appoint them for himself, for his chariots, and to be his horsemen; and some shall run before his chariots.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And he will appoint him captains over thousands, and captains over fifties; and will set them to ear his ground, and to reap his harvest, and to make his instruments of war, and instruments of his chariots.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And he will take your daughters to be confectionaries, and to be cooks, and to be bakers.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And he will take your fields, and your vineyards, and your oliveyards, even the best of them, and give them to his servants.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And he will take the tenth of your seed, and of your vineyards, and give to his officers, and to his servants.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And he will take your menservants, and your maidservants, and your goodliest young men, and your asses, and put them to his work.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He will take the tenth of your sheep: and ye shall be his servants.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And ye shall cry out in that day because of your king which ye shall have chosen you; and the Lord will not hear you in that day.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Nevertheless the people refused to obey the voice of Samuel; and they said, Nay; but we will have a king over us;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** That we also may be like all the nations; and that our king may judge us, and go out before us, and fight our battles.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And Samuel heard all the words of the people, and he rehearsed them in the ears of the Lord.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And the Lord said to Samuel, Hearken unto their voice, and make them a king. And Samuel said unto the men of Israel, Go ye every man unto his city.
