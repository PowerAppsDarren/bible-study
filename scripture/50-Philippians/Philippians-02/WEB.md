# Philippians 2 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** If there is therefore any exhortation in Christ, if any consolation of love, if any fellowship of the Spirit, if any tender mercies and compassion,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** make my joy full, by being like-minded, having the same love, being of one accord, of one mind;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** doing nothing through rivalry or through conceit, but in humility, each counting others better than himself;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** each of you not just looking to his own things, but each of you also to the things of others.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Have this in your mind, which was also in Christ Jesus,

<a id="v6"></a>**6** who, existing in the form of God, didn’t consider equality with God a thing to be grasped,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** but emptied himself, taking the form of a servant, being made in the likeness of men.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And being found in human form, he humbled himself, becoming obedient to death, yes, the death of the cross.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Therefore God also highly exalted him, and gave to him the name which is above every name;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** that at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of those in heaven, those on earth, and those under the earth,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** and that every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** So then, my beloved, even as you have always obeyed, not only in my presence, but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For it is God who works in you both to will and to work, for his good pleasure.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Do all things without murmurings and disputes,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** that you may become blameless and harmless, children of God without defect in the midst of a crooked and perverse generation, among whom you are seen as lights in the world,

<a id="v16"></a>**16** holding up the word of life; that I may have something to boast in the day of Christ, that I didn’t run in vain nor labor in vain.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Yes, and if I am poured out on the sacrifice and service of your faith, I rejoice, and rejoice with you all.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** In the same way, you also rejoice, and rejoice with me.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** But I hope in the Lord Jesus to send Timothy to you soon, that I also may be cheered up when I know how you are doing.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** For I have no one else like-minded, who will truly care about you.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For they all seek their own, not the things of Jesus Christ.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** But you know the proof of him, that, as a child serves a father, so he served with me in furtherance of the Good News.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Therefore I hope to send him at once, as soon as I see how it will go with me.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** But I trust in the Lord that I myself also will come shortly.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** But I counted it necessary to send to you Epaphroditus, my brother, fellow worker, fellow soldier, and your apostle and servant of my need;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** since he longed for you all, and was very troubled, because you had heard that he was sick.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** For indeed he was sick, nearly to death, but God had mercy on him; and not on him only, but on me also, that I might not have sorrow on sorrow.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** I have sent him therefore the more diligently, that, when you see him again, you may rejoice, and that I may be the less sorrowful.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Receive him therefore in the Lord with all joy, and hold such in honor,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** because for the work of Christ he came near to death, risking his life to supply that which was lacking in your service toward me.
