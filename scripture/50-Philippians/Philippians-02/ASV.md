# Philippians 2 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** If there is therefore any exhortation in Christ, if any consolation of love, if any fellowship of the Spirit, if any tender mercies and compassions,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** make full my joy, that ye be of the same mind, having the same love, being of one accord, of one mind;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** doing nothing through faction or through vainglory, but in lowliness of mind each counting other better than himself;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** not looking each of you to his own things, but each of you also to the things of others.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Have this mind in you, which was also in Christ Jesus:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** who, existing in the form of God, counted not the being on an equality with God a thing to be grasped,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** but emptied himself, taking the form of a servant, being made in the likeness of men;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** and being found in fashion as a man, he humbled himself, becoming obedient even unto death, yea, the death of the cross.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Wherefore also God highly exalted him, and gave unto him the name which is above every name;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** that in the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of things in heaven and things on earth and things under the earth,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** and that every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** So then, my beloved, even as ye have always obeyed, not as in my presence only, but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** for it is God who worketh in you both to will and to work, for his good pleasure.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Do all things without murmurings and questionings:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** that ye may become blameless and harmless, children of God without blemish in the midst of a crooked and perverse generation, among whom ye are seen as lights in the world,

<a id="v16"></a>**16** holding forth the word of life; that I may have whereof to glory in the day of Christ, that I did not run in vain neither labor in vain.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Yea, and if I am offered upon the sacrifice and service of your faith, I joy, and rejoice with you all:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** and in the same manner do ye also joy, and rejoice with me.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** But I hope in the Lord Jesus to send Timothy shortly unto you, that I also may be of good comfort, when I know your state.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** For I have no man likeminded, who will care truly for your state.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For they all seek their own, not the things of Jesus Christ.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** But ye know the proof of him, that, as a child serveth a father, so he served with me in furtherance of the gospel.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Him therefore I hope to send forthwith, so soon as I shall see how it will go with me:

<a id="v24"></a>**24** but I trust in the Lord that I myself also shall come shortly.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** But I counted it necessary to send to you Epaphroditus, my brother and fellow-worker and fellow-soldier, and your messenger and minister to my need;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** since he longed after you all, and was sore troubled, because ye had heard that he was sick:

<a id="v27"></a>**27** for indeed he was sick nigh unto death: but God had mercy on him; and not on him only, but on me also, that I might not have sorrow upon sorrow.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** I have sent him therefore the more diligently, that, when ye see him again, ye may rejoice, and that I may be the less sorrowful.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Receive him therefore in the Lord with all joy; and hold such in honor:

<a id="v30"></a>**30** because for the work of Christ he came nigh unto death, hazarding his life to supply that which was lacking in your service toward me.
