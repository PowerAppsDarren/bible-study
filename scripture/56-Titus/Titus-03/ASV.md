# Titus 3 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Put them in mind to be in subjection to rulers, to authorities, to be obedient, to be ready unto every good work,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** to speak evil of no man, not to be contentious, to be gentle, showing all meekness toward all men.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For we also once were foolish, disobedient, deceived, serving divers lusts and pleasures, living in malice and envy, hateful, hating one another.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But when the kindness of God our Saviour, and his love toward man, appeared,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** not by works done in righteousness, which we did ourselves, but according to his mercy he saved us, through the washing of regeneration and renewing of the Holy Spirit,

<a id="v6"></a>**6** which he poured out upon us richly, through Jesus Christ our Saviour;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** that, being justified by his grace, we might be made heirs according to the hope of eternal life.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Faithful is the saying, and concerning these things I desire that thou affirm confidently, to the end that they who have believed God may be careful to maintain good works. These things are good and profitable unto men:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** but shun foolish questionings, and genealogies, and strifes, and fightings about the law; for they are unprofitable and vain.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** A factious man after a first and second admonition refuse;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** knowing that such a one is perverted, and sinneth, being self-condemned.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** When I shall send Artemas unto thee, or Tychicus, give diligence to come unto me to Nicopolis: for there I have determined to winter.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Set forward Zenas the lawyer and Apollos on their journey diligently, that nothing be wanting unto them.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And let our people also learn to maintain good works for necessary uses, that they be not unfruitful.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** All that are with me salute thee. Salute them that love us in faith. Grace be with you all.
