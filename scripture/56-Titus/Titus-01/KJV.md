# Titus 1 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Paul, a servant of God, and an apostle of Jesus Christ, according to the faith of God’s elect, and the acknowledging of the truth which is after godliness;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** In hope of eternal life, which God, that cannot lie, promised before the world began;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But hath in due times manifested his word through preaching, which is committed unto me according to the commandment of God our Saviour;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** To Titus, mine own son after the common faith: Grace, mercy, and peace, from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ our Saviour.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For this cause left I thee in Crete, that thou shouldest set in order the things that are wanting, and ordain elders in every city, as I had appointed thee:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** If any be blameless, the husband of one wife, having faithful children not accused of riot or unruly.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For a bishop must be blameless, as the steward of God; not selfwilled, not soon angry, not given to wine, no striker, not given to filthy lucre;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But a lover of hospitality, a lover of good men, sober, just, holy, temperate;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Holding fast the faithful word as he hath been taught, that he may be able by sound doctrine both to exhort and to convince the gainsayers.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** For there are many unruly and vain talkers and deceivers, specially they of the circumcision:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Whose mouths must be stopped, who subvert whole houses, teaching things which they ought not, for filthy lucre’s sake.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** One of themselves, even a prophet of their own, said, The Cretians are alway liars, evil beasts, slow bellies.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** This witness is true. Wherefore rebuke them sharply, that they may be sound in the faith;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Not giving heed to Jewish fables, and commandments of men, that turn from the truth.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Unto the pure all things are pure: but unto them that are defiled and unbelieving is nothing pure; but even their mind and conscience is defiled.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** They profess that they know God; but in works they deny him, being abominable, and disobedient, and unto every good work reprobate.
