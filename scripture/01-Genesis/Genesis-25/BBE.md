# Genesis 25 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Abraham took another wife named Keturah.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** She became the mother of Zimran and Jokshan and Medan and Midian and Ishbak and Shuah.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And Jokshan became the father of Sheba and Dedan. And from Dedan came the Asshurim and Letushim and Leummim.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And from Midian came Ephah and Epher and Hanoch and Abida and Eldaah. All these were the offspring of Keturah.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Now Abraham gave all his property to Isaac;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But to the sons of his other women he gave offerings, and sent them away, while he was still living, into the east country.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Now the years of Abraham's life were a hundred and seventy-five.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And Abraham came to his death, an old man, full of years; and he was put to rest with his people.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And Isaac and Ishmael, his sons, put him to rest in the hollow rock of Machpelah, in the field of Ephron, the son of Zohar the Hittite, near Mamre;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The same field which Abraham got from the children of Heth: there Abraham was put to rest with Sarah, his wife.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Now after the death of Abraham, the blessing of God was with Isaac, his son.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now these are the generations of Ishmael, the son of Abraham, whose mother was Hagar the Egyptian, the servant of Sarah:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** These are the names of the sons of Ishmael by their generations: Ishmael's first son was Nebaioth; then Kedar and Adbeel and Mibsam

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And Mishma and Dumah and Massa,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Hadad and Tema, Jetur, Naphish, and Kedemah:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** These are the sons of Ishmael, and these are their names in their towns and their tent-circles; twelve chiefs with their peoples.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And the years of Ishmael's life were a hundred and thirty-seven: and he came to his end, and was put to rest with his people.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And their country was from Havilah to Shur which is east of Egypt: they took their place to the east of all their brothers.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Now these are the generations of Abraham's son Isaac:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Isaac was forty years old when he took Rebekah, the daughter of Bethuel the Aramaean of Paddan-aram, and the sister of Laban the Aramaean, to be his wife.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Isaac made prayer to the Lord for his wife because she had no children; and the Lord gave ear to his prayer, and Rebekah became with child.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And the children were fighting together inside her, and she said, If it is to be so, why am I like this? So she went to put her question to the Lord.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And the Lord said to her, Two nations are in your body, and two peoples will come to birth from you: the one will be stronger than the other, and the older will be the servant of the younger.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And when the time came for her to give birth, there were two children in her body.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And the first came out red from head to foot like a robe of hair, and they gave him the name of Esau.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And after him, his brother came out, gripping Esau's foot; and he was named Jacob: Isaac was sixty years old when she gave birth to them.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And the boys came to full growth; and Esau became a man of the open country, an expert bowman; but Jacob was a quiet man, living in tents.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Now Isaac's love was for Esau, because Esau's meat was greatly to his taste: but Rebekah had more love for Jacob.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And one day Jacob was cooking some soup when Esau came in from the fields in great need of food;

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And Esau said to Jacob, Give me a full meal of that red soup, for I am overcome with need for food: for this reason he was named Edom.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And Jacob said, First of all give me your birthright.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And Esau said, Truly, I am at the point of death: what profit is the birthright to me?

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And Jacob said, First of all give me your oath; and he gave him his oath, handing over his birthright to Jacob.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Then Jacob gave him bread and soup; and he took food and drink and went away, caring little for his birthright.
