# Genesis 25 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then again Abraham took a wife, and her name was Keturah.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And she bare him Zimran, and Jokshan, and Medan, and Midian, and Ishbak, and Shuah.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And Jokshan begat Sheba, and Dedan. And the sons of Dedan were Asshurim, and Letushim, and Leummim.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And the sons of Midian; Ephah, and Epher, and Hanoch, and Abida, and Eldaah. All these were the children of Keturah.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And Abraham gave all that he had unto Isaac.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But unto the sons of the concubines, which Abraham had, Abraham gave gifts, and sent them away from Isaac his son, while he yet lived, eastward, unto the east country.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And these are the days of the years of Abraham’s life which he lived, an hundred threescore and fifteen years.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Then Abraham gave up the ghost, and died in a good old age, an old man, and full of years; and was gathered to his people.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And his sons Isaac and Ishmael buried him in the cave of Machpelah, in the field of Ephron the son of Zohar the Hittite, which is before Mamre;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The field which Abraham purchased of the sons of Heth: there was Abraham buried, and Sarah his wife.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And it came to pass after the death of Abraham, that God blessed his son Isaac; and Isaac dwelt by the well Lahai–roi.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now these are the generations of Ishmael, Abraham’s son, whom Hagar the Egyptian, Sarah’s handmaid, bare unto Abraham:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And these are the names of the sons of Ishmael, by their names, according to their generations: the firstborn of Ishmael, Nebajoth; and Kedar, and Adbeel, and Mibsam,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And Mishma, and Dumah, and Massa,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Hadar, and Tema, Jetur, Naphish, and Kedemah:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** These are the sons of Ishmael, and these are their names, by their towns, and by their castles; twelve princes according to their nations.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And these are the years of the life of Ishmael, an hundred and thirty and seven years: and he gave up the ghost and died; and was gathered unto his people.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And they dwelt from Havilah unto Shur, that is before Egypt, as thou goest toward Assyria: and he died in the presence of all his brethren.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And these are the generations of Isaac, Abraham’s son: Abraham begat Isaac:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And Isaac was forty years old when he took Rebekah to wife, the daughter of Bethuel the Syrian of Padan–aram, the sister to Laban the Syrian.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And Isaac intreated the Lord for his wife, because she was barren: and the Lord was intreated of him, and Rebekah his wife conceived.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And the children struggled together within her; and she said, If it be so, why am I thus? And she went to enquire of the Lord.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And the Lord said unto her, Two nations are in thy womb, and two manner of people shall be separated from thy bowels; and the one people shall be stronger than the other people; and the elder shall serve the younger.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And when her days to be delivered were fulfilled, behold, there were twins in her womb.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And the first came out red, all over like an hairy garment; and they called his name Esau.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And after that came his brother out, and his hand took hold on Esau’s heel; and his name was called Jacob: and Isaac was threescore years old when she bare them.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And the boys grew: and Esau was a cunning hunter, a man of the field; and Jacob was a plain man, dwelling in tents.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And Isaac loved Esau, because he did eat of his venison: but Rebekah loved Jacob.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And Jacob sod pottage: and Esau came from the field, and he was faint:

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And Esau said to Jacob, Feed me, I pray thee, with that same red pottage; for I am faint: therefore was his name called Edom.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And Jacob said, Sell me this day thy birthright.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And Esau said, Behold, I am at the point to die: and what profit shall this birthright do to me?

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And Jacob said, Swear to me this day; and he sware unto him: and he sold his birthright unto Jacob.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Then Jacob gave Esau bread and pottage of lentiles; and he did eat and drink, and rose up, and went his way: thus Esau despised his birthright.
