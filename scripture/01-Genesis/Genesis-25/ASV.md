# Genesis 25 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Abraham took another wife, and her name was Keturah.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And she bare him Zimran, and Jokshan, and Medan, and Midian, and Ishbak, and Shuah.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And Jokshan begat Sheba, and Dedan. And the sons of Dedan were Asshurim, and Letushim, and Leummim.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And the sons of Midian: Ephah, and Epher, and Hanoch, and Abida, and Eldaah. All these were the children of Keturah.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And Abraham gave all that he had unto Isaac.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But unto the sons of the concubines, that Abraham had, Abraham gave gifts; and he sent them away from Isaac his son, while he yet lived, eastward, unto the east country.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And these are the days of the years of Abraham’s life which he lived, a hundred threescore and fifteen years.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And Abraham gave up the ghost, and died in a good old age, an old man, and full of years, and was gathered to his people.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And Isaac and Ishmael his sons buried him in the cave of Machpelah, in the field of Ephron the son of Zohar the Hittite, which is before Mamre;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** the field which Abraham purchased of the children of Heth: there was Abraham buried, and Sarah his wife.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And it came to pass after the death of Abraham, that God blessed Isaac his son: and Isaac dwelt by Beer-lahai-roi.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now these are the generations of Ishmael, Abraham’s son, whom Hagar the Egyptian, Sarah’s handmaid, bare unto Abraham:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** and these are the names of the sons of Ishmael, by their names, according to their generations: the first-born of Ishmael, Nebaioth; and Kedar, and Adbeel, and Mibsam,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** and Mishma, and Dumah, and Massa,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Hadad, and Tema, Jetur, Naphish, and Kedemah:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** these are the sons of Ishmael, and these are their names, by their villages, and by their encampments; twelve princes according to their nations.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And these are the years of the life of Ishmael, a hundred and thirty and seven years: and he gave up the ghost and died, and was gathered unto his people.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And they dwelt from Havilah unto Shur that is before Egypt, as thou goest toward Assyria: he abode over against all his brethren.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And these are the generations of Isaac, Abraham’s son: Abraham begat Isaac:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** and Isaac was forty years old when he took Rebekah, the daughter of Bethuel the Syrian of Paddan-aram, the sister of Laban the Syrian, to be his wife.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And Isaac entreated Jehovah for his wife, because she was barren: and Jehovah was entreated of him, and Rebekah his wife conceived.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And the children struggled together within her; and she said, If it be so, wherefore do I live? And she went to inquire of Jehovah.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And Jehovah said unto her,  Two nations are in thy womb, And two peoples shall be separated from thy bowels: And the one people shall be stronger than the other people; And the elder shall serve the younger.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And when her days to be delivered were fulfilled, behold, there were twins in her womb.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And the first came forth red, all over like a hairy garment; and they called his name Esau.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And after that came forth his brother, and his hand had hold on Esau’s heel; and his name was called Jacob: and Isaac was threescore years old when she bare them.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And the boys grew: and Esau was a skilful hunter, a man of the field; and Jacob was a quiet man, dwelling in tents.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Now Isaac loved Esau, because he did eat of his venison: and Rebekah loved Jacob.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And Jacob boiled pottage: and Esau came in from the field, and he was faint:

<a id="v30"></a>**30** and Esau said to Jacob, Feed me, I pray thee, with that same red pottage; for I am faint: therefore was his name called Edom.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And Jacob said, Sell me first thy birthright.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And Esau said, Behold, I am about to die: and what profit shall the birthright do to me?

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And Jacob said, Swear to me first; and he sware unto him: and he sold his birthright unto Jacob.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** And Jacob gave Esau bread and pottage of lentils; and he did eat and drink, and rose up, and went his way: so Esau despised his birthright.
