# Genesis 22 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And it came to pass after these things, that God did prove Abraham, and said unto him, Abraham; and he said, Here am I.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And he said, Take now thy son, thine only son, whom thou lovest, even Isaac, and get thee into the land of Moriah; and offer him there for a burnt-offering upon one of the mountains which I will tell thee of.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And Abraham rose early in the morning, and saddled his ass, and took two of his young men with him, and Isaac his son; and he clave the wood for the burnt-offering, and rose up, and went unto the place of which God had told him.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** On the third day Abraham lifted up his eyes, and saw the place afar off.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And Abraham said unto his young men, Abide ye here with the ass, and I and the lad will go yonder; and we will worship, and come again to you.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And Abraham took the wood of the burnt-offering, and laid it upon Isaac his son; and he took in his hand the fire and the knife; and they went both of them together.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And Isaac spake unto Abraham his father, and said, My father: and he said, Here am I, my son. And he said, Behold, the fire and the wood: but where is the lamb for a burnt-offering?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And Abraham said, God will provide himself the lamb for a burnt-offering, my son: so they went both of them together.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And they came to the place which God had told him of; and Abraham built the altar there, and laid the wood in order, and bound Isaac his son, and laid him on the altar, upon the wood.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And Abraham stretched forth his hand, and took the knife to slay his son.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And the angel of Jehovah called unto him out of heaven, and said, Abraham, Abraham: and he said, Here am I.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And he said, Lay not thy hand upon the lad, neither do thou anything unto him; for now I know that thou fearest God, seeing thou hast not withheld thy son, thine only son, from me.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And Abraham lifted up his eyes, and looked, and, behold, behind him a ram caught in the thicket by his horns: and Abraham went and took the ram, and offered him up for a burnt-offering in the stead of his son.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And Abraham called the name of that place Jehovah-jireh: as it is said to this day, In the mount of Jehovah it shall be provided.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And the angel of Jehovah called unto Abraham a second time out of heaven,

<a id="v16"></a>**16** and said, By myself have I sworn, saith Jehovah, because thou hast done this thing, and hast not withheld thy son, thine only son,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** that in blessing I will bless thee, and in multiplying I will multiply thy seed as the stars of the heavens, and as the sand which is upon the sea-shore; and thy seed shall possess the gate of his enemies;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** and in thy seed shall all the nations of the earth be blessed; because thou hast obeyed my voice.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** So Abraham returned unto his young men, and they rose up and went together to Beer-sheba; and Abraham dwelt at Beer-sheba.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And it came to pass after these things, that it was told Abraham, saying, Behold, Milcah, she also hath borne children unto thy brother Nahor:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Uz his first-born, and Buz his brother, and Kemuel the father of Aram,

<a id="v22"></a>**22** and Chesed, and Hazo, and Pildash, and Jidlaph, and Bethuel.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And Bethuel begat Rebekah: these eight did Milcah bear to Nahor, Abraham’s brother.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And his concubine, whose name was Reumah, she also bare Tebah, and Gaham, and Tahash, and Maacah.
