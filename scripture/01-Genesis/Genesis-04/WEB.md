# Genesis 4 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The man knew Eve his wife. She conceived, and gave birth to Cain, and said, “I have gotten a man with Yahweh’s help.”

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Again she gave birth, to Cain’s brother Abel. Abel was a keeper of sheep, but Cain was a tiller of the ground.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** As time passed, Cain brought an offering to Yahweh from the fruit of the ground.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Abel also brought some of the firstborn of his flock and of its fat. Yahweh respected Abel and his offering,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** but he didn’t respect Cain and his offering. Cain was very angry, and the expression on his face fell.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Yahweh said to Cain, “Why are you angry? Why has the expression of your face fallen?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** If you do well, won’t it be lifted up? If you don’t do well, sin crouches at the door. Its desire is for you, but you are to rule over it.”

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Cain said to Abel, his brother, “Let’s go into the field.” While they were in the field, Cain rose up against Abel, his brother, and killed him.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Yahweh said to Cain, “Where is Abel, your brother?”   He said, “I don’t know. Am I my brother’s keeper?”

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Yahweh said, “What have you done? The voice of your brother’s blood cries to me from the ground.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Now you are cursed because of the ground, which has opened its mouth to receive your brother’s blood from your hand.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** From now on, when you till the ground, it won’t yield its strength to you. You will be a fugitive and a wanderer in the earth.”

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Cain said to Yahweh, “My punishment is greater than I can bear.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Behold, you have driven me out this day from the surface of the ground. I will be hidden from your face, and I will be a fugitive and a wanderer in the earth. Whoever finds me will kill me.”

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Yahweh said to him, “Therefore whoever slays Cain, vengeance will be taken on him sevenfold.” Yahweh appointed a sign for Cain, so that anyone finding him would not strike him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Cain left Yahweh’s presence, and lived in the land of Nod, east of Eden.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Cain knew his wife. She conceived, and gave birth to Enoch. He built a city, and called the name of the city, after the name of his son, Enoch.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** To Enoch was born Irad. Irad became the father of Mehujael. Mehujael became the father of Methushael. Methushael became the father of Lamech.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Lamech took two wives: the name of the first one was Adah, and the name of the second one was Zillah.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Adah gave birth to Jabal, who was the father of those who dwell in tents and have livestock.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His brother’s name was Jubal, who was the father of all who handle the harp and pipe.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Zillah also gave birth to Tubal Cain, the forger of every cutting instrument of brass and iron. Tubal Cain’s sister was Naamah.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Lamech said to his wives,  “Adah and Zillah, hear my voice. You wives of Lamech, listen to my speech, for I have slain a man for wounding me, a young man for bruising me.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** If Cain will be avenged seven times, truly Lamech seventy-seven times.”

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Adam knew his wife again. She gave birth to a son, and named him Seth, “for God has given me another child instead of Abel, for Cain killed him.”

<a id="v26"></a>**26** A son was also born to Seth, and he named him Enosh. At that time men began to call on Yahweh’s name.
