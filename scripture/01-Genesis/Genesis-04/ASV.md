# Genesis 4 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And the man knew Eve his wife; and she conceived, and bare Cain, and said, I have gotten a man with the help of Jehovah.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And again she bare his brother Abel. And Abel was a keeper of sheep, but Cain was a tiller of the ground.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And in process of time it came to pass, that Cain brought of the fruit of the ground an offering unto Jehovah.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And Abel, he also brought of the firstlings of his flock and of the fat thereof. And Jehovah had respect unto Abel and to his offering:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** but unto Cain and to his offering he had not respect. And Cain was very wroth, and his countenance fell.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And Jehovah said unto Cain, Why art thou wroth? and why is thy countenance fallen?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** If thou doest well, shall it not be lifted up? and if thou doest not well, sin coucheth at the door; and unto thee shall be its desire; but do thou rule over it.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And Cain told Abel his brother. And it came to pass, when they were in the field, that Cain rose up against Abel his brother, and slew him.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And Jehovah said unto Cain, Where is Abel thy brother? And he said, I know not: am I my brother’s keeper?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And he said, What hast thou done? the voice of thy brother’s blood crieth unto me from the ground.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And now cursed art thou from the ground, which hath opened its mouth to receive thy brother’s blood from thy hand;

<a id="v12"></a>**12** when thou tillest the ground, it shall not henceforth yield unto thee its strength; a fugitive and a wanderer shalt thou be in the earth.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And Cain said unto Jehovah, My punishment is greater than I can bear.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Behold, thou hast driven me out this day from the face of the ground; and from thy face shall I be hid; and I shall be a fugitive and a wanderer in the earth; and it will come to pass, that whosoever findeth me will slay me.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And Jehovah said unto him, Therefore whosoever slayeth Cain, vengeance shall be taken on him sevenfold. And Jehovah appointed a sign for Cain, lest any finding him should smite him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And Cain went out from the presence of Jehovah, and dwelt in the land of Nod, on the east of Eden.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And Cain knew his wife; and she conceived, and bare Enoch: and he builded a city, and called the name of the city, after the name of his son, Enoch.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And unto Enoch was born Irad: and Irad begat Mehujael; and Mehujael begat Methushael; and Methushael begat Lamech.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And Lamech took unto him two wives: the name of the one was Adah, and the name of the other Zillah.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And Adah bare Jabal: he was the father of such as dwell in tents and have cattle.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And his brother’s name was Jubal: he was the father of all such as handle the harp and pipe.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And Zillah, she also bare Tubal-cain, the forger of every cutting instrument of brass and iron: and the sister of Tubal-cain was Naamah.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And Lamech said unto his wives:  Adah and Zillah, hear my voice; Ye wives of Lamech, hearken unto my speech: For I have slain a man for wounding me, And a young man for bruising me:

<a id="v24"></a>**24** If Cain shall be avenged sevenfold, Truly Lamech seventy and sevenfold.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And Adam knew his wife again; and she bare a son, and called his name Seth: For, said she, God hath appointed me another seed instead of Abel; for Cain slew him.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And to Seth, to him also there was born a son; and he called his name Enosh. Then began men to call upon the name of Jehovah.
