# Genesis 11 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And the whole earth was of one language and of one speech.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And it came to pass, as they journeyed east, that they found a plain in the land of Shinar; and they dwelt there.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And they said one to another, Come, let us make brick, and burn them thoroughly. And they had brick for stone, and slime had they for mortar.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And they said, Come, let us build us a city, and a tower, whose top may reach unto heaven, and let us make us a name; lest we be scattered abroad upon the face of the whole earth.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And Jehovah came down to see the city and the tower, which the children of men builded.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And Jehovah said, Behold, they are one people, and they have all one language; and this is what they begin to do: and now nothing will be withholden from them, which they purpose to do.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Come, let us go down, and there confound their language, that they may not understand one another’s speech.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** So Jehovah scattered them abroad from thence upon the face of all the earth: and they left off building the city.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Therefore was the name of it called Babel; because Jehovah did there confound the language of all the earth: and from thence did Jehovah scatter them abroad upon the face of all the earth.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** These are the generations of Shem. Shem was a hundred years old, and begat Arpachshad two years after the flood:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** and Shem lived after he begat Arpachshad five hundred years, and begat sons and daughters.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And Arpachshad lived five and thirty years, and begat Shelah:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** and Arpachshad lived after he begat Shelah four hundred and three years, and begat sons and daughters.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And Shelah lived thirty years, and begat Eber:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** and Shelah lived after he begat Eber four hundred and three years, and begat sons and daughters.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And Eber lived four and thirty years, and begat Peleg:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** and Eber lived after he begat Peleg four hundred and thirty years, and begat sons and daughters.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And Peleg lived thirty years, and begat Reu:

<a id="v19"></a>**19** and Peleg lived after he begat Reu two hundred and nine years, and begat sons and daughters.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And Reu lived two and thirty years, and begat Serug:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** and Reu lived after he begat Serug two hundred and seven years, and begat sons and daughters.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And Serug lived thirty years, and begat Nahor:

<a id="v23"></a>**23** and Serug lived after he begat Nahor two hundred years, and begat sons and daughters.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And Nahor lived nine and twenty years, and begat Terah:

<a id="v25"></a>**25** and Nahor lived after he begat Terah a hundred and nineteen years, and begat sons and daughters.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And Terah lived seventy years, and begat Abram, Nahor, and Haran.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Now these are the generations of Terah. Terah begat Abram, Nahor, and Haran; and Haran begat Lot.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And Haran died before his father Terah in the land of his nativity, in Ur of the Chaldees.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And Abram and Nahor took them wives: the name of Abram’s wife was Sarai; and the name of Nahor’s wife, Milcah, the daughter of Haran, the father of Milcah, and the father of Iscah.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And Sarai was barren; she had no child.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And Terah took Abram his son, and Lot the son of Haran, his son’s son, and Sarai his daughter-in-law, his son Abram’s wife; and they went forth with them from Ur of the Chaldees, to go into the land of Canaan; and they came unto Haran, and dwelt there.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And the days of Terah were two hundred and five years: and Terah died in Haran.
