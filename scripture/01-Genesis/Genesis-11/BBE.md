# Genesis 11 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And all the earth had one language and one tongue.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And it came about that in their wandering from the east, they came to a stretch of flat country in the land of Shinar, and there they made their living-place.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And they said one to another, Come, let us make bricks, burning them well. And they had bricks for stone, putting them together with sticky earth.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And they said, Come, let us make a town, and a tower whose top will go up as high as heaven; and let us make a great name for ourselves, so that we may not be wanderers over the face of the earth.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And the Lord came down to see the town and the tower which the children of men were building.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And the Lord said, See, they are all one people and have all one language; and this is only the start of what they may do: and now it will not be possible to keep them from any purpose of theirs.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Come, let us go down and take away the sense of their language, so that they will not be able to make themselves clear to one another.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** So the Lord God sent them away into every part of the earth: and they gave up building their town.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** So it was named Babel, because there the Lord took away the sense of all languages and from there the Lord sent them away over all the face of the earth.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** These are the generations of Shem. Shem was a hundred years old when he became the father of Arpachshad, two years after the great flow of waters;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And after the birth of Arpachshad, Shem went on living for five hundred years, and had sons and daughters:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And Arpachshad was thirty-five years old when he became the father of Shelah:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And after the birth of Shelah, Arpachshad went on living for four hundred and three years, and had sons and daughters:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And Shelah was thirty years old when he became the father of Eber:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And after the birth of Eber, Shelah went on living for four hundred and three years, and had sons and daughters:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And Eber was thirty-four years old when he became the father of Peleg:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And after the birth of Peleg, Eber went on living for four hundred and thirty years, and had sons and daughters:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And Peleg was thirty years old when he became the father of Reu:

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And after the birth of Reu, Peleg went on living for two hundred and nine years, and had sons and daughters:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And Reu was thirty-two years old when he became the father of Serug:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And after the birth of Serug, Reu went on living for two hundred and seven years, and had sons and daughters:

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And Serug was thirty years old when he became the father of Nahor:

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And after the birth of Nahor, Serug went on living for two hundred years, and had sons and daughters:

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And Nahor was twenty-nine years old when he became the father of Terah:

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And after the birth of Terah, Nahor went on living for a hundred and nineteen years, and had sons and daughters:

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And Terah was seventy years old when he became the father of Abram, Nahor, and Haran.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** These are the generations of Terah: Terah was the father of Abram, Nahor, and Haran; and Haran was the father of Lot.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And death came to Haran when he was with his father Terah in the land of his birth, Ur of the Chaldees.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And Abram and Nahor took wives for themselves: the name of Abram's wife was Sarai, and the name of Nahor's wife was Milcah, the daughter of Haran, the father of Milcah and Iscah.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And Sarai had no child.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And Terah took Abram, his son, and Lot, the son of Haran, and Sarai, his daughter-in-law, the wife of his son Abram and they went out from Ur of the Chaldees, to go to the land of Canaan; and they came to Haran, and were there for some time.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And all the years of Terah's life were two hundred and five: and Terah came to his end in Haran.
