# Genesis 26 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then came a time of great need in the land, like that which had been before in the days of Abraham. And Isaac went to Abimelech, king of the Philistines, at Gerar.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And the Lord came to him in a vision and said, Do not go down to Egypt; keep in the land of which I will give you knowledge:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Keep in this land, and I will be with you and give you my blessing; for to you and to your seed will I give all these lands, giving effect to the oath which I made to your father Abraham;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** I will make your seed like the stars of heaven in number, and will give them all these lands, and your seed will be a blessing to all the nations of the earth;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Because Abraham gave ear to my voice and kept my words, my rules, my orders, and my laws.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** So Isaac went on living in Gerar;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And when he was questioned by the men of the place about his wife, he said, She is my sister; fearing to say, She is my wife; for, he said, the men of the place may put me to death on account of Rebekah; because she is very beautiful.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And when he had been there for some time, Abimelech, king of the Philistines, looking through a window, saw Isaac playing with Rebekah his wife.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And he said to Isaac, It is clear that she is your wife: why then did you say, She is my sister? And Isaac said, For fear that I might be put to death because of her.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Then Abimelech said, What have you done to us? one of the people might well have had connection with your wife, and the sin would have been ours.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And Abimelech gave orders to his people that anyone touching Isaac or his wife was to be put to death.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now Isaac, planting seed in that land, got in the same year fruit a hundred times as much, for the blessing of the Lord was on him.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And his wealth became very great, increasing more and more;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For he had great wealth of flocks and herds and great numbers of servants; so that the Philistines were full of envy.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Now all the water-holes, which his father's servants had made in the days of Abraham, had been stopped up with earth by the Philistines.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And Abimelech said to Isaac, Go away from us, for you are stronger than we are.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** So Isaac went away from there, and put up his tents in the valley of Gerar, making his living-place there.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And he made again the water-holes which had been made in the days of Abraham his father, and which had been stopped up by the Philistines; and he gave them the names which his father had given them.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Now Isaac's servants made holes in the valley, and came to a spring of flowing water.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But the herdmen of Gerar had a fight with Isaac's herdmen, for they said, The spring is ours: so he gave the spring the name of Esek, because there was a fight about it.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Then they made another water-hole, and there was a fight about that, so he gave it the name of Sitnah.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Then he went away from there, and made another water-hole, about which there was no fighting: so he gave it the name of Rehoboth, for he said, Now the Lord has made room for us, and we will have fruit in this land.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And from there he went on to Beer-sheba.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** That night the Lord came to him in a vision, and said, I am the God of your father Abraham: have no fear for I am with you, blessing you, and your seed will be increased because of my servant Abraham.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Then he made an altar there, and gave worship to the name of the Lord, and he put up his tents there, and there his servants made a water-hole.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And Abimelech had come to him from Gerar, with Ahuzzath his friend and Phicol, the captain of his army.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And Isaac said to them, Why have you come to me, seeing that in your hate for me you sent me away from you?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And they said, We saw clearly that the Lord was with you: so we said, Let there be an oath between us and you, and let us make an agreement with you;

<a id="v29"></a>**29** That you will do us no damage, even as we put no hand on you, and did you nothing but good, and sent you away in peace: and now the blessing of the Lord is on you.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Then he made a feast for them, and they all had food and drink.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And early in the morning they took an oath one to the other: then Isaac sent them away and they went on their way in peace.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And that day Isaac's servants came to him and gave him word of the water-hole which they had made, and said to him, We have come to water.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And he gave it the name of Shibah: so the name of that town is Beer-sheba to this day.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** And when Esau was forty years old, he took as his wives Judith, the daughter of Beeri the Hittite, and Basemath, the daughter of Elon the Hittite:

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And Isaac and Rebekah had grief of mind because of them.
