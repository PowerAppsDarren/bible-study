# Genesis 20 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Abraham went on his way from there to the land of the South, and was living between Kadesh and Shur, in Gerar.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And Abraham said of Sarah, his wife, She is my sister: and Abimelech, king of Gerar, sent and took Sarah.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But God came to Abimelech in a dream in the night, and said to him, Truly you are a dead man because of the woman whom you have taken; for she is a man's wife.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Now Abimelech had not come near her; and he said, Lord, will you put to death an upright nation?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Did he not say to me himself, She is my sister? and she herself said, He is my brother: with an upright heart and clean hands have I done this.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And God said to him in the dream, I see that you have done this with an upright heart, and I have kept you from sinning against me: for this reason I did not let you come near her.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** So now, give the man back his wife, for he is a prophet, and let him say a prayer for you, so your life may be safe: but if you do not give her back, be certain that death will come to you and all your house.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** So Abimelech got up early in the morning and sent for all his servants and gave them word of these things, and they were full of fear.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Then Abimelech sent for Abraham, and said, What have you done to us? what wrong have I done you that you have put on me and on my kingdom so great a sin? You have done to me things which are not to be done.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And Abimelech said to Abraham, Why did you do this thing?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And Abraham said, Because it seemed to me that there was no fear of God in this place, and that they might put me to death because of my wife.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And, in fact, she is my sister, the daughter of my father, but not the daughter of my mother; and she became my wife:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And when God sent me wandering from my father's house, I said to her, Let this be the sign of your love for me; wherever we go, say of me, He is my brother.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Then Abimelech gave to Abraham sheep and oxen and men-servants and women-servants, and gave him back his wife Sarah.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And Abimelech said, See, all my land is before you; take whatever place seems good to you.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And he said to Sarah, See, I have given to your brother a thousand bits of silver so that your wrong may be put right; now your honour is clear in the eyes of all.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Then Abraham made prayer to God, and God made Abimelech well again, and his wife and his women-servants, so that they had children.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For the Lord had kept all the women of the house of Abimelech from having children, because of Sarah, Abraham's wife.
