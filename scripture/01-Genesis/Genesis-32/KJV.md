# Genesis 32 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Jacob went on his way, and the angels of God met him.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And when Jacob saw them, he said, This is God’s host: and he called the name of that place Mahanaim.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And Jacob sent messengers before him to Esau his brother unto the land of Seir, the country of Edom.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And he commanded them, saying, Thus shall ye speak unto my lord Esau; Thy servant Jacob saith thus, I have sojourned with Laban, and stayed there until now:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And I have oxen, and asses, flocks, and menservants, and womenservants: and I have sent to tell my lord, that I may find grace in thy sight.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And the messengers returned to Jacob, saying, We came to thy brother Esau, and also he cometh to meet thee, and four hundred men with him.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Then Jacob was greatly afraid and distressed: and he divided the people that was with him, and the flocks, and herds, and the camels, into two bands;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And said, If Esau come to the one company, and smite it, then the other company which is left shall escape.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And Jacob said, O God of my father Abraham, and God of my father Isaac, the Lord which saidst unto me, Return unto thy country, and to thy kindred, and I will deal well with thee:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** I am not worthy of the least of all the mercies, and of all the truth, which thou hast shewed unto thy servant; for with my staff I passed over this Jordan; and now I am become two bands.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Deliver me, I pray thee, from the hand of my brother, from the hand of Esau: for I fear him, lest he will come and smite me, and the mother with the children.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And thou saidst, I will surely do thee good, and make thy seed as the sand of the sea, which cannot be numbered for multitude.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And he lodged there that same night; and took of that which came to his hand a present for Esau his brother;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Two hundred she goats, and twenty he goats, two hundred ewes, and twenty rams,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Thirty milch camels with their colts, forty kine, and ten bulls, twenty she asses, and ten foals.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And he delivered them into the hand of his servants, every drove by themselves; and said unto his servants, Pass over before me, and put a space betwixt drove and drove.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And he commanded the foremost, saying, When Esau my brother meeteth thee, and asketh thee, saying, Whose art thou? and whither goest thou? and whose are these before thee?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Then thou shalt say, They be thy servant Jacob’s; it is a present sent unto my lord Esau: and, behold, also he is behind us.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And so commanded he the second, and the third, and all that followed the droves, saying, On this manner shall ye speak unto Esau, when ye find him.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And say ye moreover, Behold, thy servant Jacob is behind us. For he said, I will appease him with the present that goeth before me, and afterward I will see his face; peradventure he will accept of me.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** So went the present over before him: and himself lodged that night in the company.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And he rose up that night, and took his two wives, and his two womenservants, and his eleven sons, and passed over the ford Jabbok.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And he took them, and sent them over the brook, and sent over that he had.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And Jacob was left alone; and there wrestled a man with him until the breaking of the day.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And when he saw that he prevailed not against him, he touched the hollow of his thigh; and the hollow of Jacob’s thigh was out of joint, as he wrestled with him.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And he said, Let me go, for the day breaketh. And he said, I will not let thee go, except thou bless me.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And he said unto him, What is thy name? And he said, Jacob.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And he said, Thy name shall be called no more Jacob, but Israel: for as a prince hast thou power with God and with men, and hast prevailed.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And Jacob asked him, and said, Tell me, I pray thee, thy name. And he said, Wherefore is it that thou dost ask after my name? And he blessed him there.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And Jacob called the name of the place Peniel: for I have seen God face to face, and my life is preserved.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And as he passed over Penuel the sun rose upon him, and he halted upon his thigh.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Therefore the children of Israel eat not of the sinew which shrank, which is upon the hollow of the thigh, unto this day: because he touched the hollow of Jacob’s thigh in the sinew that shrank.
