# Genesis 18 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And the Lord appeared unto him in the plains of Mamre: and he sat in the tent door in the heat of the day;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And he lift up his eyes and looked, and, lo, three men stood by him: and when he saw them, he ran to meet them from the tent door, and bowed himself toward the ground,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And said, My Lord, if now I have found favour in thy sight, pass not away, I pray thee, from thy servant:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Let a little water, I pray you, be fetched, and wash your feet, and rest yourselves under the tree:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And I will fetch a morsel of bread, and comfort ye your hearts; after that ye shall pass on: for therefore are ye come to your servant. And they said, So do, as thou hast said.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And Abraham hastened into the tent unto Sarah, and said, Make ready quickly three measures of fine meal, knead it, and make cakes upon the hearth.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And Abraham ran unto the herd, and fetcht a calf tender and good, and gave it unto a young man; and he hasted to dress it.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And he took butter, and milk, and the calf which he had dressed, and set it before them; and he stood by them under the tree, and they did eat.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And they said unto him, Where is Sarah thy wife? And he said, Behold, in the tent.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And he said, I will certainly return unto thee according to the time of life; and, lo, Sarah thy wife shall have a son. And Sarah heard it in the tent door, which was behind him.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Now Abraham and Sarah were old and well stricken in age; and it ceased to be with Sarah after the manner of women.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Therefore Sarah laughed within herself, saying, After I am waxed old shall I have pleasure, my lord being old also?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And the Lord said unto Abraham, Wherefore did Sarah laugh, saying, Shall I of a surety bear a child, which am old?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Is any thing too hard for the Lord? At the time appointed I will return unto thee, according to the time of life, and Sarah shall have a son.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Then Sarah denied, saying, I laughed not; for she was afraid. And he said, Nay; but thou didst laugh.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And the men rose up from thence, and looked toward Sodom: and Abraham went with them to bring them on the way.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And the Lord said, Shall I hide from Abraham that thing which I do;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Seeing that Abraham shall surely become a great and mighty nation, and all the nations of the earth shall be blessed in him?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For I know him, that he will command his children and his household after him, and they shall keep the way of the Lord, to do justice and judgment; that the Lord may bring upon Abraham that which he hath spoken of him.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And the Lord said, Because the cry of Sodom and Gomorrah is great, and because their sin is very grievous;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** I will go down now, and see whether they have done altogether according to the cry of it, which is come unto me; and if not, I will know.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And the men turned their faces from thence, and went toward Sodom: but Abraham stood yet before the Lord.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And Abraham drew near, and said, Wilt thou also destroy the righteous with the wicked?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Peradventure there be fifty righteous within the city: wilt thou also destroy and not spare the place for the fifty righteous that are therein?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** That be far from thee to do after this manner, to slay the righteous with the wicked: and that the righteous should be as the wicked, that be far from thee: Shall not the Judge of all the earth do right?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And the Lord said, If I find in Sodom fifty righteous within the city, then I will spare all the place for their sakes.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And Abraham answered and said, Behold now, I have taken upon me to speak unto the Lord, which am but dust and ashes:

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Peradventure there shall lack five of the fifty righteous: wilt thou destroy all the city for lack of five? And he said, If I find there forty and five, I will not destroy it.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And he spake unto him yet again, and said, Peradventure there shall be forty found there. And he said, I will not do it for forty’s sake.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And he said unto him, Oh let not the Lord be angry, and I will speak: Peradventure there shall thirty be found there. And he said, I will not do it, if I find thirty there.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And he said, Behold now, I have taken upon me to speak unto the Lord: Peradventure there shall be twenty found there. And he said, I will not destroy it for twenty’s sake.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And he said, Oh let not the Lord be angry, and I will speak yet but this once: Peradventure ten shall be found there. And he said, I will not destroy it for ten’s sake.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And the Lord went his way, as soon as he had left communing with Abraham: and Abraham returned unto his place.
