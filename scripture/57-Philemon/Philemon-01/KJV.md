# Philemon 1 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Paul, a prisoner of Jesus Christ, and Timothy our brother, unto Philemon our dearly beloved, and fellowlabourer,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And to our beloved Apphia, and Archippus our fellowsoldier, and to the church in thy house:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Grace to you, and peace, from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** I thank my God, making mention of thee always in my prayers,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Hearing of thy love and faith, which thou hast toward the Lord Jesus, and toward all saints;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** That the communication of thy faith may become effectual by the acknowledging of every good thing which is in you in Christ Jesus.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For we have great joy and consolation in thy love, because the bowels of the saints are refreshed by thee, brother.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Wherefore, though I might be much bold in Christ to enjoin thee that which is convenient,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Yet for love’s sake I rather beseech thee, being such an one as Paul the aged, and now also a prisoner of Jesus Christ.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** I beseech thee for my son Onesimus, whom I have begotten in my bonds:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Which in time past was to thee unprofitable, but now profitable to thee and to me:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Whom I have sent again: thou therefore receive him, that is, mine own bowels:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Whom I would have retained with me, that in thy stead he might have ministered unto me in the bonds of the gospel:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But without thy mind would I do nothing; that thy benefit should not be as it were of necessity, but willingly.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** For perhaps he therefore departed for a season, that thou shouldest receive him for ever;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Not now as a servant, but above a servant, a brother beloved, specially to me, but how much more unto thee, both in the flesh, and in the Lord?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** If thou count me therefore a partner, receive him as myself.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** If he hath wronged thee, or oweth thee ought, put that on mine account;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** I Paul have written it with mine own hand, I will repay it: albeit I do not say to thee how thou owest unto me even thine own self besides.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Yea, brother, let me have joy of thee in the Lord: refresh my bowels in the Lord.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Having confidence in thy obedience I wrote unto thee, knowing that thou wilt also do more than I say.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** But withal prepare me also a lodging: for I trust that through your prayers I shall be given unto you.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** There salute thee Epaphras, my fellowprisoner in Christ Jesus;

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Marcus, Aristarchus, Demas, Lucas, my fellowlabourers.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit. Amen.  Written from Rome to Philemon, by Onesimus a servant.
