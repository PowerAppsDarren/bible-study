# Philemon 1 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Paul, a prisoner of Jesus Christ, and Timothy our brother, to Philemon, our dear helper in the faith,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And to Apphia, our sister, and to Archippus, our brother in God's army, and to the church in your house:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** I give praise to God at all times and make prayer for you,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Hearing of the love and the faith which you have to the Lord Jesus and to all the saints;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** That the faith which you have in common with them may be working with power, in the knowledge of every good thing in you, for Christ.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For I had great joy and comfort in your love, because the hearts of the saints have been made strong again through you, brother.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And so, though I might, in the name of Christ, give you orders to do what is right,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Still, because of love, in place of an order, I make a request to you, I, Paul, an old man and now a prisoner of Christ Jesus:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** My request is for my child Onesimus, the child of my chains,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Who in the past was of no profit to you, but now is of profit to you and to me:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Whom I have sent back to you, him who is my very heart:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Though my desire was to keep him with me, to be my servant in the chains of the good news, in your place:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But without your approval I would do nothing; so that your good works might not be forced, but done freely from your heart.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** For it is possible that for this reason he was parted from you for a time, so that you might have him for ever;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** No longer as a servant, but more than a servant, a brother, very dear to me specially, but much more to you, in the flesh as well as in the Lord.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** If then you take me to be your friend and brother, take him in as myself.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** If he has done you any wrong or is in debt to you for anything, put it to my account.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** I, Paul, writing this myself, say, I will make payment to you: and I do not say to you that you are in debt to me even for your life.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** So brother, let me have joy of you in the Lord: give new life to my heart in Christ.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Being certain that you will do my desire, I am writing to you, in the knowledge that you will do even more than I say.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And make a room ready for me; for I am hoping that through your prayers I will be given to you.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Epaphras, my brother-prisoner in Christ Jesus, sends you his love;

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And so do Mark, Aristarchus, Demas, and Luke, my brother-workers.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit. So be it.
