# 1 Peter 3 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** In like manner, ye wives, bein subjection to your own husbands; that, even if any obey not the word, they may without the word be gained by the behavior of their wives;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** beholding your chaste behaviorcoupledwith fear.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Whoseadorninglet it not be the outward adorning of braiding the hair, and of wearing jewels of gold, or of putting on apparel;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** but let it be the hidden man of the heart, in the incorruptible apparel of a meek and quiet spirit, which is in the sight of God of great price.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For after this manner aforetime the holy women also, who hoped in God, adorned themselves, being in subjection to their own husbands:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** as Sarah obeyed Abraham, calling him lord: whose children ye now are, if ye do well, and are not put in fear by any terror.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Ye husbands, in like manner, dwell withyour wivesaccording to knowledge, giving honor unto the woman, as unto the weaker vessel, as being also joint-heirs of the grace of life; to the end that your prayers be not hindered.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Finally, be ye all likeminded, compassionate, loving as brethren, tenderhearted, humbleminded:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** not rendering evil for evil, or reviling for reviling; but contrariwise blessing; for hereunto were ye called, that ye should inherit a blessing.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** For,  He that would love life, And see good days, Let him refrain his tongue from evil, And his lips that they speak no guile:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And let him turn away from evil, and do good; Let him seek peace, and pursue it.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For the eyes of the Lord are upon the righteous, And his ears unto their supplication: But the face of the Lord is upon them that do evil.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And who is he that will harm you, if ye be zealous of that which is good?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But even if ye should suffer for righteousness’ sake, blessed are ye: and fear not their fear, neither be troubled;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** but sanctify in your hearts Christ as Lord: being ready always to give answer to every man that asketh you a reason concerning the hope that is in you, yet with meekness and fear:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** having a good conscience; that, wherein ye are spoken against, they may be put to shame who revile your good manner of life in Christ.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For it is better, if the will of God should so will, that ye suffer for well-doing than for evil-doing.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Because Christ also suffered for sins once, the righteous for the unrighteous, that he might bring us to God; being put to death in the flesh, but made alive in the spirit;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** in which also he went and preached unto the spirits in prison,

<a id="v20"></a>**20** that aforetime were disobedient, when the longsuffering of God waited in the days of Noah, while the ark was a preparing, wherein few, that is, eight souls, were saved through water:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** which also after a true likeness doth now save you, even baptism, not the putting away of the filth of the flesh, but the interrogation of a good conscience toward God, through the resurrection of Jesus Christ;

<a id="v22"></a>**22** who is on the right hand of God, having gone into heaven; angels and authorities and powers being made subject unto him.
