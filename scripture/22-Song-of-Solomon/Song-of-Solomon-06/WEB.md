# Song of Songs 6 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Where has your beloved gone, you fairest among women? Where has your beloved turned, that we may seek him with you?  Beloved

<a id="v2"></a>**2** My beloved has gone down to his garden, to the beds of spices, to feed in the gardens, and to gather lilies.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I am my beloved’s, and my beloved is mine. He browses among the lilies,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** You are beautiful, my love, as Tirzah, lovely as Jerusalem, awesome as an army with banners.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Turn away your eyes from me, for they have overcome me. Your hair is like a flock of goats, that lie along the side of Gilead.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Your teeth are like a flock of ewes, which have come up from the washing; of which every one has twins; no one is bereaved among them.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Your temples are like a piece of a pomegranate behind your veil.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** There are sixty queens, eighty concubines, and virgins without number.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** My dove, my perfect one, is unique. She is her mother’s only daughter. She is the favorite one of her who bore her. The daughters saw her, and called her blessed; the queens and the concubines, and they praised her.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Who is she who looks out as the morning, beautiful as the moon, clear as the sun, and awesome as an army with banners?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I went down into the nut tree grove, to see the green plants of the valley, to see whether the vine budded, and the pomegranates were in flower.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Without realizing it, my desire set me with my royal people’s chariots.  Friends

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Return, return, Shulammite! Return, return, that we may gaze at you.  Lover  Why do you desire to gaze at the Shulammite, as at the dance of Mahanaim?
