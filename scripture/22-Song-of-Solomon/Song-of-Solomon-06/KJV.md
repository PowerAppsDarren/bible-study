# Song of Songs 6 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Whither is thy beloved gone, O thou fairest among women? whither is thy beloved turned aside? that we may seek him with thee.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** My beloved is gone down into his garden, to the beds of spices, to feed in the gardens, and to gather lilies.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I am my beloved’s, and my beloved is mine: he feedeth among the lilies.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Thou art beautiful, O my love, as Tirzah, comely as Jerusalem, terrible as an army with banners.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Turn away thine eyes from me, for they have overcome me: thy hair is as a flock of goats that appear from Gilead.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Thy teeth are as a flock of sheep which go up from the washing, whereof every one beareth twins, and there is not one barren among them.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** As a piece of a pomegranate are thy temples within thy locks.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** There are threescore queens, and fourscore concubines, and virgins without number.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** My dove, my undefiled is but one; she is the only one of her mother, she is the choice one of her that bare her. The daughters saw her, and blessed her; yea, the queens and the concubines, and they praised her.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Who is she that looketh forth as the morning, fair as the moon, clear as the sun, and terrible as an army with banners?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I went down into the garden of nuts to see the fruits of the valley, and to see whether the vine flourished, and the pomegranates budded.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Or ever I was aware, my soul made me like the chariots of Amminadib.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Return, return, O Shulamite; return, return, that we may look upon thee. What will ye see in the Shulamite? As it were the company of two armies.
