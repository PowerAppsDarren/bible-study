# Song of Songs 6 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Whither is thy beloved gone, O thou fairest among women? Whither hath thy beloved turned him, That we may seek him with thee?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** My beloved is gone down to his garden, To the beds of spices, To feed in the gardens, and to gather lilies.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I am my beloved’s, and my beloved is mine: He feedeth his flock among the lilies.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Thou art fair, O my love, as Tirzah, Comely as Jerusalem, Terrible as an army with banners.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Turn away thine eyes from me, For they have overcome me. Thy hair is as a flock of goats, That lie along the side of Gilead.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Thy teeth are like a flock of ewes, Which are come up from the washing; Whereof every one hath twins, And none is bereaved among them.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Thy temples are like a piece of a pomegranate Behind thy veil.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** There are threescore queens, and fourscore concubines, And virgins without number.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** My dove, my undefiled, is but one; She is the only one of her mother; She is the choice one of her that bare her. The daughters saw her, and called her blessed; Yea, the queens and the concubines, and they praised her.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Who is she that looketh forth as the morning, Fair as the moon, Clear as the sun, Terrible as an army with banners?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I went down into the garden of nuts, To see the green plants of the valley, To see whether the vine budded, Andthe pomegranates were in flower.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Before I was aware, my soul set me Amongthe chariots of my princely people.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Return, return, O Shulammite; Return, return, that we may look upon thee.  Why will ye look upon the Shulammite, As upon the dance of Mahanaim?
