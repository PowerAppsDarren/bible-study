# Song of Songs 5 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** I am come into my garden, my sister, my spouse: I have gathered my myrrh with my spice; I have eaten my honeycomb with my honey; I have drunk my wine with my milk: eat, O friends; drink, yea, drink abundantly, O beloved.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** I sleep, but my heart waketh: it is the voice of my beloved that knocketh, saying, Open to me, my sister, my love, my dove, my undefiled: for my head is filled with dew, and my locks with the drops of the night.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I have put off my coat; how shall I put it on? I have washed my feet; how shall I defile them?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** My beloved put in his hand by the hole of the door, and my bowels were moved for him.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** I rose up to open to my beloved; and my hands dropped with myrrh, and my fingers with sweet smelling myrrh, upon the handles of the lock.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** I opened to my beloved; but my beloved had withdrawn himself, and was gone: my soul failed when he spake: I sought him, but I could not find him; I called him, but he gave me no answer.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The watchmen that went about the city found me, they smote me, they wounded me; the keepers of the walls took away my veil from me.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** I charge you, O daughters of Jerusalem, if ye find my beloved, that ye tell him, that I am sick of love.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** What is thy beloved more than another beloved, O thou fairest among women? what is thy beloved more than another beloved, that thou dost so charge us?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** My beloved is white and ruddy, the chiefest among ten thousand.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** His head is as the most fine gold, his locks are bushy, and black as a raven.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** His eyes are as the eyes of doves by the rivers of waters, washed with milk, and fitly set.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** His cheeks are as a bed of spices, as sweet flowers: his lips like lilies, dropping sweet smelling myrrh.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** His hands are as gold rings set with the beryl: his belly is as bright ivory overlaid with sapphires.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** His legs are as pillars of marble, set upon sockets of fine gold: his countenance is as Lebanon, excellent as the cedars.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** His mouth is most sweet: yea, he is altogether lovely. This is my beloved, and this is my friend, O daughters of Jerusalem.
