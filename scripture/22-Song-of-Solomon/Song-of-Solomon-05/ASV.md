# Song of Songs 5 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** I am come into my garden, my sister, my bride: I have gathered my myrrh with my spice; I have eaten my honeycomb with my honey; I have drunk my wine with my milk. Eat, O friends; Drink, yea, drink abundantly, O beloved.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** I was asleep, but my heart waked: It is the voice of my beloved that knocketh, saying, Open to me, my sister, my love, my dove, my undefiled; For my head is filled with dew, My locks with the drops of the night.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I have put off my garment; how shall I put it on? I have washed my feet; how shall I defile them?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** My beloved put in his hand by the holeof the door, And my heart was moved for him.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** I rose up to open to my beloved; And my hands dropped with myrrh, And my fingers with liquid myrrh, Upon the handles of the bolt.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** I opened to my beloved; But my beloved had withdrawn himself, and was gone. My soul had failed me when he spake: I sought him, but I could not find him; I called him, but he gave me no answer.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The watchmen that go about the city found me, They smote me, they wounded me; The keepers of the walls took away my mantle from me.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** I adjure you, O daughters of Jerusalem, If ye find my beloved, That ye tell him, that I am sick from love.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** What is thy beloved more than another beloved, O thou fairest among women? What is thy beloved more than another beloved, That thou dost so adjure us?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** My beloved is white and ruddy, The chiefest among ten thousand.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** His head isasthe most fine gold; His locks are bushy, and black as a raven.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** His eyes are like doves beside the water-brooks, Washed with milk, and fitly set.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** His cheeks are as a bed of spices, Asbanks of sweet herbs: His lips are as lilies, dropping liquid myrrh.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** His hands areasrings of gold set with beryl: His body isasivory work overlaid with sapphires.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** His legs areaspillars of marble, set upon sockets of fine gold: His aspect is like Lebanon, excellent as the cedars.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** His mouth is most sweet; Yea, he is altogether lovely. This is my beloved, and this is my friend, O daughters of Jerusalem.
