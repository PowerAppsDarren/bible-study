# Song of Songs 8 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Oh that thou wert as my brother, That sucked the breasts of my mother! WhenI should find thee without, I would kiss thee; Yea, and none would despise me.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** I would lead thee, and bring thee into my mother’s house, Who would instruct me; I would cause thee to drink of spiced wine, Of the juice of my pomegranate.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** His left handshould beunder my head, And his right hand should embrace me.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** I adjure you, O daughters of Jerusalem, That ye stir not up, nor awake my love, Until he please.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Who is this that cometh up from the wilderness, Leaning upon her beloved?  Under the apple-tree I awakened thee: There thy mother was in travail with thee, There was she in travail that brought thee forth.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Set me as a seal upon thy heart, As a seal upon thine arm: For love is strong as death; Jealousy is cruel as Sheol; The flashes thereof are flashes of fire, A very flame of Jehovah.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Many waters cannot quench love, Neither can floods drown it: If a man would give all the substance of his house for love, He would utterly be contemned.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** We have a little sister, And she hath no breasts:  What shall we do for our sister  In the day when she shall be spoken for?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** If she be a wall, We will build upon her a turret of silver: And if she be a door, We will inclose her with boards of cedar.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** I am a wall, and my breasts like the towersthereof Then was I in his eyes as one that found peace.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Solomon had a vineyard at Baal-hamon; He let out the vineyard unto keepers; Every one for the fruit thereof was to bring a thousand pieces of silver.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** My vineyard, which is mine, is before me: Thou, O Solomon, shalt have the thousand, And those that keep the fruit thereof two hundred.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Thou that dwellest in the gardens, The companions hearken for thy voice: Cause me to hear it.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Make haste, my beloved, And be thou like to a roe or to a young hart Upon the mountains of spices.
