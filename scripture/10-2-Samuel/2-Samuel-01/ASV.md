# 2 Samuel 1 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And it came to pass after the death of Saul, when David was returned from the slaughter of the Amalekites, and David had abode two days in Ziklag;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** it came to pass on the third day, that, behold, a man came out of the camp from Saul, with his clothes rent, and earth upon his head: and so it was, when he came to David, that he fell to the earth, and did obeisance.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And David said unto him, From whence comest thou? And he said unto him, Out of the camp of Israel am I escaped.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And David said unto him, How went the matter? I pray thee, tell me. And he answered, The people are fled from the battle, and many of the people also are fallen and dead; and Saul and Jonathan his son are dead also.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And David said unto the young man that told him, How knowest thou that Saul and Jonathan his son are dead?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And the young man that told him said, As I happened by chance upon mount Gilboa, behold, Saul was leaning upon his spear; and, lo, the chariots and the horsemen followed hard after him.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And when he looked behind him, he saw me, and called unto me. And I answered, Here am I.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And he said unto me, Who art thou? And I answered him, I am an Amalekite.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And he said unto me, Stand, I pray thee, beside me, and slay me; for anguish hath taken hold of me, because my life is yet whole in me.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** So I stood beside him, and slew him, because I was sure that he could not live after that he was fallen: and I took the crown that was upon his head, and the bracelet that was on his arm, and have brought them hither unto my lord.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Then David took hold on his clothes, and rent them; and likewise all the men that were with him:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** and they mourned, and wept, and fasted until even, for Saul, and for Jonathan his son, and for the people of Jehovah, and for the house of Israel; because they were fallen by the sword.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And David said unto the young man that told him, Whence art thou? And he answered, I am the son of a sojourner, an Amalekite.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And David said unto him, How wast thou not afraid to put forth thy hand to destroy Jehovah’s anointed?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And David called one of the young men, and said, Go near, and fall upon him. And he smote him, so that he died.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And David said unto him, Thy blood be upon thy head; for thy mouth hath testified against thee, saying, I have slain Jehovah’s anointed.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And David lamented with this lamentation over Saul and over Jonathan his son

<a id="v18"></a>**18** (and he bade them teach the children of Judah the song of the bow: behold, it is written in the book of Jashar):

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Thy glory, O Israel, is slain upon thy high places! How are the mighty fallen!

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Tell it not in Gath, Publish it not in the streets of Ashkelon; Lest the daughters of the Philistines rejoice, Lest the daughters of the uncircumcised triumph.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Ye mountains of Gilboa, Let there be no dew nor rain upon you, neither fields of offerings: For there the shield of the mighty was vilely cast away, The shield of Saul, not anointed with oil.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** From the blood of the slain, from the fat of the mighty, The bow of Jonathan turned not back, And the sword of Saul returned not empty.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Saul and Jonathan were lovely and pleasant in their lives, And in their death they were not divided: They were swifter than eagles, They were stronger than lions.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Ye daughters of Israel, weep over Saul, Who clothed you in scarlet delicately, Who put ornaments of gold upon your apparel.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** How are the mighty fallen in the midst of the battle! Jonathan is slain upon thy high places.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** I am distressed for thee, my brother Jonathan: Very pleasant hast thou been unto me: Thy love to me was wonderful, Passing the love of women.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** How are the mighty fallen, And the weapons of war perished!
