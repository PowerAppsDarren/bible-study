# 2 Samuel 8 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** After this, David struck the Philistines, and subdued them; and David took the bridle of the mother city out of the hand of the Philistines.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** He struck Moab, and measured them with the line, making them to lie down on the ground; and he measured two lines to put to death, and one full line to keep alive. The Moabites became servants to David, and brought tribute.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** David struck also Hadadezer the son of Rehob, king of Zobah, as he went to recover his dominion at the River.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** David took from him one thousand seven hundred horsemen, and twenty thousand footmen: and David hamstrung all the chariot horses, but reserved of them for one hundred chariots.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** When the Syrians of Damascus came to help Hadadezer king of Zobah, David struck of the Syrians two and twenty thousand men.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Then David put garrisons in Syria of Damascus; and the Syrians became servants to David, and brought tribute. Yahweh gave victory to David wherever he went.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** David took the shields of gold that were on the servants of Hadadezer, and brought them to Jerusalem.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** From Betah and from Berothai, cities of Hadadezer, king David took exceeding much brass.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** When Toi king of Hamath heard that David had struck all the army of Hadadezer,

<a id="v10"></a>**10** then Toi sent Joram his son to king David, to greet him, and to bless him, because he had fought against Hadadezer and struck him: for Hadadezer had wars with Toi. Joram brought with him vessels of silver, and vessels of gold, and vessels of brass:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** King David also dedicated these to Yahweh, with the silver and gold that he dedicated of all the nations which he subdued;

<a id="v12"></a>**12** of Syria, and of Moab, and of the children of Ammon, and of the Philistines, and of Amalek, and of the plunder of Hadadezer, son of Rehob, king of Zobah.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** David earned a reputation when he returned from smiting the Syrians in the Valley of Salt, even eighteen thousand men.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He put garrisons in Edom; throughout all Edom put he garrisons, and all the Edomites became servants to David. Yahweh gave victory to David wherever he went.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** David reigned over all Israel; and David executed justice and righteousness to all his people.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Joab the son of Zeruiah was over the army; and Jehoshaphat the son of Ahilud was recorder;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** and Zadok the son of Ahitub, and Ahimelech the son of Abiathar, were priests; and Seraiah was scribe;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** and Benaiah the son of Jehoiada was over the Cherethites and the Pelethites; and David’s sons were chief ministers.
