# 2 Samuel 16 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And when David was a little past the top of the ascent, behold, Ziba the servant of Mephibosheth met him, with a couple of asses saddled, and upon them two hundred loaves of bread, and a hundred clusters of raisins, and a hundred of summer fruits, and a bottle of wine.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And the king said unto Ziba, What meanest thou by these? And Ziba said, The asses are for the king’s household to ride on; and the bread and summer fruit for the young men to eat; and the wine, that such as are faint in the wilderness may drink.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And the king said, And where is thy master’s son? And Ziba said unto the king, Behold, he abideth at Jerusalem; for he said, To-day will the house of Israel restore me the kingdom of my father.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Then said the king to Ziba, Behold, thine is all that pertaineth unto Mephibosheth. And Ziba said, I do obeisance; let me find favor in thy sight, my lord, O king.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And when king David came to Bahurim, behold, there came out thence a man of the family of the house of Saul, whose name was Shimei, the son of Gera; he came out, and cursed still as he came.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And he cast stones at David, and at all the servants of king David: and all the people and all the mighty men were on his right hand and on his left.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And thus said Shimei when he cursed, Begone, begone, thou man of blood, and base fellow:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Jehovah hath returned upon thee all the blood of the house of Saul, in whose stead thou hast reigned; and Jehovah hath delivered the kingdom into the hand of Absalom thy son; and, behold, thou art taken in thine own mischief, because thou art a man of blood.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Then said Abishai the son of Zeruiah unto the king, Why should this dead dog curse my lord the king? let me go over, I pray thee, and take off his head.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And the king said, What have I to do with you, ye sons of Zeruiah? Because he curseth, and because Jehovah hath said unto him, Curse David; who then shall say, Wherefore hast thou done so?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And David said to Abishai, and to all his servants, Behold, my son, who came forth from my bowels, seeketh my life: how much more may this Benjamite now do it? let him alone, and let him curse; for Jehovah hath bidden him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** It may be that Jehovah will look on the wrong done unto me, and that Jehovah will requite me good for his cursing of me this day.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** So David and his men went by the way; and Shimei went along on the hill-side over against him, and cursed as he went, and threw stones at him, and cast dust.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And the king, and all the people that were with him, came weary; and he refreshed himself there.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And Absalom, and all the people, the men of Israel, came to Jerusalem, and Ahithophel with him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And it came to pass, when Hushai the Archite, David’s friend, was come unto Absalom, that Hushai said unto Absalom, Long live the king, Long live the king.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And Absalom said to Hushai, Is this thy kindness to thy friend? why wentest thou not with thy friend?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And Hushai said unto Absalom, Nay; but whom Jehovah, and this people, and all the men of Israel have chosen, his will I be, and with him will I abide.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And again, whom should I serve? should I not serve in the presence of his son? as I have served in thy father’s presence, so will I be in thy presence.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Then said Absalom to Ahithophel, Give your counsel what we shall do.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And Ahithophel said unto Absalom, Go in unto thy father’s concubines, that he hath left to keep the house; and all Israel will hear that thou art abhorred of thy father: then will the hands of all that are with thee be strong.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** So they spread Absalom a tent upon the top of the house; and Absalom went in unto his father’s concubines in the sight of all Israel.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And the counsel of Ahithophel, which he gave in those days, was as if a man inquired at the oracle of God: so was all the counsel of Ahithophel both with David and with Absalom.
