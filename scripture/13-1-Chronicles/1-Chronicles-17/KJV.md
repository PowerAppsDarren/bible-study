# 1 Chronicles 17 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Now it came to pass, as David sat in his house, that David said to Nathan the prophet, Lo, I dwell in an house of cedars, but the ark of the covenant of the Lord remaineth under curtains.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Then Nathan said unto David, Do all that is in thine heart; for God is with thee.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And it came to pass the same night, that the word of God came to Nathan, saying,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Go and tell David my servant, Thus saith the Lord, Thou shalt not build me an house to dwell in:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For I have not dwelt in an house since the day that I brought up Israel unto this day; but have gone from tent to tent, and from one tabernacle to another.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Wheresoever I have walked with all Israel, spake I a word to any of the judges of Israel, whom I commanded to feed my people, saying, Why have ye not built me an house of cedars?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Now therefore thus shalt thou say unto my servant David, Thus saith the Lord of hosts, I took thee from the sheepcote, even from following the sheep, that thou shouldest be ruler over my people Israel:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And I have been with thee whithersoever thou hast walked, and have cut off all thine enemies from before thee, and have made thee a name like the name of the great men that are in the earth.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Also I will ordain a place for my people Israel, and will plant them, and they shall dwell in their place, and shall be moved no more; neither shall the children of wickedness waste them any more, as at the beginning,

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And since the time that I commanded judges to be over my people Israel. Moreover I will subdue all thine enemies. Furthermore I tell thee that the Lord will build thee an house.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And it shall come to pass, when thy days be expired that thou must go to be with thy fathers, that I will raise up thy seed after thee, which shall be of thy sons; and I will establish his kingdom.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** He shall build me an house, and I will stablish his throne for ever.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** I will be his father, and he shall be my son: and I will not take my mercy away from him, as I took it from him that was before thee:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But I will settle him in mine house and in my kingdom for ever: and his throne shall be established for evermore.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** According to all these words, and according to all this vision, so did Nathan speak unto David.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And David the king came and sat before the Lord, and said, Who am I, O Lord God, and what is mine house, that thou hast brought me hitherto?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And yet this was a small thing in thine eyes, O God; for thou hast also spoken of thy servant’s house for a great while to come, and hast regarded me according to the estate of a man of high degree, O Lord God.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** What can David speak more to thee for the honour of thy servant? for thou knowest thy servant.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** O Lord, for thy servant’s sake, and according to thine own heart, hast thou done all this greatness, in making known all these great things.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** O Lord, there is none like thee, neither is there any God beside thee, according to all that we have heard with our ears.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And what one nation in the earth is like thy people Israel, whom God went to redeem to be his own people, to make thee a name of greatness and terribleness, by driving out nations from before thy people, whom thou hast redeemed out of Egypt?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** For thy people Israel didst thou make thine own people for ever; and thou, Lord, becamest their God.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Therefore now, Lord, let the thing that thou hast spoken concerning thy servant and concerning his house be established for ever, and do as thou hast said.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Let it even be established, that thy name may be magnified for ever, saying, The Lord of hosts is the God of Israel, even a God to Israel: and let the house of David thy servant be established before thee.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For thou, O my God, hast told thy servant that thou wilt build him an house: therefore thy servant hath found in his heart to pray before thee.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And now, Lord, thou art God, and hast promised this goodness unto thy servant:

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Now therefore let it please thee to bless the house of thy servant, that it may be before thee for ever: for thou blessest, O Lord, and it shall be blessed for ever.
