# Amos 5 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Hear ye this word which I take up against you, even a lamentation, O house of Israel.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The virgin of Israel is fallen; she shall no more rise: she is forsaken upon her land; there is none to raise her up.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For thus saith the Lord God; The city that went out by a thousand shall leave an hundred, and that which went forth by an hundred shall leave ten, to the house of Israel.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For thus saith the Lord unto the house of Israel, Seek ye me, and ye shall live:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But seek not Beth–el, nor enter into Gilgal, and pass not to Beer–sheba: for Gilgal shall surely go into captivity, and Beth–el shall come to nought.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Seek the Lord, and ye shall live; lest he break out like fire in the house of Joseph, and devour it, and there be none to quench it in Beth–el.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Ye who turn judgment to wormwood, and leave off righteousness in the earth,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Seek him that maketh the seven stars and Orion, and turneth the shadow of death into the morning, and maketh the day dark with night: that calleth for the waters of the sea, and poureth them out upon the face of the earth: The Lord is his name:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** That strengtheneth the spoiled against the strong, so that the spoiled shall come against the fortress.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** They hate him that rebuketh in the gate, and they abhor him that speaketh uprightly.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Forasmuch therefore as your treading is upon the poor, and ye take from him burdens of wheat: ye have built houses of hewn stone, but ye shall not dwell in them; ye have planted pleasant vineyards, but ye shall not drink wine of them.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For I know your manifold transgressions and your mighty sins: they afflict the just, they take a bribe, and they turn aside the poor in the gate from their right.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Therefore the prudent shall keep silence in that time; for it is an evil time.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Seek good, and not evil, that ye may live: and so the Lord, the God of hosts, shall be with you, as ye have spoken.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Hate the evil, and love the good, and establish judgment in the gate: it may be that the Lord God of hosts will be gracious unto the remnant of Joseph.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Therefore the Lord, the God of hosts, the Lord, saith thus; Wailing shall be in all streets; and they shall say in all the highways, Alas! alas! and they shall call the husbandman to mourning, and such as are skilful of lamentation to wailing.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And in all vineyards shall be wailing: for I will pass through thee, saith the Lord.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Woe unto you that desire the day of the Lord! to what end is it for you? the day of the Lord is darkness, and not light.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** As if a man did flee from a lion, and a bear met him; or went into the house, and leaned his hand on the wall, and a serpent bit him.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Shall not the day of the Lord be darkness, and not light? even very dark, and no brightness in it?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** I hate, I despise your feast days, and I will not smell in your solemn assemblies.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Though ye offer me burnt offerings and your meat offerings, I will not accept them: neither will I regard the peace offerings of your fat beasts.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Take thou away from me the noise of thy songs; for I will not hear the melody of thy viols.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** But let judgment run down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Have ye offered unto me sacrifices and offerings in the wilderness forty years, O house of Israel?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** But ye have borne the tabernacle of your Moloch and Chiun your images, the star of your god, which ye made to yourselves.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Therefore will I cause you to go into captivity beyond Damascus, saith the Lord, whose name is The God of hosts.
