# Matthew 26 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And it came to pass, when Jesus had finished all these sayings, he said unto his disciples,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Ye know that after two days is the feast of the passover, and the Son of man is betrayed to be crucified.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Then assembled together the chief priests, and the scribes, and the elders of the people, unto the palace of the high priest, who was called Caiaphas,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And consulted that they might take Jesus by subtilty, and kill him.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But they said, Not on the feast day, lest there be an uproar among the people.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Now when Jesus was in Bethany, in the house of Simon the leper,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** There came unto him a woman having an alabaster box of very precious ointment, and poured it on his head, as he sat at meat.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But when his disciples saw it, they had indignation, saying, To what purpose is this waste?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For this ointment might have been sold for much, and given to the poor.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** When Jesus understood it, he said unto them, Why trouble ye the woman? for she hath wrought a good work upon me.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For ye have the poor always with you; but me ye have not always.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For in that she hath poured this ointment on my body, she did it for my burial.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Verily I say unto you, Wheresoever this gospel shall be preached in the whole world, there shall also this, that this woman hath done, be told for a memorial of her.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Then one of the twelve, called Judas Iscariot, went unto the chief priests,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And said unto them, What will ye give me, and I will deliver him unto you? And they covenanted with him for thirty pieces of silver.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And from that time he sought opportunity to betray him.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Now the first day of the feast of unleavened bread the disciples came to Jesus, saying unto him, Where wilt thou that we prepare for thee to eat the passover?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And he said, Go into the city to such a man, and say unto him, The Master saith, My time is at hand; I will keep the passover at thy house with my disciples.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And the disciples did as Jesus had appointed them; and they made ready the passover.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Now when the even was come, he sat down with the twelve.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And as they did eat, he said, Verily I say unto you, that one of you shall betray me.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And they were exceeding sorrowful, and began every one of them to say unto him, Lord, is it I?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And he answered and said, He that dippeth his hand with me in the dish, the same shall betray me.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The Son of man goeth as it is written of him: but woe unto that man by whom the Son of man is betrayed! it had been good for that man if he had not been born.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Then Judas, which betrayed him, answered and said, Master, is it I? He said unto him, Thou hast said.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And as they were eating, Jesus took bread, and blessed it, and brake it, and gave it to the disciples, and said, Take, eat; this is my body.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And he took the cup, and gave thanks, and gave it to them, saying, Drink ye all of it;

<a id="v28"></a>**28** For this is my blood of the new testament, which is shed for many for the remission of sins.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** But I say unto you, I will not drink henceforth of this fruit of the vine, until that day when I drink it new with you in my Father’s kingdom.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And when they had sung an hymn, they went out into the mount of Olives.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Then saith Jesus unto them, All ye shall be offended because of me this night: for it is written, I will smite the shepherd, and the sheep of the flock shall be scattered abroad.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** But after I am risen again, I will go before you into Galilee.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Peter answered and said unto him, Though all men shall be offended because of thee, yet will I never be offended.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Jesus said unto him, Verily I say unto thee, That this night, before the cock crow, thou shalt deny me thrice.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Peter said unto him, Though I should die with thee, yet will I not deny thee. Likewise also said all the disciples.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** Then cometh Jesus with them unto a place called Gethsemane, and saith unto the disciples, Sit ye here, while I go and pray yonder.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And he took with him Peter and the two sons of Zebedee, and began to be sorrowful and very heavy.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** Then saith he unto them, My soul is exceeding sorrowful, even unto death: tarry ye here, and watch with me.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And he went a little further, and fell on his face, and prayed, saying, O my Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from me: nevertheless not as I will, but as thou wilt.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** And he cometh unto the disciples, and findeth them asleep, and saith unto Peter, What, could ye not watch with me one hour?

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Watch and pray, that ye enter not into temptation: the spirit indeed is willing, but the flesh is weak.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** He went away again the second time, and prayed, saying, O my Father, if this cup may not pass away from me, except I drink it, thy will be done.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** And he came and found them asleep again: for their eyes were heavy.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** And he left them, and went away again, and prayed the third time, saying the same words.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** Then cometh he to his disciples, and saith unto them, Sleep on now, and take your rest: behold, the hour is at hand, and the Son of man is betrayed into the hands of sinners.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** Rise, let us be going: behold, he is at hand that doth betray me.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** And while he yet spake, lo, Judas, one of the twelve, came, and with him a great multitude with swords and staves, from the chief priests and elders of the people.

<a id="v48"></a>**48** Now he that betrayed him gave them a sign, saying, Whomsoever I shall kiss, that same is he: hold him fast.

<a id="v49"></a>**49** And forthwith he came to Jesus, and said, Hail, master; and kissed him.

<a id="v50"></a>**50** And Jesus said unto him, Friend, wherefore art thou come? Then came they, and laid hands on Jesus, and took him.

<a id="v51"></a>**51** And, behold, one of them which were with Jesus stretched out his hand, and drew his sword, and struck a servant of the high priest’s, and smote off his ear.

<a id="v52"></a>**52** Then said Jesus unto him, Put up again thy sword into his place: for all they that take the sword shall perish with the sword.

<a id="v53"></a>**53** Thinkest thou that I cannot now pray to my Father, and he shall presently give me more than twelve legions of angels?

<a id="v54"></a>**54** But how then shall the scriptures be fulfilled, that thus it must be?

<a id="v55"></a>**55** In that same hour said Jesus to the multitudes, Are ye come out as against a thief with swords and staves for to take me? I sat daily with you teaching in the temple, and ye laid no hold on me.

<a id="v56"></a>**56** But all this was done, that the scriptures of the prophets might be fulfilled. Then all the disciples forsook him, and fled.

<a id="v57"></a>**57** And they that had laid hold on Jesus led him away to Caiaphas the high priest, where the scribes and the elders were assembled.

<a id="v58"></a>**58** But Peter followed him afar off unto the high priest’s palace, and went in, and sat with the servants, to see the end.

<a id="v59"></a>**59** Now the chief priests, and elders, and all the council, sought false witness against Jesus, to put him to death;

<a id="v60"></a>**60** But found none: yea, though many false witnesses came, yet found they none. At the last came two false witnesses,

<a id="v61"></a>**61** And said, This fellow said, I am able to destroy the temple of God, and to build it in three days.

<a id="v62"></a>**62** And the high priest arose, and said unto him, Answerest thou nothing? what is it which these witness against thee?

<a id="v63"></a>**63** But Jesus held his peace. And the high priest answered and said unto him, I adjure thee by the living God, that thou tell us whether thou be the Christ, the Son of God.

<a id="v64"></a>**64** Jesus saith unto him, Thou hast said: nevertheless I say unto you, Hereafter shall ye see the Son of man sitting on the right hand of power, and coming in the clouds of heaven.

<a id="v65"></a>**65** Then the high priest rent his clothes, saying, He hath spoken blasphemy; what further need have we of witnesses? behold, now ye have heard his blasphemy.

<a id="v66"></a>**66** What think ye? They answered and said, He is guilty of death.

<a id="v67"></a>**67** Then did they spit in his face, and buffeted him; and others smote him with the palms of their hands,

<a id="v68"></a>**68** Saying, Prophesy unto us, thou Christ, Who is he that smote thee?

<a id="v69"></a>**69** Now Peter sat without in the palace: and a damsel came unto him, saying, Thou also wast with Jesus of Galilee.

<a id="v70"></a>**70** But he denied before them all, saying, I know not what thou sayest.

<a id="v71"></a>**71** And when he was gone out into the porch, another maid saw him, and said unto them that were there, This fellow was also with Jesus of Nazareth.

<a id="v72"></a>**72** And again he denied with an oath, I do not know the man.

<a id="v73"></a>**73** And after a while came unto him they that stood by, and said to Peter, Surely thou also art one of them; for thy speech bewrayeth thee.

<a id="v74"></a>**74** Then began he to curse and to swear, saying, I know not the man. And immediately the cock crew.

<a id="v75"></a>**75** And Peter remembered the word of Jesus, which said unto him, Before the cock crow, thou shalt deny me thrice. And he went out, and wept bitterly.
