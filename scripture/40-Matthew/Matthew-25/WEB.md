# Matthew 25 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “Then the Kingdom of Heaven will be like ten virgins, who took their lamps, and went out to meet the bridegroom.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Five of them were foolish, and five were wise.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Those who were foolish, when they took their lamps, took no oil with them,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** but the wise took oil in their vessels with their lamps.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Now while the bridegroom delayed, they all slumbered and slept.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But at midnight there was a cry, ‘Behold! The bridegroom is coming! Come out to meet him!’

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Then all those virgins arose, and trimmed their lamps.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The foolish said to the wise, ‘Give us some of your oil, for our lamps are going out.’

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But the wise answered, saying, ‘What if there isn’t enough for us and you? You go rather to those who sell, and buy for yourselves.’

<a id="v10"></a>**10** While they went away to buy, the bridegroom came, and those who were ready went in with him to the marriage feast, and the door was shut.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Afterward the other virgins also came, saying, ‘Lord, Lord, open to us.’

<a id="v12"></a>**12** But he answered, ‘Most certainly I tell you, I don’t know you.’

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Watch therefore, for you don’t know the day nor the hour in which the Son of Man is coming.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** “For it is like a man, going into another country, who called his own servants, and entrusted his goods to them.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** To one he gave five talents, to another two, to another one; to each according to his own ability. Then he went on his journey.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Immediately he who received the five talents went and traded with them, and made another five talents.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** In the same way, he also who got the two gained another two.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But he who received the one went away and dug in the earth, and hid his lord’s money.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** “Now after a long time the lord of those servants came, and reconciled accounts with them.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He who received the five talents came and brought another five talents, saying, ‘Lord, you delivered to me five talents. Behold, I have gained another five talents besides them.’

<a id="v21"></a>**21** “His lord said to him, ‘Well done, good and faithful servant. You have been faithful over a few things, I will set you over many things. Enter into the joy of your lord.’

<a id="v22"></a>**22** “He also who got the two talents came and said, ‘Lord, you delivered to me two talents. Behold, I have gained another two talents besides them.’

<a id="v23"></a>**23** “His lord said to him, ‘Well done, good and faithful servant. You have been faithful over a few things, I will set you over many things. Enter into the joy of your lord.’

<a id="v24"></a>**24** “He also who had received the one talent came and said, ‘Lord, I knew you that you are a hard man, reaping where you did not sow, and gathering where you did not scatter.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** I was afraid, and went away and hid your talent in the earth. Behold, you have what is yours.’

<a id="v26"></a>**26** “But his lord answered him, ‘You wicked and slothful servant. You knew that I reap where I didn’t sow, and gather where I didn’t scatter.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** You ought therefore to have deposited my money with the bankers, and at my coming I should have received back my own with interest.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Take away therefore the talent from him, and give it to him who has the ten talents.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For to everyone who has will be given, and he will have abundance, but from him who doesn’t have, even that which he has will be taken away.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Throw out the unprofitable servant into the outer darkness, where there will be weeping and gnashing of teeth.’

<a id="v31"></a>**31** “But when the Son of Man comes in his glory, and all the holy angels with him, then he will sit on the throne of his glory.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Before him all the nations will be gathered, and he will separate them one from another, as a shepherd separates the sheep from the goats.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** He will set the sheep on his right hand, but the goats on the left.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Then the King will tell those on his right hand, ‘Come, blessed of my Father, inherit the Kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world;

<a id="v35"></a>**35** for I was hungry, and you gave me food to eat. I was thirsty, and you gave me drink. I was a stranger, and you took me in.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** I was naked, and you clothed me. I was sick, and you visited me. I was in prison, and you came to me.’

<a id="v37"></a>**37** “Then the righteous will answer him, saying, ‘Lord, when did we see you hungry, and feed you; or thirsty, and give you a drink?

<a id="v38"></a>**38** When did we see you as a stranger, and take you in; or naked, and clothe you?

<a id="v39"></a>**39** When did we see you sick, or in prison, and come to you?’

<a id="v40"></a>**40** “The King will answer them, ‘Most certainly I tell you, because you did it to one of the least of these my brothers, you did it to me.’

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Then he will say also to those on the left hand, ‘Depart from me, you cursed, into the eternal fire which is prepared for the devil and his angels;

<a id="v42"></a>**42** for I was hungry, and you didn’t give me food to eat; I was thirsty, and you gave me no drink;

<a id="v43"></a>**43** I was a stranger, and you didn’t take me in; naked, and you didn’t clothe me; sick, and in prison, and you didn’t visit me.’

<a id="v44"></a>**44** “Then they will also answer, saying, ‘Lord, when did we see you hungry, or thirsty, or a stranger, or naked, or sick, or in prison, and didn’t help you?’

<a id="v45"></a>**45** “Then he will answer them, saying, ‘Most certainly I tell you, because you didn’t do it to one of the least of these, you didn’t do it to me.’

<a id="v46"></a>**46** These will go away into eternal punishment, but the righteous into eternal life.”
