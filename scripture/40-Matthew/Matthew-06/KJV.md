# Matthew 6 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Take heed that ye do not your alms before men, to be seen of them: otherwise ye have no reward of your Father which is in heaven.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Therefore when thou doest thine alms, do not sound a trumpet before thee, as the hypocrites do in the synagogues and in the streets, that they may have glory of men. Verily I say unto you, They have their reward.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But when thou doest alms, let not thy left hand know what thy right hand doeth:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** That thine alms may be in secret: and thy Father which seeth in secret himself shall reward thee openly.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And when thou prayest, thou shalt not be as the hypocrites are: for they love to pray standing in the synagogues and in the corners of the streets, that they may be seen of men. Verily I say unto you, They have their reward.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But thou, when thou prayest, enter into thy closet, and when thou hast shut thy door, pray to thy Father which is in secret; and thy Father which seeth in secret shall reward thee openly.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** But when ye pray, use not vain repetitions, as the heathen do: for they think that they shall be heard for their much speaking.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Be not ye therefore like unto them: for your Father knoweth what things ye have need of, before ye ask him.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** After this manner therefore pray ye: Our Father which art in heaven, Hallowed be thy name.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Thy kingdom come. Thy will be done in earth, as it is in heaven.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Give us this day our daily bread.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And forgive us our debts, as we forgive our debtors.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil: For thine is the kingdom, and the power, and the glory, for ever. Amen.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For if ye forgive men their trespasses, your heavenly Father will also forgive you:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** But if ye forgive not men their trespasses, neither will your Father forgive your trespasses.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Moreover when ye fast, be not, as the hypocrites, of a sad countenance: for they disfigure their faces, that they may appear unto men to fast. Verily I say unto you, They have their reward.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** But thou, when thou fastest, anoint thine head, and wash thy face;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** That thou appear not unto men to fast, but unto thy Father which is in secret: and thy Father, which seeth in secret, shall reward thee openly.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Lay not up for yourselves treasures upon earth, where moth and rust doth corrupt, and where thieves break through and steal:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven, where neither moth nor rust doth corrupt, and where thieves do not break through nor steal:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The light of the body is the eye: if therefore thine eye be single, thy whole body shall be full of light.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But if thine eye be evil, thy whole body shall be full of darkness. If therefore the light that is in thee be darkness, how great is that darkness!

<a id="v24"></a>**24** No man can serve two masters: for either he will hate the one, and love the other; or else he will hold to the one, and despise the other. Ye cannot serve God and mammon.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Therefore I say unto you, Take no thought for your life, what ye shall eat, or what ye shall drink; nor yet for your body, what ye shall put on. Is not the life more than meat, and the body than raiment?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Behold the fowls of the air: for they sow not, neither do they reap, nor gather into barns; yet your heavenly Father feedeth them. Are ye not much better than they?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Which of you by taking thought can add one cubit unto his stature?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And why take ye thought for raiment? Consider the lilies of the field, how they grow; they toil not, neither do they spin:

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And yet I say unto you, That even Solomon in all his glory was not arrayed like one of these.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Wherefore, if God so clothe the grass of the field, which to day is, and to morrow is cast into the oven, shall he not much more clothe you, O ye of little faith?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Therefore take no thought, saying, What shall we eat? or, What shall we drink? or, Wherewithal shall we be clothed?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** (For after all these things do the Gentiles seek:) for your heavenly Father knoweth that ye have need of all these things.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** But seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things shall be added unto you.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Take therefore no thought for the morrow: for the morrow shall take thought for the things of itself. Sufficient unto the day is the evil thereof.
