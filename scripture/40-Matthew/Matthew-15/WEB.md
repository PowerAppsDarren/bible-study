# Matthew 15 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Pharisees and scribes came to Jesus from Jerusalem, saying,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Why do your disciples disobey the tradition of the elders? For they don’t wash their hands when they eat bread.”

<a id="v3"></a>**3** He answered them, “Why do you also disobey the commandment of God because of your tradition?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For God commanded, ‘Honor your father and your mother,’and, ‘He who speaks evil of father or mother, let him be put to death.’

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But you say, ‘Whoever may tell his father or his mother, “Whatever help you might otherwise have gotten from me is a gift devoted to God,”

<a id="v6"></a>**6** he shall not honor his father or mother.’ You have made the commandment of God void because of your tradition.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** You hypocrites! Well did Isaiah prophesy of you, saying,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** ‘These people draw near to me with their mouth, and honor me with their lips; but their heart is far from me.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And in vain do they worship me, teaching as doctrine rules made by men.’”

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He summoned the multitude, and said to them, “Hear, and understand.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** That which enters into the mouth doesn’t defile the man; but that which proceeds out of the mouth, this defiles the man.”

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Then the disciples came, and said to him, “Do you know that the Pharisees were offended, when they heard this saying?”

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But he answered, “Every plant which my heavenly Father didn’t plant will be uprooted.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Leave them alone. They are blind guides of the blind. If the blind guide the blind, both will fall into a pit.”

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Peter answered him, “Explain the parable to us.”

<a id="v16"></a>**16** So Jesus said, “Do you also still not understand?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Don’t you understand that whatever goes into the mouth passes into the belly, and then out of the body?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But the things which proceed out of the mouth come out of the heart, and they defile the man.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For out of the heart come evil thoughts, murders, adulteries, sexual sins, thefts, false testimony, and blasphemies.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** These are the things which defile the man; but to eat with unwashed hands doesn’t defile the man.”

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Jesus went out from there, and withdrew into the region of Tyre and Sidon.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Behold, a Canaanite woman came out from those borders, and cried, saying, “Have mercy on me, Lord, you son of David! My daughter is severely possessed by a demon!”

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But he answered her not a word.   His disciples came and begged him, saying, “Send her away; for she cries after us.”

<a id="v24"></a>**24** But he answered, “I wasn’t sent to anyone but the lost sheep of the house of Israel.”

<a id="v25"></a>**25** But she came and worshiped him, saying, “Lord, help me.”

<a id="v26"></a>**26** But he answered, “It is not appropriate to take the children’s bread and throw it to the dogs.”

<a id="v27"></a>**27** But she said, “Yes, Lord, but even the dogs eat the crumbs which fall from their masters’ table.”

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Then Jesus answered her, “Woman, great is your faith! Be it done to you even as you desire.” And her daughter was healed from that hour.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Jesus departed there, and came near to the sea of Galilee; and he went up into the mountain, and sat there.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Great multitudes came to him, having with them the lame, blind, mute, maimed, and many others, and they put them down at his feet. He healed them,

<a id="v31"></a>**31** so that the multitude wondered when they saw the mute speaking, injured whole, lame walking, and blind seeing—and they glorified the God of Israel.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Jesus summoned his disciples and said, “I have compassion on the multitude, because they continue with me now three days and have nothing to eat. I don’t want to send them away fasting, or they might faint on the way.”

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The disciples said to him, “Where should we get so many loaves in a deserted place as to satisfy so great a multitude?”

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Jesus said to them, “How many loaves do you have?”   They said, “Seven, and a few small fish.”

<a id="v35"></a>**35** He commanded the multitude to sit down on the ground;

<a id="v36"></a>**36** and he took the seven loaves and the fish. He gave thanks and broke them, and gave to the disciples, and the disciples to the multitudes.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** They all ate, and were filled. They took up seven baskets full of the broken pieces that were left over.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** Those who ate were four thousand men, besides women and children.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** Then he sent away the multitudes, got into the boat, and came into the borders of Magdala.
