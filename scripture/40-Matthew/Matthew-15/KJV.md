# Matthew 15 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then came to Jesus scribes and Pharisees, which were of Jerusalem, saying,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Why do thy disciples transgress the tradition of the elders? for they wash not their hands when they eat bread.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But he answered and said unto them, Why do ye also transgress the commandment of God by your tradition?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For God commanded, saying, Honour thy father and mother: and, He that curseth father or mother, let him die the death.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But ye say, Whosoever shall say to his father or his mother, It is a gift, by whatsoever thou mightest be profited by me;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And honour not his father or his mother, he shall be free. Thus have ye made the commandment of God of none effect by your tradition.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Ye hypocrites, well did Esaias prophesy of you, saying,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** This people draweth nigh unto me with their mouth, and honoureth me with their lips; but their heart is far from me.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But in vain they do worship me, teaching for doctrines the commandments of men.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And he called the multitude, and said unto them, Hear, and understand:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Not that which goeth into the mouth defileth a man; but that which cometh out of the mouth, this defileth a man.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Then came his disciples, and said unto him, Knowest thou that the Pharisees were offended, after they heard this saying?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But he answered and said, Every plant, which my heavenly Father hath not planted, shall be rooted up.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Let them alone: they be blind leaders of the blind. And if the blind lead the blind, both shall fall into the ditch.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Then answered Peter and said unto him, Declare unto us this parable.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And Jesus said, Are ye also yet without understanding?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Do not ye yet understand, that whatsoever entereth in at the mouth goeth into the belly, and is cast out into the draught?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But those things which proceed out of the mouth come forth from the heart; and they defile the man.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For out of the heart proceed evil thoughts, murders, adulteries, fornications, thefts, false witness, blasphemies:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** These are the things which defile a man: but to eat with unwashen hands defileth not a man.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Then Jesus went thence, and departed into the coasts of Tyre and Sidon.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And, behold, a woman of Canaan came out of the same coasts, and cried unto him, saying, Have mercy on me, O Lord, thou Son of David; my daughter is grievously vexed with a devil.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But he answered her not a word. And his disciples came and besought him, saying, Send her away; for she crieth after us.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** But he answered and said, I am not sent but unto the lost sheep of the house of Israel.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Then came she and worshipped him, saying, Lord, help me.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** But he answered and said, It is not meet to take the children’s bread, and to cast it to dogs.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And she said, Truth, Lord: yet the dogs eat of the crumbs which fall from their masters’ table.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Then Jesus answered and said unto her, O woman, great is thy faith: be it unto thee even as thou wilt. And her daughter was made whole from that very hour.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And Jesus departed from thence, and came nigh unto the sea of Galilee; and went up into a mountain, and sat down there.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And great multitudes came unto him, having with them those that were lame, blind, dumb, maimed, and many others, and cast them down at Jesus’ feet; and he healed them:

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Insomuch that the multitude wondered, when they saw the dumb to speak, the maimed to be whole, the lame to walk, and the blind to see: and they glorified the God of Israel.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Then Jesus called his disciples unto him, and said, I have compassion on the multitude, because they continue with me now three days, and have nothing to eat: and I will not send them away fasting, lest they faint in the way.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And his disciples say unto him, Whence should we have so much bread in the wilderness, as to fill so great a multitude?

<a id="v34"></a>**34** And Jesus saith unto them, How many loaves have ye? And they said, Seven, and a few little fishes.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And he commanded the multitude to sit down on the ground.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And he took the seven loaves and the fishes, and gave thanks, and brake them, and gave to his disciples, and the disciples to the multitude.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And they did all eat, and were filled: and they took up of the broken meat that was left seven baskets full.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And they that did eat were four thousand men, beside women and children.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And he sent away the multitude, and took ship, and came into the coasts of Magdala.
