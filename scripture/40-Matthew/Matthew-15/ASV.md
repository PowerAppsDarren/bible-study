# Matthew 15 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then there come to Jesus from Jerusalem Pharisees and scribes, saying,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Why do thy disciples transgress the tradition of the elders? for they wash not their hands when they eat bread.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And he answered and said unto them, Why do ye also transgress the commandment of God because of your tradition?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For God said, Honor thy father and thy mother: and, He that speaketh evil of father or mother, let him die the death.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But ye say, Whosoever shall say to his father or his mother, That wherewith thou mightest have been profited by me is given to God;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** he shall not honor his father. And ye have made void the word of God because of your tradition.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Ye hypocrites, well did Isaiah prophesy of you, saying,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** This people honoreth me with their lips; But their heart is far from me.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But in vain do they worship me, Teachingas theirdoctrines the precepts of men.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And he called to him the multitude, and said unto them, Hear, and understand:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Not that which entereth into the mouth defileth the man; but that which proceedeth out of the mouth, this defileth the man.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Then came the disciples, and said unto him, Knowest thou that the Pharisees were offended, when they heard this saying?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But he answered and said, Every plant which my heavenly Father planted not, shall be rooted up.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Let them alone: they are blind guides. And if the blind guide the blind, both shall fall into a pit.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And Peter answered and said unto him, Declare unto us the parable.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And he said, Are ye also even yet without understanding?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Perceive ye not, that whatsoever goeth into the mouth passeth into the belly, and is cast out into the draught?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But the things which proceed out of the mouth come forth out of the heart; and they defile the man.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For out of the heart come forth evil thoughts, murders, adulteries, fornications, thefts, false witness, railings:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** these are the things which defile the man; but to eat with unwashen hands defileth not the man.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And Jesus went out thence, and withdrew into the parts of Tyre and Sidon.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And behold, a Canaanitish woman came out from those borders, and cried, saying, Have mercy on me, O Lord, thou son of David; my daughter is grievously vexed with a demon.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But he answered her not a word. And his disciples came and besought him, saying, Send her away; for she crieth after us.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** But he answered and said, I was not sent but unto the lost sheep of the house of Israel.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** But she came and worshipped him, saying, Lord, help me.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And he answered and said, It is not meet to take the children’s bread and cast it to the dogs.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** But she said, Yea, Lord: for even the dogs eat of the crumbs which fall from their masters’ table.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Then Jesus answered and said unto her, O woman, great is thy faith: be it done unto thee even as thou wilt. And her daughter was healed from that hour.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And Jesus departed thence, and came nigh unto the sea of Galilee; and he went up into the mountain, and sat there.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And there came unto him great multitudes, having with them the lame, blind, dumb, maimed, and many others, and they cast them down at his feet; and he healed them:

<a id="v31"></a>**31** insomuch that the multitude wondered, when they saw the dumb speaking, the maimed whole, and the lame walking, and the blind seeing: and they glorified the God of Israel.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And Jesus called unto him his disciples, and said, I have compassion on the multitude, because they continue with me now three days and have nothing to eat: and I would not send them away fasting, lest haply they faint on the way.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And the disciples say unto him, Whence should we have so many loaves in a desert place as to fill so great a multitude?

<a id="v34"></a>**34** And Jesus said unto them, How many loaves have ye? And they said, Seven, and a few small fishes.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And he commanded the multitude to sit down on the ground;

<a id="v36"></a>**36** and he took the seven loaves and the fishes; and he gave thanks and brake, and gave to the disciples, and the disciples to the multitudes.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And they all ate, and were filled: and they took up that which remained over of the broken pieces, seven baskets full.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And they that did eat were four thousand men, besides women and children.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And he sent away the multitudes, and entered into the boat, and came into the borders of Magadan.
