# Judges 5 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** At that time Deborah and Barak, the son of Abinoam, made this song, saying:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Because of the flowing hair of the fighters in Israel, because the people gave themselves freely, give praise to the Lord.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Give attention, O kings; give ear, O rulers; I, even I, will make a song to the Lord; I will make melody to the Lord, the God of Israel.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Lord, when you went out from Seir, moving like an army from the field of Edom, the earth was shaking and the heavens were troubled, and the clouds were dropping water.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The mountains were shaking before the Lord, before the Lord, the God of Israel.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** In the days of Shamgar, the son of Anath, in the days of Jael, the highways were not used, and travellers went by side roads.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Country towns were no more in Israel, *** were no more, till you, Deborah, came up, till you came up as a mother in Israel.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They had no one to make arms, there were no more armed men in the towns; was there a body-cover or a spear to be seen among forty thousand in Israel?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Come, you rulers of Israel, you who gave yourselves freely among the people: give praise to the Lord.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Let them give thought to it, who go on white asses, and those who are walking on the road.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Give ear to the women laughing by the water-springs; there they will give again the story of the upright acts of the Lord, all the upright acts of his arm in Israel.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Awake! awake! Deborah: awake! awake! give a song: Up! Barak, and take prisoner those who took you prisoner, O son of Abinoam.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Then the chiefs went down to the doors; the Lord's people went down among the strong ones.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Out of Ephraim they came down into the valley; after you, Benjamin, among your tribesmen; from Machir came down the captains, and from Zebulun those in whose hand is the ruler's rod.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Your chiefs, Issachar, were with Deborah; and Naphtali was true to Barak; into the valley they went rushing out at his feet. In Reuben there were divisions, and great searchings of heart.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Why did you keep quiet among the sheep, hearing nothing but the watchers piping to the flocks?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Gilead was living over Jordan; and Dan was waiting in his ships; Asher kept in his place by the sea's edge, living by his inlets.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** It was the people of Zebulun who put their lives in danger, even to death, with Naphtali on the high places of the field.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The kings came on to the fight, the kings of Canaan were warring; in Taanach by the waters of Megiddo: they took no profit in money.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The stars from heaven were fighting; from their highways they were fighting against Sisera.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The river Kishon took them violently away, stopping their flight, the river Kishon. Give praise, O my soul, to the strength of the Lord!

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Then loudly the feet of the horses were sounding with the stamping, the stamping of their war-horses.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** A curse, a curse on Meroz! said the angel of the Lord. A bitter curse on her townspeople! Because they came not to the help of the Lord, to the help of the Lord among the strong ones.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Blessings be on Jael, more than on all women! Blessings greater than on any in the tents!

<a id="v25"></a>**25** His request was for water, she gave him milk; she put butter before him on a fair plate.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** She put out her hand to the tent-pin, and her right hand to the workman's hammer; and she gave Sisera a blow, crushing his head, wounding and driving through his brow.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Bent at her feet he went down, he was stretched out; bent at her feet he went down; where he was bent down, there he went down in death.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Looking out from the window she gave a cry, the mother of Sisera was crying out through the window, Why is his carriage so long in coming? When will the noise of his wheels be sounding?

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Her wise women gave answer to her, yes, she made answer again to herself,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Are they not getting, are they not parting the goods among them: a young girl or two to every man; and to Sisera robes of coloured needlework, worked in fair colours on this side and on that, for the neck of the queen?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** So may destruction come on all your haters, O Lord; but let your lovers be like the sun going out in his strength. And for forty years the land had peace.
