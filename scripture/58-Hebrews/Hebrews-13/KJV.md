# Hebrews 13 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Let brotherly love continue.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Be not forgetful to entertain strangers: for thereby some have entertained angels unawares.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Remember them that are in bonds, as bound with them; and them which suffer adversity, as being yourselves also in the body.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Marriage is honourable in all, and the bed undefiled: but whoremongers and adulterers God will judge.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Let your conversation be without covetousness; and be content with such things as ye have: for he hath said, I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** So that we may boldly say, The Lord is my helper, and I will not fear what man shall do unto me.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Remember them which have the rule over you, who have spoken unto you the word of God: whose faith follow, considering the end of their conversation.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Be not carried about with divers and strange doctrines. For it is a good thing that the heart be established with grace; not with meats, which have not profited them that have been occupied therein.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** We have an altar, whereof they have no right to eat which serve the tabernacle.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For the bodies of those beasts, whose blood is brought into the sanctuary by the high priest for sin, are burned without the camp.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Wherefore Jesus also, that he might sanctify the people with his own blood, suffered without the gate.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Let us go forth therefore unto him without the camp, bearing his reproach.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For here have we no continuing city, but we seek one to come.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** By him therefore let us offer the sacrifice of praise to God continually, that is, the fruit of our lips giving thanks to his name.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But to do good and to communicate forget not: for with such sacrifices God is well pleased.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Obey them that have the rule over you, and submit yourselves: for they watch for your souls, as they that must give account, that they may do it with joy, and not with grief: for that is unprofitable for you.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Pray for us: for we trust we have a good conscience, in all things willing to live honestly.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** But I beseech you the rather to do this, that I may be restored to you the sooner.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Now the God of peace, that brought again from the dead our Lord Jesus, that great shepherd of the sheep, through the blood of the everlasting covenant,

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Make you perfect in every good work to do his will, working in you that which is wellpleasing in his sight, through Jesus Christ; to whom be glory for ever and ever. Amen.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And I beseech you, brethren, suffer the word of exhortation: for I have written a letter unto you in few words.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Know ye that our brother Timothy is set at liberty; with whom, if he come shortly, I will see you.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Salute all them that have the rule over you, and all the saints. They of Italy salute you.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Grace be with you all. Amen.  Written to the Hebrews from Italy by Timothy.
