# Hebrews 3 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wherefore, holy brethren, partakers of the heavenly calling, consider the Apostle and High Priest of our profession, Christ Jesus;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Who was faithful to him that appointed him, as also Moses was faithful in all his house.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For this man was counted worthy of more glory than Moses, inasmuch as he who hath builded the house hath more honour than the house.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For every house is builded by some man; but he that built all things is God.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And Moses verily was faithful in all his house, as a servant, for a testimony of those things which were to be spoken after;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But Christ as a son over his own house; whose house are we, if we hold fast the confidence and the rejoicing of the hope firm unto the end.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Wherefore (as the Holy Ghost saith, To day if ye will hear his voice,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Harden not your hearts, as in the provocation, in the day of temptation in the wilderness:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** When your fathers tempted me, proved me, and saw my works forty years.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Wherefore I was grieved with that generation, and said, They do alway err in their heart; and they have not known my ways.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** So I sware in my wrath, They shall not enter into my rest.)

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Take heed, brethren, lest there be in any of you an evil heart of unbelief, in departing from the living God.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But exhort one another daily, while it is called To day; lest any of you be hardened through the deceitfulness of sin.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For we are made partakers of Christ, if we hold the beginning of our confidence stedfast unto the end;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** While it is said, To day if ye will hear his voice, harden not your hearts, as in the provocation.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For some, when they had heard, did provoke: howbeit not all that came out of Egypt by Moses.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** But with whom was he grieved forty years? was it not with them that had sinned, whose carcases fell in the wilderness?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And to whom sware he that they should not enter into his rest, but to them that believed not?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** So we see that they could not enter in because of unbelief.
