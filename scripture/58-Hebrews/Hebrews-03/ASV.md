# Hebrews 3 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wherefore, holy brethren, partakers of a heavenly calling, consider the Apostle and High Priest of our confession, evenJesus;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** who was faithful to him that appointed him, as also was Moses in all his house.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For he hath been counted worthy of more glory than Moses, by so much as he that built the house hath more honor than the house.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For every house is builded by some one; but he that built all things is God.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And Moses indeed was faithful in all his house as a servant, for a testimony of those things which were afterward to be spoken;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** but Christ as a son, over his house; whose house are we, if we hold fast our boldness and the glorying of our hope firm unto the end.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Wherefore, even as the Holy Spirit saith,  To-day if ye shall hear his voice,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Harden not your hearts, as in the provocation, Like as in the day of the trial in the wilderness,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Where your fathers triedmeby proving me, And saw my works forty years.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Wherefore I was displeased with this generation, And said, They do always err in their heart: But they did not know my ways;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** As I sware in my wrath, They shall not enter into my rest.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Take heed, brethren, lest haply there shall be in any one of you an evil heart of unbelief, in falling away from the living God:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** but exhort one another day by day, so long as it is called To-day; lest any one of you be hardened by the deceitfulness of sin:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** for we are become partakers of Christ, if we hold fast the beginning of our confidence firm unto the end:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** while it is said,  To-day if ye shall hear his voice, Harden not your hearts, as in the provocation.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For who, when they heard, did provoke? nay, did not all they that came out of Egypt by Moses?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And with whom was he displeased forty years? was it not with them that sinned, whose bodies fell in the wilderness?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And to whom sware he that they should not enter into his rest, but to them that were disobedient?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And we see that they were not able to enter in because of unbelief.
