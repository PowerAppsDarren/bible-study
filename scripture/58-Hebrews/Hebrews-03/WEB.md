# Hebrews 3 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Therefore, holy brothers, partakers of a heavenly calling, consider the Apostle and High Priest of our confession, Jesus;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** who was faithful to him who appointed him, as also was Moses in all his house.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For he has been counted worthy of more glory than Moses, because he who built the house has more honor than the house.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For every house is built by someone; but he who built all things is God.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Moses indeed was faithful in all his house as a servant, for a testimony of those things which were afterward to be spoken,

<a id="v6"></a>**6** but Christ is faithful as a Son over his house; whose house we are, if we hold fast our confidence and the glorying of our hope firm to the end.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Therefore, even as the Holy Spirit says,  “Today if you will hear his voice,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** don’t harden your hearts, as in the rebellion, like as in the day of the trial in the wilderness,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** where your fathers tested me by proving me, and saw my works for forty years.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Therefore I was displeased with that generation, and said, ‘They always err in their heart, but they didn’t know my ways;’

<a id="v11"></a>**11** as I swore in my wrath, ‘They will not enter into my rest.’”

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Beware, brothers, lest perhaps there be in any one of you an evil heart of unbelief, in falling away from the living God;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** but exhort one another day by day, so long as it is called “today”; lest any one of you be hardened by the deceitfulness of sin.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For we have become partakers of Christ, if we hold fast the beginning of our confidence firm to the end:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** while it is said,  “Today if you will hear his voice, don’t harden your hearts, as in the rebellion.”

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For who, when they heard, rebelled? No, didn’t all those who came out of Egypt by Moses?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** With whom was he displeased forty years? Wasn’t it with those who sinned, whose bodies fell in the wilderness?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** To whom did he swear that they wouldn’t enter into his rest, but to those who were disobedient?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** We see that they were not able to enter in because of unbelief.
