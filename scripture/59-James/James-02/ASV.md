# James 2 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My brethren, hold not the faith of our Lord Jesus Christ, the Lordof glory, with respect of persons.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For if there come into your synagogue a man with a gold ring, in fine clothing, and there come in also a poor man in vile clothing;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** and ye have regard to him that weareth the fine clothing, and say, Sit thou here in a good place; and ye say to the poor man, Stand thou there, or sit under my footstool;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** do ye not make distinctions among yourselves, and become judges with evil thoughts?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Hearken, my beloved brethren; did not God choose them that are poor as to the world to be rich in faith, and heirs of the kingdom which he promised to them that love him?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But ye have dishonored the poor man. Do not the rich oppress you, and themselves drag you before the judgment-seats?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Do not they blaspheme the honorable name by which ye are called?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Howbeit if ye fulfil the royal law, according to the scripture, Thou shalt love thy neighbor as thyself, ye do well:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** but if ye have respect of persons, ye commit sin, being convicted by the law as transgressors.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** For whosoever shall keep the whole law, and yet stumble in one point, he is become guilty of all.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For he that said, Do not commit adultery, said also, Do not kill. Now if thou dost not commit adultery, but killest, thou art become a transgressor of the law.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** So speak ye, and so do, as men that are to be judged by a law of liberty.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For judgment is without mercy to him that hath showed no mercy: mercy glorieth against judgment.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** What doth it profit, my brethren, if a man say he hath faith, but have not works? can that faith save him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** If a brother or sister be naked and in lack of daily food,

<a id="v16"></a>**16** and one of you say unto them, Go in peace, be ye warmed and filled; and yet ye give them not the things needful to the body; what doth it profit?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Even so faith, if it have not works, is dead in itself.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Yea, a man will say, Thou hast faith, and I have works: show me thy faith apart from thy works, and I by my works will show thee my faith.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Thou believest that God is one; thou doest well: the demons also believe, and shudder.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But wilt thou know, O vain man, that faith apart from works is barren?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Was not Abraham our father justified by works, in that he offered up Isaac his son upon the altar?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Thou seest that faith wrought with his works, and by works was faith made perfect;

<a id="v23"></a>**23** and the scripture was fulfilled which saith, And Abraham believed God, and it was reckoned unto him for righteousness; and he was called the friend of God.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Ye see that by works a man is justified, and not only by faith.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And in like manner was not also Rahab the harlot justified by works, in that she received the messengers, and sent them out another way?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For as the body apart from the spirit is dead, even so faith apart from works is dead.
