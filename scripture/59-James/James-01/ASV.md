# James 1 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** James, a servant of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ, to the twelve tribes which are of the Dispersion, greeting.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Count it all joy, my brethren, when ye fall into manifold temptations;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** knowing that the proving of your faith worketh patience.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And let patience have its perfect work, that ye may be perfect and entire, lacking in nothing.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But if any of you lacketh wisdom, let him ask of God, who giveth to all liberally and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But let him ask in faith, nothing doubting: for he that doubteth is like the surge of the sea driven by the wind and tossed.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For let not that man think that he shall receive anything of the Lord;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** a doubleminded man, unstable in all his ways.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But let the brother of low degree glory in his high estate:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** and the rich, in that he is made low: because as the flower of the grass he shall pass away.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For the sun ariseth with the scorching wind, and withereth the grass; and the flower thereof falleth, and the grace of the fashion of it perisheth: so also shall the rich man fade away in his goings.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Blessed is the man that endureth temptation; for when he hath been approved, he shall receive the crown of life, which the Lord promised to them that love him.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Let no man say when he is tempted, I am tempted of God; for God cannot be tempted with evil, and he himself tempteth no man:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** but each man is tempted, when he is drawn away by his own lust, and enticed.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Then the lust, when it hath conceived, beareth sin: and the sin, when it is fullgrown, bringeth forth death.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Be not deceived, my beloved brethren.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of lights, with whom can be no variation, neither shadow that is cast by turning.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Of his own will he brought us forth by the word of truth, that we should be a kind of firstfruits of his creatures.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Ye knowthis, my beloved brethren. But let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** for the wrath of man worketh not the righteousness of God.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Wherefore putting away all filthiness and overflowing of wickedness, receive with meekness the implanted word, which is able to save your souls.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** But be ye doers of the word, and not hearers only, deluding your own selves.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For if any one is a hearer of the word and not a doer, he is like unto a man beholding his natural face in a mirror:

<a id="v24"></a>**24** for he beholdeth himself, and goeth away, and straightway forgetteth what manner of man he was.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** But he that looketh into the perfect law, the law of liberty, and so continueth, being not a hearer that forgetteth but a doer that worketh, this man shall be blessed in his doing.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** If any man thinketh himself to be religious, while he bridleth not his tongue but deceiveth his heart, this man’s religion is vain.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Pure religion and undefiled before our God and Father is this, to visit the fatherless and widows in their affliction, and to keep oneself unspotted from the world.
