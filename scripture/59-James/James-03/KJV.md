# James 3 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My brethren, be not many masters, knowing that we shall receive the greater condemnation.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For in many things we offend all. If any man offend not in word, the same is a perfect man, and able also to bridle the whole body.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Behold, we put bits in the horses’ mouths, that they may obey us; and we turn about their whole body.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Behold also the ships, which though they be so great, and are driven of fierce winds, yet are they turned about with a very small helm, whithersoever the governor listeth.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Even so the tongue is a little member, and boasteth great things. Behold, how great a matter a little fire kindleth!

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And the tongue is a fire, a world of iniquity: so is the tongue among our members, that it defileth the whole body, and setteth on fire the course of nature; and it is set on fire of hell.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For every kind of beasts, and of birds, and of serpents, and of things in the sea, is tamed, and hath been tamed of mankind:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But the tongue can no man tame; it is an unruly evil, full of deadly poison.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Therewith bless we God, even the Father; and therewith curse we men, which are made after the similitude of God.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Out of the same mouth proceedeth blessing and cursing. My brethren, these things ought not so to be.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Doth a fountain send forth at the same place sweet water and bitter?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Can the fig tree, my brethren, bear olive berries? either a vine, figs? so can no fountain both yield salt water and fresh.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Who is a wise man and endued with knowledge among you? let him shew out of a good conversation his works with meekness of wisdom.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But if ye have bitter envying and strife in your hearts, glory not, and lie not against the truth.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** This wisdom descendeth not from above, but is earthly, sensual, devilish.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For where envying and strife is, there is confusion and every evil work.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** But the wisdom that is from above is first pure, then peaceable, gentle, and easy to be intreated, full of mercy and good fruits, without partiality, and without hypocrisy.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And the fruit of righteousness is sown in peace of them that make peace.
