# James 3 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Let not many of you be teachers, my brothers, knowing that we will receive heavier judgment.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For in many things we all stumble. If anyone doesn’t stumble in word, the same is a perfect man, able to bridle the whole body also.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Indeed, we put bits into the horses’ mouths so that they may obey us, and we guide their whole body.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Behold, the ships also, though they are so big and are driven by fierce winds, are yet guided by a very small rudder, wherever the pilot desires.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** So the tongue is also a little member, and boasts great things. See how a small fire can spread to a large forest!

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And the tongue is a fire. The world of iniquity among our members is the tongue, which defiles the whole body, and sets on fire the course of nature, and is set on fire by Gehenna.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For every kind of animal, bird, creeping thing, and thing in the sea, is tamed, and has been tamed by mankind.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But nobody can tame the tongue. It is a restless evil, full of deadly poison.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** With it we bless our God and Father, and with it we curse men, who are made in the image of God.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Out of the same mouth comes blessing and cursing. My brothers, these things ought not to be so.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Does a spring send out from the same opening fresh and bitter water?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Can a fig tree, my brothers, yield olives, or a vine figs? Thus no spring yields both salt water and fresh water.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Who is wise and understanding among you? Let him show by his good conduct that his deeds are done in gentleness of wisdom.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But if you have bitter jealousy and selfish ambition in your heart, don’t boast and don’t lie against the truth.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** This wisdom is not that which comes down from above, but is earthly, sensual, and demonic.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For where jealousy and selfish ambition are, there is confusion and every evil deed.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** But the wisdom that is from above is first pure, then peaceful, gentle, reasonable, full of mercy and good fruits, without partiality, and without hypocrisy.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Now the fruit of righteousness is sown in peace by those who make peace.
