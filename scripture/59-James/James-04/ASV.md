# James 4 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Whence comewars and whence come fightings among you? come they not hence, even of your pleasures that war in your members?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Ye lust, and have not: ye kill, and covet, and cannot obtain: ye fight and war; ye have not, because ye ask not.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Ye ask, and receive not, because ye ask amiss, that ye may spend it in your pleasures.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Ye adulteresses, know ye not that the friendship of the world is enmity with God? Whosoever therefore would be a friend of the world maketh himself an enemy of God.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Or think ye that the scripture speaketh in vain? Doth the spirit which he made to dwell in us long unto envying?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But he giveth more grace. Wherefore the scripture saith, God resisteth the proud, but giveth grace to the humble.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Be subject therefore unto God; but resist the devil, and he will flee from you.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Draw nigh to God, and he will draw nigh to you. Cleanse your hands, ye sinners; and purify your hearts, ye doubleminded.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Be afflicted, and mourn, and weep: let your laughter be turned to mourning, and your joy to heaviness.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Humble yourselves in the sight of the Lord, and he shall exalt you.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Speak not one against another, brethren. He that speaketh against a brother, or judgeth his brother, speaketh against the law, and judgeth the law: but if thou judgest the law, thou art not a doer of the law, but a judge.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** One only is the lawgiver and judge, even he who is able to save and to destroy: but who art thou that judgest thy neighbor?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Come now, ye that say, To-day or to-morrow we will go into this city, and spend a year there, and trade, and get gain:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** whereas ye know not what shall be on the morrow. What is your life? For ye are a vapor that appeareth for a little time, and then vanisheth away.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** For that ye ought to say, If the Lord will, we shall both live, and do this or that.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But now ye glory in your vauntings: all such glorying is evil.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** To him therefore that knoweth to do good, and doeth it not, to him it is sin.
