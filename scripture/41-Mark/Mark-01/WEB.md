# Mark 1 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The beginning of the Good News of Jesus Christ, the Son of God.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As it is written in the prophets,  “Behold, I send my messenger before your face, who will prepare your way before you.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The voice of one crying in the wilderness, ‘Make ready the way of the Lord! Make his paths straight!’”

<a id="v4"></a>**4** John came baptizing in the wilderness and preaching the baptism of repentance for forgiveness of sins.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** All the country of Judea and all those of Jerusalem went out to him. They were baptized by him in the Jordan river, confessing their sins.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** John was clothed with camel’s hair and a leather belt around his waist. He ate locusts and wild honey.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He preached, saying, “After me comes he who is mightier than I, the thong of whose sandals I am not worthy to stoop down and loosen.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** I baptized you in water, but he will baptize you in the Holy Spirit.”

<a id="v9"></a>**9** In those days, Jesus came from Nazareth of Galilee, and was baptized by John in the Jordan.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Immediately coming up from the water, he saw the heavens parting, and the Spirit descending on him like a dove.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** A voice came out of the sky, “You are my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased.”

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Immediately the Spirit drove him out into the wilderness.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He was there in the wilderness forty days tempted by Satan. He was with the wild animals; and the angels were serving him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Now after John was taken into custody, Jesus came into Galilee, preaching the Good News of the Kingdom of God,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** and saying, “The time is fulfilled, and the Kingdom of God is at hand! Repent, and believe in the Good News.”

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Passing along by the sea of Galilee, he saw Simon and Andrew the brother of Simon casting a net into the sea, for they were fishermen.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Jesus said to them, “Come after me, and I will make you into fishers for men.”

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Immediately they left their nets, and followed him.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Going on a little further from there, he saw James the son of Zebedee, and John, his brother, who were also in the boat mending the nets.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Immediately he called them, and they left their father, Zebedee, in the boat with the hired servants, and went after him.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** They went into Capernaum, and immediately on the Sabbath day he entered into the synagogue and taught.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** They were astonished at his teaching, for he taught them as having authority, and not as the scribes.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Immediately there was in their synagogue a man with an unclean spirit, and he cried out,

<a id="v24"></a>**24** saying, “Ha! What do we have to do with you, Jesus, you Nazarene? Have you come to destroy us? I know you who you are: the Holy One of God!”

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Jesus rebuked him, saying, “Be quiet, and come out of him!”

<a id="v26"></a>**26** The unclean spirit, convulsing him and crying with a loud voice, came out of him.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** They were all amazed, so that they questioned among themselves, saying, “What is this? A new teaching? For with authority he commands even the unclean spirits, and they obey him!”

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The report of him went out immediately everywhere into all the region of Galilee and its surrounding area.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Immediately, when they had come out of the synagogue, they came into the house of Simon and Andrew, with James and John.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Now Simon’s wife’s mother lay sick with a fever, and immediately they told him about her.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** He came and took her by the hand, and raised her up. The fever left her, and she served them.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** At evening, when the sun had set, they brought to him all who were sick, and those who were possessed by demons.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** All the city was gathered together at the door.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** He healed many who were sick with various diseases, and cast out many demons. He didn’t allow the demons to speak, because they knew him.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Early in the morning, while it was still dark, he rose up and went out, and departed into a deserted place, and prayed there.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** Simon and those who were with him followed after him;

<a id="v37"></a>**37** and they found him, and told him, “Everyone is looking for you.”

<a id="v38"></a>**38** He said to them, “Let’s go elsewhere into the next towns, that I may preach there also, because I came out for this reason.”

<a id="v39"></a>**39** He went into their synagogues throughout all Galilee, preaching and casting out demons.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** A leper came to him, begging him, kneeling down to him, and saying to him, “If you want to, you can make me clean.”

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Being moved with compassion, he stretched out his hand, and touched him, and said to him, “I want to. Be made clean.”

<a id="v42"></a>**42** When he had said this, immediately the leprosy departed from him, and he was made clean.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** He strictly warned him, and immediately sent him out,

<a id="v44"></a>**44** and said to him, “See you say nothing to anybody, but go show yourself to the priest, and offer for your cleansing the things which Moses commanded, for a testimony to them.”

<a id="v45"></a>**45** But he went out, and began to proclaim it much, and to spread about the matter, so that Jesus could no more openly enter into a city, but was outside in desert places: and they came to him from everywhere.
