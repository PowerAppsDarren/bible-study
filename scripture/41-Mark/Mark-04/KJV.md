# Mark 4 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And he began again to teach by the sea side: and there was gathered unto him a great multitude, so that he entered into a ship, and sat in the sea; and the whole multitude was by the sea on the land.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And he taught them many things by parables, and said unto them in his doctrine,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Hearken; Behold, there went out a sower to sow:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And it came to pass, as he sowed, some fell by the way side, and the fowls of the air came and devoured it up.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And some fell on stony ground, where it had not much earth; and immediately it sprang up, because it had no depth of earth:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But when the sun was up, it was scorched; and because it had no root, it withered away.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And some fell among thorns, and the thorns grew up, and choked it, and it yielded no fruit.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And other fell on good ground, and did yield fruit that sprang up and increased; and brought forth, some thirty, and some sixty, and some an hundred.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And he said unto them, He that hath ears to hear, let him hear.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And when he was alone, they that were about him with the twelve asked of him the parable.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And he said unto them, Unto you it is given to know the mystery of the kingdom of God: but unto them that are without, all these things are done in parables:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** That seeing they may see, and not perceive; and hearing they may hear, and not understand; lest at any time they should be converted, and their sins should be forgiven them.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And he said unto them, Know ye not this parable? and how then will ye know all parables?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The sower soweth the word.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And these are they by the way side, where the word is sown; but when they have heard, Satan cometh immediately, and taketh away the word that was sown in their hearts.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And these are they likewise which are sown on stony ground; who, when they have heard the word, immediately receive it with gladness;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And have no root in themselves, and so endure but for a time: afterward, when affliction or persecution ariseth for the word’s sake, immediately they are offended.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And these are they which are sown among thorns; such as hear the word,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And the cares of this world, and the deceitfulness of riches, and the lusts of other things entering in, choke the word, and it becometh unfruitful.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And these are they which are sown on good ground; such as hear the word, and receive it, and bring forth fruit, some thirtyfold, some sixty, and some an hundred.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And he said unto them, Is a candle brought to be put under a bushel, or under a bed? and not to be set on a candlestick?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** For there is nothing hid, which shall not be manifested; neither was any thing kept secret, but that it should come abroad.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If any man have ears to hear, let him hear.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And he said unto them, Take heed what ye hear: with what measure ye mete, it shall be measured to you: and unto you that hear shall more be given.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For he that hath, to him shall be given: and he that hath not, from him shall be taken even that which he hath.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And he said, So is the kingdom of God, as if a man should cast seed into the ground;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And should sleep, and rise night and day, and the seed should spring and grow up, he knoweth not how.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** For the earth bringeth forth fruit of herself; first the blade, then the ear, after that the full corn in the ear.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** But when the fruit is brought forth, immediately he putteth in the sickle, because the harvest is come.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And he said, Whereunto shall we liken the kingdom of God? or with what comparison shall we compare it?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** It is like a grain of mustard seed, which, when it is sown in the earth, is less than all the seeds that be in the earth:

<a id="v32"></a>**32** But when it is sown, it groweth up, and becometh greater than all herbs, and shooteth out great branches; so that the fowls of the air may lodge under the shadow of it.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And with many such parables spake he the word unto them, as they were able to hear it.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** But without a parable spake he not unto them: and when they were alone, he expounded all things to his disciples.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And the same day, when the even was come, he saith unto them, Let us pass over unto the other side.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And when they had sent away the multitude, they took him even as he was in the ship. And there were also with him other little ships.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And there arose a great storm of wind, and the waves beat into the ship, so that it was now full.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And he was in the hinder part of the ship, asleep on a pillow: and they awake him, and say unto him, Master, carest thou not that we perish?

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And he arose, and rebuked the wind, and said unto the sea, Peace, be still. And the wind ceased, and there was a great calm.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** And he said unto them, Why are ye so fearful? how is it that ye have no faith?

<a id="v41"></a>**41** And they feared exceedingly, and said one to another, What manner of man is this, that even the wind and the sea obey him?
