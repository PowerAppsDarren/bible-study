# Mark 7 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And there are gathered together unto him the Pharisees, and certain of the scribes, who had come from Jerusalem,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** and had seen that some of his disciples ate their bread with defiled, that is, unwashen, hands.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** (For the Pharisees, and all the Jews, except they wash their hands diligently, eat not, holding the tradition of the elders;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** and when they come from the marketplace, except they bathe themselves, they eat not; and many other things there are, which they have received to hold, washings of cups, and pots, and brasen vessels.)

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And the Pharisees and the scribes ask him, Why walk not thy disciples according to the tradition of the elders, but eat their bread with defiled hands?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And he said unto them, Well did Isaiah prophesy of you hypocrites, as it is written,  This people honoreth me with their lips, But their heart is far from me.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** But in vain do they worship me, Teachingas theirdoctrines the precepts of men.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Ye leave the commandment of God, and hold fast the tradition of men.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And he said unto them, Full well do ye reject the commandment of God, that ye may keep your tradition.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** For Moses said, Honor thy father and thy mother; and, He that speaketh evil of father or mother, let him die the death:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** but ye say, If a man shall say to his father or his mother, That wherewith thou mightest have been profited by me is Corban, that is to say, Given to God;

<a id="v12"></a>**12** ye no longer suffer him to do aught for his father or his mother;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** making void the word of God by your tradition, which ye have delivered: and many such like things ye do.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And he called to him the multitude again, and said unto them, Hear me all of you, and understand:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** there is nothing from without the man, that going into him can defile him; but the things which proceed out of the man are those that defile the man.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And when he was entered into the house from the multitude, his disciples asked of him the parable.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And he saith unto them, Are ye so without understanding also? Perceive ye not, that whatsoever from without goeth into the man, it cannot defile him;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** because it goeth not into his heart, but into his belly, and goeth out into the draught? This he said, making all meats clean.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And he said, That which proceedeth out of the man, that defileth the man.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For from within, out of the heart of men, evil thoughts proceed, fornications, thefts, murders, adulteries,

<a id="v22"></a>**22** covetings, wickednesses, deceit, lasciviousness, an evil eye, railing, pride, foolishness:

<a id="v23"></a>**23** all these evil things proceed from within, and defile the man.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And from thence he arose, and went away into the borders of Tyre and Sidon. And he entered into a house, and would have no man know it; and he could not be hid.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** But straightway a woman, whose little daughter had an unclean spirit, having heard of him, came and fell down at his feet.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Now the woman was a Greek, a Syrophoenician by race. And she besought him that he would cast forth the demon out of her daughter.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And he said unto her, Let the children first be filled: for it is not meet to take the children’s bread and cast it to the dogs.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** But she answered and saith unto him, Yea, Lord; even the dogs under the table eat of the children’s crumbs.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And he said unto her, For this saying go thy way; the demon is gone out of thy daughter.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And she went away unto her house, and found the child laid upon the bed, and the demon gone out.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And again he went out from the borders of Tyre, and came through Sidon unto the sea of Galilee, through the midst of the borders of Decapolis.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** And they bring unto him one that was deaf, and had an impediment in his speech; and they beseech him to lay his hand upon him.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And he took him aside from the multitude privately, and put his fingers into his ears, and he spat, and touched his tongue;

<a id="v34"></a>**34** and looking up to heaven, he sighed, and saith unto him, Ephphatha, that is, Be opened.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And his ears were opened, and the bond of his tongue was loosed, and he spake plain.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And he charged them that they should tell no man: but the more he charged them, so much the more a great deal they published it.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And they were beyond measure astonished, saying, He hath done all things well; he maketh even the deaf to hear, and the dumb to speak.
