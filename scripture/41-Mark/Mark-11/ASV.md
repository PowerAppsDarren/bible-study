# Mark 11 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And when they draw nigh unto Jerusalem, unto Bethphage and Bethany, at the mount of Olives, he sendeth two of his disciples,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** and saith unto them, Go your way into the village that is over against you: and straightway as ye enter into it, ye shall find a colt tied, whereon no man ever yet sat; loose him, and bring him.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And if any one say unto you, Why do ye this? say ye, The Lord hath need of him; and straightway he will send him back hither.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And they went away, and found a colt tied at the door without in the open street; and they loose him.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And certain of them that stood there said unto them, What do ye, loosing the colt?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And they said unto them even as Jesus had said: and they let them go.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And they bring the colt unto Jesus, and cast on him their garments; and he sat upon him.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And many spread their garments upon the way; and others branches, which they had cut from the fields.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And they that went before, and they that followed, cried, Hosanna; Blessed is he that cometh in the name of the Lord:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Blessed is the kingdom that cometh, the kingdom of our father David: Hosanna in the highest.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And he entered into Jerusalem, into the temple; and when he had looked round about upon all things, it being now eventide, he went out unto Bethany with the twelve.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And on the morrow, when they were come out from Bethany, he hungered.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And seeing a fig tree afar off having leaves, he came, if haply he might find anything thereon: and when he came to it, he found nothing but leaves; for it was not the season of figs.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And he answered and said unto it, No man eat fruit from thee henceforward for ever. And his disciples heard it.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And they come to Jerusalem: and he entered into the temple, and began to cast out them that sold and them that bought in the temple, and overthrew the tables of the money-changers, and the seats of them that sold the doves;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** and he would not suffer that any man should carry a vessel through the temple.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And he taught, and said unto them, Is it not written, My house shall be called a house of prayer for all the nations? but ye have made it a den of robbers.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And the chief priests and the scribes heard it, and sought how they might destroy him: for they feared him, for all the multitude was astonished at his teaching.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And every evening he went forth out of the city.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And as they passed by in the morning, they saw the fig tree withered away from the roots.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And Peter calling to remembrance saith unto him, Rabbi, behold, the fig tree which thou cursedst is withered away.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And Jesus answering saith unto them, Have faith in God.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Verily I say unto you, Whosoever shall say unto this mountain, Be thou taken up and cast into the sea; and shall not doubt in his heart, but shall believe that what he saith cometh to pass; he shall have it.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Therefore I say unto you, All things whatsoever ye pray and ask for, believe that ye receive them, and ye shall have them.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And whensoever ye stand praying, forgive, if ye have aught against any one; that your Father also who is in heaven may forgive you your trespasses.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And they come again to Jerusalem: and as he was walking in the temple, there come to him the chief priests, and the scribes, and the elders;

<a id="v28"></a>**28** and they said unto him, By what authority doest thou these things? or who gave thee this authority to do these things?

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And Jesus said unto them, I will ask of you one question, and answer me, and I will tell you by what authority I do these things.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** The baptism of John, was it from heaven, or from men? answer me.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And they reasoned with themselves, saying, If we shall say, From heaven; he will say, Why then did ye not believe him?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** But should we say, From men—they feared the people: for all verily held John to be a prophet.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And they answered Jesus and say, We know not. And Jesus saith unto them, Neither tell I you by what authority I do these things.
