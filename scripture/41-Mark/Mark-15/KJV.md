# Mark 15 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And straightway in the morning the chief priests held a consultation with the elders and scribes and the whole council, and bound Jesus, and carried him away, and delivered him to Pilate.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And Pilate asked him, Art thou the King of the Jews? And he answering said unto him, Thou sayest it.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And the chief priests accused him of many things: but he answered nothing.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And Pilate asked him again, saying, Answerest thou nothing? behold how many things they witness against thee.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But Jesus yet answered nothing; so that Pilate marvelled.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Now at that feast he released unto them one prisoner, whomsoever they desired.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And there was one named Barabbas, which lay bound with them that had made insurrection with him, who had committed murder in the insurrection.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And the multitude crying aloud began to desire him to do as he had ever done unto them.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But Pilate answered them, saying, Will ye that I release unto you the King of the Jews?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** For he knew that the chief priests had delivered him for envy.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** But the chief priests moved the people, that he should rather release Barabbas unto them.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And Pilate answered and said again unto them, What will ye then that I shall do unto him whom ye call the King of the Jews?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And they cried out again, Crucify him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Then Pilate said unto them, Why, what evil hath he done? And they cried out the more exceedingly, Crucify him.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And so Pilate, willing to content the people, released Barabbas unto them, and delivered Jesus, when he had scourged him, to be crucified.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And the soldiers led him away into the hall, called Pretorium; and they call together the whole band.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And they clothed him with purple, and platted a crown of thorns, and put it about his head,

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And began to salute him, Hail, King of the Jews!

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And they smote him on the head with a reed, and did spit upon him, and bowing their knees worshipped him.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And when they had mocked him, they took off the purple from him, and put his own clothes on him, and led him out to crucify him.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And they compel one Simon a Cyrenian, who passed by, coming out of the country, the father of Alexander and Rufus, to bear his cross.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And they bring him unto the place Golgotha, which is, being interpreted, The place of a skull.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And they gave him to drink wine mingled with myrrh: but he received it not.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And when they had crucified him, they parted his garments, casting lots upon them, what every man should take.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And it was the third hour, and they crucified him.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And the superscription of his accusation was written over, THE KING OF THE JEWS.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And with him they crucify two thieves; the one on his right hand, and the other on his left.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And the scripture was fulfilled, which saith, And he was numbered with the transgressors.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And they that passed by railed on him, wagging their heads, and saying, Ah, thou that destroyest the temple, and buildest it in three days,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Save thyself, and come down from the cross.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Likewise also the chief priests mocking said among themselves with the scribes, He saved others; himself he cannot save.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Let Christ the King of Israel descend now from the cross, that we may see and believe. And they that were crucified with him reviled him.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And when the sixth hour was come, there was darkness over the whole land until the ninth hour.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** And at the ninth hour Jesus cried with a loud voice, saying, Eloi, Eloi, lama sabachthani? which is, being interpreted, My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And some of them that stood by, when they heard it, said, Behold, he calleth Elias.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And one ran and filled a spunge full of vinegar, and put it on a reed, and gave him to drink, saying, Let alone; let us see whether Elias will come to take him down.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And Jesus cried with a loud voice, and gave up the ghost.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And when the centurion, which stood over against him, saw that he so cried out, and gave up the ghost, he said, Truly this man was the Son of God.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** There were also women looking on afar off: among whom was Mary Magdalene, and Mary the mother of James the less and of Joses, and Salome;

<a id="v41"></a>**41** (Who also, when he was in Galilee, followed him, and ministered unto him;) and many other women which came up with him unto Jerusalem.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** And now when the even was come, because it was the preparation, that is, the day before the sabbath,

<a id="v43"></a>**43** Joseph of Arimathea, an honourable counsellor, which also waited for the kingdom of God, came, and went in boldly unto Pilate, and craved the body of Jesus.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** And Pilate marvelled if he were already dead: and calling unto him the centurion, he asked him whether he had been any while dead.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** And when he knew it of the centurion, he gave the body to Joseph.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** And he bought fine linen, and took him down, and wrapped him in the linen, and laid him in a sepulchre which was hewn out of a rock, and rolled a stone unto the door of the sepulchre.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** And Mary Magdalene and Mary the mother of Joses beheld where he was laid.
