# Mark 15 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And straightway in the morning the chief priests with the elders and scribes, and the whole council, held a consultation, and bound Jesus, and carried him away, and delivered him up to Pilate.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And Pilate asked him, Art thou the King of the Jews? And he answering saith unto him, Thou sayest.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And the chief priests accused him of many things.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And Pilate again asked him, saying, Answerest thou nothing? behold how many things they accuse thee of.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But Jesus no more answered anything; insomuch that Pilate marvelled.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Now at the feast he used to release unto them one prisoner, whom they asked of him.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And there was one called Barabbas, lying bound with them that had made insurrection, men who in the insurrection had committed murder.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And the multitude went up and began to ask him to do as he was wont to do unto them.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And Pilate answered them, saying, Will ye that I release unto you the King of the Jews?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** For he perceived that for envy the chief priests had delivered him up.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** But the chief priests stirred up the multitude, that he should rather release Barabbas unto them.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And Pilate again answered and said unto them, What then shall I do unto him whom ye call the King of the Jews?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And they cried out again, Crucify him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And Pilate said unto them, Why, what evil hath he done? But they cried out exceedingly, Crucify him.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And Pilate, wishing to content the multitude, released unto them Barabbas, and delivered Jesus, when he had scourged him, to be crucified.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And the soldiers led him away within the court, which is the Prætorium; and they call together the whole band.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And they clothe him with purple, and platting a crown of thorns, they put it on him;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** and they began to salute him, Hail, King of the Jews!

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And they smote his head with a reed, and spat upon him, and bowing their knees worshipped him.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And when they had mocked him, they took off from him the purple, and put on him his garments. And they lead him out to crucify him.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And they compel one passing by, Simon of Cyrene, coming from the country, the father of Alexander and Rufus, to go with them, that he might bear his cross.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And they bring him unto the place Golgotha, which is, being interpreted, The place of a skull.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And they offered him wine mingled with myrrh: but he received it not.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And they crucify him, and part his garments among them, casting lots upon them, what each should take.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And it was the third hour, and they crucified him.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And the superscription of his accusation was written over, THE KING OF THE JEWS.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And with him they crucify two robbers; one on his right hand, and one on his left.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And they that passed by railed on him, wagging their heads, and saying, Ha! thou that destroyest the temple, and buildest it in three days,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** save thyself, and come down from the cross.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** In like manner also the chief priests mocking him among themselves with the scribes said, He saved others; himself he cannot save.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Let the Christ, the King of Israel, now come down from the cross, that we may see and believe. And they that were crucified with him reproached him.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And when the sixth hour was come, there was darkness over the whole land until the ninth hour.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** And at the ninth hour Jesus cried with a loud voice, Eloi, Eloi, lama sabachthani? which is, being interpreted, My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And some of them that stood by, when they heard it, said, Behold, he calleth Elijah.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And one ran, and filling a sponge full of vinegar, put it on a reed, and gave him to drink, saying, Let be; let us see whether Elijah cometh to take him down.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And Jesus uttered a loud voice, and gave up the ghost.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And the veil of the temple was rent in two from the top to the bottom.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And when the centurion, who stood by over against him, saw that he so gave up the ghost, he said, Truly this man was the Son of God.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** And there were also women beholding from afar: among whom were both Mary Magdalene, and Mary the mother of James the less and of Joses, and Salome;

<a id="v41"></a>**41** who, when he was in Galilee, followed him, and ministered unto him; and many other women that came up with him unto Jerusalem.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** And when even was now come, because it was the Preparation, that is, the day before the sabbath,

<a id="v43"></a>**43** there came Joseph of Arimathæa, a councillor of honorable estate, who also himself was looking for the kingdom of God; and he boldly went in unto Pilate, and asked for the body of Jesus.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** And Pilate marvelled if he were already dead: and calling unto him the centurion, he asked him whether he had been any while dead.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** And when he learned it of the centurion, he granted the corpse to Joseph.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** And he bought a linen cloth, and taking him down, wound him in the linen cloth, and laid him in a tomb which had been hewn out of a rock; and he rolled a stone against the door of the tomb.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** And Mary Magdalene and Mary the mother of Joses beheld where he was laid.
