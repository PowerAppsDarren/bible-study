# Mark 15 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Immediately in the morning the chief priests, with the elders and scribes, and the whole council, held a consultation, and bound Jesus, and carried him away, and delivered him up to Pilate.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Pilate asked him, “Are you the King of the Jews?”   He answered, “So you say.”

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The chief priests accused him of many things.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Pilate again asked him, “Have you no answer? See how many things they testify against you!”

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But Jesus made no further answer, so that Pilate marveled.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Now at the feast he used to release to them one prisoner, whom they asked of him.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** There was one called Barabbas, bound with those who had made insurrection, men who in the insurrection had committed murder.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The multitude, crying aloud, began to ask him to do as he always did for them.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Pilate answered them, saying, “Do you want me to release to you the King of the Jews?”

<a id="v10"></a>**10** For he perceived that for envy the chief priests had delivered him up.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** But the chief priests stirred up the multitude, that he should release Barabbas to them instead.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Pilate again asked them, “What then should I do to him whom you call the King of the Jews?”

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They cried out again, “Crucify him!”

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Pilate said to them, “Why, what evil has he done?”   But they cried out exceedingly, “Crucify him!”

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Pilate, wishing to please the multitude, released Barabbas to them, and handed over Jesus, when he had flogged him, to be crucified.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The soldiers led him away within the court, which is the Praetorium; and they called together the whole cohort.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** They clothed him with purple, and weaving a crown of thorns, they put it on him.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** They began to salute him, “Hail, King of the Jews!”

<a id="v19"></a>**19** They struck his head with a reed, and spat on him, and bowing their knees, did homage to him.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** When they had mocked him, they took the purple off of him, and put his own garments on him. They led him out to crucify him.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** They compelled one passing by, coming from the country, Simon of Cyrene, the father of Alexander and Rufus, to go with them, that he might bear his cross.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** They brought him to the place called Golgotha, which is, being interpreted, “The place of a skull.”

<a id="v23"></a>**23** They offered him wine mixed with myrrh to drink, but he didn’t take it.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Crucifying him, they parted his garments among them, casting lots on them, what each should take.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** It was the third hour, and they crucified him.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** The superscription of his accusation was written over him, “THE KING OF THE JEWS.”

<a id="v27"></a>**27** With him they crucified two robbers; one on his right hand, and one on his left.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The Scripture was fulfilled, which says, “He was numbered with transgressors.”

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Those who passed by blasphemed him, wagging their heads, and saying, “Ha! You who destroy the temple, and build it in three days,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** save yourself, and come down from the cross!”

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Likewise, also the chief priests mocking among themselves with the scribes said, “He saved others. He can’t save himself.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Let the Christ, the King of Israel, now come down from the cross, that we may see and believe him.” Those who were crucified with him insulted him.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** When the sixth hour had come, there was darkness over the whole land until the ninth hour.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** At the ninth hour Jesus cried with a loud voice, saying, “Eloi, Eloi, lama sabachthani?” which is, being interpreted, “My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?”

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Some of those who stood by, when they heard it, said, “Behold, he is calling Elijah.”

<a id="v36"></a>**36** One ran, and filling a sponge full of vinegar, put it on a reed, and gave it to him to drink, saying, “Let him be. Let’s see whether Elijah comes to take him down.”

<a id="v37"></a>**37** Jesus cried out with a loud voice, and gave up the spirit.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** The veil of the temple was torn in two from the top to the bottom.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** When the centurion, who stood by opposite him, saw that he cried out like this and breathed his last, he said, “Truly this man was the Son of God!”

<a id="v40"></a>**40** There were also women watching from afar, among whom were both Mary Magdalene, and Mary the mother of James the less and of Joses, and Salome;

<a id="v41"></a>**41** who, when he was in Galilee, followed him, and served him; and many other women who came up with him to Jerusalem.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** When evening had now come, because it was the Preparation Day, that is, the day before the Sabbath,

<a id="v43"></a>**43** Joseph of Arimathaea, a prominent council member who also himself was looking for the Kingdom of God, came. He boldly went in to Pilate, and asked for Jesus’ body.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** Pilate marveled if he were already dead; and summoning the centurion, he asked him whether he had been dead long.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** When he found out from the centurion, he granted the body to Joseph.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** He bought a linen cloth, and taking him down, wound him in the linen cloth, and laid him in a tomb which had been cut out of a rock. He rolled a stone against the door of the tomb.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** Mary Magdalene and Mary, the mother of Joses, saw where he was laid.
