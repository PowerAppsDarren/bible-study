# Hosea 12 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Ephraim feedeth on wind, and followeth after the east wind: he daily increaseth lies and desolation; and they do make a covenant with the Assyrians, and oil is carried into Egypt.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The Lord hath also a controversy with Judah, and will punish Jacob according to his ways; according to his doings will he recompense him.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** He took his brother by the heel in the womb, and by his strength he had power with God:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Yea, he had power over the angel, and prevailed: he wept, and made supplication unto him: he found him in Beth–el, and there he spake with us;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Even the Lord God of hosts; the Lord is his memorial.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Therefore turn thou to thy God: keep mercy and judgment, and wait on thy God continually.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He is a merchant, the balances of deceit are in his hand: he loveth to oppress.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And Ephraim said, Yet I am become rich, I have found me out substance: in all my labours they shall find none iniquity in me that were sin.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And I that am the Lord thy God from the land of Egypt will yet make thee to dwell in tabernacles, as in the days of the solemn feast.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** I have also spoken by the prophets, and I have multiplied visions, and used similitudes, by the ministry of the prophets.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Is there iniquity in Gilead? surely they are vanity: they sacrifice bullocks in Gilgal; yea, their altars are as heaps in the furrows of the fields.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And Jacob fled into the country of Syria, and Israel served for a wife, and for a wife he kept sheep.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And by a prophet the Lord brought Israel out of Egypt, and by a prophet was he preserved.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Ephraim provoked him to anger most bitterly: therefore shall he leave his blood upon him, and his reproach shall his Lord return unto him.
