# Hosea 9 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Rejoice not, O Israel, for joy, as other people: for thou hast gone a whoring from thy God, thou hast loved a reward upon every cornfloor.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The floor and the winepress shall not feed them, and the new wine shall fail in her.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** They shall not dwell in the Lord’s land; but Ephraim shall return to Egypt, and they shall eat unclean things in Assyria.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** They shall not offer wine offerings to the Lord, neither shall they be pleasing unto him: their sacrifices shall be unto them as the bread of mourners; all that eat thereof shall be polluted: for their bread for their soul shall not come into the house of the Lord.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** What will ye do in the solemn day, and in the day of the feast of the Lord?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For, lo, they are gone because of destruction: Egypt shall gather them up, Memphis shall bury them: the pleasant places for their silver, nettles shall possess them: thorns shall be in their tabernacles.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The days of visitation are come, the days of recompence are come; Israel shall know it: the prophet is a fool, the spiritual man is mad, for the multitude of thine iniquity, and the great hatred.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The watchman of Ephraim was with my God: but the prophet is a snare of a fowler in all his ways, and hatred in the house of his God.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** They have deeply corrupted themselves, as in the days of Gibeah: therefore he will remember their iniquity, he will visit their sins.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** I found Israel like grapes in the wilderness; I saw your fathers as the firstripe in the fig tree at her first time: but they went to Baal–peor, and separated themselves unto that shame; and their abominations were according as they loved.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** As for Ephraim, their glory shall fly away like a bird, from the birth, and from the womb, and from the conception.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Though they bring up their children, yet will I bereave them, that there shall not be a man left: yea, woe also to them when I depart from them!

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Ephraim, as I saw Tyrus, is planted in a pleasant place: but Ephraim shall bring forth his children to the murderer.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Give them, O Lord: what wilt thou give? give them a miscarrying womb and dry breasts.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** All their wickedness is in Gilgal: for there I hated them: for the wickedness of their doings I will drive them out of mine house, I will love them no more: all their princes are revolters.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Ephraim is smitten, their root is dried up, they shall bear no fruit: yea, though they bring forth, yet will I slay even the beloved fruit of their womb.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** My God will cast them away, because they did not hearken unto him: and they shall be wanderers among the nations.
