# Jonah 4 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** But this seemed very wrong to Jonah, and he was angry.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And he made prayer to the Lord and said, O Lord, is this not what I said when I was still in my country? This is why I took care to go in flight to Tarshish: for I was certain that you were a loving God, full of pity, slow to be angry and great in mercy, and ready to be turned from your purpose of evil.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** So now, O Lord, give ear to my prayer and take my life from me; for death is better for me than life.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And the Lord said, Have you any right to be angry?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Then Jonah went out of the town, and took his seat on the east side of the town and made himself a roof of branches and took his seat under its shade till he saw what would become of the town.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And the Lord God made a vine come up over Jonah to give him shade over his head. And Jonah was very glad because of the vine.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** But early on the morning after, God made ready a worm for the destruction of the vine, and it became dry and dead.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Then when the sun came up, God sent a burning east wind: and so great was the heat of the sun on his head that Jonah was overcome, and, requesting death for himself, said, Death is better for me than life.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And the Lord said to Jonah, Have you any right to be angry about the vine? And he said, I have a right to be truly angry.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And the Lord said, You had pity on the vine, for which you did no work and for the growth of which you were not responsible; which came up in a night and came to an end in a night;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And am I not to have mercy on Nineveh, that great town, in which there are more than a hundred and twenty thousand persons without the power of judging between right and left, as well as much cattle?
