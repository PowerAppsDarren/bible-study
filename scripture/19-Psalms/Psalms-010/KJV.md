# Psalms 10 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Why standest thou afar off, O Lord? why hidest thou thyself in times of trouble?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The wicked in his pride doth persecute the poor: let them be taken in the devices that they have imagined.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For the wicked boasteth of his heart’s desire, and blesseth the covetous, whom the Lord abhorreth.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The wicked, through the pride of his countenance, will not seek after God: God is not in all his thoughts.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** His ways are always grievous; thy judgments are far above out of his sight: as for all his enemies, he puffeth at them.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He hath said in his heart, I shall not be moved: for I shall never be in adversity.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** His mouth is full of cursing and deceit and fraud: under his tongue is mischief and vanity.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He sitteth in the lurking places of the villages: in the secret places doth he murder the innocent: his eyes are privily set against the poor.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He lieth in wait secretly as a lion in his den: he lieth in wait to catch the poor: he doth catch the poor, when he draweth him into his net.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He croucheth, and humbleth himself, that the poor may fall by his strong ones.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He hath said in his heart, God hath forgotten: he hideth his face; he will never see it.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Arise, O Lord; O God, lift up thine hand: forget not the humble.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Wherefore doth the wicked contemn God? he hath said in his heart, Thou wilt not require it.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Thou hast seen it; for thou beholdest mischief and spite, to requite it with thy hand: the poor committeth himself unto thee; thou art the helper of the fatherless.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Break thou the arm of the wicked and the evil man: seek out his wickedness till thou find none.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The Lord is King for ever and ever: the heathen are perished out of his land.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Lord, thou hast heard the desire of the humble: thou wilt prepare their heart, thou wilt cause thine ear to hear:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** To judge the fatherless and the oppressed, that the man of the earth may no more oppress.
