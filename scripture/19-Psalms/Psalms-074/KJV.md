# Psalms 74 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** O God, why hast thou cast us off for ever? why doth thine anger smoke against the sheep of thy pasture?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Remember thy congregation, which thou hast purchased of old; the rod of thine inheritance, which thou hast redeemed; this mount Zion, wherein thou hast dwelt.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Lift up thy feet unto the perpetual desolations; even all that the enemy hath done wickedly in the sanctuary.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Thine enemies roar in the midst of thy congregations; they set up their ensigns for signs.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A man was famous according as he had lifted up axes upon the thick trees.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But now they break down the carved work thereof at once with axes and hammers.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** They have cast fire into thy sanctuary, they have defiled by casting down the dwelling place of thy name to the ground.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They said in their hearts, Let us destroy them together: they have burned up all the synagogues of God in the land.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** We see not our signs: there is no more any prophet: neither is there among us any that knoweth how long.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** O God, how long shall the adversary reproach? shall the enemy blaspheme thy name for ever?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Why withdrawest thou thy hand, even thy right hand? pluck it out of thy bosom.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For God is my King of old, working salvation in the midst of the earth.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Thou didst divide the sea by thy strength: thou brakest the heads of the dragons in the waters.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Thou brakest the heads of leviathan in pieces, and gavest him to be meat to the people inhabiting the wilderness.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Thou didst cleave the fountain and the flood: thou driedst up mighty rivers.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The day is thine, the night also is thine: thou hast prepared the light and the sun.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Thou hast set all the borders of the earth: thou hast made summer and winter.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Remember this, that the enemy hath reproached, O Lord, and that the foolish people have blasphemed thy name.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** O deliver not the soul of thy turtledove unto the multitude of the wicked: forget not the congregation of thy poor for ever.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Have respect unto the covenant: for the dark places of the earth are full of the habitations of cruelty.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** O let not the oppressed return ashamed: let the poor and needy praise thy name.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Arise, O God, plead thine own cause: remember how the foolish man reproacheth thee daily.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Forget not the voice of thine enemies: the tumult of those that rise up against thee increaseth continually.
