# Psalms 74 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Of God, why have you put us away from you for ever? why is the fire of your wrath smoking against the sheep who are your care?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Keep in mind your band of worshippers, for whom you gave payment in the days which are past, whom you took for yourself as the people of your heritage; even this mountain of Zion, which has been your resting-place.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Go up and see the unending destruction; all the evil which your haters have done in the holy place;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Sending out their voices like lions among your worshippers; they have put up their signs to be seen.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** They are cutting down, like a man whose blade is lifted up against the thick trees.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Your doors are broken down with hammers and iron blades.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** They have put on fire your holy place; they have made the place of your name unclean, pulling it down to the earth.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They have said in their hearts, Let us put an end to them all together; they have given over to the fire all God's places of worship in the land.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** We do not see our signs: there is no longer any prophet, or anyone among us to say how long.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** O God, how long will those who are against us say cruel things? will the hater go on looking down on your name for ever?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Why are you keeping back your hand, and covering your right hand in your robe?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For from the past God is my King, working salvation in the earth.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The sea was parted in two by your strength; the heads of the great sea-beasts were broken.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The heads of the great snake were crushed by you; you gave them as food to the fishes of the sea.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** You made valleys for fountains and springs; you made the ever-flowing rivers dry.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The day is yours and the night is yours: you made the light and the sun.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** By you all the limits of the earth were fixed; you have made summer and winter.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Keep this in mind, O Lord, that your haters have said cruel things, and that your name has been looked down on by a people of evil behaviour.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** O give not the soul of your dove to the hawk; let not the life of the poor go out of your memory for ever.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Keep in mind your undertaking; for the dark places of the earth are full of pride and cruel acts.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** O let not the crushed be turned back in shame; let the low man and the poor give praise to your name.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Up! O God, be the judge of your cause; keep in mind the bitter things which the man of evil behaviour says against you every day.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Keep in mind the voice of your haters; the outcry of those who come against you goes up every day.
