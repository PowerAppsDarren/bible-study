# Psalms 74 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** O God, why hast thou cast us off for ever? Why doth thine anger smoke against the sheep of thy pasture?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Remember thy congregation, which thou hast gotten of old, Which thou hast redeemed to be the tribe of thine inheritance; Andmount Zion, wherein thou hast dwelt.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Lift up thy feet unto the perpetual ruins, All the evil that the enemy hath done in the sanctuary.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Thine adversaries have roared in the midst of thine assembly; They have set up their ensigns for signs.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** They seemed as men that lifted up Axes upon a thicket of trees.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And now all the carved work thereof They break down with hatchet and hammers.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** They have set thy sanctuary on fire; They have profaned the dwelling-place of thy name by casting it to the ground.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They said in their heart, Let us make havoc of them altogether: They have burned up all the synagogues of God in the land.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** We see not our signs: There is no more any prophet; Neither is there among us any that knoweth how long.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** How long, O God, shall the adversary reproach? Shall the enemy blaspheme thy name for ever?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Why drawest thou back thy hand, even thy right hand? Pluck itout of thy bosom and consume them.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Yet God is my King of old, Working salvation in the midst of the earth.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Thou didst divide the sea by thy strength: Thou brakest the heads of the sea-monsters in the waters.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Thou brakest the heads of leviathan in pieces; Thou gavest him to be food to the people inhabiting the wilderness.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Thou didst cleave fountain and flood: Thou driedst up mighty rivers.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The day is thine, the night also is thine: Thou hast prepared the light and the sun.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Thou hast set all the borders of the earth: Thou hast made summer and winter.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Remember this, that the enemy hath reproached, O Jehovah, And that a foolish people hath blasphemed thy name.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Oh deliver not the soul of thy turtle-dove unto the wild beast: Forget not the life of thy poor for ever.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Have respect unto the covenant; For the dark places of the earth are full of the habitations of violence.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Oh let not the oppressed return ashamed: Let the poor and needy praise thy name.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Arise, O God, plead thine own cause: Remember how the foolish man reproacheth thee all the day.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Forget not the voice of thine adversaries: The tumult of those that rise up against thee ascendeth continually.
