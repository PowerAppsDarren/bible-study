# Psalms 90 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Lord, thou hast been our dwelling place in all generations.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Before the mountains were brought forth, or ever thou hadst formed the earth and the world, even from everlasting to everlasting, thou art God.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Thou turnest man to destruction; and sayest, Return, ye children of men.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For a thousand years in thy sight are but as yesterday when it is past, and as a watch in the night.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Thou carriest them away as with a flood; they are as a sleep: in the morning they are like grass which groweth up.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** In the morning it flourisheth, and groweth up; in the evening it is cut down, and withereth.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For we are consumed by thine anger, and by thy wrath are we troubled.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Thou hast set our iniquities before thee, our secret sins in the light of thy countenance.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For all our days are passed away in thy wrath: we spend our years as a tale that is told.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The days of our years are threescore years and ten; and if by reason of strength they be fourscore years, yet is their strength labour and sorrow; for it is soon cut off, and we fly away.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Who knoweth the power of thine anger? even according to thy fear, so is thy wrath.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** So teach us to number our days, that we may apply our hearts unto wisdom.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Return, O Lord, how long? and let it repent thee concerning thy servants.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** O satisfy us early with thy mercy; that we may rejoice and be glad all our days.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Make us glad according to the days wherein thou hast afflicted us, and the years wherein we have seen evil.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Let thy work appear unto thy servants, and thy glory unto their children.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And let the beauty of the Lord our God be upon us: and establish thou the work of our hands upon us; yea, the work of our hands establish thou it.
