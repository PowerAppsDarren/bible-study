# Psalms 107 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** O give thanks unto the Lord, for he is good: for his mercy endureth for ever.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Let the redeemed of the Lord say so, whom he hath redeemed from the hand of the enemy;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And gathered them out of the lands, from the east, and from the west, from the north, and from the south.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** They wandered in the wilderness in a solitary way; they found no city to dwell in.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Hungry and thirsty, their soul fainted in them.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Then they cried unto the Lord in their trouble, and he delivered them out of their distresses.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And he led them forth by the right way, that they might go to a city of habitation.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Oh that men would praise the Lord for his goodness, and for his wonderful works to the children of men!

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For he satisfieth the longing soul, and filleth the hungry soul with goodness.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Such as sit in darkness and in the shadow of death, being bound in affliction and iron;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Because they rebelled against the words of God, and contemned the counsel of the most High:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Therefore he brought down their heart with labour; they fell down, and there was none to help.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Then they cried unto the Lord in their trouble, and he saved them out of their distresses.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He brought them out of darkness and the shadow of death, and brake their bands in sunder.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Oh that men would praise the Lord for his goodness, and for his wonderful works to the children of men!

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For he hath broken the gates of brass, and cut the bars of iron in sunder.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Fools because of their transgression, and because of their iniquities, are afflicted.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Their soul abhorreth all manner of meat; and they draw near unto the gates of death.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Then they cry unto the Lord in their trouble, and he saveth them out of their distresses.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He sent his word, and healed them, and delivered them from their destructions.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Oh that men would praise the Lord for his goodness, and for his wonderful works to the children of men!

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And let them sacrifice the sacrifices of thanksgiving, and declare his works with rejoicing.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** They that go down to the sea in ships, that do business in great waters;

<a id="v24"></a>**24** These see the works of the Lord, and his wonders in the deep.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For he commandeth, and raiseth the stormy wind, which lifteth up the waves thereof.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** They mount up to the heaven, they go down again to the depths: their soul is melted because of trouble.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** They reel to and fro, and stagger like a drunken man, and are at their wits’ end.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Then they cry unto the Lord in their trouble, and he bringeth them out of their distresses.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** He maketh the storm a calm, so that the waves thereof are still.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Then are they glad because they be quiet; so he bringeth them unto their desired haven.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Oh that men would praise the Lord for his goodness, and for his wonderful works to the children of men!

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Let them exalt him also in the congregation of the people, and praise him in the assembly of the elders.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** He turneth rivers into a wilderness, and the watersprings into dry ground;

<a id="v34"></a>**34** A fruitful land into barrenness, for the wickedness of them that dwell therein.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** He turneth the wilderness into a standing water, and dry ground into watersprings.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And there he maketh the hungry to dwell, that they may prepare a city for habitation;

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And sow the fields, and plant vineyards, which may yield fruits of increase.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** He blesseth them also, so that they are multiplied greatly; and suffereth not their cattle to decrease.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** Again, they are minished and brought low through oppression, affliction, and sorrow.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** He poureth contempt upon princes, and causeth them to wander in the wilderness, where there is no way.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Yet setteth he the poor on high from affliction, and maketh him families like a flock.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** The righteous shall see it, and rejoice: and all iniquity shall stop her mouth.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** Whoso is wise, and will observe these things, even they shall understand the lovingkindness of the Lord.
