# Psalms 11 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** In the Lord put I my trust: how say ye to my soul, Flee as a bird to your mountain?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For, lo, the wicked bend their bow, they make ready their arrow upon the string, that they may privily shoot at the upright in heart.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** If the foundations be destroyed, what can the righteous do?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The Lord is in his holy temple, the Lord’s throne is in heaven: his eyes behold, his eyelids try, the children of men.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The Lord trieth the righteous: but the wicked and him that loveth violence his soul hateth.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Upon the wicked he shall rain snares, fire and brimstone, and an horrible tempest: this shall be the portion of their cup.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For the righteous Lord loveth righteousness; his countenance doth behold the upright.
