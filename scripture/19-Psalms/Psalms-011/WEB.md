# Psalms 11 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** In Yahweh, I take refuge. How can you say to my soul, “Flee as a bird to your mountain!”

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For, behold, the wicked bend their bows. They set their arrows on the strings, that they may shoot in darkness at the upright in heart.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** If the foundations are destroyed, what can the righteous do?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Yahweh is in his holy temple. Yahweh is on his throne in heaven. His eyes observe. His eyes examine the children of men.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Yahweh examines the righteous, but the wicked and him who loves violence his soul hates.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** On the wicked he will rain blazing coals; fire, sulfur, and scorching wind shall be the portion of their cup.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For Yahweh is righteous. He loves righteousness. The upright shall see his face.
