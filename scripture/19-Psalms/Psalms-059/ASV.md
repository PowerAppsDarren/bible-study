# Psalms 59 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Deliver me from mine enemies, O my God: Set me on high from them that rise up against me.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Deliver me from the workers of iniquity, And save me from the bloodthirsty men.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For, lo, they lie in wait for my soul; The mighty gather themselves together against me: Not for my transgression, nor for my sin, O Jehovah.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** They run and prepare themselves without my fault: Awake thou to help me, and behold.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Even thou, O Jehovah God of hosts, the God of Israel, Arise to visit all the nations: Be not merciful to any wicked transgressors. [Selah

<a id="v6"></a>**6** They return at evening, they howl like a dog, And go round about the city.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Behold, they belch out with their mouth; Swords are in their lips: For who, say they, doth hear?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But thou, O Jehovah, wilt laugh at them; Thou wilt have all the nations in derision.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Because ofhis strength I will give heed unto thee; For God is my high tower.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** My God with his lovingkindness will meet me: God will let me see my desire upon mine enemies.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Slay them not, lest my people forget: Scatter them by thy power, and bring them down, O Lord our shield.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Forthe sin of their mouth, and the words of their lips, Let them even be taken in their pride, And for cursing and lying which they speak.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Consume them in wrath, consume them, so that they shall be no more: And let them know that God ruleth in Jacob, Unto the ends of the earth. [Selah

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And at evening let them return, let them howl like a dog, And go round about the city.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** They shall wander up and down for food, And tarry all night if they be not satisfied.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But I will sing of thy strength; Yea, I will sing aloud of thy lovingkindness in the morning: For thou hast been my high tower, And a refuge in the day of my distress.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Unto thee, O my strength, will I sing praises: For God is my high tower, the God of my mercy.
