# Psalms 19 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The heavens declare the glory of God; and the firmament sheweth his handywork.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Day unto day uttereth speech, and night unto night sheweth knowledge.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** There is no speech nor language, where their voice is not heard.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Their line is gone out through all the earth, and their words to the end of the world. In them hath he set a tabernacle for the sun,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Which is as a bridegroom coming out of his chamber, and rejoiceth as a strong man to run a race.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** His going forth is from the end of the heaven, and his circuit unto the ends of it: and there is nothing hid from the heat thereof.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The law of the Lord is perfect, converting the soul: the testimony of the Lord is sure, making wise the simple.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The statutes of the Lord are right, rejoicing the heart: the commandment of the Lord is pure, enlightening the eyes.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The fear of the Lord is clean, enduring for ever: the judgments of the Lord are true and righteous altogether.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** More to be desired are they than gold, yea, than much fine gold: sweeter also than honey and the honeycomb.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Moreover by them is thy servant warned: and in keeping of them there is great reward.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Who can understand his errors? cleanse thou me from secret faults.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Keep back thy servant also from presumptuous sins; let them not have dominion over me: then shall I be upright, and I shall be innocent from the great transgression.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Let the words of my mouth, and the meditation of my heart, be acceptable in thy sight, O Lord, my strength, and my redeemer.
