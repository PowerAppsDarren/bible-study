# Psalms 73 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Surely God is good to Israel, Evento such as are pure in heart.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** But as for me, my feet were almost gone; My steps had well nigh slipped.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For I was envious at the arrogant, When I saw the prosperity of the wicked.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For there are no pangs in their death; But their strength is firm.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** They are not in trouble asothermen; Neither are they plagued likeothermen.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Therefore pride is as a chain about their neck; Violence covereth them as a garment.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Their eyes stand out with fatness: They have more than heart could wish.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They scoff, and in wickedness utter oppression: They speak loftily.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** They have set their mouth in the heavens, And their tongue walketh through the earth.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Therefore his people return hither: And waters of a fullcupare drained by them.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And they say, How doth God know? And is there knowledge in the Most High?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Behold, these are the wicked; And, being alway at ease, they increase in riches.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Surely in vain have I cleansed my heart, And washed my hands in innocency;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For all the day long have I been plagued, And chastened every morning.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** If I had said, I will speak thus; Behold, I had dealt treacherously with the generation of thy children.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** When I thought how I might know this, It was too painful for me;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Until I went into the sanctuary of God, And considered their latter end.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Surely thou settest them in slippery places: Thou castest them down to destruction.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** How are they become a desolation in a moment! They are utterly consumed with terrors.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** As a dream when one awaketh, So, O Lord, when thou awakest, thou wilt despise their image.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For my soul was grieved, And I was pricked in my heart:

<a id="v22"></a>**22** So brutish was I, and ignorant; I wasasa beast before thee.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Nevertheless I am continually with thee: Thou hast holden my right hand.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Thou wilt guide me with thy counsel, And afterward receive me to glory.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Whom have I in heavenbut thee? And there is none upon earth that I desire besides thee.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** My flesh and my heart faileth; ButGod is the strength of my heart and my portion for ever.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** For, lo, they that are far from thee shall perish: Thou hast destroyed all them that play the harlot, departing from thee.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** But it is good for me to draw near unto God: I have made the Lord Jehovah my refuge, That I may tell of all thy works.
