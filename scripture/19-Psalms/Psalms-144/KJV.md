# Psalms 144 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Blessed be the Lord my strength, which teacheth my hands to war, and my fingers to fight:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** My goodness, and my fortress; my high tower, and my deliverer; my shield, and he in whom I trust; who subdueth my people under me.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Lord, what is man, that thou takest knowledge of him! or the son of man, that thou makest account of him!

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Man is like to vanity: his days are as a shadow that passeth away.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Bow thy heavens, O Lord, and come down: touch the mountains, and they shall smoke.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Cast forth lightning, and scatter them: shoot out thine arrows, and destroy them.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Send thine hand from above; rid me, and deliver me out of great waters, from the hand of strange children;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Whose mouth speaketh vanity, and their right hand is a right hand of falsehood.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** I will sing a new song unto thee, O God: upon a psaltery and an instrument of ten strings will I sing praises unto thee.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** It is he that giveth salvation unto kings: who delivereth David his servant from the hurtful sword.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Rid me, and deliver me from the hand of strange children, whose mouth speaketh vanity, and their right hand is a right hand of falsehood:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** That our sons may be as plants grown up in their youth; that our daughters may be as corner stones, polished after the similitude of a palace:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** That our garners may be full, affording all manner of store: that our sheep may bring forth thousands and ten thousands in our streets:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** That our oxen may be strong to labour; that there be no breaking in, nor going out; that there be no complaining in our streets.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Happy is that people, that is in such a case: yea, happy is that people, whose God is the Lord.
