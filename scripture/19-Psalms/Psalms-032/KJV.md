# Psalms 32 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Blessed is he whose transgression is forgiven, whose sin is covered.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Blessed is the man unto whom the Lord imputeth not iniquity, and in whose spirit there is no guile.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** When I kept silence, my bones waxed old through my roaring all the day long.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For day and night thy hand was heavy upon me: my moisture is turned into the drought of summer. Selah.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** I acknowledged my sin unto thee, and mine iniquity have I not hid. I said, I will confess my transgressions unto the Lord; and thou forgavest the iniquity of my sin. Selah.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For this shall every one that is godly pray unto thee in a time when thou mayest be found: surely in the floods of great waters they shall not come nigh unto him.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Thou art my hiding place; thou shalt preserve me from trouble; thou shalt compass me about with songs of deliverance. Selah.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** I will instruct thee and teach thee in the way which thou shalt go: I will guide thee with mine eye.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Be ye not as the horse, or as the mule, which have no understanding: whose mouth must be held in with bit and bridle, lest they come near unto thee.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Many sorrows shall be to the wicked: but he that trusteth in the Lord, mercy shall compass him about.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Be glad in the Lord, and rejoice, ye righteous: and shout for joy, all ye that are upright in heart.
