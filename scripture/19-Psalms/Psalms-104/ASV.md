# Psalms 104 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Bless Jehovah, O my soul. O Jehovah my God, thou art very great; Thou art clothed with honor and majesty:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Who coverest thyself with light as with a garment; Who stretchest out the heavens like a curtain;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Who layeth the beams of his chambers in the waters; Who maketh the clouds his chariot; Who walketh upon the wings of the wind;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Who maketh winds his messengers; Flames of fire his ministers;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Who laid the foundations of the earth, That it should not be moved for ever.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Thou coveredst it with the deep as with a vesture; The waters stood above the mountains.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** At thy rebuke they fled; At the voice of thy thunder they hasted away

<a id="v8"></a>**8** (The mountains rose, the valleys sank down) Unto the place which thou hadst founded for them.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Thou hast set a bound that they may not pass over; That they turn not again to cover the earth.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He sendeth forth springs into the valleys; They run among the mountains;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** They give drink to every beast of the field; The wild asses quench their thirst.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** By them the birds of the heavens have their habitation; They sing among the branches.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He watereth the mountains from his chambers: The earth is filled with the fruit of thy works.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He causeth the grass to grow for the cattle, And herb for the service of man; That he may bring forth food out of the earth,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And wine that maketh glad the heart of man, Andoil to make his face to shine, And bread that strengtheneth man’s heart.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The trees of Jehovah are filledwith moisture, The cedars of Lebanon, which he hath planted;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Where the birds make their nests: As for the stork, the fir-trees are her house.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The high mountains are for the wild goats; The rocks are a refuge for the conies.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He appointed the moon for seasons: The sun knoweth his going down.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Thou makest darkness, and it is night, Wherein all the beasts of the forest creep forth.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The young lions roar after their prey, And seek their food from God.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The sun ariseth, they get them away, And lay them down in their dens.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Man goeth forth unto his work And to his labor until the evening.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** O Jehovah, how manifold are thy works! In wisdom hast thou made them all: The earth is full of thy riches.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Yonder is the sea, great and wide, Wherein are things creeping innumerable, Both small and great beasts.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** There go the ships; There is leviathan, whom thou hast formed to play therein.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** These wait all for thee, That thou mayest give them their food in due season.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Thou givest unto them, they gather; Thou openest thy hand, they are satisfied with good.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Thou hidest thy face, they are troubled; Thou takest away their breath, they die, And return to their dust.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Thou sendest forth thy Spirit, they are created; And thou renewest the face of the ground.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Let the glory of Jehovah endure for ever; Let Jehovah rejoice in his works:

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Who looketh on the earth, and it trembleth; He toucheth the mountains, and they smoke.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** I will sing unto Jehovah as long as I live: I will sing praise to my God while I have any being.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Let my meditation be sweet unto him: I will rejoice in Jehovah.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Let sinners be consumed out of the earth. And let the wicked be no more. Bless Jehovah, O my soul. Praise ye Jehovah.
