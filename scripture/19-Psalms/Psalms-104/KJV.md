# Psalms 104 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Bless the Lord, O my soul. O Lord my God, thou art very great; thou art clothed with honour and majesty.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Who coverest thyself with light as with a garment: who stretchest out the heavens like a curtain:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Who layeth the beams of his chambers in the waters: who maketh the clouds his chariot: who walketh upon the wings of the wind:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Who maketh his angels spirits; his ministers a flaming fire:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Who laid the foundations of the earth, that it should not be removed for ever.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Thou coveredst it with the deep as with a garment: the waters stood above the mountains.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** At thy rebuke they fled; at the voice of thy thunder they hasted away.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They go up by the mountains; they go down by the valleys unto the place which thou hast founded for them.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Thou hast set a bound that they may not pass over; that they turn not again to cover the earth.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He sendeth the springs into the valleys, which run among the hills.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** They give drink to every beast of the field: the wild asses quench their thirst.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** By them shall the fowls of the heaven have their habitation, which sing among the branches.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He watereth the hills from his chambers: the earth is satisfied with the fruit of thy works.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He causeth the grass to grow for the cattle, and herb for the service of man: that he may bring forth food out of the earth;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And wine that maketh glad the heart of man, and oil to make his face to shine, and bread which strengtheneth man’s heart.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The trees of the Lord are full of sap; the cedars of Lebanon, which he hath planted;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Where the birds make their nests: as for the stork, the fir trees are her house.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The high hills are a refuge for the wild goats; and the rocks for the conies.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He appointed the moon for seasons: the sun knoweth his going down.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Thou makest darkness, and it is night: wherein all the beasts of the forest do creep forth.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The young lions roar after their prey, and seek their meat from God.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The sun ariseth, they gather themselves together, and lay them down in their dens.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Man goeth forth unto his work and to his labour until the evening.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** O Lord, how manifold are thy works! in wisdom hast thou made them all: the earth is full of thy riches.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** So is this great and wide sea, wherein are things creeping innumerable, both small and great beasts.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** There go the ships: there is that leviathan, whom thou hast made to play therein.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** These wait all upon thee; that thou mayest give them their meat in due season.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** That thou givest them they gather: thou openest thine hand, they are filled with good.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Thou hidest thy face, they are troubled: thou takest away their breath, they die, and return to their dust.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Thou sendest forth thy spirit, they are created: and thou renewest the face of the earth.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The glory of the Lord shall endure for ever: the Lord shall rejoice in his works.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He looketh on the earth, and it trembleth: he toucheth the hills, and they smoke.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** I will sing unto the Lord as long as I live: I will sing praise to my God while I have my being.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** My meditation of him shall be sweet: I will be glad in the Lord.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Let the sinners be consumed out of the earth, and let the wicked be no more. Bless thou the Lord, O my soul. Praise ye the Lord.
