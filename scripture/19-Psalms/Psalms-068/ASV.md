# Psalms 68 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Let God arise, let his enemies be scattered; Let them also that hate him flee before him.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As smoke is driven away, so drive them away: As wax melteth before the fire, So let the wicked perish at the presence of God.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But let the righteous be glad; let them exult before God: Yea, let them rejoice with gladness.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Sing unto God, sing praises to his name: Cast up a highway for him that rideth through the deserts; His name is Jehovah; and exult ye before him.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A father of the fatherless, and a judge of the widows, Is God in his holy habitation.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** God setteth the solitary in families: He bringeth out the prisoners into prosperity; But the rebellious dwell in a parched land.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** O God, when thou wentest forth before thy people, When thou didst march through the wilderness; [Selah

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The earth trembled, The heavens also dropped rain at the presence of God: Yon Sinai trembled at the presence of God, the God of Israel.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Thou, O God, didst send a plentiful rain, Thou didst confirm thine inheritance, when it was weary.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Thy congregation dwelt therein: Thou, O God, didst prepare of thy goodness for the poor.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The Lord giveth the word: The women that publish the tidings are a great host.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Kings of armies flee, they flee; And she that tarrieth at home divideth the spoil.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** When ye lie among the sheepfolds, It is asthe wings of a dove covered with silver, And her pinions with yellow gold.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** When the Almighty scattered kings therein, It was as whenit snoweth in Zalmon.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** A mountain of God is the mountain of Bashan; A high mountain is the mountain of Bashan.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Why look ye askance, ye high mountains, At the mountain which God hath desired for his abode? Yea, Jehovah will dwell in it for ever.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The chariots of God are twenty thousand, even thousands upon thousands: The Lord is among them, as in Sinai, in the sanctuary.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Thou hast ascended on high, thou hast led away captives; Thou hast received gifts among men, Yea, among the rebellious also, that Jehovah God might dwell with them.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Blessed be the Lord, who daily beareth our burden, Even the God who is our salvation. [Selah

<a id="v20"></a>**20** God is unto us a God of deliverances; And unto Jehovah the Lord belongeth escape from death.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** But God will smite through the head of his enemies, The hairy scalp of such a one as goeth on still in his guiltiness.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The Lord said, I will bring again from Bashan, I will bringthem again from the depths of the sea;

<a id="v23"></a>**23** That thou mayest crush them, dipping thy foot in blood, That the tongue of thy dogs may have its portion from thine enemies.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** They have seen thy goings, O God, Even the goings of my God, my King, into the sanctuary.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The singers went before, the minstrels followed after, In the midst of the damsels playing with timbrels.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Bless ye God in the congregations, Even the Lord, ye that are of the fountain of Israel.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** There is little Benjamin their ruler, The princes of Judah and their council, The princes of Zebulun, the princes of Naphtali.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Thy God hath commanded thy strength: Strengthen, O God, that which thou hast wrought for us.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Because of thy temple at Jerusalem Kings shall bring presents unto thee.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Rebuke the wild beast of the reeds, The multitude of the bulls, with the calves of the peoples, Trampling under foot the pieces of silver: He hath scattered the peoples that delight in war.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Princes shall come out of Egypt; Ethiopia shall haste to stretch out her hands unto God.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Sing unto God, ye kingdoms of the earth; Oh sing praises unto the Lord; [Selah

<a id="v33"></a>**33** To him that rideth upon the heaven of heavens, which are of old; Lo, he uttereth his voice, a mighty voice.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Ascribe ye strength unto God: His excellency is over Israel, And his strength is in the skies.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** O God, thou art terrible out of thy holy places: The God of Israel, he giveth strength and power unto his people. Blessed be God.
