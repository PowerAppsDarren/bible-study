# Psalms 68 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Let God arise, let his enemies be scattered: let them also that hate him flee before him.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As smoke is driven away, so drive them away: as wax melteth before the fire, so let the wicked perish at the presence of God.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But let the righteous be glad; let them rejoice before God: yea, let them exceedingly rejoice.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Sing unto God, sing praises to his name: extol him that rideth upon the heavens by his name Jah, and rejoice before him.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** A father of the fatherless, and a judge of the widows, is God in his holy habitation.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** God setteth the solitary in families: he bringeth out those which are bound with chains: but the rebellious dwell in a dry land.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** O God, when thou wentest forth before thy people, when thou didst march through the wilderness; Selah:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The earth shook, the heavens also dropped at the presence of God: even Sinai itself was moved at the presence of God, the God of Israel.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Thou, O God, didst send a plentiful rain, whereby thou didst confirm thine inheritance, when it was weary.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Thy congregation hath dwelt therein: thou, O God, hast prepared of thy goodness for the poor.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The Lord gave the word: great was the company of those that published it.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Kings of armies did flee apace: and she that tarried at home divided the spoil.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Though ye have lien among the pots, yet shall ye be as the wings of a dove covered with silver, and her feathers with yellow gold.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** When the Almighty scattered kings in it, it was white as snow in Salmon.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The hill of God is as the hill of Bashan; an high hill as the hill of Bashan.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Why leap ye, ye high hills? this is the hill which God desireth to dwell in; yea, the Lord will dwell in it for ever.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The chariots of God are twenty thousand, even thousands of angels: the Lord is among them, as in Sinai, in the holy place.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Thou hast ascended on high, thou hast led captivity captive: thou hast received gifts for men; yea, for the rebellious also, that the Lord God might dwell among them.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Blessed be the Lord, who daily loadeth us with benefits, even the God of our salvation. Selah.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He that is our God is the God of salvation; and unto God the Lord belong the issues from death.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** But God shall wound the head of his enemies, and the hairy scalp of such an one as goeth on still in his trespasses.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The Lord said, I will bring again from Bashan, I will bring my people again from the depths of the sea:

<a id="v23"></a>**23** That thy foot may be dipped in the blood of thine enemies, and the tongue of thy dogs in the same.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** They have seen thy goings, O God; even the goings of my God, my King, in the sanctuary.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The singers went before, the players on instruments followed after; among them were the damsels playing with timbrels.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Bless ye God in the congregations, even the Lord, from the fountain of Israel.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** There is little Benjamin with their ruler, the princes of Judah and their council, the princes of Zebulun, and the princes of Naphtali.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Thy God hath commanded thy strength: strengthen, O God, that which thou hast wrought for us.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Because of thy temple at Jerusalem shall kings bring presents unto thee.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Rebuke the company of spearmen, the multitude of the bulls, with the calves of the people, till every one submit himself with pieces of silver: scatter thou the people that delight in war.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Princes shall come out of Egypt; Ethiopia shall soon stretch out her hands unto God.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Sing unto God, ye kingdoms of the earth; O sing praises unto the Lord; Selah:

<a id="v33"></a>**33** To him that rideth upon the heavens of heavens, which were of old; lo, he doth send out his voice, and that a mighty voice.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Ascribe ye strength unto God: his excellency is over Israel, and his strength is in the clouds.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** O God, thou art terrible out of thy holy places: the God of Israel is he that giveth strength and power unto his people. Blessed be God.
