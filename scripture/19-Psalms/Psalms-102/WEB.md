# Psalms 102 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Hear my prayer, Yahweh! Let my cry come to you.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Don’t hide your face from me in the day of my distress. Turn your ear to me. Answer me quickly in the day when I call.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For my days consume away like smoke. My bones are burned as a torch.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** My heart is blighted like grass, and withered, for I forget to eat my bread.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** By reason of the voice of my groaning, my bones stick to my skin.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** I am like a pelican of the wilderness. I have become as an owl of the waste places.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** I watch, and have become like a sparrow that is alone on the housetop.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** My enemies reproach me all day. Those who are mad at me use my name as a curse.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For I have eaten ashes like bread, and mixed my drink with tears,

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Because of your indignation and your wrath, for you have taken me up, and thrown me away.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** My days are like a long shadow. I have withered like grass.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** But you, Yahweh, will remain forever; your renown endures to all generations.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** You will arise and have mercy on Zion; for it is time to have pity on her. Yes, the set time has come.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For your servants take pleasure in her stones, and have pity on her dust.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** So the nations will fear Yahweh’s name; all the kings of the earth your glory.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For Yahweh has built up Zion. He has appeared in his glory.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He has responded to the prayer of the destitute, and has not despised their prayer.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** This will be written for the generation to come. A people which will be created will praise Yah.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For he has looked down from the height of his sanctuary. From heaven, Yahweh saw the earth;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** to hear the groans of the prisoner; to free those who are condemned to death;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** that men may declare Yahweh’s name in Zion, and his praise in Jerusalem;

<a id="v22"></a>**22** when the peoples are gathered together, the kingdoms, to serve Yahweh.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He weakened my strength along the course. He shortened my days.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** I said, “My God, don’t take me away in the midst of my days. Your years are throughout all generations.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Of old, you laid the foundation of the earth. The heavens are the work of your hands.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** They will perish, but you will endure. Yes, all of them will wear out like a garment. You will change them like a cloak, and they will be changed.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** But you are the same. Your years will have no end.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The children of your servants will continue. Their seed will be established before you.”
