# Psalms 81 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Sing aloud unto God our strength: make a joyful noise unto the God of Jacob.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Take a psalm, and bring hither the timbrel, the pleasant harp with the psaltery.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Blow up the trumpet in the new moon, in the time appointed, on our solemn feast day.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For this was a statute for Israel, and a law of the God of Jacob.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** This he ordained in Joseph for a testimony, when he went out through the land of Egypt: where I heard a language that I understood not.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** I removed his shoulder from the burden: his hands were delivered from the pots.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Thou calledst in trouble, and I delivered thee; I answered thee in the secret place of thunder: I proved thee at the waters of Meribah. Selah.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Hear, O my people, and I will testify unto thee: O Israel, if thou wilt hearken unto me;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** There shall no strange god be in thee; neither shalt thou worship any strange god.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** I am the Lord thy God, which brought thee out of the land of Egypt: open thy mouth wide, and I will fill it.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** But my people would not hearken to my voice; and Israel would none of me.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** So I gave them up unto their own hearts’ lust: and they walked in their own counsels.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Oh that my people had hearkened unto me, and Israel had walked in my ways!

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I should soon have subdued their enemies, and turned my hand against their adversaries.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The haters of the Lord should have submitted themselves unto him: but their time should have endured for ever.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He should have fed them also with the finest of the wheat: and with honey out of the rock should I have satisfied thee.
