# Psalms 81 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Sing aloud to God, our strength! Make a joyful shout to the God of Jacob!

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Raise a song, and bring here the tambourine, the pleasant lyre with the harp.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Blow the trumpet at the New Moon, at the full moon, on our feast day.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For it is a statute for Israel, an ordinance of the God of Jacob.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** He appointed it in Joseph for a testimony, when he went out over the land of Egypt, I heard a language that I didn’t know.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** “I removed his shoulder from the burden. His hands were freed from the basket.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** You called in trouble, and I delivered you. I answered you in the secret place of thunder. I tested you at the waters of Meribah.” Selah.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** “Hear, my people, and I will testify to you, Israel, if you would listen to me!

<a id="v9"></a>**9** There shall be no strange god in you, neither shall you worship any foreign god.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** I am Yahweh, your God, who brought you up out of the land of Egypt. Open your mouth wide, and I will fill it.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** But my people didn’t listen to my voice. Israel desired none of me.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** So I let them go after the stubbornness of their hearts, that they might walk in their own counsels.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Oh that my people would listen to me, that Israel would walk in my ways!

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I would soon subdue their enemies, and turn my hand against their adversaries.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The haters of Yahweh would cringe before him, and their punishment would last forever.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But he would have also fed them with the finest of the wheat. I will satisfy you with honey out of the rock.”
