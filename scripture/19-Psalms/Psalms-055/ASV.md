# Psalms 55 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Give ear to my prayer, O God; And hide not thyself from my supplication.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Attend unto me, and answer me: I am restless in my complaint, and moan,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Because of the voice of the enemy, Because of the oppression of the wicked; For they cast iniquity upon me, And in anger they persecute me.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** My heart is sore pained within me: And the terrors of death are fallen upon me.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Fearfulness and trembling are come upon me, And horror hath overwhelmed me.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And I said, Oh that I had wings like a dove! Then would I fly away, and be at rest.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Lo, then would I wander far off, I would lodge in the wilderness. [Selah

<a id="v8"></a>**8** I would haste me to a shelter From the stormy wind and tempest.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Destroy, O Lord, and divide their tongue; For I have seen violence and strife in the city.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Day and night they go about it upon the walls thereof: Iniquity also and mischief are in the midst of it.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Wickedness is in the midst thereof: Oppression and guile depart not from its streets.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For it was not an enemy that reproached me; Then I could have borne it: Neither was it he that hated me that did magnify himself against me; Then I would have hid myself from him:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But it was thou, a man mine equal, My companion, and my familiar friend.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** We took sweet counsel together; We walked in the house of God with the throng.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Let death come suddenly upon them, Let them go down alive into Sheol; For wickedness is in their dwelling, in the midst of them.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** As for me, I will call upon God; And Jehovah will save me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Evening, and morning, and at noonday, will I complain, and moan; And he will hear my voice.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He hath redeemed my soul in peace from the battle that was against me; For they were many that strove with me.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** God will hear, and answer them, Even he that abideth of old, [Selah The menwho have no changes, And who fear not God.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He hath put forth his hands against such as were at peace with him: He hath profaned his covenant.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His mouth was smooth as butter, But his heart was war: His words were softer than oil, Yet were they drawn swords.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Cast thy burden upon Jehovah, and he will sustain thee: He will never suffer the righteous to be moved.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But thou, O God, wilt bring them down into the pit of destruction: Bloodthirsty and deceitful men shall not live out half their days; But I will trust in thee.
