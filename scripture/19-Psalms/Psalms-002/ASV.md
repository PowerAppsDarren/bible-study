# Psalms 2 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Why do the nations rage, And the peoples meditate a vain thing?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The kings of the earth set themselves, And the rulers take counsel together, Against Jehovah, and against his anointed, saying,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Let us break their bonds asunder, And cast away their cords from us.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** He that sitteth in the heavens will laugh: The Lord will have them in derision.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Then will he speak unto them in his wrath, And vex them in his sore displeasure:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Yet I have set my king  Upon my holy hill of Zion.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** I will tell of the decree: Jehovah said unto me, Thou art my son; This day have I begotten thee.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Ask of me, and I will givetheethe nations for thine inheritance, And the uttermost parts of the earth for thy possession.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Thou shalt break them with a rod of iron; Thou shalt dash them in pieces like a potter’s vessel.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Now therefore be wise, O ye kings: Be instructed, ye judges of the earth.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Serve Jehovah with fear, And rejoice with trembling.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Kiss the son, lest he be angry, and ye perish in the way, For his wrath will soon be kindled. Blessed are all they that take refuge in him.
