# Psalms 41 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Blessed is he who considers the poor. Yahweh will deliver him in the day of evil.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Yahweh will preserve him, and keep him alive. He shall be blessed on the earth, and he will not surrender him to the will of his enemies.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Yahweh will sustain him on his sickbed, and restore him from his bed of illness.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** I said, “Yahweh, have mercy on me! Heal me, for I have sinned against you.”

<a id="v5"></a>**5** My enemies speak evil against me: “When will he die, and his name perish?”

<a id="v6"></a>**6** If he comes to see me, he speaks falsehood. His heart gathers iniquity to itself. When he goes abroad, he tells it.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** All who hate me whisper together against me. They imagine the worst for me.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** “An evil disease”, they say, “has afflicted him. Now that he lies he shall rise up no more.”

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Yes, my own familiar friend, in whom I trusted, who ate bread with me, has lifted up his heel against me.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** But you, Yahweh, have mercy on me, and raise me up, that I may repay them.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** By this I know that you delight in me, because my enemy doesn’t triumph over me.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** As for me, you uphold me in my integrity, and set me in your presence forever.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Blessed be Yahweh, the God of Israel, from everlasting and to everlasting! Amen and amen.
