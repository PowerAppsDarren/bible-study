# Psalms 137 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** By the rivers of Babylon, there we sat down, yea, we wept, when we remembered Zion.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** We hanged our harps upon the willows in the midst thereof.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For there they that carried us away captive required of us a song; and they that wasted us required of us mirth, saying, Sing us one of the songs of Zion.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** How shall we sing the Lord’s song in a strange land?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If I forget thee, O Jerusalem, let my right hand forget her cunning.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** If I do not remember thee, let my tongue cleave to the roof of my mouth; if I prefer not Jerusalem above my chief joy.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Remember, O Lord, the children of Edom in the day of Jerusalem; who said, Rase it, rase it, even to the foundation thereof.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** O daughter of Babylon, who art to be destroyed; happy shall he be, that rewardeth thee as thou hast served us.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Happy shall he be, that taketh and dasheth thy little ones against the stones.
