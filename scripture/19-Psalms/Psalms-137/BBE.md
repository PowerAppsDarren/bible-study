# Psalms 137 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** By the rivers of Babylon we were seated, weeping at the memory of Zion,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Hanging our instruments of music on the trees by the waterside.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For there those who had taken us prisoners made request for a song; and those who had taken away all we had gave us orders to be glad, saying, Give us one of the songs of Zion.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** How may we give the Lord's song in a strange land?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If I keep not your memory, O Jerusalem, let not my right hand keep the memory of its art.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** If I let you go out of my thoughts, and if I do not put Jerusalem before my greatest joy, let my tongue be fixed to the roof of my mouth.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** O Lord, keep in mind against the children of Edom the day of Jerusalem; how they said, Let it be uncovered, uncovered even to its base.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** O daughter of Babylon, whose fate is destruction; happy is the man who does to you what you have done to us.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Happy is the man who takes your little ones, crushing them against the rocks.
