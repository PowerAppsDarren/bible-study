# Psalms 137 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** By the rivers of Babylon, There we sat down, yea, we wept, When we remembered Zion.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Upon the willows in the midst thereof We hanged up our harps.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For there they that led us captive required of us songs, And they that wasted us required of us mirth, saying, Sing us one of the songs of Zion.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** How shall we sing Jehovah’s song In a foreign land?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If I forget thee, O Jerusalem, Let my right hand forget her skill.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Let my tongue cleave to the roof of my mouth, If I remember thee not; If I prefer not Jerusalem Above my chief joy.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Remember, O Jehovah, against the children of Edom The day of Jerusalem; Who said, Rase it, rase it, Even to the foundation thereof.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** O daughter of Babylon, that art to be destroyed, Happy shall he be, that rewardeth thee As thou hast served us.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Happy shall he be, that taketh and dasheth thy little ones Against the rock.
