# Psalms 137 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** By the rivers of Babylon, there we sat down. Yes, we wept, when we remembered Zion.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** On the willows in its midst, we hung up our harps.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For there, those who led us captive asked us for songs. Those who tormented us demanded songs of joy: “Sing us one of the songs of Zion!”

<a id="v4"></a>**4** How can we sing Yahweh’s song in a foreign land?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If I forget you, Jerusalem, let my right hand forget its skill.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Let my tongue stick to the roof of my mouth if I don’t remember you; if I don’t prefer Jerusalem above my chief joy.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Remember, Yahweh, against the children of Edom, the day of Jerusalem; who said, “Raze it! Raze it even to its foundation!”

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Daughter of Babylon, doomed to destruction, he will be happy who rewards you, as you have served us.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Happy shall he be, who takes and dashes your little ones against the rock.
