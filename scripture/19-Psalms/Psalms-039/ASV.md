# Psalms 39 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** I said, I will take heed to my ways, That I sin not with my tongue: I will keep my mouth with a bridle, While the wicked is before me.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** I was dumb with silence, I held my peace, even from good; And my sorrow was stirred.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** My heart was hot within me; While I was musing the fire burned; Then spake I with my tongue:

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Jehovah, make me to know mine end, And the measure of my days, what it is; Let me know how frail I am.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Behold, thou hast made my daysas handbreadths; And my life-time is as nothing before thee: Surely every man at his best estate is altogether vanity. [Selah

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Surely every man walketh in a vain show; Surely they are disquieted in vain: He heapeth up riches, and knoweth not who shall gather them.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And now, Lord, what wait I for? My hope is in thee.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Deliver me from all my transgressions: Make me not the reproach of the foolish.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** I was dumb, I opened not my mouth; Because thou didst it.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Remove thy stroke away from me: I am consumed by the blow of thy hand.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** When thou with rebukes dost correct man for iniquity, Thou makest his beauty to consume away like a moth: Surely every man is vanity. [Selah

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Hear my prayer, O Jehovah, and give ear unto my cry; Hold not thy peace at my tears: For I am a stranger with thee, A sojourner, as all my fathers were.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Oh spare me, that I may recover strength, Before I go hence, and be no more.
