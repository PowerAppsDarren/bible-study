# Psalms 39 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** I said, I will take heed to my ways, that I sin not with my tongue: I will keep my mouth with a bridle, while the wicked is before me.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** I was dumb with silence, I held my peace, even from good; and my sorrow was stirred.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** My heart was hot within me, while I was musing the fire burned: then spake I with my tongue,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Lord, make me to know mine end, and the measure of my days, what it is; that I may know how frail I am.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Behold, thou hast made my days as an handbreadth; and mine age is as nothing before thee: verily every man at his best state is altogether vanity. Selah.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Surely every man walketh in a vain shew: surely they are disquieted in vain: he heapeth up riches, and knoweth not who shall gather them.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And now, Lord, what wait I for? my hope is in thee.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Deliver me from all my transgressions: make me not the reproach of the foolish.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** I was dumb, I opened not my mouth; because thou didst it.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Remove thy stroke away from me: I am consumed by the blow of thine hand.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** When thou with rebukes dost correct man for iniquity, thou makest his beauty to consume away like a moth: surely every man is vanity. Selah.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Hear my prayer, O Lord, and give ear unto my cry; hold not thy peace at my tears: for I am a stranger with thee, and a sojourner, as all my fathers were.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** O spare me, that I may recover strength, before I go hence, and be no more.
