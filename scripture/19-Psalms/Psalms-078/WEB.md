# Psalms 78 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Hear my teaching, my people. Turn your ears to the words of my mouth.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** I will open my mouth in a parable. I will utter dark sayings of old,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Which we have heard and known, and our fathers have told us.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** We will not hide them from their children, telling to the generation to come the praises of Yahweh, his strength, and his wondrous works that he has done.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For he established a testimony in Jacob, and appointed a teaching in Israel, which he commanded our fathers, that they should make them known to their children;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** that the generation to come might know, even the children who should be born; who should arise and tell their children,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** that they might set their hope in God, and not forget the works of God, but keep his commandments,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** and might not be as their fathers, a stubborn and rebellious generation, a generation that didn’t make their hearts loyal, whose spirit was not steadfast with God.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The children of Ephraim, being armed and carrying bows, turned back in the day of battle.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** They didn’t keep God’s covenant, and refused to walk in his law.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** They forgot his doings, his wondrous works that he had shown them.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** He did marvelous things in the sight of their fathers, in the land of Egypt, in the field of Zoan.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He split the sea, and caused them to pass through. He made the waters stand as a heap.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** In the daytime he also led them with a cloud, and all night with a light of fire.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He split rocks in the wilderness, and gave them drink abundantly as out of the depths.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He brought streams also out of the rock, and caused waters to run down like rivers.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Yet they still went on to sin against him, to rebel against the Most High in the desert.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** They tempted God in their heart by asking food according to their desire.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Yes, they spoke against God. They said, “Can God prepare a table in the wilderness?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Behold, he struck the rock, so that waters gushed out, and streams overflowed. Can he give bread also? Will he provide flesh for his people?”

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Therefore Yahweh heard, and was angry. A fire was kindled against Jacob, anger also went up against Israel,

<a id="v22"></a>**22** because they didn’t believe in God, and didn’t trust in his salvation.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Yet he commanded the skies above, and opened the doors of heaven.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He rained down manna on them to eat, and gave them food from the sky.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Man ate the bread of angels. He sent them food to the full.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He caused the east wind to blow in the sky. By his power he guided the south wind.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He rained also flesh on them as the dust; winged birds as the sand of the seas.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** He let them fall in the midst of their camp, around their habitations.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** So they ate, and were well filled. He gave them their own desire.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** They didn’t turn from their cravings. Their food was yet in their mouths,

<a id="v31"></a>**31** when the anger of God went up against them, killed some of their fattest, and struck down the young men of Israel.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For all this they still sinned, and didn’t believe in his wondrous works.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Therefore he consumed their days in vanity, and their years in terror.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** When he killed them, then they inquired after him. They returned and sought God earnestly.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** They remembered that God was their rock, the Most High God, their redeemer.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** But they flattered him with their mouth, and lied to him with their tongue.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** For their heart was not right with him, neither were they faithful in his covenant.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** But he, being merciful, forgave iniquity, and didn’t destroy them. Yes, many times he turned his anger away, and didn’t stir up all his wrath.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** He remembered that they were but flesh, a wind that passes away, and doesn’t come again.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** How often they rebelled against him in the wilderness, and grieved him in the desert!

<a id="v41"></a>**41** They turned again and tempted God, and provoked the Holy One of Israel.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** They didn’t remember his hand, nor the day when he redeemed them from the adversary;

<a id="v43"></a>**43** how he set his signs in Egypt, his wonders in the field of Zoan,

<a id="v44"></a>**44** he turned their rivers into blood, and their streams, so that they could not drink.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** He sent among them swarms of flies, which devoured them; and frogs, which destroyed them.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** He gave also their increase to the caterpillar, and their labor to the locust.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** He destroyed their vines with hail, their sycamore fig trees with frost.

<a id="v48"></a>**48** He gave over their livestock also to the hail, and their flocks to hot thunderbolts.

<a id="v49"></a>**49** He threw on them the fierceness of his anger, wrath, indignation, and trouble, and a band of angels of evil.

<a id="v50"></a>**50** He made a path for his anger. He didn’t spare their soul from death, but gave their life over to the pestilence,

<a id="v51"></a>**51** and struck all the firstborn in Egypt, the chief of their strength in the tents of Ham.

<a id="v52"></a>**52** But he led out his own people like sheep, and guided them in the wilderness like a flock.

<a id="v53"></a>**53** He led them safely, so that they weren’t afraid, but the sea overwhelmed their enemies.

<a id="v54"></a>**54** He brought them to the border of his sanctuary, to this mountain, which his right hand had taken.

<a id="v55"></a>**55** He also drove out the nations before them, allotted them for an inheritance by line, and made the tribes of Israel to dwell in their tents.

<a id="v56"></a>**56** Yet they tempted and rebelled against the Most High God, and didn’t keep his testimonies;

<a id="v57"></a>**57** but turned back, and dealt treacherously like their fathers. They were turned aside like a deceitful bow.

<a id="v58"></a>**58** For they provoked him to anger with their high places, and moved him to jealousy with their engraved images.

<a id="v59"></a>**59** When God heard this, he was angry, and greatly abhorred Israel;

<a id="v60"></a>**60** So that he abandoned the tent of Shiloh, the tent which he placed among men;

<a id="v61"></a>**61** and delivered his strength into captivity, his glory into the adversary’s hand.

<a id="v62"></a>**62** He also gave his people over to the sword, and was angry with his inheritance.

<a id="v63"></a>**63** Fire devoured their young men. Their virgins had no wedding song.

<a id="v64"></a>**64** Their priests fell by the sword, and their widows couldn’t weep.

<a id="v65"></a>**65** Then the Lord awakened as one out of sleep, like a mighty man who shouts by reason of wine.

<a id="v66"></a>**66** He struck his adversaries backward. He put them to a perpetual reproach.

<a id="v67"></a>**67** Moreover he rejected the tent of Joseph, and didn’t choose the tribe of Ephraim,

<a id="v68"></a>**68** But chose the tribe of Judah, Mount Zion which he loved.

<a id="v69"></a>**69** He built his sanctuary like the heights, like the earth which he has established forever.

<a id="v70"></a>**70** He also chose David his servant, and took him from the sheepfolds;

<a id="v71"></a>**71** from following the ewes that have their young, he brought him to be the shepherd of Jacob, his people, and Israel, his inheritance.

<a id="v72"></a>**72** So he was their shepherd according to the integrity of his heart, and guided them by the skillfulness of his hands.
