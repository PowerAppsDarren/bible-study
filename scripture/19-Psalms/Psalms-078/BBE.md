# Psalms 78 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Give ear, O my people, to my law; let your ears be bent down to the words of my mouth.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Opening my mouth I will give out a story, even the dark sayings of old times;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Which have come to our hearing and our knowledge, as they were given to us by our fathers.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** We will not keep them secret from our children; we will make clear to the coming generation the praises of the Lord and his strength, and the great works of wonder which he has done.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** He put up a witness in Jacob, and made a law in Israel; which he gave to our fathers so that they might give knowledge of them to their children;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** So that the generation to come might have knowledge of them, even the children of the future, who would give word of them to their children;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** So that they might put their hope in God, and not let God's works go out of their minds, but keep his laws;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And not be like their fathers, a stiff-necked and uncontrolled generation; a generation whose heart was hard, whose spirit was not true to God.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The children of Ephraim, armed with bows, were turned back on the day of the fight.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** They were not ruled by God's word, and they would not go in the way of his law;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** They let his works go out of their memory, and the wonders which he had made them see.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** He did great works before the eyes of their fathers, in the land of Egypt, in the fields of Zoan.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The sea was cut in two so that they might go through; the waters were massed together on this side and on that.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** In the daytime he was guiding them in the cloud, and all through the night with a light of fire.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The rocks of the waste land were broken by his power, and he gave them drink as out of the deep waters.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He made streams come out of the rock; and waters came flowing down like rivers.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And they went on sinning against him even more, turning away from the Most High in the waste land;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Testing God in their hearts, requesting meat for their desire.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** They said bitter words against God, saying, Is God able to make ready a table in the waste land?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** See, the rock was cut open by his power, so that the water came rushing out, and overflowing streams; is he able to give us bread? is he able to get meat for his people?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** So these things came to the Lord's ears, and he was angry; and a fire was lighted against Jacob, and wrath came up against Israel;

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Because they had no faith in God, and no hope in his salvation.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And he gave orders to the clouds on high, and the doors of heaven were open;

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And he sent down manna like rain for their food, and gave them the grain of heaven.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Man took part in the food of strong ones; he sent them meat in full measure.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He sent an east wind from heaven, driving on the south wind by his power.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He sent down meat on them like dust, and feathered birds like the sand of the sea,

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And he let it come down into their resting-place, round about their tents.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** So they had food and were full; for he gave them their desire;

<a id="v30"></a>**30** But they were not turned from their desires; and while the food was still in their mouths,

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The wrath of God came on them, and put to death the fattest of them, and put an end to the young men of Israel.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For all this they went on sinning even more, and had no faith in his great wonders.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** So their days were wasted like a breath, and their years in trouble.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** When he sent death on them, then they made search for him; turning to him and looking for him with care;

<a id="v35"></a>**35** In the memory that God was their Rock, and the Most High God their saviour.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** But their lips were false to him, and their tongues were untrue to him;

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And their hearts were not right with him, and they did not keep their agreement with him.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** But he, being full of pity, has forgiveness for sin, and does not put an end to man: frequently turning back his wrath, and not being violently angry.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** So he kept in mind that they were only flesh; a breath which is quickly gone, and will not come again.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** How frequently did they go against him in the waste land, and give him cause for grief in the dry places!

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Again they put God to the test, and gave pain to the Holy One of Israel.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** They did not keep in mind the work of his hand, or the day when he took them from the power of their haters;

<a id="v43"></a>**43** How he had done his signs in Egypt, and his wonders in the field of Zoan;

<a id="v44"></a>**44** So that their rivers were turned to blood, and they were not able to get drink from their streams.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** He sent different sorts of flies among them, poisoning their flesh; and frogs for their destruction.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** He gave the increase of their fields to worms, the fruits of their industry to the locusts.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** He sent ice for the destruction of their vines; their trees were damaged by the bitter cold.

<a id="v48"></a>**48** Ice was rained down on their cattle; thunderstorms sent destruction among the flocks.

<a id="v49"></a>**49** He sent on them the heat of his wrath, his bitter disgust, letting loose evil angels among them.

<a id="v50"></a>**50** He let his wrath have its way; he did not keep back their soul from death, but gave their life to disease.

<a id="v51"></a>**51** He gave to destruction all the first sons of Egypt; the first-fruits of their strength in the tents of Ham;

<a id="v52"></a>**52** But he took his people out like sheep, guiding them in the waste land like a flock.

<a id="v53"></a>**53** He took them on safely so that they had no fear; but their haters were covered by the sea.

<a id="v54"></a>**54** And he was their guide to his holy land, even to the mountain, which his right hand had made his;

<a id="v55"></a>**55** Driving out nations before them, marking out the line of their heritage, and giving the people of Israel their tents for a resting-place.

<a id="v56"></a>**56** But they were bitter against the Most High God, testing him, and not keeping his laws;

<a id="v57"></a>**57** Their hearts were turned back and untrue like their fathers; they were turned to one side like a twisted bow.

<a id="v58"></a>**58** They made him angry with their high places; moving him to wrath with their images.

<a id="v59"></a>**59** When this came to God's ears he was very angry, and gave up Israel completely;

<a id="v60"></a>**60** So that he went away from the holy place in Shiloh, the tent which he had put among men;

<a id="v61"></a>**61** And he let his strength be taken prisoner, and gave his glory into the hands of his hater.

<a id="v62"></a>**62** He gave his people up to the sword, and was angry with his heritage.

<a id="v63"></a>**63** Their young men were burned in the fire; and their virgins were not praised in the bride-song.

<a id="v64"></a>**64** Their priests were put to death by the sword, and their widows made no weeping for them.

<a id="v65"></a>**65** Then was the Lord like one awaking from sleep, and like a strong man crying out because of wine.

<a id="v66"></a>**66** His haters were turned back by his blows and shamed for ever.

<a id="v67"></a>**67** And he put the tent of Joseph on one side, and took not the tribe of Ephraim;

<a id="v68"></a>**68** But he took the tribe of Judah for himself, and the mountain of Zion, in which he had pleasure.

<a id="v69"></a>**69** And he made his holy place like the high heaven, like the earth which is fixed by him for ever.

<a id="v70"></a>**70** He took David to be his servant, taking him from the place of the flocks;

<a id="v71"></a>**71** From looking after the sheep which were giving milk, he took him to give food to Jacob his people, and to Israel his heritage.

<a id="v72"></a>**72** So he gave them food with an upright heart, guiding them by the wisdom of his hands.
