# Psalms 106 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Praise ye the Lord. O give thanks unto the Lord; for he is good: for his mercy endureth for ever.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Who can utter the mighty acts of the Lord? who can shew forth all his praise?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Blessed are they that keep judgment, and he that doeth righteousness at all times.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Remember me, O Lord, with the favour that thou bearest unto thy people: O visit me with thy salvation;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** That I may see the good of thy chosen, that I may rejoice in the gladness of thy nation, that I may glory with thine inheritance.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** We have sinned with our fathers, we have committed iniquity, we have done wickedly.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Our fathers understood not thy wonders in Egypt; they remembered not the multitude of thy mercies; but provoked him at the sea, even at the Red sea.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Nevertheless he saved them for his name’s sake, that he might make his mighty power to be known.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He rebuked the Red sea also, and it was dried up: so he led them through the depths, as through the wilderness.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And he saved them from the hand of him that hated them, and redeemed them from the hand of the enemy.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And the waters covered their enemies: there was not one of them left.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Then believed they his words; they sang his praise.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They soon forgat his works; they waited not for his counsel:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But lusted exceedingly in the wilderness, and tempted God in the desert.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And he gave them their request; but sent leanness into their soul.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** They envied Moses also in the camp, and Aaron the saint of the Lord.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The earth opened and swallowed up Dathan, and covered the company of Abiram.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And a fire was kindled in their company; the flame burned up the wicked.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** They made a calf in Horeb, and worshipped the molten image.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Thus they changed their glory into the similitude of an ox that eateth grass.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** They forgat God their saviour, which had done great things in Egypt;

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Wondrous works in the land of Ham, and terrible things by the Red sea.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Therefore he said that he would destroy them, had not Moses his chosen stood before him in the breach, to turn away his wrath, lest he should destroy them.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Yea, they despised the pleasant land, they believed not his word:

<a id="v25"></a>**25** But murmured in their tents, and hearkened not unto the voice of the Lord.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Therefore he lifted up his hand against them, to overthrow them in the wilderness:

<a id="v27"></a>**27** To overthrow their seed also among the nations, and to scatter them in the lands.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** They joined themselves also unto Baal–peor, and ate the sacrifices of the dead.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Thus they provoked him to anger with their inventions: and the plague brake in upon them.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Then stood up Phinehas, and executed judgment: and so the plague was stayed.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And that was counted unto him for righteousness unto all generations for evermore.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** They angered him also at the waters of strife, so that it went ill with Moses for their sakes:

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Because they provoked his spirit, so that he spake unadvisedly with his lips.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** They did not destroy the nations, concerning whom the Lord commanded them:

<a id="v35"></a>**35** But were mingled among the heathen, and learned their works.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And they served their idols: which were a snare unto them.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** Yea, they sacrificed their sons and their daughters unto devils,

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And shed innocent blood, even the blood of their sons and of their daughters, whom they sacrificed unto the idols of Canaan: and the land was polluted with blood.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** Thus were they defiled with their own works, and went a whoring with their own inventions.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** Therefore was the wrath of the Lord kindled against his people, insomuch that he abhorred his own inheritance.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** And he gave them into the hand of the heathen; and they that hated them ruled over them.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** Their enemies also oppressed them, and they were brought into subjection under their hand.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** Many times did he deliver them; but they provoked him with their counsel, and were brought low for their iniquity.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** Nevertheless he regarded their affliction, when he heard their cry:

<a id="v45"></a>**45** And he remembered for them his covenant, and repented according to the multitude of his mercies.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** He made them also to be pitied of all those that carried them captives.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** Save us, O Lord our God, and gather us from among the heathen, to give thanks unto thy holy name, and to triumph in thy praise.

<a id="v48"></a>**48** Blessed be the Lord God of Israel from everlasting to everlasting: and let all the people say, Amen. Praise ye the Lord.
