# Psalms 106 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Praise Yahweh! Give thanks to Yahweh, for he is good, for his loving kindness endures forever.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Who can utter the mighty acts of Yahweh, or fully declare all his praise?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Blessed are those who keep justice. Blessed is one who does what is right at all times.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Remember me, Yahweh, with the favor that you show to your people. Visit me with your salvation,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** that I may see the prosperity of your chosen, that I may rejoice in the gladness of your nation, that I may glory with your inheritance.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** We have sinned with our fathers. We have committed iniquity. We have done wickedly.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Our fathers didn’t understand your wonders in Egypt. They didn’t remember the multitude of your loving kindnesses, but were rebellious at the sea, even at the Red Sea.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Nevertheless he saved them for his name’s sake, that he might make his mighty power known.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He rebuked the Red Sea also, and it was dried up; so he led them through the depths, as through a desert.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He saved them from the hand of him who hated them, and redeemed them from the hand of the enemy.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The waters covered their adversaries. There was not one of them left.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Then they believed his words. They sang his praise.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They soon forgot his works. They didn’t wait for his counsel,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** but gave in to craving in the desert, and tested God in the wasteland.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He gave them their request, but sent leanness into their soul.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** They envied Moses also in the camp, and Aaron, Yahweh’s saint.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The earth opened and swallowed up Dathan, and covered the company of Abiram.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** A fire was kindled in their company. The flame burned up the wicked.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** They made a calf in Horeb, and worshiped a molten image.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Thus they exchanged their glory for an image of a bull that eats grass.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** They forgot God, their Savior, who had done great things in Egypt,

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Wondrous works in the land of Ham, and awesome things by the Red Sea.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Therefore he said that he would destroy them, had Moses, his chosen, not stood before him in the breach, to turn away his wrath, so that he wouldn’t destroy them.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Yes, they despised the pleasant land. They didn’t believe his word,

<a id="v25"></a>**25** but murmured in their tents, and didn’t listen to Yahweh’s voice.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Therefore he swore to them that he would overthrow them in the wilderness,

<a id="v27"></a>**27** that he would overthrow their seed among the nations, and scatter them in the lands.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** They joined themselves also to Baal Peor, and ate the sacrifices of the dead.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Thus they provoked him to anger with their deeds. The plague broke in on them.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Then Phinehas stood up, and executed judgment, so the plague was stopped.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** That was credited to him for righteousness, for all generations to come.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** They angered him also at the waters of Meribah, so that Moses was troubled for their sakes;

<a id="v33"></a>**33** because they were rebellious against his spirit, he spoke rashly with his lips.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** They didn’t destroy the peoples, as Yahweh commanded them,

<a id="v35"></a>**35** but mixed themselves with the nations, and learned their works.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** They served their idols, which became a snare to them.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** Yes, they sacrificed their sons and their daughters to demons.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** They shed innocent blood, even the blood of their sons and of their daughters, whom they sacrificed to the idols of Canaan. The land was polluted with blood.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** Thus were they defiled with their works, and prostituted themselves in their deeds.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** Therefore Yahweh burned with anger against his people. He abhorred his inheritance.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** He gave them into the hand of the nations. Those who hated them ruled over them.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** Their enemies also oppressed them. They were brought into subjection under their hand.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** Many times he delivered them, but they were rebellious in their counsel, and were brought low in their iniquity.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** Nevertheless he regarded their distress, when he heard their cry.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** He remembered for them his covenant, and repented according to the multitude of his loving kindnesses.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** He made them also to be pitied by all those who carried them captive.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** Save us, Yahweh, our God, gather us from among the nations, to give thanks to your holy name, to triumph in your praise!

<a id="v48"></a>**48** Blessed be Yahweh, the God of Israel, from everlasting even to everlasting! Let all the people say, “Amen.” Praise Yah!
