# Romans 14 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Him that is weak in the faith receive ye, but not to doubtful disputations.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For one believeth that he may eat all things: another, who is weak, eateth herbs.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Let not him that eateth despise him that eateth not; and let not him which eateth not judge him that eateth: for God hath received him.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Who art thou that judgest another man’s servant? to his own master he standeth or falleth. Yea, he shall be holden up: for God is able to make him stand.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** One man esteemeth one day above another: another esteemeth every day alike. Let every man be fully persuaded in his own mind.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He that regardeth the day, regardeth it unto the Lord; and he that regardeth not the day, to the Lord he doth not regard it. He that eateth, eateth to the Lord, for he giveth God thanks; and he that eateth not, to the Lord he eateth not, and giveth God thanks.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For none of us liveth to himself, and no man dieth to himself.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For whether we live, we live unto the Lord; and whether we die, we die unto the Lord: whether we live therefore, or die, we are the Lord’s.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For to this end Christ both died, and rose, and revived, that he might be Lord both of the dead and living.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** But why dost thou judge thy brother? or why dost thou set at nought thy brother? for we shall all stand before the judgment seat of Christ.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For it is written, As I live, saith the Lord, every knee shall bow to me, and every tongue shall confess to God.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** So then every one of us shall give account of himself to God.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Let us not therefore judge one another any more: but judge this rather, that no man put a stumblingblock or an occasion to fall in his brother’s way.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I know, and am persuaded by the Lord Jesus, that there is nothing unclean of itself: but to him that esteemeth any thing to be unclean, to him it is unclean.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** But if thy brother be grieved with thy meat, now walkest thou not charitably. Destroy not him with thy meat, for whom Christ died.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Let not then your good be evil spoken of:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For the kingdom of God is not meat and drink; but righteousness, and peace, and joy in the Holy Ghost.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For he that in these things serveth Christ is acceptable to God, and approved of men.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Let us therefore follow after the things which make for peace, and things wherewith one may edify another.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** For meat destroy not the work of God. All things indeed are pure; but it is evil for that man who eateth with offence.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** It is good neither to eat flesh, nor to drink wine, nor any thing whereby thy brother stumbleth, or is offended, or is made weak.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Hast thou faith? have it to thyself before God. Happy is he that condemneth not himself in that thing which he alloweth.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And he that doubteth is damned if he eat, because he eateth not of faith: for whatsoever is not of faith is sin.
