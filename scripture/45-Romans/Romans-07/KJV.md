# Romans 7 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Know ye not, brethren, (for I speak to them that know the law,) how that the law hath dominion over a man as long as he liveth?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For the woman which hath an husband is bound by the law to her husband so long as he liveth; but if the husband be dead, she is loosed from the law of her husband.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** So then if, while her husband liveth, she be married to another man, she shall be called an adulteress: but if her husband be dead, she is free from that law; so that she is no adulteress, though she be married to another man.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Wherefore, my brethren, ye also are become dead to the law by the body of Christ; that ye should be married to another, even to him who is raised from the dead, that we should bring forth fruit unto God.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For when we were in the flesh, the motions of sins, which were by the law, did work in our members to bring forth fruit unto death.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But now we are delivered from the law, that being dead wherein we were held; that we should serve in newness of spirit, and not in the oldness of the letter.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** What shall we say then? Is the law sin? God forbid. Nay, I had not known sin, but by the law: for I had not known lust, except the law had said, Thou shalt not covet.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But sin, taking occasion by the commandment, wrought in me all manner of concupiscence. For without the law sin was dead.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For I was alive without the law once: but when the commandment came, sin revived, and I died.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And the commandment, which was ordained to life, I found to be unto death.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For sin, taking occasion by the commandment, deceived me, and by it slew me.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Wherefore the law is holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Was then that which is good made death unto me? God forbid. But sin, that it might appear sin, working death in me by that which is good; that sin by the commandment might become exceeding sinful.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For we know that the law is spiritual: but I am carnal, sold under sin.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** For that which I do I allow not: for what I would, that do I not; but what I hate, that do I.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If then I do that which I would not, I consent unto the law that it is good.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Now then it is no more I that do it, but sin that dwelleth in me.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For I know that in me (that is, in my flesh,) dwelleth no good thing: for to will is present with me; but how to perform that which is good I find not.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For the good that I would I do not: but the evil which I would not, that I do.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Now if I do that I would not, it is no more I that do it, but sin that dwelleth in me.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** I find then a law, that, when I would do good, evil is present with me.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** For I delight in the law of God after the inward man:

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But I see another law in my members, warring against the law of my mind, and bringing me into captivity to the law of sin which is in my members.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** O wretched man that I am! who shall deliver me from the body of this death?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** I thank God through Jesus Christ our Lord. So then with the mind I myself serve the law of God; but with the flesh the law of sin.
