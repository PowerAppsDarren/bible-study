# Romans 2 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Therefore you are without excuse, O man, whoever you are who judge. For in that which you judge another, you condemn yourself. For you who judge practice the same things.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** We know that the judgment of God is according to truth against those who practice such things.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Do you think this, O man who judges those who practice such things, and do the same, that you will escape the judgment of God?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Or do you despise the riches of his goodness, forbearance, and patience, not knowing that the goodness of God leads you to repentance?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But according to your hardness and unrepentant heart you are treasuring up for yourself wrath in the day of wrath, revelation, and of the righteous judgment of God;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** who “will pay back to everyone according to their works:”

<a id="v7"></a>**7** to those who by patience in well-doing seek for glory, honor, and incorruptibility, eternal life;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** but to those who are self-seeking, and don’t obey the truth, but obey unrighteousness, will be wrath and indignation,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** oppression and anguish, on every soul of man who works evil, to the Jew first, and also to the Greek.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** But glory, honor, and peace go to every man who works good, to the Jew first, and also to the Greek.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For there is no partiality with God.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For as many as have sinned without law will also perish without the law. As many as have sinned under the law will be judged by the law.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For it isn’t the hearers of the law who are righteous before God, but the doers of the law will be justified

<a id="v14"></a>**14** (for when Gentiles who don’t have the law do by nature the things of the law, these, not having the law, are a law to themselves,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** in that they show the work of the law written in their hearts, their conscience testifying with them, and their thoughts among themselves accusing or else excusing them)

<a id="v16"></a>**16** in the day when God will judge the secrets of men, according to my Good News, by Jesus Christ.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Indeed you bear the name of a Jew, and rest on the law, and glory in God,

<a id="v18"></a>**18** and know his will, and approve the things that are excellent, being instructed out of the law,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** and are confident that you yourself are a guide of the blind, a light to those who are in darkness,

<a id="v20"></a>**20** a corrector of the foolish, a teacher of babies, having in the law the form of knowledge and of the truth.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** You therefore who teach another, don’t you teach yourself? You who preach that a man shouldn’t steal, do you steal?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** You who say a man shouldn’t commit adultery. Do you commit adultery? You who abhor idols, do you rob temples?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** You who glory in the law, through your disobedience of the law do you dishonor God?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For “the name of God is blasphemed among the Gentiles because of you,”  just as it is written.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For circumcision indeed profits, if you are a doer of the law, but if you are a transgressor of the law, your circumcision has become uncircumcision.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** If therefore the uncircumcised keep the ordinances of the law, won’t his uncircumcision be accounted as circumcision?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Won’t the uncircumcision which is by nature, if it fulfills the law, judge you, who with the letter and circumcision are a transgressor of the law?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** For he is not a Jew who is one outwardly, neither is that circumcision which is outward in the flesh;

<a id="v29"></a>**29** but he is a Jew who is one inwardly, and circumcision is that of the heart, in the spirit not in the letter; whose praise is not from men, but from God.
