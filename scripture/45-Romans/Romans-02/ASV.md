# Romans 2 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wherefore thou art without excuse, O man, whosoever thou art that judgest: for wherein thou judgest another, thou condemnest thyself; for thou that judgest dost practise the same things.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And we know that the judgment of God is according to truth against them that practise such things.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And reckonest thou this, O man, who judgest them that practise such things, and doest the same, that thou shalt escape the judgment of God?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Or despisest thou the riches of his goodness and forbearance and longsuffering, not knowing that the goodness of God leadeth thee to repentance?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** but after thy hardness and impenitent heart treasurest up for thyself wrath in the day of wrath and revelation of the righteous judgment of God;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** who will render to every man according to his works:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** to them that by patience in well-doing seek for glory and honor and incorruption, eternal life:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** but unto them that are factious, and obey not the truth, but obey unrighteousness, shall be wrath and indignation,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** tribulation and anguish, upon every soul of man that worketh evil, of the Jew first, and also of the Greek;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** but glory and honor and peace to every man that worketh good, to the Jew first, and also to the Greek:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** for there is no respect of persons with God.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For as many as have sinned without the law shall also perish without the law: and as many as have sinned under the law shall be judged by the law;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** for not the hearers of the law are just before God, but the doers of the law shall be justified;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** (for when Gentiles that have not the law do by nature the things of the law, these, not having the law, are the law unto themselves;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** in that they show the work of the law written in their hearts, their conscience bearing witness therewith, and their thoughts one with another accusing or else excusing them);

<a id="v16"></a>**16** in the day when God shall judge the secrets of men, according to my gospel, by Jesus Christ.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** But if thou bearest the name of a Jew, and restest upon the law, and gloriest in God,

<a id="v18"></a>**18** and knowest his will, and approvest the things that are excellent, being instructed out of the law,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** and art confident that thou thyself art a guide of the blind, a light of them that are in darkness,

<a id="v20"></a>**20** a corrector of the foolish, a teacher of babes, having in the law the form of knowledge and of the truth;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** thou therefore that teachest another, teachest thou not thyself? thou that preachest a man should not steal, dost thou steal?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** thou that sayest a man should not commit adultery, dost thou commit adultery? thou that abhorrest idols, dost thou rob temples?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** thou who gloriest in the law, through thy transgression of the law dishonorest thou God?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For the name of God is blasphemed among the Gentiles because of you, even as it is written.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For circumcision indeed profiteth, if thou be a doer of the law: but if thou be a transgressor of the law, thy circumcision is become uncircumcision.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** If therefore the uncircumcision keep the ordinances of the law, shall not his uncircumcision be reckoned for circumcision?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** and shall not the uncircumcision which is by nature, if it fulfil the law, judge thee, who with the letter and circumcision art a transgressor of the law?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** For he is not a Jew who is one outwardly; neither is that circumcision which is outward in the flesh:

<a id="v29"></a>**29** but he is a Jew who is one inwardly; and circumcision is that of the heart, in the spirit not in the letter; whose praise is not of men, but of God.
