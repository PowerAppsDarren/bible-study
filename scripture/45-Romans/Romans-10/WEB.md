# Romans 10 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Brothers, my heart’s desire and my prayer to God is for Israel, that they may be saved.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For I testify about them that they have a zeal for God, but not according to knowledge.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For being ignorant of God’s righteousness, and seeking to establish their own righteousness, they didn’t subject themselves to the righteousness of God.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For Christ is the fulfillment of the law for righteousness to everyone who believes.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For Moses writes about the righteousness of the law, “The one who does them will live by them.”

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But the righteousness which is of faith says this, “Don’t say in your heart, ‘Who will ascend into heaven?’ (that is, to bring Christ down);

<a id="v7"></a>**7** or, ‘Who will descend into the abyss?’ (that is, to bring Christ up from the dead.)”

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But what does it say? “The word is near you, in your mouth, and in your heart”; that is, the word of faith, which we preach:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** that if you will confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord, and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** For with the heart, one believes unto righteousness; and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For the Scripture says, “Whoever believes in him will not be disappointed.”

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For there is no distinction between Jew and Greek; for the same Lord is Lord of all, and is rich to all who call on him.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For, “Whoever will call on the name of the Lord will be saved.”

<a id="v14"></a>**14** How then will they call on him in whom they have not believed? How will they believe in him whom they have not heard? How will they hear without a preacher?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And how will they preach unless they are sent? As it is written:  “How beautiful are the feet of those who preach the Good News of peace, who bring glad tidings of good things!”

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But they didn’t all listen to the glad news. For Isaiah says, “Lord, who has believed our report?”

<a id="v17"></a>**17** So faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But I say, didn’t they hear? Yes, most certainly,  “Their sound went out into all the earth, their words to the ends of the world.”

<a id="v19"></a>**19** But I ask, didn’t Israel know? First Moses says,  “I will provoke you to jealousy with that which is no nation, with a nation void of understanding I will make you angry.”

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Isaiah is very bold, and says,  “I was found by those who didn’t seek me. I was revealed to those who didn’t ask for me.”

<a id="v21"></a>**21** But as to Israel he says, “All day long I stretched out my hands to a disobedient and contrary people.”
