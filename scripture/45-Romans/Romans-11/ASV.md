# Romans 11 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** I say then, Did God cast off his people? God forbid. For I also am an Israelite, of the seed of Abraham, of the tribe of Benjamin.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** God did not cast off his people which he foreknew. Or know ye not what the scripture saith of Elijah? how he pleadeth with God against Israel:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Lord, they have killed thy prophets, they have digged down thine altars; and I am left alone, and they seek my life.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But what saith the answer of God unto him? I have left for myself seven thousand men, who have not bowed the knee to Baal.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Even so then at this present time also there is a remnant according to the election of grace.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** But if it is by grace, it is no more of works: otherwise grace is no more grace.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** What then? That which Israel seeketh for, that he obtained not; but the election obtained it, and the rest were hardened:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** according as it is written, God gave them a spirit of stupor, eyes that they should not see, and ears that they should not hear, unto this very day.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And David saith,  Let their table be made a snare, and a trap, And a stumblingblock, and a recompense unto them:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Let their eyes be darkened, that they may not see, And bow thou down their back always.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I say then, Did they stumble that they might fall? God forbid: but by their fall salvation is come unto the Gentiles, to provoke them to jealousy.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now if their fall is the riches of the world, and their loss the riches of the Gentiles; how much more their fulness?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But I speak to you that are Gentiles. Inasmuch then as I am an apostle of Gentiles, I glorify my ministry;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** if by any means I may provoke to jealousy them that are my flesh, and may save some of them.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** For if the casting away of them is the reconciling of the world, what shall the receiving of them be, but life from the dead?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And if the firstfruit is holy, so is the lump: and if the root is holy, so are the branches.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** But if some of the branches were broken off, and thou, being a wild olive, wast grafted in among them, and didst become partaker with them of the root of the fatness of the olive tree;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** glory not over the branches: but if thou gloriest, it is not thou that bearest the root, but the root thee.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Thou wilt say then, Branches were broken off, that I might be grafted in.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Well; by their unbelief they were broken off, and thou standest by thy faith. Be not highminded, but fear:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** for if God spared not the natural branches, neither will he spare thee.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Behold then the goodness and severity of God: toward them that fell, severity; but toward thee, God’s goodness, if thou continue in his goodness: otherwise thou also shalt be cut off.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And they also, if they continue not in their unbelief, shall be grafted in: for God is able to graft them in again.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For if thou wast cut out of that which is by nature a wild olive tree, and wast grafted contrary to nature into a good olive tree; how much more shall these, which are the natural branches, be grafted into their own olive tree?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For I would not, brethren, have you ignorant of this mystery, lest ye be wise in your own conceits, that a hardening in part hath befallen Israel, until the fulness of the Gentiles be come in;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** and so all Israel shall be saved: even as it is written,  There shall come out of Zion the Deliverer; He shall turn away ungodliness from Jacob:

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And this is my covenant unto them, When I shall take away their sins.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** As touching the gospel, they are enemies for your sake: but as touching the election, they are beloved for the fathers’ sake.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For the gifts and the calling of God are not repented of.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** For as ye in time past were disobedient to God, but now have obtained mercy by their disobedience,

<a id="v31"></a>**31** even so have these also now been disobedient, that by the mercy shown to you they also may now obtain mercy.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For God hath shut up all unto disobedience, that he might have mercy upon all.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and the knowledge of God! how unsearchable are his judgments, and his ways past tracing out!

<a id="v34"></a>**34** For who hath known the mind of the Lord? or who hath been his counsellor?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** or who hath first given to him, and it shall be recompensed unto him again?

<a id="v36"></a>**36** For of him, and through him, and unto him, are all things. To him be the glory for ever. Amen.
