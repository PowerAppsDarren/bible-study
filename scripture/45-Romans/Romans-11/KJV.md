# Romans 11 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** I say then, Hath God cast away his people? God forbid. For I also am an Israelite, of the seed of Abraham, of the tribe of Benjamin.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** God hath not cast away his people which he foreknew. Wot ye not what the scripture saith of Elias? how he maketh intercession to God against Israel, saying,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Lord, they have killed thy prophets, and digged down thine altars; and I am left alone, and they seek my life.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But what saith the answer of God unto him? I have reserved to myself seven thousand men, who have not bowed the knee to the image of Baal.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Even so then at this present time also there is a remnant according to the election of grace.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And if by grace, then is it no more of works: otherwise grace is no more grace. But if it be of works, then is it no more grace: otherwise work is no more work.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** What then? Israel hath not obtained that which he seeketh for; but the election hath obtained it, and the rest were blinded

<a id="v8"></a>**8** (According as it is written, God hath given them the spirit of slumber, eyes that they should not see, and ears that they should not hear;) unto this day.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And David saith, Let their table be made a snare, and a trap, and a stumblingblock, and a recompence unto them:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Let their eyes be darkened, that they may not see, and bow down their back alway.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I say then, Have they stumbled that they should fall? God forbid: but rather through their fall salvation is come unto the Gentiles, for to provoke them to jealousy.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now if the fall of them be the riches of the world, and the diminishing of them the riches of the Gentiles; how much more their fulness?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For I speak to you Gentiles, inasmuch as I am the apostle of the Gentiles, I magnify mine office:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If by any means I may provoke to emulation them which are my flesh, and might save some of them.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** For if the casting away of them be the reconciling of the world, what shall the receiving of them be, but life from the dead?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For if the firstfruit be holy, the lump is also holy: and if the root be holy, so are the branches.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And if some of the branches be broken off, and thou, being a wild olive tree, wert graffed in among them, and with them partakest of the root and fatness of the olive tree;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Boast not against the branches. But if thou boast, thou bearest not the root, but the root thee.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Thou wilt say then, The branches were broken off, that I might be graffed in.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Well; because of unbelief they were broken off, and thou standest by faith. Be not highminded, but fear:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For if God spared not the natural branches, take heed lest he also spare not thee.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Behold therefore the goodness and severity of God: on them which fell, severity; but toward thee, goodness, if thou continue in his goodness: otherwise thou also shalt be cut off.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And they also, if they abide not still in unbelief, shall be graffed in: for God is able to graff them in again.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For if thou wert cut out of the olive tree which is wild by nature, and wert graffed contrary to nature into a good olive tree: how much more shall these, which be the natural branches, be graffed into their own olive tree?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For I would not, brethren, that ye should be ignorant of this mystery, lest ye should be wise in your own conceits; that blindness in part is happened to Israel, until the fulness of the Gentiles be come in.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And so all Israel shall be saved: as it is written, There shall come out of Sion the Deliverer, and shall turn away ungodliness from Jacob:

<a id="v27"></a>**27** For this is my covenant unto them, when I shall take away their sins.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** As concerning the gospel, they are enemies for your sakes: but as touching the election, they are beloved for the fathers’ sakes.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For the gifts and calling of God are without repentance.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** For as ye in times past have not believed God, yet have now obtained mercy through their unbelief:

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Even so have these also now not believed, that through your mercy they also may obtain mercy.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For God hath concluded them all in unbelief, that he might have mercy upon all.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God! how unsearchable are his judgments, and his ways past finding out!

<a id="v34"></a>**34** For who hath known the mind of the Lord? or who hath been his counsellor?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Or who hath first given to him, and it shall be recompensed unto him again?

<a id="v36"></a>**36** For of him, and through him, and to him, are all things: to whom be glory for ever. Amen.
