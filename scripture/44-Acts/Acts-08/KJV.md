# Acts 8 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Saul was consenting unto his death. And at that time there was a great persecution against the church which was at Jerusalem; and they were all scattered abroad throughout the regions of Judea and Samaria, except the apostles.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And devout men carried Stephen to his burial, and made great lamentation over him.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** As for Saul, he made havock of the church, entering into every house, and haling men and women committed them to prison.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Therefore they that were scattered abroad went every where preaching the word.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Then Philip went down to the city of Samaria, and preached Christ unto them.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And the people with one accord gave heed unto those things which Philip spake, hearing and seeing the miracles which he did.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For unclean spirits, crying with loud voice, came out of many that were possessed with them: and many taken with palsies, and that were lame, were healed.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And there was great joy in that city.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But there was a certain man, called Simon, which beforetime in the same city used sorcery, and bewitched the people of Samaria, giving out that himself was some great one:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** To whom they all gave heed, from the least to the greatest, saying, This man is the great power of God.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And to him they had regard, because that of long time he had bewitched them with sorceries.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** But when they believed Philip preaching the things concerning the kingdom of God, and the name of Jesus Christ, they were baptized, both men and women.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Then Simon himself believed also: and when he was baptized, he continued with Philip, and wondered, beholding the miracles and signs which were done.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Now when the apostles which were at Jerusalem heard that Samaria had received the word of God, they sent unto them Peter and John:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Who, when they were come down, prayed for them, that they might receive the Holy Ghost:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** (For as yet he was fallen upon none of them: only they were baptized in the name of the Lord Jesus.)

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Then laid they their hands on them, and they received the Holy Ghost.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And when Simon saw that through laying on of the apostles’ hands the Holy Ghost was given, he offered them money,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Saying, Give me also this power, that on whomsoever I lay hands, he may receive the Holy Ghost.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But Peter said unto him, Thy money perish with thee, because thou hast thought that the gift of God may be purchased with money.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Thou hast neither part nor lot in this matter: for thy heart is not right in the sight of God.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Repent therefore of this thy wickedness, and pray God, if perhaps the thought of thine heart may be forgiven thee.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For I perceive that thou art in the gall of bitterness, and in the bond of iniquity.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Then answered Simon, and said, Pray ye to the Lord for me, that none of these things which ye have spoken come upon me.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And they, when they had testified and preached the word of the Lord, returned to Jerusalem, and preached the gospel in many villages of the Samaritans.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And the angel of the Lord spake unto Philip, saying, Arise, and go toward the south unto the way that goeth down from Jerusalem unto Gaza, which is desert.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And he arose and went: and, behold, a man of Ethiopia, an eunuch of great authority under Candace queen of the Ethiopians, who had the charge of all her treasure, and had come to Jerusalem for to worship,

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Was returning, and sitting in his chariot read Esaias the prophet.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Then the Spirit said unto Philip, Go near, and join thyself to this chariot.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And Philip ran thither to him, and heard him read the prophet Esaias, and said, Understandest thou what thou readest?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And he said, How can I, except some man should guide me? And he desired Philip that he would come up and sit with him.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** The place of the scripture which he read was this, He was led as a sheep to the slaughter; and like a lamb dumb before his shearer, so opened he not his mouth:

<a id="v33"></a>**33** In his humiliation his judgment was taken away: and who shall declare his generation? for his life is taken from the earth.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** And the eunuch answered Philip, and said, I pray thee, of whom speaketh the prophet this? of himself, or of some other man?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Then Philip opened his mouth, and began at the same scripture, and preached unto him Jesus.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And as they went on their way, they came unto a certain water: and the eunuch said, See, here is water; what doth hinder me to be baptized?

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And Philip said, If thou believest with all thine heart, thou mayest. And he answered and said, I believe that Jesus Christ is the Son of God.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And he commanded the chariot to stand still: and they went down both into the water, both Philip and the eunuch; and he baptized him.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And when they were come up out of the water, the Spirit of the Lord caught away Philip, that the eunuch saw him no more: and he went on his way rejoicing.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** But Philip was found at Azotus: and passing through he preached in all the cities, till he came to Cesarea.
