# Acts 8 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Saul gave approval to his death. Now at that time a violent attack was started against the church in Jerusalem; and all but the Apostles went away into all parts of Judaea and Samaria.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And God-fearing men put Stephen's body in its last resting-place, making great weeping over him.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But Saul was burning with hate against the church, going into every house and taking men and women and putting them in prison.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But those who had gone in flight went everywhere preaching the word.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And Philip went down to Samaria and was teaching them about Christ.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And all the people gave attention to the words which Philip said, when they saw the signs which he did.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For unclean spirits came out from those who had them, crying with a loud voice; and a number of those who were ill and broken in body were made well.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And there was much joy in that town.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But there was a certain man named Simon, who in the past had been a wonder-worker and a cause of surprise to the people of Samaria, saying that he himself was a great man:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** To whom they all gave attention, from the smallest to the greatest, saying, This man is that power of God which is named Great.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And they gave attention to him, because for a long time his wonder-working powers had kept them under his control.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** But when they had faith in the good news given by Philip about the kingdom of God and the name of Jesus Christ, a number of men and women had baptism.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And Simon himself had faith and, having had baptism, he went with Philip and, seeing the signs and the great wonders which he did, he was full of surprise.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Now when the Apostles at Jerusalem had news that the people of Samaria had taken the word of God into their hearts, they sent to them Peter and John;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Who, when they came there, made prayer for them, that the Holy Spirit might be given to them:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For up to that time he had not come on any of them; only baptism had been given to them in the name of the Lord Jesus.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Then they put their hands on them, and the Holy Spirit came on them.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Now when Simon saw that the Holy Spirit was given through the touch of the Apostles' hands, he made them an offering of money, saying,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Give me this power, so that when I put my hands on anyone he may get the Holy Spirit.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But Peter said, May your money come to destruction with you, because you had the idea that what is freely given by God may be got for a price.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** You have no part in this business, because your heart is not right before God.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Let your heart be changed, and make prayer to God that you may have forgiveness for your evil thoughts.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For I see that you are prisoned in bitter envy and the chains of sin.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And Simon, answering, said, Make prayer for me to the Lord, so that these things which you have said may not come on me.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** So they, having given their witness and made clear the word of the Lord, went back to Jerusalem, giving the good news on their way in a number of the small towns of Samaria.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** But an angel of the Lord said to Philip, Get up, and go to the south, to the road which goes from Jerusalem to Gaza, through the waste land.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And he went and there was a man of Ethiopia, a servant of great authority under Candace, queen of the Ethiopians, and controller of all her property, who had come up to Jerusalem for worship;

<a id="v28"></a>**28** He was going back, seated in his carriage, and was reading the book of the prophet Isaiah.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And the Spirit said to Philip, Go near, and get on his carriage.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And Philip, running up to him, saw that he was reading Isaiah the prophet, and said to him, Is the sense of what you are reading clear to you?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And he said, How is that possible when I have no guide? And he made Philip get up by his side.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Now the place in the book where he was reading was this: He was taken, like a sheep, to be put to death; and as a lamb is quiet when its wool is being cut, so he made no sound:

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Being of low degree, his cause was not given a hearing: who has knowledge of his family? for his life is cut off from the earth.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** And the Ethiopian said to Philip, About whom are these words said by the prophet? about himself, or some other?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** So Philip, starting from this writing, gave him the good news about Jesus.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And while they were going on their way, they came to some water, and the Ethiopian said, See, here is water; why may I not have baptism?

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And he gave orders for the carriage to be stopped, and the two of them went down into the water, and Philip gave him baptism.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And when they came up out of the water, the Spirit of the Lord took Philip away; and the Ethiopian saw him no more, for he went on his way full of joy.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** But Philip came to Azotus, and went through all the towns, preaching the good news, till he came to Caesarea.
