# Acts 8 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Saul was consenting unto his death. And there arose on that day a great persecution against the church which was in Jerusalem; and they were all scattered abroad throughout the regions of Judæa and Samaria, except the apostles.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And devout men buried Stephen, and made great lamentation over him.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But Saul laid waste the church, entering into every house, and dragging men and women committed them to prison.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** They therefore that were scattered abroad went about preaching the word.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And Philip went down to the city of Samaria, and proclaimed unto them the Christ.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And the multitudes gave heed with one accord unto the things that were spoken by Philip, when they heard, and saw the signs which he did.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For from many of those that had unclean spirits, they came out, crying with a loud voice: and many that were palsied, and that were lame, were healed.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And there was much joy in that city.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But there was a certain man, Simon by name, who beforetime in the city used sorcery, and amazed the people of Samaria, giving out that himself was some great one:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** to whom they all gave heed, from the least to the greatest, saying, This man is that power of God which is called Great.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And they gave heed to him, because that of long time he had amazed them with his sorceries.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** But when they believed Philip preaching good tidings concerning the kingdom of God and the name of Jesus Christ, they were baptized, both men and women.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And Simon also himself believed: and being baptized, he continued with Philip; and beholding signs and great miracles wrought, he was amazed.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Now when the apostles that were at Jerusalem heard that Samaria had received the word of God, they sent unto them Peter and John:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** who, when they were come down, prayed for them, that they might receive the Holy Spirit:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** for as yet it was fallen upon none of them: only they had been baptized into the name of the Lord Jesus.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Then laid they their hands on them, and they received the Holy Spirit.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Now when Simon saw that through the laying on of the apostles’ hands the Holy Spirit was given, he offered them money,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** saying, Give me also this power, that on whomsoever I lay my hands, he may receive the Holy Spirit.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But Peter said unto him, Thy silver perish with thee, because thou hast thought to obtain the gift of God with money.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Thou hast neither part nor lot in this matter: for thy heart is not right before God.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Repent therefore of this thy wickedness, and pray the Lord, if perhaps the thought of thy heart shall be forgiven thee.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For I see that thou art in the gall of bitterness and in the bond of iniquity.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And Simon answered and said, Pray ye for me to the Lord, that none of the things which ye have spoken come upon me.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** They therefore, when they had testified and spoken the word of the Lord, returned to Jerusalem, and preached the gospel to many villages of the Samaritans.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** But an angel of the Lord spake unto Philip, saying, Arise, and go toward the south unto the way that goeth down from Jerusalem unto Gaza: the same is desert.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And he arose and went: and behold, a man of Ethiopia, a eunuch of great authority under Candace, queen of the Ethiopians, who was over all her treasure, who had come to Jerusalem to worship;

<a id="v28"></a>**28** and he was returning and sitting in his chariot, and was reading the prophet Isaiah.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And the Spirit said unto Philip, Go near, and join thyself to this chariot.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And Philip ran to him, and heard him reading Isaiah the prophet, and said, Understandest thou what thou readest?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And he said, How can I, except some one shall guide me? And he besought Philip to come up and sit with him.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Now the passage of the scripture which he was reading was this,  He was led as a sheep to the slaughter; And as a lamb before his shearer is dumb, So he openeth not his mouth:

<a id="v33"></a>**33** In his humiliation his judgment was taken away: His generation who shall declare? For his life is taken from the earth.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** And the eunuch answered Philip, and said, I pray thee, of whom speaketh the prophet this? of himself, or of some other?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And Philip opened his mouth, and beginning from this scripture, preached unto him Jesus.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And as they went on the way, they came unto a certain water; and the eunuch saith, Behold, here is water; what doth hinder me to be baptized?

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And he commanded the chariot to stand still: and they both went down into the water, both Philip and the eunuch; and he baptized him.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And when they came up out of the water, the Spirit of the Lord caught away Philip; and the eunuch saw him no more, for he went on his way rejoicing.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** But Philip was found at Azotus: and passing through he preached the gospel to all the cities, till he came to Cæsarea.
