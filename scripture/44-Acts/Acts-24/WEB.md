# Acts 24 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** After five days, the high priest, Ananias, came down with certain elders and an orator, one Tertullus. They informed the governor against Paul.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** When he was called, Tertullus began to accuse him, saying, “Seeing that by you we enjoy much peace, and that excellent measures are coming to this nation,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** we accept it in all ways and in all places, most excellent Felix, with all thankfulness.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But, that I don’t delay you, I entreat you to bear with us and hear a few words.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For we have found this man to be a plague, an instigator of insurrections among all the Jews throughout the world, and a ringleader of the sect of the Nazarenes.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He even tried to profane the temple, and we arrested him.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** By examining him yourself you may ascertain all these things of which we accuse him.”

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The Jews also joined in the attack, affirming that these things were so.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** When the governor had beckoned to him to speak, Paul answered, “Because I know that you have been a judge of this nation for many years, I cheerfully make my defense,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** seeing that you can recognize that it is not more than twelve days since I went up to worship at Jerusalem.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** In the temple they didn’t find me disputing with anyone or stirring up a crowd, either in the synagogues, or in the city.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Nor can they prove to you the things of which they now accuse me.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But this I confess to you, that after the Way, which they call a sect, so I serve the God of our fathers, believing all things which are according to the law, and which are written in the prophets;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** having hope toward God, which these also themselves look for, that there will be a resurrection of the dead, both of the just and unjust.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Herein I also practice always having a conscience void of offense toward God and men.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Now after some years, I came to bring gifts for the needy to my nation, and offerings;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** amid which certain Jews from Asia found me purified in the temple, not with a mob, nor with turmoil.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** They ought to have been here before you, and to make accusation, if they had anything against me.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Or else let these men themselves say what injustice they found in me when I stood before the council,

<a id="v21"></a>**21** unless it is for this one thing that I cried standing among them, ‘Concerning the resurrection of the dead I am being judged before you today!’”

<a id="v22"></a>**22** But Felix, having more exact knowledge concerning the Way, deferred them, saying, “When Lysias, the commanding officer, comes down, I will decide your case.”

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He ordered the centurion that Paul should be kept in custody, and should have some privileges, and not to forbid any of his friends to serve him or to visit him.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** But after some days, Felix came with Drusilla, his wife, who was a Jewess, and sent for Paul, and heard him concerning the faith in Christ Jesus.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** As he reasoned about righteousness, self-control, and the judgment to come, Felix was terrified, and answered, “Go your way for this time, and when it is convenient for me, I will summon you.”

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Meanwhile, he also hoped that money would be given to him by Paul, that he might release him. Therefore also he sent for him more often, and talked with him.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** But when two years were fulfilled, Felix was succeeded by Porcius Festus, and desiring to gain favor with the Jews, Felix left Paul in bonds.
