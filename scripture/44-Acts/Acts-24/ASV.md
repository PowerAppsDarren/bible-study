# Acts 24 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And after five days the high priest Ananias came down with certain elders, andwithan orator, one Tertullus; and they informed the governor against Paul.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And when he was called, Tertullus began to accuse him, saying,  Seeing that by thee we enjoy much peace, and that by thy providence evils are corrected for this nation,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** we accept it in all ways and in all places, most excellent Felix, with all thankfulness.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But, that I be not further tedious unto thee, I entreat thee to hear us of thy clemency a few words.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For we have found this man a pestilent fellow, and a mover of insurrections among all the Jews throughout the world, and a ringleader of the sect of the Nazarenes:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** who moreover assayed to profane the temple: on whom also we laid hold:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** from whom thou wilt be able, by examining him thyself, to take knowledge of all these things whereof we accuse him.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And the Jews also joined in the charge, affirming that these things were so.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And when the governor had beckoned unto him to speak, Paul answered,  Forasmuch as I know that thou hast been of many years a judge unto this nation, I cheerfully make my defence:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** seeing that thou canst take knowledge that it is not more than twelve days since I went up to worship at Jerusalem:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** and neither in the temple did they find me disputing with any man or stirring up a crowd, nor in the synagogues, nor in the city.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Neither can they prove to thee the things whereof they now accuse me.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But this I confess unto thee, that after the Way which they call a sect, so serve I the God of our fathers, believing all things which are according to the law, and which are written in the prophets;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** having hope toward God, which these also themselves look for, that there shall be a resurrection both of the just and unjust.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Herein I also exercise myself to have a conscience void of offence toward God and men always.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Now after some years I came to bring alms to my nation, and offerings:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** amidst which they found me purified in the temple, with no crowd, nor yet with tumult: but there were certain Jews from Asia—

<a id="v19"></a>**19** who ought to have been here before thee, and to make accusation, if they had aught against me.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Or else let these men themselves say what wrong-doing they found when I stood before the council,

<a id="v21"></a>**21** except it be for this one voice, that I cried standing among them, Touching the resurrection of the dead I am called in question before you this day.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** But Felix, having more exact knowledge concerning the Way, deferred them, saying, When Lysias the chief captain shall come down, I will determine your matter.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And he gave order to the centurion that he should be kept in charge, and should have indulgence; and not to forbid any of his friends to minister unto him.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** But after certain days, Felix came with Drusilla, his wife, who was a Jewess, and sent for Paul, and heard him concerning the faith in Christ Jesus.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And as he reasoned of righteousness, and self-control, and the judgment to come, Felix was terrified, and answered, Go thy way for this time; and when I have a convenient season, I will call thee unto me.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He hoped withal that money would be given him of Paul: wherefore also he sent for him the oftener, and communed with him.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** But when two years were fulfilled, Felix was succeeded by Porcius Festus; and desiring to gain favor with the Jews, Felix left Paul in bonds.
