# Acts 11 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Now the Apostles and the brothers who were in Judaea had news that the word of God had been given to the Gentiles.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And when Peter came to Jerusalem, those who kept the rule of circumcision had an argument with him,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Saying, You went to men without circumcision, and took food with them.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But Peter gave them an account of it all in order, saying to them,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** I was in the town of Joppa, at prayer: and falling into a deep sleep, I saw in a vision a vessel like a great cloth let down from heaven, and it came down to me:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And looking on it with attention I saw in it all sorts of beasts and birds.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And a voice came to my ears saying, Come, Peter; take them for food.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But I said, No, Lord; for nothing common or unclean has ever come into my mouth.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But the voice, coming a second time from heaven, said, What God has made clean, do not you make common.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And this was done three times, and they were all taken up again into heaven.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And at that minute, three men, sent from Caesarea, came to the house where we were.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And the Spirit gave me orders to go with them, doubting nothing. And these six brothers came with me; and we went into that man's house:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And he gave us an account of how he had seen the angel in his house, saying, Send to Joppa, and get Simon, named Peter, to come to you;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Who will say words to you through which you and all your family may get salvation.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And, while I was talking to them, the Holy Spirit came on them, as on us at first.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And the words of the Lord came into my mind, how he said, The baptism of John was with water, but you will have baptism with the Holy Spirit.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** If then God gave them, when they had faith in the Lord Jesus Christ, the same as he gave to us, who was I to go against God?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And hearing these things they said nothing more, but gave glory to God, saying, Then to the Gentiles as to us has God given a change of heart, so that they may have life.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Then those who had gone away at the time of the trouble about Stephen, went as far as Phoenicia and Cyprus, preaching to the Jews only.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But some of them, men of Cyprus and Cyrene, when they came to Antioch, gave the good news about the Lord Jesus to the Greeks.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And the power of the Lord was with them, and a great number had faith and were turned to the Lord.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And news of them came to the ears of the church at Jerusalem: and they sent Barnabas as far as Antioch:

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Who, when he came and saw the grace of God, was glad; and he made clear to them the need of keeping near the Lord with all the strength of their hearts:

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For he was a good man and full of the Holy Spirit and of faith: and a great number were joined to the Lord.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Then he went on to Tarsus, looking for Saul;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And when he had come across him, he took him to Antioch. And they were with the church there for a year, teaching the people; and the disciples were first given the name of Christians in Antioch.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Now in those days prophets came from Jerusalem to Antioch.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And one of them, named Agabus, said publicly through the Spirit that there would be serious need of food all over the earth: which came about in the time of Claudius.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And the disciples, everyone as he was able, made a decision to send help to the brothers living in Judaea:

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Which they did, sending it to the rulers of the church by the hand of Barnabas and Saul.
