# Acts 6 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Now in those days, when the number of the disciples was multiplying, a complaint arose from the Hellenists against the Hebrews, because their widows were neglected in the daily service.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The twelve summoned the multitude of the disciples and said, “It is not appropriate for us to forsake the word of God and serve tables.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Therefore select from among you, brothers, seven men of good report, full of the Holy Spirit and of wisdom, whom we may appoint over this business.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But we will continue steadfastly in prayer and in the ministry of the word.”

<a id="v5"></a>**5** These words pleased the whole multitude. They chose Stephen, a man full of faith and of the Holy Spirit, Philip, Prochorus, Nicanor, Timon, Parmenas, and Nicolaus, a proselyte of Antioch;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** whom they set before the apostles. When they had prayed, they laid their hands on them.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The word of God increased and the number of the disciples multiplied in Jerusalem exceedingly. A great company of the priests were obedient to the faith.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Stephen, full of faith and power, performed great wonders and signs among the people.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But some of those who were of the synagogue called “The Libertines”, and of the Cyrenians, of the Alexandrians, and of those of Cilicia and Asia arose, disputing with Stephen.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** They weren’t able to withstand the wisdom and the Spirit by which he spoke.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Then they secretly induced men to say, “We have heard him speak blasphemous words against Moses and God.”

<a id="v12"></a>**12** They stirred up the people, the elders, and the scribes, and came against him and seized him, and brought him in to the council,

<a id="v13"></a>**13** and set up false witnesses who said, “This man never stops speaking blasphemous words against this holy place and the law.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For we have heard him say that this Jesus of Nazareth will destroy this place, and will change the customs which Moses delivered to us.”

<a id="v15"></a>**15** All who sat in the council, fastening their eyes on him, saw his face like it was the face of an angel.
