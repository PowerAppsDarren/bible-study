# Numbers 23 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Balaam said unto Balak, Build me here seven altars, and prepare me here seven bullocks and seven rams.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And Balak did as Balaam had spoken; and Balak and Balaam offered on every altar a bullock and a ram.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And Balaam said unto Balak, Stand by thy burnt-offering, and I will go: peradventure Jehovah will come to meet me; and whatsoever he showeth me I will tell thee. And he went to a bare height.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And God met Balaam: and he said unto him, I have prepared the seven altars, and I have offered up a bullock and a ram on every altar.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And Jehovah put a word in Balaam’s mouth, and said, Return unto Balak, and thus thou shalt speak.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And he returned unto him, and, lo, he was standing by his burnt-offering, he, and all the princes of Moab.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And he took up his parable, and said,  From Aram hath Balak brought me, The king of Moab from the mountains of the East: Come, curse me Jacob, And come, defy Israel.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** How shall I curse, whom God hath not cursed? And how shall I defy, whom Jehovah hath not defied?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For from the top of the rocks I see him, And from the hills I behold him: Lo, it is a people that dwelleth alone, And shall not be reckoned among the nations.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Who can count the dust of Jacob, Or number the fourth part of Israel? Let me die the death of the righteous, And let my last end be like his!

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And Balak said unto Balaam, What hast thou done unto me? I took thee to curse mine enemies, and, behold, thou hast blessed them altogether.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And he answered and said, Must I not take heed to speak that which Jehovah putteth in my mouth?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And Balak said unto him, Come, I pray thee, with me unto another place, from whence thou mayest see them; thou shalt see but the utmost part of them, and shalt not see them all: and curse me them from thence.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And he took him into the field of Zophim, to the top of Pisgah, and built seven altars, and offered up a bullock and a ram on every altar.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And he said unto Balak, Stand here by thy burnt-offering, while I meet Jehovah yonder.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And Jehovah met Balaam, and put a word in his mouth, and said, Return unto Balak, and thus shalt thou speak.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And he came to him, and, lo, he was standing by his burnt-offering, and the princes of Moab with him. And Balak said unto him, What hath Jehovah spoken?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And he took up his parable, and said,  Rise up, Balak, and hear; Hearken unto me, thou son of Zippor:

<a id="v19"></a>**19** God is not a man, that he should lie, Neither the son of man, that he should repent: Hath he said, and will he not do it? Or hath he spoken, and will he not make it good?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Behold, I have receivedcommandmentto bless: And he hath blessed, and I cannot reverse it.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He hath not beheld iniquity in Jacob; Neither hath he seen perverseness in Israel: Jehovah his God is with him, And the shout of a king is among them.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** God bringeth them forth out of Egypt; He hath as it were the strength of the wild-ox.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Surely there is no enchantment with Jacob; Neither is there any divination with Israel: Now shall it be said of Jacob and of Israel, What hath God wrought!

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Behold, the people riseth up as a lioness, And as a lion doth he lift himself up: He shall not lie down until he eat of the prey, And drink the blood of the slain.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And Balak said unto Balaam, Neither curse them at all, nor bless them at all.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** But Balaam answered and said unto Balak, Told not I thee, saying, All that Jehovah speaketh, that I must do?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And Balak said unto Balaam, Come now, I will take thee unto another place; peradventure it will please God that thou mayest curse me them from thence.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And Balak took Balaam unto the top of Peor, that looketh down upon the desert.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And Balaam said unto Balak, Build me here seven altars, and prepare me here seven bullocks and seven rams.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** And Balak did as Balaam had said, and offered up a bullock and a ram on every altar.
