# Numbers 12 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Now Miriam and Aaron said evil against Moses, because of the Cushite woman to whom he was married, for he had taken a Cushite woman as his wife.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And they said, Have the words of the Lord been given to Moses only? have they not come to us? And the Lord took note of it.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Now the man Moses was more gentle than any other man on earth.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And suddenly the Lord said to Moses and Aaron and Miriam, Come out, you three, to the Tent of meeting. And the three of them went out.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And the Lord came down in a pillar of cloud, taking his place at the door of the Tent, and made Aaron and Miriam come before him.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And he said, Now give ear to my words: if there is a prophet among you I will give him knowledge of myself in a vision and will let my words come to him in a dream.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** My servant Moses is not so; he is true to me in all my house:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** With him I will have talk mouth to mouth, openly and not in dark sayings; and with his eyes he will see the form of the Lord: why then had you no fear of saying evil against my servant Moses?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And burning with wrath against them, the Lord went away.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And the cloud was moved from over the Tent; and straight away Miriam became a leper, as white as snow: and Aaron, looking at Miriam, saw that she was a leper.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Then Aaron said to Moses, O my lord, let not our sin be on our heads, for we have done foolishly and are sinners.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Let her not be as one dead, whose flesh is half wasted when he comes out from the body of his mother.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And Moses, crying to the Lord, said, Let my prayer come before you, O God, and make her well.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And the Lord said to Moses, If her father had put a mark of shame on her, would she not be shamed for seven days? Let her be shut up outside the tent-circle for seven days, and after that she may come in again.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** So Miriam was shut up outside the tent-circle for seven days: and the people did not go forward on their journey till Miriam had come in again.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** After that, the people went on from Hazeroth and put up their tents in the waste land of Paran.
