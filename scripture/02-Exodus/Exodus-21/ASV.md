# Exodus 21 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Now these are the ordinances which thou shalt set before them.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** If thou buy a Hebrew servant, six years he shall serve: and in the seventh he shall go out free for nothing.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** If he come in by himself, he shall go out by himself: if he be married, then his wife shall go out with him.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** If his master give him a wife and she bear him sons or daughters; the wife and her children shall be her master’s, and he shall go out by himself.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But if the servant shall plainly say, I love my master, my wife, and my children; I will not go out free:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** then his master shall bring him unto God, and shall bring him to the door, or unto the door-post; and his master shall bore his ear through with an awl; and he shall serve him for ever.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And if a man sell his daughter to be a maid-servant, she shall not go out as the men-servants do.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** If she please not her master, who hath espoused her to himself, then shall he let her be redeemed: to sell her unto a foreign people he shall have no power, seeing he hath dealt deceitfully with her.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And if he espouse her unto his son, he shall deal with her after the manner of daughters.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** If he take him another wife; her food, her raiment, and her duty of marriage, shall he not diminish.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And if he do not these three things unto her, then shall she go out for nothing, without money.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** He that smiteth a man, so that he dieth, shall surely be put to death.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And if a man lie not in wait, but God deliver him into his hand; then I will appoint thee a place whither he shall flee.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And if a man come presumptuously upon his neighbor, to slay him with guile; thou shalt take him from mine altar, that he may die.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And he that smiteth his father, or his mother, shall be surely put to death.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And he that stealeth a man, and selleth him, or if he be found in his hand, he shall surely be put to death.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And he that curseth his father or his mother, shall surely be put to death.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And if men contend, and one smite the other with a stone, or with his fist, and he die not, but keep his bed;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** if he rise again, and walk abroad upon his staff, then shall he that smote him be quit: only he shall pay for the loss of his time, and shall cause him to be thoroughly healed.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And if a man smite his servant, or his maid, with a rod, and he die under his hand; he shall surely be punished.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Notwithstanding, if he continue a day or two, he shall not be punished: for he is his money.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And if men strive together, and hurt a woman with child, so that her fruit depart, and yet no harm follow; he shall be surely fined, according as the woman’s husband shall lay upon him; and he shall pay as the judges determine.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But if any harm follow, then thou shalt give life for life,

<a id="v24"></a>**24** eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot,

<a id="v25"></a>**25** burning for burning, wound for wound, stripe for stripe.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And if a man smite the eye of his servant, or the eye of his maid, and destroy it; he shall let him go free for his eye’s sake.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And if he smite out his man-servant’s tooth, or his maid-servant’s tooth, he shall let him go free for his tooth’s sake.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And if an ox gore a man or a woman to death, the ox shall be surely stoned, and its flesh shall not be eaten; but the owner of the ox shall be quit.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** But if the ox was wont to gore in time past, and it hath been testified to its owner, and he hath not kept it in, but it hath killed a man or a woman; the ox shall be stoned, and its owner also shall be put to death.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** If there be laid on him a ransom, then he shall give for the redemption of his life whatsoever is laid upon him.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Whether it have gored a son, or have gored a daughter, according to this judgment shall it be done unto him.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** If the ox gore a man-servant or a maid-servant, there shall be given unto their master thirty shekels of silver, and the ox shall be stoned.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** And if a man shall open a pit, or if a man shall dig a pit and not cover it, and an ox or an ass fall therein,

<a id="v34"></a>**34** the owner of the pit shall make it good; he shall give money unto the owner thereof, and the dead beast shall be his.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** And if one man’s ox hurt another’s, so that it dieth, then they shall sell the live ox, and divide the price of it; and the dead also they shall divide.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** Or if it be known that the ox was wont to gore in time past, and its owner hath not kept it in; he shall surely pay ox for ox, and the dead beast shall be his own.
