# Ezekiel 9 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then he cried in my ears with a loud voice, saying, Cause those who are in charge of the city to draw near, every man with his destroying weapon in his hand.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Behold, six men came from the way of the upper gate, which lies toward the north, every man with his slaughter weapon in his hand; and one man in their midst clothed in linen, with a writer’s inkhorn by his side. They went in, and stood beside the bronze altar.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The glory of the God of Israel was gone up from the cherub, whereupon it was, to the threshold of the house: and he called to the man clothed in linen, who had the writer’s inkhorn by his side.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Yahweh said to him, Go through the midst of the city, through the midst of Jerusalem, and set a mark on the foreheads of the men that sigh and that cry over all the abominations that are done in its midst.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** To the others he said in my hearing, Go through the city after him, and strike: don’t let your eye spare, neither have pity;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** kill utterly the old man, the young man and the virgin, and little children and women; but don’t come near any man on whom is the mark: and begin at my sanctuary. Then they began at the old men that were before the house.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He said to them, “Defile the house, and fill the courts with the slain. Go out!”   They went out, and struck in the city.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** While they were killing, and I was left, I fell on my face, and cried, and said, “Ah Lord Yahweh! will you destroy all the residue of Israel in your pouring out of your wrath on Jerusalem?”

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Then he said to me, “The iniquity of the house of Israel and Judah is exceedingly great, and the land is full of blood, and the city full of perversion: for they say, ‘Yahweh has forsaken the land, and Yahweh doesn’t see.’

<a id="v10"></a>**10** As for me also, my eye shall not spare, neither will I have pity, but I will bring their way on their head.”

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Behold, the man clothed in linen, who had the inkhorn by his side, reported the matter, saying, “I have done as you have commanded me.”
