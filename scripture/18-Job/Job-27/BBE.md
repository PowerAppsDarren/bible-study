# Job 27 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Job again took up the word and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** By the life of God, who has taken away my right; and of the Ruler of all, who has made my soul bitter;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** (For all my breath is still in me, and the spirit of God is my life;)

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Truly, there is no deceit in my lips, and my tongue does not say what is false.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Let it be far from me! I will certainly not say that you are right! I will come to death before I give up my righteousness.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** I will keep it safe, and will not let it go: my heart has nothing to say against any part of my life.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Let my hater be like the evil man, and let him who comes against me be as the sinner.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For what is the hope of the sinner when he is cut off, when God takes back his soul?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Will his cry come to the ears of God when he is in trouble?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Will he take delight in the Ruler of all, and make his prayer to God at all times?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I will give you teaching about the hand of God; I will not keep secret from you what is in the mind of the Ruler of all.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Truly, you have all seen it yourselves; why then have you become completely foolish?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** This is the punishment of the evil-doer from God, and the heritage given to the cruel by the Ruler of all.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If his children are increased, it is for the sword; and his offspring have not enough bread.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** When those of his house who are still living come to their end by disease, they are not put into the earth, and their widows are not weeping for them.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Though he may get silver together like dust, and make ready great stores of clothing;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He may get them ready, but the upright will put them on, and he who is free from sin will take the silver for a heritage.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** His house has no more strength than a spider's thread, or a watchman's tent.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He goes to rest full of wealth, but does so for the last time: on opening his eyes, he sees it there no longer.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Fears overtake him like rushing waters; in the night the storm-wind takes him away.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The east wind takes him up and he is gone; he is forced violently out of his place.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** God sends his arrows against him without mercy; he goes in flight before his hand.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Men make signs of joy because of him, driving him from his place with sounds of hissing.
