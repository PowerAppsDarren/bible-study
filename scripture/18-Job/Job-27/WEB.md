# Job 27 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Job again took up his parable, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “As God lives, who has taken away my right, the Almighty, who has made my soul bitter.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** (For the length of my life is still in me, and the spirit of God is in my nostrils);

<a id="v4"></a>**4** surely my lips shall not speak unrighteousness, neither shall my tongue utter deceit.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Far be it from me that I should justify you. Until I die I will not put away my integrity from me.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** I hold fast to my righteousness, and will not let it go. My heart shall not reproach me so long as I live.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** “Let my enemy be as the wicked. Let him who rises up against me be as the unrighteous.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For what is the hope of the godless, when he is cut off, when God takes away his life?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Will God hear his cry when trouble comes on him?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Will he delight himself in the Almighty, and call on God at all times?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I will teach you about the hand of God. That which is with the Almighty will I not conceal.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Behold, all of you have seen it yourselves; why then have you become altogether vain?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** “This is the portion of a wicked man with God, the heritage of oppressors, which they receive from the Almighty.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If his children are multiplied, it is for the sword. His offspring shall not be satisfied with bread.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Those who remain of him shall be buried in death. His widows shall make no lamentation.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Though he heap up silver as the dust, and prepare clothing as the clay;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** he may prepare it, but the just shall put it on, and the innocent shall divide the silver.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He builds his house as the moth, as a booth which the watchman makes.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He lies down rich, but he shall not do so again. He opens his eyes, and he is not.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Terrors overtake him like waters. A storm steals him away in the night.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The east wind carries him away, and he departs. It sweeps him out of his place.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** For it hurls at him, and does not spare, as he flees away from his hand.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Men shall clap their hands at him, and shall hiss him out of his place.
