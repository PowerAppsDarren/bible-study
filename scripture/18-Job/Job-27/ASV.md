# Job 27 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Job again took up his parable, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As God liveth, who hath taken away my right, And the Almighty, who hath vexed my soul

<a id="v3"></a>**3** (For my life is yet whole in me, And the spirit of God is in my nostrils);

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Surely my lips shall not speak unrighteousness, Neither shall my tongue utter deceit.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Far be it from me that I should justify you: Till I die I will not put away mine integrity from me.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** My righteousness I hold fast, and will not let it go: My heart shall not reproachmeso long as I live.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Let mine enemy be as the wicked, And let him that riseth up against me be as the unrighteous.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For what is the hope of the godless, though he get him gain, When God taketh away his soul?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Will God hear his cry, When trouble cometh upon him?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Will he delight himself in the Almighty, And call upon God at all times?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I will teach you concerning the hand of God; That which is with the Almighty will I not conceal.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Behold, all ye yourselves have seen it; Why then are ye become altogether vain?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** This is the portion of a wicked man with God, And the heritage of oppressors, which they receive from the Almighty:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If his children be multiplied, it is for the sword; And his offspring shall not be satisfied with bread.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Those that remain of him shall be buried in death, And his widows shall make no lamentation.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Though he heap up silver as the dust, And prepare raiment as the clay;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He may prepare it, but the just shall put it on, And the innocent shall divide the silver.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He buildeth his house as the moth, And as a booth which the keeper maketh.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He lieth down rich, but he shall not be gathered to his fathers; He openeth his eyes, and he is not.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Terrors overtake him like waters; A tempest stealeth him away in the night.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The east wind carrieth him away, and he departeth; And it sweepeth him out of his place.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** ForGodshall hurl at him, and not spare: He would fain flee out of his hand.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Men shall clap their hands at him, And shall hiss him out of his place.
