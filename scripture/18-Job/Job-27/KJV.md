# Job 27 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Moreover Job continued his parable, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As God liveth, who hath taken away my judgment; and the Almighty, who hath vexed my soul;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** All the while my breath is in me, and the spirit of God is in my nostrils;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** My lips shall not speak wickedness, nor my tongue utter deceit.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** God forbid that I should justify you: till I die I will not remove mine integrity from me.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** My righteousness I hold fast, and will not let it go: my heart shall not reproach me so long as I live.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Let mine enemy be as the wicked, and he that riseth up against me as the unrighteous.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For what is the hope of the hypocrite, though he hath gained, when God taketh away his soul?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Will God hear his cry when trouble cometh upon him?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Will he delight himself in the Almighty? will he always call upon God?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I will teach you by the hand of God: that which is with the Almighty will I not conceal.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Behold, all ye yourselves have seen it; why then are ye thus altogether vain?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** This is the portion of a wicked man with God, and the heritage of oppressors, which they shall receive of the Almighty.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If his children be multiplied, it is for the sword: and his offspring shall not be satisfied with bread.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Those that remain of him shall be buried in death: and his widows shall not weep.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Though he heap up silver as the dust, and prepare raiment as the clay;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He may prepare it, but the just shall put it on, and the innocent shall divide the silver.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He buildeth his house as a moth, and as a booth that the keeper maketh.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The rich man shall lie down, but he shall not be gathered: he openeth his eyes, and he is not.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Terrors take hold on him as waters, a tempest stealeth him away in the night.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The east wind carrieth him away, and he departeth: and as a storm hurleth him out of his place.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** For God shall cast upon him, and not spare: he would fain flee out of his hand.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Men shall clap their hands at him, and shall hiss him out of his place.
