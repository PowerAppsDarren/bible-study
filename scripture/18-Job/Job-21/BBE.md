# Job 21 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Job made answer and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Give attention with care to my words; and let this be your comfort.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Let me say what is in my mind, and after that, go on making sport of me.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** As for me, is my outcry against man? is it then to be wondered at if my spirit is troubled?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Take note of me and be full of wonder, put your hand on your mouth.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** At the very thought of it my flesh is shaking with fear.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Why is life given to the evil-doers? why do they become old and strong in power?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Their children are ever with them, and their offspring before their eyes.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Their houses are free from fear, and the rod of God does not come on them.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Their ox is ready at all times to give seed; their cow gives birth, without dropping her young.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** They send out their young ones like a flock, and their children have pleasure in the dance,

<a id="v12"></a>**12** They make songs to the instruments of music, and are glad at the sound of the pipe.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Their days come to an end without trouble, and suddenly they go down to the underworld.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Though they said to God, Go away from us, for we have no desire for the knowledge of your ways.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** What is the Ruler of all, that we may give him worship? and what profit is it to us to make prayer to him?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Truly, is not their well-being in their power? (The purpose of the evil-doers is far from me.)

<a id="v17"></a>**17** How frequently is the light of the evil-doers put out, or does trouble come on them? how frequently does his wrath take them with cords?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** How frequently are they as dry stems before the wind, or as grass taken away by the storm-wind?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** You say, God keeps punishment stored up for his children. Let him send it on the man himself, so that he may have the punishment of it!

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Let his eyes see his trouble, and let him be full of the wrath of the Ruler of all!

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For what interest has he in his house after him, when the number of his months is ended?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Is anyone able to give teaching to God? for he is the judge of those who are on high.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** One comes to his end in complete well-being, full of peace and quiet:

<a id="v24"></a>**24** His buckets are full of milk, and there is no loss of strength in his bones.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And another comes to his end with a bitter soul, without ever tasting good.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Together they go down to the dust, and are covered by the worm.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** See, I am conscious of your thoughts, and of your violent purposes against me;

<a id="v28"></a>**28** For you say, Where is the house of the ruler, and where is the tent of the evil-doer?

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Have you not put the question to the travellers, and do you not take note of their experience?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** How the evil man goes free in the day of trouble, and has salvation in the day of wrath?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Who will make his way clear to his face? and if he has done a thing, who gives him punishment for it?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He is taken to his last resting-place, and keeps watch over it.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The earth of the valley covering his bones is sweet to him, and all men come after him, as there were unnumbered before him.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Why then do you give me comfort with words in which there is no profit, when you see that there is nothing in your answers but deceit?
