# Job 21 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** But Job answered and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Hear diligently my speech, and let this be your consolations.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Suffer me that I may speak; and after that I have spoken, mock on.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** As for me, is my complaint to man? and if it were so, why should not my spirit be troubled?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Mark me, and be astonished, and lay your hand upon your mouth.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Even when I remember I am afraid, and trembling taketh hold on my flesh.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Wherefore do the wicked live, become old, yea, are mighty in power?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Their seed is established in their sight with them, and their offspring before their eyes.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Their houses are safe from fear, neither is the rod of God upon them.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Their bull gendereth, and faileth not; their cow calveth, and casteth not her calf.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** They send forth their little ones like a flock, and their children dance.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** They take the timbrel and harp, and rejoice at the sound of the organ.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They spend their days in wealth, and in a moment go down to the grave.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Therefore they say unto God, Depart from us; for we desire not the knowledge of thy ways.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** What is the Almighty, that we should serve him? and what profit should we have, if we pray unto him?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Lo, their good is not in their hand: the counsel of the wicked is far from me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** How oft is the candle of the wicked put out! and how oft cometh their destruction upon them! God distributeth sorrows in his anger.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** They are as stubble before the wind, and as chaff that the storm carrieth away.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** God layeth up his iniquity for his children: he rewardeth him, and he shall know it.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** His eyes shall see his destruction, and he shall drink of the wrath of the Almighty.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For what pleasure hath he in his house after him, when the number of his months is cut off in the midst?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Shall any teach God knowledge? seeing he judgeth those that are high.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** One dieth in his full strength, being wholly at ease and quiet.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** His breasts are full of milk, and his bones are moistened with marrow.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And another dieth in the bitterness of his soul, and never eateth with pleasure.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** They shall lie down alike in the dust, and the worms shall cover them.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Behold, I know your thoughts, and the devices which ye wrongfully imagine against me.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** For ye say, Where is the house of the prince? and where are the dwelling places of the wicked?

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Have ye not asked them that go by the way? and do ye not know their tokens,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** That the wicked is reserved to the day of destruction? they shall be brought forth to the day of wrath.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Who shall declare his way to his face? and who shall repay him what he hath done?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Yet shall he be brought to the grave, and shall remain in the tomb.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The clods of the valley shall be sweet unto him, and every man shall draw after him, as there are innumerable before him.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** How then comfort ye me in vain, seeing in your answers there remaineth falsehood?
