# Job 21 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Job answered and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Hear diligently my speech; And let this be your consolations.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Suffer me, and I also will speak; And after that I have spoken, mock on.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** As for me, is my complaint to man? And why should I not be impatient?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Mark me, and be astonished, And lay your hand upon your mouth.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Even when I remember I am troubled, And horror taketh hold on my flesh.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Wherefore do the wicked live, Become old, yea, wax mighty in power?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Their seed is established with them in their sight, And their offspring before their eyes.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Their houses are safe from fear, Neither is the rod of God upon them.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Their bull gendereth, and faileth not; Their cow calveth, and casteth not her calf.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** They send forth their little ones like a flock, And their children dance.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** They sing to the timbrel and harp, And rejoice at the sound of the pipe.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They spend their days in prosperity, And in a moment they go down to Sheol.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** And they say unto God, Depart from us; For we desire not the knowledge of thy ways.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** What is the Almighty, that we should serve him? And what profit should we have, if we pray unto him?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Lo, their prosperity is not in their hand: The counsel of the wicked is far from me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** How oft is it that the lamp of the wicked is put out? That their calamity cometh upon them? ThatGod distributeth sorrows in his anger?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** That they are as stubble before the wind, And as chaff that the storm carrieth away?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Ye say, God layeth up his iniquity for his children. Let him recompense it unto himself, that he may know it:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Let his own eyes see his destruction, And let him drink of the wrath of the Almighty.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For what careth he for his house after him, When the number of his months is cut off?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Shall any teach God knowledge, Seeing he judgeth those that are high?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** One dieth in his full strength, Being wholly at ease and quiet:

<a id="v24"></a>**24** His pails are full of milk, And the marrow of his bones is moistened.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And another dieth in bitterness of soul, And never tasteth of good.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** They lie down alike in the dust, And the worm covereth them.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Behold, I know your thoughts, And the devices wherewith ye would wrong me.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** For ye say, Where is the house of the prince? And where is the tent wherein the wicked dwelt?

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Have ye not asked wayfaring men? And do ye not know their evidences,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** That the evil man is reserved to the day of calamity? That they are led forth to the day of wrath?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Who shall declare his way to his face? And who shall repay him what he hath done?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Yet shall he be borne to the grave, And men shall keep watch over the tomb.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The clods of the valley shall be sweet unto him, And all men shall draw after him, As there were innumerable before him.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** How then comfort ye me in vain, Seeing in your answers there remaineth only falsehood?
