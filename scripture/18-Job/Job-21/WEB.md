# Job 21 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Job answered,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Listen diligently to my speech. Let this be your consolation.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Allow me, and I also will speak; After I have spoken, mock on.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** As for me, is my complaint to man? Why shouldn’t I be impatient?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Look at me, and be astonished. Lay your hand on your mouth.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** When I remember, I am troubled. Horror takes hold of my flesh.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** “Why do the wicked live, become old, yes, and grow mighty in power?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Their child is established with them in their sight, their offspring before their eyes.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Their houses are safe from fear, neither is the rod of God upon them.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Their bulls breed without fail. Their cows calve, and don’t miscarry.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** They send out their little ones like a flock. Their children dance.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** They sing to the tambourine and harp, and rejoice at the sound of the pipe.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They spend their days in prosperity. In an instant they go down to Sheol.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** They tell God, ‘Depart from us, for we don’t want to know about your ways.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** What is the Almighty, that we should serve him? What profit should we have, if we pray to him?’

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Behold, their prosperity is not in their hand. The counsel of the wicked is far from me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** “How often is it that the lamp of the wicked is put out, that their calamity comes on them, that God distributes sorrows in his anger?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** How often is it that they are as stubble before the wind, as chaff that the storm carries away?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** You say, ‘God lays up his iniquity for his children.’ Let him recompense it to himself, that he may know it.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Let his own eyes see his destruction. Let him drink of the wrath of the Almighty.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For what does he care for his house after him, when the number of his months is cut off?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** “Shall any teach God knowledge, since he judges those who are high?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** One dies in his full strength, being wholly at ease and quiet.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** His pails are full of milk. The marrow of his bones is moistened.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Another dies in bitterness of soul, and never tastes of good.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** They lie down alike in the dust. The worm covers them.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** “Behold, I know your thoughts, the devices with which you would wrong me.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** For you say, ‘Where is the house of the prince? Where is the tent in which the wicked lived?’

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Haven’t you asked wayfaring men? Don’t you know their evidences,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** that the evil man is reserved to the day of calamity, That they are led out to the day of wrath?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Who shall declare his way to his face? Who shall repay him what he has done?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Yet he will be borne to the grave. Men shall keep watch over the tomb.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The clods of the valley shall be sweet to him. All men shall draw after him, as there were innumerable before him.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** So how can you comfort me with nonsense, because in your answers there remains only falsehood?”
