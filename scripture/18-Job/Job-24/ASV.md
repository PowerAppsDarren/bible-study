# Job 24 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Why are times not laid up by the Almighty? And why do not they that know him see his days?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** There are that remove the landmarks; They violently take away flocks, and feed them.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** They drive away the ass of the fatherless; They take the widow’s ox for a pledge.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** They turn the needy out of the way: The poor of the earth all hide themselves.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Behold, as wild asses in the desert They go forth to their work, seeking diligently for food; The wildernessyieldeththem bread for their children.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** They cut their provender in the field; And they glean the vintage of the wicked.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** They lie all night naked without clothing, And have no covering in the cold.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They are wet with the showers of the mountains, And embrace the rock for want of a shelter.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** There are that pluck the fatherless from the breast, And take a pledge of the poor;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** So thatthey go about naked without clothing, And being hungry they carry the sheaves.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** They make oil within the walls of these men; They treadtheirwinepresses, and suffer thirst.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** From out of the populous city men groan, And the soul of the wounded crieth out: Yet God regardeth not the folly.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** These are of them that rebel against the light; They know not the ways thereof, Nor abide in the paths thereof.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The murderer riseth with the light; He killeth the poor and needy; And in the night he is as a thief.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The eye also of the adulterer waiteth for the twilight, Saying, No eye shall see me: And he disguiseth his face.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** In the dark they dig through houses: They shut themselves up in the day-time; They know not the light.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For the morning is to all of them as thick darkness; For they know the terrors of the thick darkness.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Swiftly theypass awayupon the face of the waters; Their portion is cursed in the earth: They turn not into the way of the vineyards.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Drought and heat consume the snow waters: So dothSheolthose thathave sinned.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The womb shall forget him; The worm shall feed sweetly on him; He shall be no more remembered; And unrighteousness shall be broken as a tree.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He devoureth the barren that beareth not, And doeth not good to the widow.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** YetGod preserveth the mighty by his power: He riseth up that hath no assurance of life.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** God giveth them to be in security, and they rest thereon; And his eyes are upon their ways.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** They are exalted; yet a little while, and they are gone; Yea, they are brought low, they are taken out of the way as all others, And are cut off as the tops of the ears of grain.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And if it be not so now, who will prove me a liar, And make my speech nothing worth?
