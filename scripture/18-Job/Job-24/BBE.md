# Job 24 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Why are times not stored up by the Ruler of all, and why do those who have knowledge of him not see his days?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The landmarks are changed by evil men, they violently take away flocks, together with their keepers.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** They send away the ass of him who has no father, they take the widow's ox for debt.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The crushed are turned out of the way; all the poor of the earth go into a secret place together.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Like asses in the waste land they go out to their work, looking for food with care; from the waste land they get bread for their children.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** They get mixed grain from the field, and they take away the late fruit from the vines of those who have wealth.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** They take their rest at night without clothing, and have no cover in the cold.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They are wet with the rain of the mountains, and get into the cracks of the rock for cover.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The child without a father is forced from its mother's breast, and they take the young children of the poor for debt.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Others go about without clothing, and though they have no food, they get in the grain from the fields.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Between the lines of olive-trees they make oil; though they have no drink, they are crushing out the grapes.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** From the town come sounds of pain from those who are near death, and the soul of the wounded is crying out for help; but God does not take note of their prayer.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Then there are those who are haters of the light, who have no knowledge of its ways, and do not go in them.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He who is purposing death gets up before day, so that he may put to death the poor and those in need.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And the man whose desire is for the wife of another is waiting for the evening, saying, No eye will see me; and he puts a cover on his face. And in the night the thief goes about;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** In the dark he makes holes in the walls of houses: in the daytime they are shutting themselves up, they have no knowledge of the light.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For the middle of the night is as morning to them, they are not troubled by the fear of the dark.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** They go quickly on the face of the waters; their heritage is cursed in the earth; the steps of the crusher of grapes are not turned to their vine-garden.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Snow waters become dry with the heat: so do sinners go down into the underworld.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The public place of his town has no more knowledge of him, and his name has gone from the memory of men: he is rooted up like a dead tree.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He is not kind to the widow, and he has no pity for her child.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** But God by his power gives long life to the strong; he gets up again, though he has no hope of life.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He takes away his fear of danger and gives him support; and his eyes are on his ways.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For a short time they are lifted up; then they are gone; they are made low, they are pulled off like fruit, and like the heads of grain they are cut off.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And if it is not so, now, who will make it clear that my words are false, and that what I say is of no value?
