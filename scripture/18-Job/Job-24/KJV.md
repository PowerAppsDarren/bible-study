# Job 24 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Why, seeing times are not hidden from the Almighty, do they that know him not see his days?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Some remove the landmarks; they violently take away flocks, and feed thereof.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** They drive away the ass of the fatherless, they take the widow’s ox for a pledge.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** They turn the needy out of the way: the poor of the earth hide themselves together.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Behold, as wild asses in the desert, go they forth to their work; rising betimes for a prey: the wilderness yieldeth food for them and for their children.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** They reap every one his corn in the field: and they gather the vintage of the wicked.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** They cause the naked to lodge without clothing, that they have no covering in the cold.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They are wet with the showers of the mountains, and embrace the rock for want of a shelter.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** They pluck the fatherless from the breast, and take a pledge of the poor.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** They cause him to go naked without clothing, and they take away the sheaf from the hungry;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Which make oil within their walls, and tread their winepresses, and suffer thirst.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Men groan from out of the city, and the soul of the wounded crieth out: yet God layeth not folly to them.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They are of those that rebel against the light; they know not the ways thereof, nor abide in the paths thereof.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The murderer rising with the light killeth the poor and needy, and in the night is as a thief.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The eye also of the adulterer waiteth for the twilight, saying, No eye shall see me: and disguiseth his face.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** In the dark they dig through houses, which they had marked for themselves in the daytime: they know not the light.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For the morning is to them even as the shadow of death: if one know them, they are in the terrors of the shadow of death.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He is swift as the waters; their portion is cursed in the earth: he beholdeth not the way of the vineyards.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Drought and heat consume the snow waters: so doth the grave those which have sinned.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The womb shall forget him; the worm shall feed sweetly on him; he shall be no more remembered; and wickedness shall be broken as a tree.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He evil entreateth the barren that beareth not: and doeth not good to the widow.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He draweth also the mighty with his power: he riseth up, and no man is sure of life.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Though it be given him to be in safety, whereon he resteth; yet his eyes are upon their ways.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** They are exalted for a little while, but are gone and brought low; they are taken out of the way as all other, and cut off as the tops of the ears of corn.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And if it be not so now, who will make me a liar, and make my speech nothing worth?
