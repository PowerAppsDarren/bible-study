# Job 40 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Moreover Jehovah answered Job, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Shall he that cavilleth contend with the Almighty? He that argueth with God, let him answer it.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Then Job answered Jehovah, and said,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Behold, I am of small account; what shall I answer thee? I lay my hand upon my mouth.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Once have I spoken, and I will not answer; Yea, twice, but I will proceed no further.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Then Jehovah answered Job out of the whirlwind, and said,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Gird up thy loins now like a man: I will demand of thee, and declare thou unto me.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Wilt thou even annul my judgment? Wilt thou condemn me, that thou mayest be justified?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Or hast thou an arm like God? And canst thou thunder with a voice like him?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Deck thyself now with excellency and dignity; And array thyself with honor and majesty.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Pour forth the overflowings of thine anger; And look upon every one that is proud, and abase him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Look on every one that is proud, and bring him low; And tread down the wicked where they stand.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Hide them in the dust together; Bind their faces in the hiddenplace.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Then will I also confess of thee That thine own right hand can save thee.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Behold now, behemoth, which I made as well as thee; He eateth grass as an ox.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Lo now, his strength is in his loins, And his force is in the muscles of his belly.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He moveth his tail like a cedar: The sinews of his thighs are knit together.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** His bones areastubes of brass; His limbs are like bars of iron.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He is the chief of the ways of God: Heonlythat made him giveth him his sword.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Surely the mountains bring him forth food, Where all the beasts of the field do play.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He lieth under the lotus-trees, In the covert of the reed, and the fen.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The lotus-trees cover him with their shade; The willows of the brook compass him about.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Behold, if a river overflow, he trembleth not; He is confident, though a Jordan swell even to his mouth.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Shall any take him when he is on the watch, Or pierce through his nose with a snare?
