# Job 40 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Moreover the Lord answered Job, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Shall he that contendeth with the Almighty instruct him? he that reproveth God, let him answer it.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Then Job answered the Lord, and said,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Behold, I am vile; what shall I answer thee? I will lay mine hand upon my mouth.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Once have I spoken; but I will not answer: yea, twice; but I will proceed no further.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Then answered the Lord unto Job out of the whirlwind, and said,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Gird up thy loins now like a man: I will demand of thee, and declare thou unto me.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Wilt thou also disannul my judgment? wilt thou condemn me, that thou mayest be righteous?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Hast thou an arm like God? or canst thou thunder with a voice like him?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Deck thyself now with majesty and excellency; and array thyself with glory and beauty.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Cast abroad the rage of thy wrath: and behold every one that is proud, and abase him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Look on every one that is proud, and bring him low; and tread down the wicked in their place.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Hide them in the dust together; and bind their faces in secret.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Then will I also confess unto thee that thine own right hand can save thee.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Behold now behemoth, which I made with thee; he eateth grass as an ox.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Lo now, his strength is in his loins, and his force is in the navel of his belly.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He moveth his tail like a cedar: the sinews of his stones are wrapped together.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** His bones are as strong pieces of brass; his bones are like bars of iron.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He is the chief of the ways of God: he that made him can make his sword to approach unto him.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Surely the mountains bring him forth food, where all the beasts of the field play.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He lieth under the shady trees, in the covert of the reed, and fens.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The shady trees cover him with their shadow; the willows of the brook compass him about.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Behold, he drinketh up a river, and hasteth not: he trusteth that he can draw up Jordan into his mouth.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He taketh it with his eyes: his nose pierceth through snares.
