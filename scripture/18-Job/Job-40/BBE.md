# Job 40 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** ...

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Will he who is protesting give teaching to the Ruler of all? Let him who has arguments to put forward against God give an answer.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And Job said in answer to the Lord,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Truly, I am of no value; what answer may I give to you? I will put my hand on my mouth.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** I have said once, and even twice, what was in my mind, but I will not do so again.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Then the Lord made answer to Job out of the storm-wind, and said,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Get your strength together like a man of war: I will put questions to you, and you will give me the answers.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Will you even make my right of no value? will you say that I am wrong in order to make clear that you are right?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Have you an arm like God? have you a voice of thunder like his?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Put on the ornaments of your pride; be clothed with glory and power:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Let your wrath be overflowing; let your eyes see all the sons of pride, and make them low.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Send destruction on all who are lifted up, pulling down the sinners from their places.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Let them be covered together in the dust; let their faces be dark in the secret place of the underworld.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Then I will give praise to you, saying that your right hand is able to give you salvation.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** See now the Great Beast, whom I made, even as I made you; he takes grass for food, like the ox.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** His strength is in his body, and his force in the muscles of his stomach.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** His tail is curving like a cedar; the muscles of his legs are joined together.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** His bones are pipes of brass, his legs are like rods of iron.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He is the chief of the ways of God, made by him for his pleasure.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He takes the produce of the mountains, where all the beasts of the field are at play.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He takes his rest under the trees of the river, and in the pool, under the shade of the water-plants.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He is covered by the branches of the trees; the grasses of the stream are round him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Truly, if the river is overflowing, it gives him no cause for fear; he has no sense of danger, even if Jordan is rushing against his mouth.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Will anyone take him when he is on the watch, or put metal teeth through his nose?
