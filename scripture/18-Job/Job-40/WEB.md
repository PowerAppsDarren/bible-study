# Job 40 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Moreover Yahweh answered Job,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Shall he who argues contend with the Almighty? He who argues with God, let him answer it.”

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Then Job answered Yahweh,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** “Behold, I am of small account. What shall I answer you? I lay my hand on my mouth.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** I have spoken once, and I will not answer; Yes, twice, but I will proceed no further.”

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Then Yahweh answered Job out of the whirlwind,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** “Now brace yourself like a man. I will question you, and you will answer me.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Will you even annul my judgment? Will you condemn me, that you may be justified?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Or do you have an arm like God? Can you thunder with a voice like him?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** “Now deck yourself with excellency and dignity. Array yourself with honor and majesty.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Pour out the fury of your anger. Look at everyone who is proud, and bring him low.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Look at everyone who is proud, and humble him. Crush the wicked in their place.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Hide them in the dust together. Bind their faces in the hidden place.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Then I will also admit to you that your own right hand can save you.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** “See now, behemoth, which I made as well as you. He eats grass as an ox.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Look now, his strength is in his thighs. His force is in the muscles of his belly.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He moves his tail like a cedar. The sinews of his thighs are knit together.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** His bones are like tubes of brass. His limbs are like bars of iron.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He is the chief of the ways of God. He who made him gives him his sword.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Surely the mountains produce food for him, where all the animals of the field play.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He lies under the lotus trees, in the covert of the reed, and the marsh.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The lotuses cover him with their shade. The willows of the brook surround him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Behold, if a river overflows, he doesn’t tremble. He is confident, though the Jordan swells even to his mouth.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Shall any take him when he is on the watch, or pierce through his nose with a snare?
