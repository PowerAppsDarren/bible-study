# Job 16 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Job answered and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** I have heard many such things: miserable comforters are ye all.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Shall vain words have an end? or what emboldeneth thee that thou answerest?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** I also could speak as ye do: if your soul were in my soul’s stead, I could heap up words against you, and shake mine head at you.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But I would strengthen you with my mouth, and the moving of my lips should asswage your grief.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Though I speak, my grief is not asswaged: and though I forbear, what am I eased?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** But now he hath made me weary: thou hast made desolate all my company.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And thou hast filled me with wrinkles, which is a witness against me: and my leanness rising up in me beareth witness to my face.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He teareth me in his wrath, who hateth me: he gnasheth upon me with his teeth; mine enemy sharpeneth his eyes upon me.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** They have gaped upon me with their mouth; they have smitten me upon the cheek reproachfully; they have gathered themselves together against me.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** God hath delivered me to the ungodly, and turned me over into the hands of the wicked.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** I was at ease, but he hath broken me asunder: he hath also taken me by my neck, and shaken me to pieces, and set me up for his mark.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** His archers compass me round about, he cleaveth my reins asunder, and doth not spare; he poureth out my gall upon the ground.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He breaketh me with breach upon breach, he runneth upon me like a giant.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** I have sewed sackcloth upon my skin, and defiled my horn in the dust.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** My face is foul with weeping, and on my eyelids is the shadow of death;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Not for any injustice in mine hands: also my prayer is pure.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** O earth, cover not thou my blood, and let my cry have no place.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Also now, behold, my witness is in heaven, and my record is on high.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** My friends scorn me: but mine eye poureth out tears unto God.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** O that one might plead for a man with God, as a man pleadeth for his neighbour!

<a id="v22"></a>**22** When a few years are come, then I shall go the way whence I shall not return.
