# Job 19 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Job made answer and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** How long will you make my life bitter, crushing me with words?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Ten times now you have made sport of me; it gives you no sense of shame to do me wrong.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And, truly, if I have been in error, the effect of my error is only on myself.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If you make yourselves great against me, using my punishment as an argument against me,

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Be certain that it is God who has done me wrong, and has taken me in his net.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Truly, I make an outcry against the violent man, but there is no answer: I give a cry for help, but no one takes up my cause.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** My way is walled up by him so that I may not go by: he has made my roads dark.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He has put off my glory from me, and taken the crown from my head.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** I am broken down by him on every side, and I am gone; my hope is uprooted like a tree.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** His wrath is burning against me, and I am to him as one of his haters.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** His armies come on together, they make their road high against me, and put up their tents round mine.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He has taken my brothers far away from me; they have seen my fate and have become strange to me.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** My relations and my near friends have given me up, and those living in my house have put me out of their minds.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** I am strange to my women-servants, and seem to them as one from another country.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** At my cry my servant gives me no answer, and I have to make a prayer to him.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** My breath is strange to my wife, and I am disgusting to the offspring of my mother's body.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Even young children have no respect for me; when I get up their backs are turned on me.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** All the men of my circle keep away from me; and those dear to me are turned against me.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** My bones are joined to my skin, and I have got away with my flesh in my teeth.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Have pity on me, have pity on me, O my friends! for the hand of God is on me.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Why are you cruel to me, like God, for ever saying evil against me?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If only my words might be recorded! if they might be put in writing in a book!

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And with an iron pen and lead be cut into the rock for ever!

<a id="v25"></a>**25** But I am certain that he who will take up my cause is living, and that in time to come he will take his place on the dust;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And ... without my flesh I will see God;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Whom I will see on my side, and not as one strange to me. My heart is broken with desire.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** If you say, How cruel we will be to him! because the root of sin is clearly in him:

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Be in fear of the sword, for the sword is the punishment for such things, so that you may be certain that there is a judge.
