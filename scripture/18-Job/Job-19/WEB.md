# Job 19 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Job answered,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “How long will you torment me, and crush me with words?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** You have reproached me ten times. You aren’t ashamed that you attack me.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** If it is true that I have erred, my error remains with myself.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If indeed you will magnify yourselves against me, and plead against me my reproach;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** know now that God has subverted me, and has surrounded me with his net.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** “Behold, I cry out of wrong, but I am not heard. I cry for help, but there is no justice.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He has walled up my way so that I can’t pass, and has set darkness in my paths.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He has stripped me of my glory, and taken the crown from my head.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He has broken me down on every side, and I am gone. My hope he has plucked up like a tree.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He has also kindled his wrath against me. He counts me among his adversaries.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** His troops come on together, build a siege ramp against me, and encamp around my tent.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** “He has put my brothers far from me. My acquaintances are wholly estranged from me.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** My relatives have gone away. My familiar friends have forgotten me.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Those who dwell in my house, and my maids, count me for a stranger. I am an alien in their sight.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I call to my servant, and he gives me no answer. I beg him with my mouth.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** My breath is offensive to my wife. I am loathsome to the children of my own mother.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Even young children despise me. If I arise, they speak against me.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** All my familiar friends abhor me. They whom I loved have turned against me.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** My bones stick to my skin and to my flesh. I have escaped by the skin of my teeth.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** “Have pity on me, have pity on me, you my friends; for the hand of God has touched me.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Why do you persecute me as God, and are not satisfied with my flesh?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** “Oh that my words were now written! Oh that they were inscribed in a book!

<a id="v24"></a>**24** That with an iron pen and lead they were engraved in the rock forever!

<a id="v25"></a>**25** But as for me, I know that my Redeemer lives. In the end, he will stand upon the earth.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** After my skin is destroyed, then in my flesh shall I see God,

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Whom I, even I, shall see on my side. My eyes shall see, and not as a stranger.   “My heart is consumed within me.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** If you say, ‘How we will persecute him!’ because the root of the matter is found in me,

<a id="v29"></a>**29** be afraid of the sword, for wrath brings the punishments of the sword, that you may know there is a judgment.”
