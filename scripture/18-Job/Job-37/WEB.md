# Job 37 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “Yes, at this my heart trembles, and is moved out of its place.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Hear, oh, hear the noise of his voice, the sound that goes out of his mouth.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** He sends it out under the whole sky, and his lightning to the ends of the earth.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** After it a voice roars. He thunders with the voice of his majesty. He doesn’t hold back anything when his voice is heard.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** God thunders marvelously with his voice. He does great things, which we can’t comprehend.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For he says to the snow, ‘Fall on the earth;’ likewise to the shower of rain, and to the showers of his mighty rain.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He seals up the hand of every man, that all men whom he has made may know it.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Then the animals take cover, and remain in their dens.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Out of its room comes the storm, and cold out of the north.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** By the breath of God, ice is given, and the breadth of the waters is frozen.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Yes, he loads the thick cloud with moisture. He spreads abroad the cloud of his lightning.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** It is turned around by his guidance, that they may do whatever he commands them on the surface of the habitable world,

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Whether it is for correction, or for his land, or for loving kindness, that he causes it to come.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** “Listen to this, Job. Stand still, and consider the wondrous works of God.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Do you know how God controls them, and causes the lightning of his cloud to shine?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Do you know the workings of the clouds, the wondrous works of him who is perfect in knowledge?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** You whose clothing is warm, when the earth is still by reason of the south wind?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Can you, with him, spread out the sky, which is strong as a cast metal mirror?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Teach us what we shall tell him, for we can’t make our case by reason of darkness.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Shall it be told him that I would speak? Or should a man wish that he were swallowed up?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Now men don’t see the light which is bright in the skies, but the wind passes, and clears them.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Out of the north comes golden splendor. With God is awesome majesty.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** We can’t reach the Almighty. He is exalted in power. In justice and great righteousness, he will not oppress.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Therefore men revere him. He doesn’t regard any who are wise of heart.”
