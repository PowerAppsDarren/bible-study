# Job 37 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** At this also my heart trembleth, and is moved out of his place.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Hear attentively the noise of his voice, and the sound that goeth out of his mouth.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** He directeth it under the whole heaven, and his lightning unto the ends of the earth.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** After it a voice roareth: he thundereth with the voice of his excellency; and he will not stay them when his voice is heard.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** God thundereth marvellously with his voice; great things doeth he, which we cannot comprehend.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For he saith to the snow, Be thou on the earth; likewise to the small rain, and to the great rain of his strength.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He sealeth up the hand of every man; that all men may know his work.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Then the beasts go into dens, and remain in their places.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Out of the south cometh the whirlwind: and cold out of the north.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** By the breath of God frost is given: and the breadth of the waters is straitened.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Also by watering he wearieth the thick cloud: he scattereth his bright cloud:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And it is turned round about by his counsels: that they may do whatsoever he commandeth them upon the face of the world in the earth.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He causeth it to come, whether for correction, or for his land, or for mercy.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Hearken unto this, O Job: stand still, and consider the wondrous works of God.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Dost thou know when God disposed them, and caused the light of his cloud to shine?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Dost thou know the balancings of the clouds, the wondrous works of him which is perfect in knowledge?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** How thy garments are warm, when he quieteth the earth by the south wind?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Hast thou with him spread out the sky, which is strong, and as a molten looking glass?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Teach us what we shall say unto him; for we cannot order our speech by reason of darkness.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Shall it be told him that I speak? if a man speak, surely he shall be swallowed up.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And now men see not the bright light which is in the clouds: but the wind passeth, and cleanseth them.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Fair weather cometh out of the north: with God is terrible majesty.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Touching the Almighty, we cannot find him out: he is excellent in power, and in judgment, and in plenty of justice: he will not afflict.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Men do therefore fear him: he respecteth not any that are wise of heart.
