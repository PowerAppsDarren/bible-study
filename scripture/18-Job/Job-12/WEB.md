# Job 12 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Job answered,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “No doubt, but you are the people, and wisdom shall die with you.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But I have understanding as well as you; I am not inferior to you. Yes, who doesn’t know such things as these?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** I am like one who is a joke to his neighbor, I, who called on God, and he answered. The just, the blameless man is a joke.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** In the thought of him who is at ease there is contempt for misfortune. It is ready for them whose foot slips.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The tents of robbers prosper. Those who provoke God are secure, who carry their God in their hands.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** “But ask the animals, now, and they shall teach you; the birds of the sky, and they shall tell you.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Or speak to the earth, and it shall teach you. The fish of the sea shall declare to you.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Who doesn’t know that in all these, the hand of Yahweh has done this,

<a id="v10"></a>**10** in whose hand is the life of every living thing, and the breath of all mankind?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Doesn’t the ear try words, even as the palate tastes its food?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** With aged men is wisdom, in length of days understanding.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** “With God is wisdom and might. He has counsel and understanding.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Behold, he breaks down, and it can’t be built again. He imprisons a man, and there can be no release.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Behold, he withholds the waters, and they dry up. Again, he sends them out, and they overturn the earth.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** With him is strength and wisdom. The deceived and the deceiver are his.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He leads counselors away stripped. He makes judges fools.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He loosens the bond of kings. He binds their waist with a belt.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He leads priests away stripped, and overthrows the mighty.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He removes the speech of those who are trusted, and takes away the understanding of the elders.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He pours contempt on princes, and loosens the belt of the strong.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He uncovers deep things out of darkness, and brings out to light the shadow of death.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He increases the nations, and he destroys them. He enlarges the nations, and he leads them captive.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He takes away understanding from the chiefs of the people of the earth, and causes them to wander in a wilderness where there is no way.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** They grope in the dark without light. He makes them stagger like a drunken man.
