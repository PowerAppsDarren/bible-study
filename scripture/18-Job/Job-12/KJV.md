# Job 12 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Job answered and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** No doubt but ye are the people, and wisdom shall die with you.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But I have understanding as well as you; I am not inferior to you: yea, who knoweth not such things as these?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** I am as one mocked of his neighbour, who calleth upon God, and he answereth him: the just upright man is laughed to scorn.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** He that is ready to slip with his feet is as a lamp despised in the thought of him that is at ease.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The tabernacles of robbers prosper, and they that provoke God are secure; into whose hand God bringeth abundantly.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** But ask now the beasts, and they shall teach thee; and the fowls of the air, and they shall tell thee:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Or speak to the earth, and it shall teach thee: and the fishes of the sea shall declare unto thee.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Who knoweth not in all these that the hand of the Lord hath wrought this?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** In whose hand is the soul of every living thing, and the breath of all mankind.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Doth not the ear try words? and the mouth taste his meat?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** With the ancient is wisdom; and in length of days understanding.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** With him is wisdom and strength, he hath counsel and understanding.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Behold, he breaketh down, and it cannot be built again: he shutteth up a man, and there can be no opening.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Behold, he withholdeth the waters, and they dry up: also he sendeth them out, and they overturn the earth.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** With him is strength and wisdom: the deceived and the deceiver are his.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He leadeth counsellors away spoiled, and maketh the judges fools.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He looseth the bond of kings, and girdeth their loins with a girdle.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He leadeth princes away spoiled, and overthroweth the mighty.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** He removeth away the speech of the trusty, and taketh away the understanding of the aged.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He poureth contempt upon princes, and weakeneth the strength of the mighty.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He discovereth deep things out of darkness, and bringeth out to light the shadow of death.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He increaseth the nations, and destroyeth them: he enlargeth the nations, and straiteneth them again.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He taketh away the heart of the chief of the people of the earth, and causeth them to wander in a wilderness where there is no way.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** They grope in the dark without light, and he maketh them to stagger like a drunken man.
