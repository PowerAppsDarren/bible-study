# Job 8 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then answered Bildad the Shuhite, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** How long wilt thou speak these things? Andhow longshall the words of thy mouth be like a mighty wind?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Doth God pervert justice? Or doth the Almighty pervert righteousness?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** If thy children have sinned against him, And he hath delivered them into the hand of their transgression;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If thou wouldest seek diligently unto God, And make thy supplication to the Almighty;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** If thou wert pure and upright: Surely now he would awake for thee, And make the habitation of thy righteousness prosperous.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And though thy beginning was small, Yet thy latter end would greatly increase.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For inquire, I pray thee, of the former age, And apply thyself to that which their fathers have searched out

<a id="v9"></a>**9** (For we are but of yesterday, and know nothing, Because our days upon earth are a shadow);

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Shall not they teach thee, and tell thee, And utter words out of their heart?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Can the rush grow up without mire? Can the flag grow without water?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Whilst it is yet in its greenness, and not cut down, It withereth before any other herb.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** So are the paths of all that forget God; And the hope of the godless man shall perish:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Whose confidence shall break in sunder, And whose trust is a spider’s web.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He shall lean upon his house, but it shall not stand: He shall hold fast thereby, but it shall not endure.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He is green before the sun, And his shoots go forth over his garden.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** His roots are wrapped about the stone-heap, He beholdeth the place of stones.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** If he be destroyed from his place, Then it shall deny him, saying, I have not seen thee.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Behold, this is the joy of his way; And out of the earth shall others spring.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Behold, God will not cast away a perfect man, Neither will he uphold the evil-doers.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He will yet fill thy mouth with laughter, And thy lips with shouting.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** They that hate thee shall be clothed with shame; And the tent of the wicked shall be no more.
