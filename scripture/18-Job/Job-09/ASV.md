# Job 9 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Job answered and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Of a truth I know that it is so: But how can man be just with God?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** If he be pleased to contend with him, He cannot answer him one of a thousand.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** He iswise in heart, and mighty in strength: Who hath hardened himself against him, and prospered?—

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Himthat removeth the mountains, and they know it not, When he overturneth them in his anger;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** That shaketh the earth out of its place, And the pillars thereof tremble;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** That commandeth the sun, and it riseth not, And sealeth up the stars;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** That alone stretcheth out the heavens, And treadeth upon the waves of the sea;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** That maketh the Bear, Orion, and the Pleiades, And the chambers of the south;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** That doeth great things past finding out, Yea, marvellous things without number.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Lo, he goeth by me, and I see him not: He passeth on also, but I perceive him not.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Behold, he seizeth the prey, who can hinder him? Who will say unto him, What doest thou?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** God will not withdraw his anger; The helpers of Rahab do stoop under him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** How much less shall I answer him, And choose out my words to reason with him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Whom, though I were righteous, yet would I not answer; I would make supplication to my judge.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If I had called, and he had answered me, Yet would I not believe that he hearkened unto my voice.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For he breaketh me with a tempest, And multiplieth my wounds without cause.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He will not suffer me to take my breath, But filleth me with bitterness.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Ifwe speakof strength, lo, he ismighty! And if of justice, Who, saith he, will summon me?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Though I be righteous, mine own mouth shall condemn me: Though I be perfect, it shall prove me perverse.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** I am perfect; I regard not myself; I despise my life.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** It is all one; therefore I say, He destroyeth the perfect and the wicked.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If the scourge slay suddenly, He will mock at the trial of the innocent.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The earth is given into the hand of the wicked; He covereth the faces of the judges thereof: Ifit benothe, who then is it?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Now my days are swifter than a post: They flee away, they see no good.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** They are passed away as the swift ships; As the eagle that swoopeth on the prey.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** If I say, I will forget my complaint, I will put off my sad countenance, and be of good cheer;

<a id="v28"></a>**28** I am afraid of all my sorrows, I know that thou wilt not hold me innocent.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** I shall be condemned; Why then do I labor in vain?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** If I wash myself with snow water, And make my hands never so clean;

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Yet wilt thou plunge me in the ditch, And mine own clothes shall abhor me.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For he is not a man, as I am, that I should answer him, That we should come together in judgment.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** There is no umpire betwixt us, That might lay his hand upon us both.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Let him take his rod away from me, And let not his terror make me afraid:

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Then would I speak, and not fear him; For I am not so in myself.
