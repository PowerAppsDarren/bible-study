# Job 9 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Job answered,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Truly I know that it is so, but how can man be just with God?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** If he is pleased to contend with him, he can’t answer him one time in a thousand.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** God who is wise in heart, and mighty in strength: who has hardened himself against him, and prospered?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** He removes the mountains, and they don’t know it, when he overturns them in his anger.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He shakes the earth out of its place. Its pillars tremble.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He commands the sun, and it doesn’t rise, and seals up the stars.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He alone stretches out the heavens, and treads on the waves of the sea.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He makes the Bear, Orion, and the Pleiades, and the rooms of the south.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He does great things past finding out; yes, marvelous things without number.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Behold, he goes by me, and I don’t see him. He passes on also, but I don’t perceive him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Behold, he snatches away. Who can hinder him? Who will ask him, ‘What are you doing?’

<a id="v13"></a>**13** “God will not withdraw his anger. The helpers of Rahab stoop under him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** How much less shall I answer him, And choose my words to argue with him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Though I were righteous, yet I wouldn’t answer him. I would make supplication to my judge.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If I had called, and he had answered me, yet I wouldn’t believe that he listened to my voice.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For he breaks me with a storm, and multiplies my wounds without cause.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He will not allow me to catch my breath, but fills me with bitterness.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** If it is a matter of strength, behold, he is mighty! If of justice, ‘Who,’ says he, ‘will summon me?’

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Though I am righteous, my own mouth shall condemn me. Though I am blameless, it shall prove me perverse.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** I am blameless. I don’t respect myself. I despise my life.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** “It is all the same. Therefore I say he destroys the blameless and the wicked.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If the scourge kills suddenly, he will mock at the trial of the innocent.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The earth is given into the hand of the wicked. He covers the faces of its judges. If not he, then who is it?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** “Now my days are swifter than a runner. They flee away, they see no good,

<a id="v26"></a>**26** They have passed away as the swift ships, as the eagle that swoops on the prey.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** If I say, ‘I will forget my complaint, I will put off my sad face, and cheer up;’

<a id="v28"></a>**28** I am afraid of all my sorrows, I know that you will not hold me innocent.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** I shall be condemned. Why then do I labor in vain?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** If I wash myself with snow, and cleanse my hands with lye,

<a id="v31"></a>**31** yet you will plunge me in the ditch. My own clothes shall abhor me.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For he is not a man, as I am, that I should answer him, that we should come together in judgment.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** There is no umpire between us, that might lay his hand on us both.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Let him take his rod away from me. Let his terror not make me afraid;

<a id="v35"></a>**35** then I would speak, and not fear him, for I am not so in myself.
