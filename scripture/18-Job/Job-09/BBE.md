# Job 9 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Job made answer and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Truly, I see that it is so: and how is it possible for a man to get his right before God?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** If a man was desiring to go to law with him, he would not be able to give him an answer to one out of a thousand questions.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** He is wise in heart and great in strength: who ever made his face hard against him, and any good came of it?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** It is he who takes away the mountains without their knowledge, overturning them in his wrath:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Who is moving the earth out of its place, so that its pillars are shaking:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Who gives orders to the sun, and it does not give its light; and who keeps the stars from shining.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** By whose hand the heavens were stretched out, and who is walking on the waves of the sea:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Who made the Bear and Orion, and the Pleiades, and the store-houses of the south:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Who does great things not to be searched out; yes, wonders without number.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** See, he goes past me and I see him not: he goes on before, but I have no knowledge of him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** If he puts out his hand to take, by whom may it be turned back? who may say to him, What are you doing?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** God's wrath may not be turned back; the helpers of Rahab were bent down under him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** How much less may I give an answer to him, using the right words in argument with him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Even if my cause was good, I would not be able to give an answer; I would make request for grace from him who was against me.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If I had sent for him to be present, and he had come, I would have no faith that he would give ear to my voice.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For I would be crushed by his storm, my wounds would be increased without cause.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He would not let me take my breath, but I would be full of bitter grief.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** If it is a question of strength, he says, Here I am! and if it is a question of a cause at law, he says, Who will give me a fixed day?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Though I was in the right, he would say that I was in the wrong; I have done no evil; but he says that I am a sinner.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** I have done no wrong; I give no thought to what becomes of me; I have no desire for life.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** It is all the same to me; so I say, He puts an end to the sinner and to him who has done no wrong together.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If death comes suddenly through disease, he makes sport of the fate of those who have done no wrong.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The land is given into the power of the evil-doer; the faces of its judges are covered; if not by him, then who has done it?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** My days go quicker than a post-runner: they go in flight, they see no good.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** They go rushing on like reed-boats, like an eagle dropping suddenly on its food.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** If I say, I will put my grief out of mind, I will let my face be sad no longer and I will be bright;

<a id="v28"></a>**28** I go in fear of all my pains; I am certain that I will not be free from sin in your eyes.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** You will not let me be clear of sin! why then do I take trouble for nothing?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** If I am washed with snow water, and make my hands clean with soap;

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Then you will have me pushed into the dust, so that I will seem disgusting to my very clothing.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For he is not a man as I am, that I might give him an answer, that we might come together before a judge.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** There is no one to give a decision between us, who might have control over us.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Let him take away his rod from me and not send his fear on me:

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Then I would say what is in my mind without fear of him; for there is no cause of fear in myself.
