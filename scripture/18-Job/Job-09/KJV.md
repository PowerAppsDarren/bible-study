# Job 9 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Job answered and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** I know it is so of a truth: but how should man be just with God?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** If he will contend with him, he cannot answer him one of a thousand.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** He is wise in heart, and mighty in strength: who hath hardened himself against him, and hath prospered?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Which removeth the mountains, and they know not: which overturneth them in his anger.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Which shaketh the earth out of her place, and the pillars thereof tremble.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Which commandeth the sun, and it riseth not; and sealeth up the stars.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Which alone spreadeth out the heavens, and treadeth upon the waves of the sea.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Which maketh Arcturus, Orion, and Pleiades, and the chambers of the south.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Which doeth great things past finding out; yea, and wonders without number.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Lo, he goeth by me, and I see him not: he passeth on also, but I perceive him not.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Behold, he taketh away, who can hinder him? who will say unto him, What doest thou?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** If God will not withdraw his anger, the proud helpers do stoop under him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** How much less shall I answer him, and choose out my words to reason with him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Whom, though I were righteous, yet would I not answer, but I would make supplication to my judge.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If I had called, and he had answered me; yet would I not believe that he had hearkened unto my voice.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For he breaketh me with a tempest, and multiplieth my wounds without cause.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He will not suffer me to take my breath, but filleth me with bitterness.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** If I speak of strength, lo, he is strong: and if of judgment, who shall set me a time to plead?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** If I justify myself, mine own mouth shall condemn me: if I say, I am perfect, it shall also prove me perverse.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Though I were perfect, yet would I not know my soul: I would despise my life.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** This is one thing, therefore I said it, He destroyeth the perfect and the wicked.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If the scourge slay suddenly, he will laugh at the trial of the innocent.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The earth is given into the hand of the wicked: he covereth the faces of the judges thereof; if not, where, and who is he?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Now my days are swifter than a post: they flee away, they see no good.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** They are passed away as the swift ships: as the eagle that hasteth to the prey.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** If I say, I will forget my complaint, I will leave off my heaviness, and comfort myself:

<a id="v28"></a>**28** I am afraid of all my sorrows, I know that thou wilt not hold me innocent.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** If I be wicked, why then labour I in vain?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** If I wash myself with snow water, and make my hands never so clean;

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Yet shalt thou plunge me in the ditch, and mine own clothes shall abhor me.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** For he is not a man, as I am, that I should answer him, and we should come together in judgment.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Neither is there any daysman betwixt us, that might lay his hand upon us both.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Let him take his rod away from me, and let not his fear terrify me:

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Then would I speak, and not fear him; but it is not so with me.
