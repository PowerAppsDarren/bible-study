# Job 20 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Zophar the Naamathite made answer and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For this cause my thoughts are troubling me and driving me on.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I have to give ear to arguments which put me to shame, and your answers to me are wind without wisdom.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Have you knowledge of this from early times, when man was placed on the earth,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** That the pride of the sinner is short, and the joy of the evil-doer but for a minute?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Though he is lifted up to the heavens, and his head goes up to the clouds;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Like the waste from his body he comes to an end for ever: those who have seen him say, Where is he?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He is gone like a dream, and is not seen again; he goes in flight like a vision of the night.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The eye which saw him sees him no longer; and his place has no more knowledge of him.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** His children are hoping that the poor will be kind to them, and his hands give back his wealth.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** His bones are full of young strength, but it will go down with him into the dust.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Though evil-doing is sweet in his mouth, and he keeps it secretly under his tongue;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Though he takes care of it, and does not let it go, but keeps it still in his mouth;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** His food becomes bitter in his stomach; the poison of snakes is inside him.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He takes down wealth as food, and sends it up again; it is forced out of his stomach by God.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He takes the poison of snakes into his mouth, the tongue of the snake is the cause of his death.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Let him not see the rivers of oil, the streams of honey and milk.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He is forced to give back the fruit of his work, and may not take it for food; he has no joy in the profit of his trading.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Because he has been cruel to the poor, turning away from them in their trouble; because he has taken a house by force which he did not put up;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** There is no peace for him in his wealth, and no salvation for him in those things in which he took delight.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He had never enough for his desire; for this cause his well-being will quickly come to an end.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Even when his wealth is great, he is full of care, for the hand of everyone who is in trouble is turned against him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** God gives him his desire, and sends the heat of his wrath on him, making it come down on him like rain.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He may go in flight from the iron spear, but the arrow from the bow of brass will go through him;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** He is pulling it out, and it comes out of his back; and its shining point comes out of his side; he is overcome by fears.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** All his wealth is stored up for the dark: a fire not made by man sends destruction on him, and on everything in his tent.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The heavens make clear his sin, and the earth gives witness against him.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The produce of his house is taken away into another country, like things given into the hands of others in the day of wrath.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** This is the reward of the evil man, and the heritage given to him by God.
