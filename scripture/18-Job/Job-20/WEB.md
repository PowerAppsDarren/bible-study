# Job 20 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Zophar the Naamathite answered,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Therefore do my thoughts give answer to me, even by reason of my haste that is in me.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I have heard the reproof which puts me to shame. The spirit of my understanding answers me.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Don’t you know this from old time, since man was placed on earth,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** that the triumphing of the wicked is short, the joy of the godless but for a moment?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Though his height mount up to the heavens, and his head reach to the clouds,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** yet he shall perish forever like his own dung. Those who have seen him shall say, ‘Where is he?’

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He shall fly away as a dream, and shall not be found. Yes, he shall be chased away like a vision of the night.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The eye which saw him shall see him no more, neither shall his place any more see him.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** His children shall seek the favor of the poor. His hands shall give back his wealth.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** His bones are full of his youth, but youth shall lie down with him in the dust.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** “Though wickedness is sweet in his mouth, though he hide it under his tongue,

<a id="v13"></a>**13** though he spare it, and will not let it go, but keep it still within his mouth;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** yet his food in his bowels is turned. It is cobra venom within him.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He has swallowed down riches, and he shall vomit them up again. God will cast them out of his belly.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He shall suck cobra venom. The viper’s tongue shall kill him.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He shall not look at the rivers, the flowing streams of honey and butter.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** That for which he labored he shall restore, and shall not swallow it down. According to the substance that he has gotten, he shall not rejoice.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For he has oppressed and forsaken the poor. He has violently taken away a house, and he shall not build it up.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** “Because he knew no quietness within him, he shall not save anything of that in which he delights.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** There was nothing left that he didn’t devour, therefore his prosperity shall not endure.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** In the fullness of his sufficiency, distress shall overtake him. The hand of everyone who is in misery shall come on him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** When he is about to fill his belly, God will cast the fierceness of his wrath on him. It will rain on him while he is eating.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He shall flee from the iron weapon. The bronze arrow shall strike him through.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** He draws it out, and it comes out of his body. Yes, the glittering point comes out of his liver. Terrors are on him.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** All darkness is laid up for his treasures. An unfanned fire shall devour him. It shall consume that which is left in his tent.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The heavens shall reveal his iniquity. The earth shall rise up against him.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The increase of his house shall depart. They shall rush away in the day of his wrath.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** This is the portion of a wicked man from God, the heritage appointed to him by God.”
