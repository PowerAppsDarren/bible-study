# Job 20 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then answered Zophar the Naamathite, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Therefore do my thoughts cause me to answer, and for this I make haste.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I have heard the check of my reproach, and the spirit of my understanding causeth me to answer.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Knowest thou not this of old, since man was placed upon earth,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** That the triumphing of the wicked is short, and the joy of the hypocrite but for a moment?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Though his excellency mount up to the heavens, and his head reach unto the clouds;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Yet he shall perish for ever like his own dung: they which have seen him shall say, Where is he?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He shall fly away as a dream, and shall not be found: yea, he shall be chased away as a vision of the night.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The eye also which saw him shall see him no more; neither shall his place any more behold him.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** His children shall seek to please the poor, and his hands shall restore their goods.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** His bones are full of the sin of his youth, which shall lie down with him in the dust.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Though wickedness be sweet in his mouth, though he hide it under his tongue;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Though he spare it, and forsake it not; but keep it still within his mouth:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Yet his meat in his bowels is turned, it is the gall of asps within him.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He hath swallowed down riches, and he shall vomit them up again: God shall cast them out of his belly.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He shall suck the poison of asps: the viper’s tongue shall slay him.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** He shall not see the rivers, the floods, the brooks of honey and butter.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** That which he laboured for shall he restore, and shall not swallow it down: according to his substance shall the restitution be, and he shall not rejoice therein.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Because he hath oppressed and hath forsaken the poor; because he hath violently taken away an house which he builded not;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Surely he shall not feel quietness in his belly, he shall not save of that which he desired.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** There shall none of his meat be left; therefore shall no man look for his goods.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** In the fulness of his sufficiency he shall be in straits: every hand of the wicked shall come upon him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** When he is about to fill his belly, God shall cast the fury of his wrath upon him, and shall rain it upon him while he is eating.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He shall flee from the iron weapon, and the bow of steel shall strike him through.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** It is drawn, and cometh out of the body; yea, the glittering sword cometh out of his gall: terrors are upon him.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** All darkness shall be hid in his secret places: a fire not blown shall consume him; it shall go ill with him that is left in his tabernacle.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** The heaven shall reveal his iniquity; and the earth shall rise up against him.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The increase of his house shall depart, and his goods shall flow away in the day of his wrath.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** This is the portion of a wicked man from God, and the heritage appointed unto him by God.
