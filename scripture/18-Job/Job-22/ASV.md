# Job 22 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then answered Eliphaz the Temanite, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Can a man be profitable unto God? Surely he that is wise is profitable unto himself.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Is it any pleasure to the Almighty, that thou art righteous? Or is it gainto him, that thou makest thy ways perfect?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Is it for thy fear of him that he reproveth thee, That he entereth with thee into judgment?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Is not thy wickedness great? Neither is there any end to thine iniquities.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For thou hast taken pledges of thy brother for nought, And stripped the naked of their clothing.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Thou hast not given water to the weary to drink, And thou hast withholden bread from the hungry.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But as for the mighty man, he had the earth; And the honorable man, he dwelt in it.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Thou hast sent widows away empty, And the arms of the fatherless have been broken.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Therefore snares are round about thee, And sudden fear troubleth thee,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Or darkness, so that thou canst not see, And abundance of waters cover thee.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Is not God in the height of heaven? And behold the height of the stars, how high they are!

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And thou sayest, What doth God know? Can he judge through the thick darkness?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Thick clouds are a covering to him, so that he seeth not; And he walketh on the vault of heaven.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Wilt thou keep the old way Which wicked men have trodden?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Who were snatched away before their time, Whose foundation was poured out as a stream,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Who said unto God, Depart from us; And, What can the Almighty do for us?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Yet he filled their houses with good things: But the counsel of the wicked is far from me.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The righteous see it, and are glad; And the innocent laugh them to scorn,

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Saying, Surely they that did rise up against us are cut off, And the remnant of them the fire hath consumed.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Acquaint now thyself with him, and be at peace: Thereby good shall come unto thee.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Receive, I pray thee, the law from his mouth, And lay up his words in thy heart.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If thou return to the Almighty, thou shalt be built up, If thou put away unrighteousness far from thy tents.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And lay thou thy treasure in the dust, Andthe gold ofOphir among the stones of the brooks;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And the Almighty will be thy treasure, And precious silver unto thee.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For then shalt thou delight thyself in the Almighty, And shalt lift up thy face unto God.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Thou shalt make thy prayer unto him, and he will hear thee; And thou shalt pay thy vows.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Thou shalt also decree a thing, and it shall be established unto thee; And light shall shine upon thy ways.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** When they casttheedown, thou shalt say, There is lifting up; And the humble person he will save.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** He will deliverevenhim that is not innocent: Yea, he shall be delivered through the cleanness of thy hands.
