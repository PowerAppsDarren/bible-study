# Job 22 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Eliphaz the Temanite answered and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Can a man be profitable unto God, as he that is wise may be profitable unto himself?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Is it any pleasure to the Almighty, that thou art righteous? or is it gain to him, that thou makest thy ways perfect?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Will he reprove thee for fear of thee? will he enter with thee into judgment?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Is not thy wickedness great? and thine iniquities infinite?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For thou hast taken a pledge from thy brother for nought, and stripped the naked of their clothing.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Thou hast not given water to the weary to drink, and thou hast withholden bread from the hungry.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But as for the mighty man, he had the earth; and the honourable man dwelt in it.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Thou hast sent widows away empty, and the arms of the fatherless have been broken.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Therefore snares are round about thee, and sudden fear troubleth thee;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Or darkness, that thou canst not see; and abundance of waters cover thee.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Is not God in the height of heaven? and behold the height of the stars, how high they are!

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And thou sayest, How doth God know? can he judge through the dark cloud?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Thick clouds are a covering to him, that he seeth not; and he walketh in the circuit of heaven.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Hast thou marked the old way which wicked men have trodden?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Which were cut down out of time, whose foundation was overflown with a flood:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Which said unto God, Depart from us: and what can the Almighty do for them?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Yet he filled their houses with good things: but the counsel of the wicked is far from me.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The righteous see it, and are glad: and the innocent laugh them to scorn.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Whereas our substance is not cut down, but the remnant of them the fire consumeth.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Acquaint now thyself with him, and be at peace: thereby good shall come unto thee.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Receive, I pray thee, the law from his mouth, and lay up his words in thine heart.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If thou return to the Almighty, thou shalt be built up, thou shalt put away iniquity far from thy tabernacles.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Then shalt thou lay up gold as dust, and the gold of Ophir as the stones of the brooks.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Yea, the Almighty shall be thy defence, and thou shalt have plenty of silver.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For then shalt thou have thy delight in the Almighty, and shalt lift up thy face unto God.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Thou shalt make thy prayer unto him, and he shall hear thee, and thou shalt pay thy vows.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Thou shalt also decree a thing, and it shall be established unto thee: and the light shall shine upon thy ways.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** When men are cast down, then thou shalt say, There is lifting up; and he shall save the humble person.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** He shall deliver the island of the innocent: and it is delivered by the pureness of thine hands.
