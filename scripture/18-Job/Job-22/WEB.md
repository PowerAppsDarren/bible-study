# Job 22 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Eliphaz the Temanite answered,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Can a man be profitable to God? Surely he who is wise is profitable to himself.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Is it any pleasure to the Almighty, that you are righteous? Or does it benefit him, that you make your ways perfect?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Is it for your piety that he reproves you, that he enters with you into judgment?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Isn’t your wickedness great? Neither is there any end to your iniquities.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For you have taken pledges from your brother for nothing, and stripped the naked of their clothing.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** You haven’t given water to the weary to drink, and you have withheld bread from the hungry.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But as for the mighty man, he had the earth. The honorable man, he lived in it.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** You have sent widows away empty, and the arms of the fatherless have been broken.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Therefore snares are around you. Sudden fear troubles you,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** or darkness, so that you can not see, and floods of waters cover you.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** “Isn’t God in the heights of heaven? See the height of the stars, how high they are!

<a id="v13"></a>**13** You say, ‘What does God know? Can he judge through the thick darkness?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Thick clouds are a covering to him, so that he doesn’t see. He walks on the vault of the sky.’

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Will you keep the old way, which wicked men have trodden,

<a id="v16"></a>**16** who were snatched away before their time, whose foundation was poured out as a stream,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** who said to God, ‘Depart from us;’ and, ‘What can the Almighty do for us?’

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Yet he filled their houses with good things, but the counsel of the wicked is far from me.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The righteous see it, and are glad. The innocent ridicule them,

<a id="v20"></a>**20** saying, ‘Surely those who rose up against us are cut off. The fire has consumed their remnant.’

<a id="v21"></a>**21** “Acquaint yourself with him, now, and be at peace. Thereby good shall come to you.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Please receive instruction from his mouth, and lay up his words in your heart.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If you return to the Almighty, you shall be built up, if you put away unrighteousness far from your tents.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Lay your treasure in the dust, the gold of Ophir among the stones of the brooks.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The Almighty will be your treasure, and precious silver to you.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For then you will delight yourself in the Almighty, and shall lift up your face to God.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** You shall make your prayer to him, and he will hear you. You shall pay your vows.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** You shall also decree a thing, and it shall be established to you. Light shall shine on your ways.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** When they cast down, you shall say, ‘be lifted up.’ He will save the humble person.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** He will even deliver him who is not innocent. Yes, he shall be delivered through the cleanness of your hands.”
