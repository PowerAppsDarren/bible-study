# Job 3 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** After this Job opened his mouth, and cursed the day of his birth.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Job answered:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** “Let the day perish in which I was born, the night which said, ‘There is a boy conceived.’

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Let that day be darkness. Don’t let God from above seek for it, neither let the light shine on it.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Let darkness and the shadow of death claim it for their own. Let a cloud dwell on it. Let all that makes black the day terrify it.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** As for that night, let thick darkness seize on it. Let it not rejoice among the days of the year. Let it not come into the number of the months.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Behold, let that night be barren. Let no joyful voice come therein.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Let them curse it who curse the day, who are ready to rouse up leviathan.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Let the stars of its twilight be dark. Let it look for light, but have none, neither let it see the eyelids of the morning,

<a id="v10"></a>**10** because it didn’t shut up the doors of my mother’s womb, nor did it hide trouble from my eyes.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** “Why didn’t I die from the womb? Why didn’t I give up the spirit when my mother bore me?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Why did the knees receive me? Or why the breast, that I should nurse?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For now should I have lain down and been quiet. I should have slept, then I would have been at rest,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** with kings and counselors of the earth, who built up waste places for themselves;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** or with princes who had gold, who filled their houses with silver:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** or as a hidden untimely birth I had not been, as infants who never saw light.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** There the wicked cease from troubling. There the weary are at rest.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** There the prisoners are at ease together. They don’t hear the voice of the taskmaster.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The small and the great are there. The servant is free from his master.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** “Why is light given to him who is in misery, life to the bitter in soul,

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Who long for death, but it doesn’t come; and dig for it more than for hidden treasures,

<a id="v22"></a>**22** who rejoice exceedingly, and are glad, when they can find the grave?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Why is light given to a man whose way is hidden, whom God has hedged in?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For my sighing comes before I eat. My groanings are poured out like water.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For the thing which I fear comes on me, That which I am afraid of comes to me.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** I am not at ease, neither am I quiet, neither have I rest; but trouble comes.”
