# Job 3 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** After this opened Job his mouth, and cursed his day.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And Job spake, and said,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Let the day perish wherein I was born, and the night in which it was said, There is a man child conceived.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Let that day be darkness; let not God regard it from above, neither let the light shine upon it.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Let darkness and the shadow of death stain it; let a cloud dwell upon it; let the blackness of the day terrify it.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** As for that night, let darkness seize upon it; let it not be joined unto the days of the year, let it not come into the number of the months.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Lo, let that night be solitary, let no joyful voice come therein.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Let them curse it that curse the day, who are ready to raise up their mourning.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Let the stars of the twilight thereof be dark; let it look for light, but have none; neither let it see the dawning of the day:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Because it shut not up the doors of my mother’s womb, nor hid sorrow from mine eyes.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Why died I not from the womb? why did I not give up the ghost when I came out of the belly?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Why did the knees prevent me? or why the breasts that I should suck?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For now should I have lain still and been quiet, I should have slept: then had I been at rest,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** With kings and counsellors of the earth, which built desolate places for themselves;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Or with princes that had gold, who filled their houses with silver:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Or as an hidden untimely birth I had not been; as infants which never saw light.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** There the wicked cease from troubling; and there the weary be at rest.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** There the prisoners rest together; they hear not the voice of the oppressor.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The small and great are there; and the servant is free from his master.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Wherefore is light given to him that is in misery, and life unto the bitter in soul;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Which long for death, but it cometh not; and dig for it more than for hid treasures;

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Which rejoice exceedingly, and are glad, when they can find the grave?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Why is light given to a man whose way is hid, and whom God hath hedged in?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For my sighing cometh before I eat, and my roarings are poured out like the waters.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For the thing which I greatly feared is come upon me, and that which I was afraid of is come unto me.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** I was not in safety, neither had I rest, neither was I quiet; yet trouble came.
