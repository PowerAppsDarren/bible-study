# Job 3 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then, opening his mouth, and cursing the day of his birth,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Job made answer and said,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Let destruction take the day of my birth, and the night on which it was said, A man child has come into the world.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** That day--let it be dark; let not God take note of it from on high, and let not the light be shining on it;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Let the dark and the black night take it for themselves; let it be covered with a cloud; let the dark shades of day send fear on it.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** That night--let the thick dark take it; let it not have joy among the days of the year; let it not come into the number of the months.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** As for that night, let it have no fruit; let no voice of joy be sounded in it;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Let it be cursed by those who put a curse on the day; who are ready to make Leviathan awake.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Let its morning stars be dark; let it be looking for light, but may it not have any; let it not see the eyes of the dawn.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Because it did not keep the doors of my mother's body shut, so that trouble might be veiled from my eyes.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Why did death not take me when I came out of my mother's body, why did I not, when I came out, give up my last breath?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Why did the knees take me, or why the breasts that they might give me milk?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For then I might have gone to my rest in quiet, and in sleep have been in peace,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** With kings and the wise ones of the earth, who put up great houses for themselves;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Or with rulers who had gold, and whose houses were full of silver;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Or as a child dead at birth I might never have come into existence; like young children who have not seen the light.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** There the passions of the evil are over, and those whose strength has come to an end have rest.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** There the prisoners are at peace together; the voice of the overseer comes not again to their ears.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The small and the great are there, and the servant is free from his master.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Why does he give light to him who is in trouble, and life to the bitter in soul;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** To those whose desire is for death, but it comes not; who are searching for it more than for secret wealth;

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Who are glad with great joy, and full of delight when they come to their last resting-place;

<a id="v23"></a>**23** To a man whose way is veiled, and who is shut in by God?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** In place of my food I have grief, and cries of sorrow come from me like water.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For I have a fear and it comes on me, and my heart is greatly troubled.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** I have no peace, no quiet, and no rest; nothing but pain comes on me.
