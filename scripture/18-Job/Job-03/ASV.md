# Job 3 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** After this opened Job his mouth, and cursed his day.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And Job answered and said:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Let the day perish wherein I was born, And the night which said, There is a man-child conceived.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Let that day be darkness; Let not God from above seek for it, Neither let the light shine upon it.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Let darkness and the shadow of death claim it for their own; Let a cloud dwell upon it; Let all that maketh black the day terrify it.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** As for that night, let thick darkness seize upon it: Let it not rejoice among the days of the year; Let it not come into the number of the months.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Lo, let that night be barren; Let no joyful voice come therein.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Let them curse it that curse the day, Who are ready to rouse up leviathan.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Let the stars of the twilight thereof be dark: Let it look for light, but have none; Neither let it behold the eyelids of the morning:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Because it shut not up the doors of mymother’swomb, Nor hid trouble from mine eyes.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Why died I not from the womb? Why did I not give up the ghost when my mother bare me?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Why did the knees receive me? Or why the breasts, that I should suck?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For now should I have lain down and been quiet; I should have slept; then had I been at rest,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** With kings and counsellors of the earth, Who built up waste places for themselves;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Or with princes that had gold, Who filled their houses with silver:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Or as a hidden untimely birth I had not been, As infants that never saw light.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** There the wicked cease from troubling; And there the weary are at rest.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** There the prisoners are at ease together; They hear not the voice of the taskmaster.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The small and the great are there: And the servant is free from his master.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Wherefore is light given to him that is in misery, And life unto the bitter in soul;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Who long for death, but it cometh not, And dig for it more than for hid treasures;

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Who rejoice exceedingly, And are glad, when they can find the grave?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Why is light givento a man whose way is hid, And whom God hath hedged in?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For my sighing cometh before I eat, And my groanings are poured out like water.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For the thing which I fear cometh upon me, And that which I am afraid of cometh unto me.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** I am not at ease, neither am I quiet, neither have I rest; But trouble cometh.
