# Job 36 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Elihu also proceeded, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Suffer me a little, and I will shew thee that I have yet to speak on God’s behalf.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I will fetch my knowledge from afar, and will ascribe righteousness to my Maker.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For truly my words shall not be false: he that is perfect in knowledge is with thee.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Behold, God is mighty, and despiseth not any: he is mighty in strength and wisdom.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He preserveth not the life of the wicked: but giveth right to the poor.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He withdraweth not his eyes from the righteous: but with kings are they on the throne; yea, he doth establish them for ever, and they are exalted.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And if they be bound in fetters, and be holden in cords of affliction;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Then he sheweth them their work, and their transgressions that they have exceeded.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He openeth also their ear to discipline, and commandeth that they return from iniquity.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** If they obey and serve him, they shall spend their days in prosperity, and their years in pleasures.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** But if they obey not, they shall perish by the sword, and they shall die without knowledge.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But the hypocrites in heart heap up wrath: they cry not when he bindeth them.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** They die in youth, and their life is among the unclean.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He delivereth the poor in his affliction, and openeth their ears in oppression.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Even so would he have removed thee out of the strait into a broad place, where there is no straitness; and that which should be set on thy table should be full of fatness.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** But thou hast fulfilled the judgment of the wicked: judgment and justice take hold on thee.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Because there is wrath, beware lest he take thee away with his stroke: then a great ransom cannot deliver thee.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Will he esteem thy riches? no, not gold, nor all the forces of strength.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Desire not the night, when people are cut off in their place.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Take heed, regard not iniquity: for this hast thou chosen rather than affliction.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Behold, God exalteth by his power: who teacheth like him?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Who hath enjoined him his way? or who can say, Thou hast wrought iniquity?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Remember that thou magnify his work, which men behold.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Every man may see it; man may behold it afar off.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Behold, God is great, and we know him not, neither can the number of his years be searched out.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** For he maketh small the drops of water: they pour down rain according to the vapour thereof:

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Which the clouds do drop and distil upon man abundantly.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Also can any understand the spreadings of the clouds, or the noise of his tabernacle?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Behold, he spreadeth his light upon it, and covereth the bottom of the sea.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** For by them judgeth he the people; he giveth meat in abundance.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** With clouds he covereth the light; and commandeth it not to shine by the cloud that cometh betwixt.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The noise thereof sheweth concerning it, the cattle also concerning the vapour.
