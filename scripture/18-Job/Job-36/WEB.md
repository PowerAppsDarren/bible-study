# Job 36 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Elihu also continued, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Bear with me a little, and I will show you; for I still have something to say on God’s behalf.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I will get my knowledge from afar, and will ascribe righteousness to my Maker.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For truly my words are not false. One who is perfect in knowledge is with you.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** “Behold, God is mighty, and doesn’t despise anyone. He is mighty in strength of understanding.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He doesn’t preserve the life of the wicked, but gives to the afflicted their right.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He doesn’t withdraw his eyes from the righteous, but with kings on the throne, he sets them forever, and they are exalted.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** If they are bound in fetters, and are taken in the cords of afflictions,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** then he shows them their work, and their transgressions, that they have behaved themselves proudly.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He also opens their ears to instruction, and commands that they return from iniquity.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** If they listen and serve him, they shall spend their days in prosperity, and their years in pleasures.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** But if they don’t listen, they shall perish by the sword; they shall die without knowledge.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** “But those who are godless in heart lay up anger. They don’t cry for help when he binds them.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** They die in youth. Their life perishes among the unclean.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He delivers the afflicted by their affliction, and opens their ear in oppression.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Yes, he would have allured you out of distress, into a broad place, where there is no restriction. That which is set on your table would be full of fatness.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** “But you are full of the judgment of the wicked. Judgment and justice take hold of you.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Don’t let riches entice you to wrath, neither let the great size of a bribe turn you aside.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Would your wealth sustain you in distress, or all the might of your strength?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Don’t desire the night, when people are cut off in their place.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Take heed, don’t regard iniquity; for you have chosen this rather than affliction.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Behold, God is exalted in his power. Who is a teacher like him?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Who has prescribed his way for him? Or who can say, ‘You have committed unrighteousness?’

<a id="v24"></a>**24** “Remember that you magnify his work, whereof men have sung.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** All men have looked on it. Man sees it afar off.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Behold, God is great, and we don’t know him. The number of his years is unsearchable.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** For he draws up the drops of water, which distill in rain from his vapor,

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Which the skies pour down and which drop on man abundantly.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Yes, can any understand the spreading of the clouds, and the thunderings of his pavilion?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Behold, he spreads his light around him. He covers the bottom of the sea.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** For by these he judges the people. He gives food in abundance.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He covers his hands with the lightning, and commands it to strike the mark.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Its noise tells about him, and the livestock also concerning the storm that comes up.
