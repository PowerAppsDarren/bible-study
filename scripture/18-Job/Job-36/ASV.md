# Job 36 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Elihu also proceeded, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Suffer me a little, and I will show thee; For I have yet somewhat to say on God’s behalf.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I will fetch my knowledge from afar, And will ascribe righteousness to my Maker.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For truly my words are not false: One that is perfect in knowledge is with thee.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Behold, God is mighty, and despiseth not any: He is mighty in strength of understanding.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** He preserveth not the life of the wicked, But giveth to the afflicted their right.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He withdraweth not his eyes from the righteous:  But with kings upon the throne  He setteth them for ever, and they are exalted.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And if they be bound in fetters, And be taken in the cords of affliction;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Then he showeth them their work, And their transgressions, that they have behaved themselves proudly.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He openeth also their ear to instruction, And commandeth that they return from iniquity.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** If they hearken and serve him, They shall spend their days in prosperity, And their years in pleasures.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** But if they hearken not, they shall perish by the sword, And they shall die without knowledge.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But they that are godless in heart lay up anger: They cry not for help when he bindeth them.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** They die in youth, And their lifeperisheth among the unclean.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** He delivereth the afflicted by their affliction, And openeth their ear in oppression.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Yea, he would have allured thee out of distress Into a broad place, where there is no straitness; And that which is set on thy table would be full of fatness.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** But thou art full of the judgment of the wicked: Judgment and justice take holdon thee.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For let not wrath stir thee up against chastisements; Neither let the greatness of the ransom turn thee aside.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Will thy cry avail, that thou be not in distress, Or all the forces ofthystrength?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Desire not the night, When peoples are cut off in their place.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Take heed, regard not iniquity: For this hast thou chosen rather than affliction.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Behold, God doeth loftily in his power: Who is a teacher like unto him?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Who hath enjoined him his way? Or who can say, Thou hast wrought unrighteousness?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Remember that thou magnify his work, Whereof men have sung.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** All men have looked thereon; Man beholdeth it afar off.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Behold, God is great, and we know him not; The number of his years is unsearchable.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** For he draweth up the drops of water, Which distil in rain from his vapor,

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Which the skies pour down  And drop upon man abundantly.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Yea, can any understand the spreadings of the clouds, The thunderings of his pavilion?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Behold, he spreadeth his light around him; And he covereth the bottom of the sea.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** For by these he judgeth the peoples; He giveth food in abundance.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He covereth his hands with the lightning, And giveth it a charge that it strike the mark.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The noise thereof telleth concerning him, The cattle also concerningthe stormthat cometh up.
