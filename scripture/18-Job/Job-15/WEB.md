# Job 15 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Eliphaz the Temanite answered,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Should a wise man answer with vain knowledge, and fill himself with the east wind?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Should he reason with unprofitable talk, or with speeches with which he can do no good?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Yes, you do away with fear, and hinder devotion before God.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For your iniquity teaches your mouth, and you choose the language of the crafty.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Your own mouth condemns you, and not I. Yes, your own lips testify against you.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** “Are you the first man who was born? Or were you brought out before the hills?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Have you heard the secret counsel of God? Do you limit wisdom to yourself?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** What do you know, that we don’t know? What do you understand, which is not in us?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** With us are both the gray-headed and the very aged men, much elder than your father.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Are the consolations of God too small for you, even the word that is gentle toward you?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Why does your heart carry you away? Why do your eyes flash,

<a id="v13"></a>**13** That you turn your spirit against God, and let such words go out of your mouth?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** What is man, that he should be clean? What is he who is born of a woman, that he should be righteous?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Behold, he puts no trust in his holy ones. Yes, the heavens are not clean in his sight;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** how much less one who is abominable and corrupt, a man who drinks iniquity like water!

<a id="v17"></a>**17** “I will show you, listen to me; that which I have seen I will declare:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** (Which wise men have told by their fathers, and have not hidden it;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** to whom alone the land was given, and no stranger passed among them):

<a id="v20"></a>**20** the wicked man writhes in pain all his days, even the number of years that are laid up for the oppressor.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** A sound of terrors is in his ears. In prosperity the destroyer shall come on him.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He doesn’t believe that he shall return out of darkness. He is waited for by the sword.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He wanders abroad for bread, saying, ‘Where is it?’ He knows that the day of darkness is ready at his hand.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Distress and anguish make him afraid. They prevail against him, as a king ready to the battle.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Because he has stretched out his hand against God, and behaves himself proudly against the Almighty;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** he runs at him with a stiff neck, with the thick shields of his bucklers;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** because he has covered his face with his fatness, and gathered fat on his thighs.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** He has lived in desolate cities, in houses which no one inhabited, which were ready to become heaps.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** He shall not be rich, neither shall his substance continue, neither shall their possessions be extended on the earth.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** He shall not depart out of darkness. The flame shall dry up his branches. By the breath of God’s mouth shall he go away.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Let him not trust in emptiness, deceiving himself; for emptiness shall be his reward.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** It shall be accomplished before his time. His branch shall not be green.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** He shall shake off his unripe grape as the vine, and shall cast off his flower as the olive tree.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** For the company of the godless shall be barren, and fire shall consume the tents of bribery.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** They conceive mischief, and produce iniquity. Their heart prepares deceit.”
