# Job 15 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then answered Eliphaz the Temanite, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Should a wise man utter vain knowledge, and fill his belly with the east wind?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Should he reason with unprofitable talk? or with speeches wherewith he can do no good?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Yea, thou castest off fear, and restrainest prayer before God.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For thy mouth uttereth thine iniquity, and thou choosest the tongue of the crafty.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Thine own mouth condemneth thee, and not I: yea, thine own lips testify against thee.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Art thou the first man that was born? or wast thou made before the hills?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Hast thou heard the secret of God? and dost thou restrain wisdom to thyself?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** What knowest thou, that we know not? what understandest thou, which is not in us?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** With us are both the grayheaded and very aged men, much elder than thy father.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Are the consolations of God small with thee? is there any secret thing with thee?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Why doth thine heart carry thee away? and what do thy eyes wink at,

<a id="v13"></a>**13** That thou turnest thy spirit against God, and lettest such words go out of thy mouth?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** What is man, that he should be clean? and he which is born of a woman, that he should be righteous?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Behold, he putteth no trust in his saints; yea, the heavens are not clean in his sight.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** How much more abominable and filthy is man, which drinketh iniquity like water?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** I will shew thee, hear me; and that which I have seen I will declare;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Which wise men have told from their fathers, and have not hid it:

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Unto whom alone the earth was given, and no stranger passed among them.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The wicked man travaileth with pain all his days, and the number of years is hidden to the oppressor.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** A dreadful sound is in his ears: in prosperity the destroyer shall come upon him.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He believeth not that he shall return out of darkness, and he is waited for of the sword.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He wandereth abroad for bread, saying, Where is it? he knoweth that the day of darkness is ready at his hand.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Trouble and anguish shall make him afraid; they shall prevail against him, as a king ready to the battle.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For he stretcheth out his hand against God, and strengtheneth himself against the Almighty.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He runneth upon him, even on his neck, upon the thick bosses of his bucklers:

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Because he covereth his face with his fatness, and maketh collops of fat on his flanks.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And he dwelleth in desolate cities, and in houses which no man inhabiteth, which are ready to become heaps.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** He shall not be rich, neither shall his substance continue, neither shall he prolong the perfection thereof upon the earth.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** He shall not depart out of darkness; the flame shall dry up his branches, and by the breath of his mouth shall he go away.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Let not him that is deceived trust in vanity: for vanity shall be his recompence.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** It shall be accomplished before his time, and his branch shall not be green.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** He shall shake off his unripe grape as the vine, and shall cast off his flower as the olive.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** For the congregation of hypocrites shall be desolate, and fire shall consume the tabernacles of bribery.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** They conceive mischief, and bring forth vanity, and their belly prepareth deceit.
