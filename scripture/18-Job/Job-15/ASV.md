# Job 15 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then answered Eliphaz the Temanite, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Should a wise man make answer with vain knowledge, And fill himself with the east wind?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Should he reason with unprofitable talk, Or with speeches wherewith he can do no good?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Yea, thou doest away with fear, And hinderest devotion before God.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For thine iniquity teacheth thy mouth, And thou choosest the tongue of the crafty.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Thine own mouth condemneth thee, and not I; Yea, thine own lips testify against thee.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Art thou the first man that was born? Or wast thou brought forth before the hills?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Hast thou heard the secret counsel of God? And dost thou limit wisdom to thyself?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** What knowest thou, that we know not? What understandest thou, which is not in us?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** With us are both the gray-headed and the very aged men, Much elder than thy father.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Are the consolations of God too small for thee, Even the word that is gentle toward thee?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Why doth thy heart carry thee away? And why do thine eyes flash,

<a id="v13"></a>**13** That against God thou turnest thy spirit, And lettest words go out of thy mouth?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** What is man, that he should be clean? And he that is born of a woman, that he should be righteous?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Behold, he putteth no trust in his holy ones; Yea, the heavens are not clean in his sight:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** How much less one that is abominable and corrupt, A man that drinketh iniquity like water!

<a id="v17"></a>**17** I will show thee, hear thou me;  And that which I have seen I will declare

<a id="v18"></a>**18** (Which wise men have told From their fathers, and have not hid it;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Unto whom alone the land was given, And no stranger passed among them):

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The wicked man travaileth with pain all his days, Even the number of years that are laid up for the oppressor.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** A sound of terrors is in his ears; In prosperity the destroyer shall come upon him.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He believeth not that he shall return out of darkness, And he is waited for of the sword.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** He wandereth abroad for bread, saying, Where is it? He knoweth that the day of darkness is ready at his hand.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Distress and anguish make him afraid; They prevail against him, as a king ready to the battle.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Because he hath stretched out his hand against God, And behaveth himself proudly against the Almighty;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He runneth upon him with astiffneck, With the thick bosses of his bucklers;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Because he hath covered his face with his fatness, And gathered fat upon his loins;

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And he hath dwelt in desolate cities, In houses which no man inhabited, Which were ready to become heaps;

<a id="v29"></a>**29** He shall not be rich, neither shall his substance continue, Neither shall their possessions be extended on the earth.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** He shall not depart out of darkness; The flame shall dry up his branches, And by the breath ofGod’smouth shall he go away.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Let him not trust in vanity, deceiving himself; For vanity shall be his recompense.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** It shall be accomplished before his time, And his branch shall not be green.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** He shall shake off his unripe grape as the vine, And shall cast off his flower as the olive-tree.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** For the company of the godless shall be barren, And fire shall consume the tents of bribery.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** They conceive mischief, and bring forth iniquity, And their heart prepareth deceit.
