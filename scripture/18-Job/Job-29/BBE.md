# Job 29 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Job again took up the word and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** If only I might again be as I was in the months which are past, in the days when God was watching over me!

<a id="v3"></a>**3** When his light was shining over my head, and when I went through the dark by his light.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** As I was in my flowering years, when my tent was covered by the hand of God;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** While the Ruler of all was still with me, and my children were round me;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** When my steps were washed with milk, and rivers of oil were flowing out of the rock for me.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** When I went out of my door to go up to the town, and took my seat in the public place,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The young men saw me, and went away, and the old men got up from their seats;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The rulers kept quiet, and put their hands on their mouths;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The chiefs kept back their words, and their tongues were joined to the roofs of their mouths.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For when it came to their ears, men said that I was truly happy; and when their eyes saw, they gave witness to me;

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For I was a saviour to the poor when he was crying for help, to the child with no father, and to him who had no supporter.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The blessing of him who was near to destruction came on me, and I put a song of joy into the widow's heart.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I put on righteousness as my clothing, and was full of it; right decisions were to me a robe and a head-dress.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** I was eyes to the blind, and feet to him who had no power of walking.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I was a father to the poor, searching out the cause of him who was strange to me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** By me the great teeth of the evil-doer were broken, and I made him give up what he had violently taken away.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Then I said, I will come to my end with my children round me, my days will be as the sand in number;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** My root will be open to the waters, and the night mist will be on my branches,

<a id="v20"></a>**20** My glory will be ever new, and my bow will be readily bent in my hand.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Men gave ear to me, waiting and keeping quiet for my suggestions.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** After I had said what was in my mind, they were quiet and let my words go deep into their hearts;

<a id="v23"></a>**23** They were waiting for me as for the rain, opening their mouths wide as for the spring rains.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** I was laughing at them when they had no hope, and the light of my face was never clouded by their fear.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** I took my place as a chief, guiding them on their way, and I was as a king among his army. ...
