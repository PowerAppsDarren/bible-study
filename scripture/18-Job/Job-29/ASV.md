# Job 29 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Job again took up his parable, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Oh that I were as in the months of old, As in the days when God watched over me;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** When his lamp shined upon my head, And by his light I walked through darkness;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** As I was in the ripeness of my days, When the friendship of God was upon my tent;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** When the Almighty was yet with me, And my children were about me;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** When my steps were washed with butter, And the rock poured me out streams of oil!

<a id="v7"></a>**7** When I went forth to the gate unto the city, When I prepared my seat in the street,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The young men saw me and hid themselves, And the aged rose up and stood;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The princes refrained from talking, And laid their hand on their mouth;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The voice of the nobles was hushed, And their tongue cleaved to the roof of their mouth.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For when the ear heardme, then it blessed me; And when the eye sawme, it gave witness unto me:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Because I delivered the poor that cried, The fatherless also, that had none to help him.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The blessing of him that was ready to perish came upon me; And I caused the widow’s heart to sing for joy.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I put on righteousness, and it clothed me: My justice was as a robe and a diadem.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** I was eyes to the blind, And feet was I to the lame.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I was a father to the needy: And the cause of him that I knew not I searched out.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And I brake the jaws of the unrighteous, And plucked the prey out of his teeth.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Then I said, I shall die in my nest, And I shall multiply my days as the sand:

<a id="v19"></a>**19** My root is spread out to the waters, And the dew lieth all night upon my branch:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** My glory is fresh in me, And my bow is renewed in my hand.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Unto me men gave ear, and waited, And kept silence for my counsel.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** After my words they spake not again; And my speech distilled upon them.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And they waited for me as for the rain; And they opened their mouth wideasfor the latter rain.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** I smiled on them, when they had no confidence; And the light of my countenance they cast not down.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** I chose out their way, and sataschief, And dwelt as a king in the army, As one that comforteth the mourners.
