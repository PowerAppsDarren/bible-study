# Job 29 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Moreover Job continued his parable, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Oh that I were as in months past, as in the days when God preserved me;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** When his candle shined upon my head, and when by his light I walked through darkness;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** As I was in the days of my youth, when the secret of God was upon my tabernacle;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** When the Almighty was yet with me, when my children were about me;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** When I washed my steps with butter, and the rock poured me out rivers of oil;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** When I went out to the gate through the city, when I prepared my seat in the street!

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The young men saw me, and hid themselves: and the aged arose, and stood up.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The princes refrained talking, and laid their hand on their mouth.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The nobles held their peace, and their tongue cleaved to the roof of their mouth.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** When the ear heard me, then it blessed me; and when the eye saw me, it gave witness to me:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Because I delivered the poor that cried, and the fatherless, and him that had none to help him.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The blessing of him that was ready to perish came upon me: and I caused the widow’s heart to sing for joy.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I put on righteousness, and it clothed me: my judgment was as a robe and a diadem.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** I was eyes to the blind, and feet was I to the lame.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I was a father to the poor: and the cause which I knew not I searched out.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And I brake the jaws of the wicked, and plucked the spoil out of his teeth.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Then I said, I shall die in my nest, and I shall multiply my days as the sand.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** My root was spread out by the waters, and the dew lay all night upon my branch.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** My glory was fresh in me, and my bow was renewed in my hand.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Unto me men gave ear, and waited, and kept silence at my counsel.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** After my words they spake not again; and my speech dropped upon them.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And they waited for me as for the rain; and they opened their mouth wide as for the latter rain.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** If I laughed on them, they believed it not; and the light of my countenance they cast not down.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** I chose out their way, and sat chief, and dwelt as a king in the army, as one that comforteth the mourners.
