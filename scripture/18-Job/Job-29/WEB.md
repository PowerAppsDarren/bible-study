# Job 29 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Job again took up his parable, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Oh that I were as in the months of old, as in the days when God watched over me;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** when his lamp shone on my head, and by his light I walked through darkness,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** as I was in the ripeness of my days, when the friendship of God was in my tent,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** when the Almighty was yet with me, and my children were around me,

<a id="v6"></a>**6** when my steps were washed with butter, and the rock poured out streams of oil for me,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** when I went out to the city gate, when I prepared my seat in the street.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The young men saw me and hid themselves. The aged rose up and stood.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The princes refrained from talking, and laid their hand on their mouth.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The voice of the nobles was hushed, and their tongue stuck to the roof of their mouth.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For when the ear heard me, then it blessed me; and when the eye saw me, it commended me:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Because I delivered the poor who cried, and the fatherless also, who had no one to help him,

<a id="v13"></a>**13** the blessing of him who was ready to perish came on me, and I caused the widow’s heart to sing for joy.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I put on righteousness, and it clothed me. My justice was as a robe and a diadem.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** I was eyes to the blind, and feet to the lame.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I was a father to the needy. The cause of him who I didn’t know, I searched out.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** I broke the jaws of the unrighteous, and plucked the prey out of his teeth.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Then I said, ‘I shall die in my own house, I shall number my days as the sand.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** My root is spread out to the waters. The dew lies all night on my branch.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** My glory is fresh in me. My bow is renewed in my hand.’

<a id="v21"></a>**21** “Men listened to me, waited, and kept silence for my counsel.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** After my words they didn’t speak again. My speech fell on them.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** They waited for me as for the rain. Their mouths drank as with the spring rain.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** I smiled on them when they had no confidence. They didn’t reject the light of my face.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** I chose out their way, and sat as chief. I lived as a king in the army, as one who comforts the mourners.
