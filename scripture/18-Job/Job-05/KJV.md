# Job 5 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Call now, if there be any that will answer thee; and to which of the saints wilt thou turn?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For wrath killeth the foolish man, and envy slayeth the silly one.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I have seen the foolish taking root: but suddenly I cursed his habitation.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** His children are far from safety, and they are crushed in the gate, neither is there any to deliver them.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Whose harvest the hungry eateth up, and taketh it even out of the thorns, and the robber swalloweth up their substance.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Although affliction cometh not forth of the dust, neither doth trouble spring out of the ground;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Yet man is born unto trouble, as the sparks fly upward.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** I would seek unto God, and unto God would I commit my cause:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Which doeth great things and unsearchable; marvellous things without number:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Who giveth rain upon the earth, and sendeth waters upon the fields:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** To set up on high those that be low; that those which mourn may be exalted to safety.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** He disappointeth the devices of the crafty, so that their hands cannot perform their enterprise.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He taketh the wise in their own craftiness: and the counsel of the froward is carried headlong.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** They meet with darkness in the daytime, and grope in the noonday as in the night.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** But he saveth the poor from the sword, from their mouth, and from the hand of the mighty.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** So the poor hath hope, and iniquity stoppeth her mouth.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Behold, happy is the man whom God correcteth: therefore despise not thou the chastening of the Almighty:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For he maketh sore, and bindeth up: he woundeth, and his hands make whole.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He shall deliver thee in six troubles: yea, in seven there shall no evil touch thee.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** In famine he shall redeem thee from death: and in war from the power of the sword.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Thou shalt be hid from the scourge of the tongue: neither shalt thou be afraid of destruction when it cometh.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** At destruction and famine thou shalt laugh: neither shalt thou be afraid of the beasts of the earth.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For thou shalt be in league with the stones of the field: and the beasts of the field shall be at peace with thee.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And thou shalt know that thy tabernacle shall be in peace; and thou shalt visit thy habitation, and shalt not sin.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Thou shalt know also that thy seed shall be great, and thine offspring as the grass of the earth.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Thou shalt come to thy grave in a full age, like as a shock of corn cometh in in his season.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Lo this, we have searched it, so it is; hear it, and know thou it for thy good.
