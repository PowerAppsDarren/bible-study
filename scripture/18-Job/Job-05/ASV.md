# Job 5 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Call now; is there any that will answer thee? And to which of the holy ones wilt thou turn?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For vexation killeth the foolish man, And jealousy slayeth the silly one.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I have seen the foolish taking root: But suddenly I cursed his habitation.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** His children are far from safety, And they are crushed in the gate, Neither is there any to deliver them:

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Whose harvest the hungry eateth up, And taketh it even out of the thorns; And the snare gapeth for their substance.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For affliction cometh not forth from the dust, Neither doth trouble spring out of the ground;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** But man is born unto trouble, As the sparks fly upward.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But as for me, I would seek unto God, And unto God would I commit my cause;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Who doeth great things and unsearchable, Marvellous things without number:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Who giveth rain upon the earth, And sendeth waters upon the fields;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** So that he setteth up on high those that are low, And those that mourn are exalted to safety.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** He frustrateth the devices of the crafty, So that their hands cannot perform their enterprise.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He taketh the wise in their own craftiness; And the counsel of the cunning is carried headlong.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** They meet with darkness in the day-time, And grope at noonday as in the night.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** But he saveth from the sword of their mouth, Even the needy from the hand of the mighty.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** So the poor hath hope, And iniquity stoppeth her mouth.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Behold, happy is the man whom God correcteth: Therefore despise not thou the chastening of the Almighty.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For he maketh sore, and bindeth up; He woundeth, and his hands make whole.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He will deliver thee in six troubles; Yea, in seven there shall no evil touch thee.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** In famine he will redeem thee from death; And in war from the power of the sword.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Thou shalt be hid from the scourge of the tongue; Neither shalt thou be afraid of destruction when it cometh.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** At destruction and dearth thou shalt laugh; Neither shalt thou be afraid of the beasts of the earth.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For thou shalt be in league with the stones of the field; And the beasts of the field shall be at peace with thee.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And thou shalt know that thy tent is in peace; And thou shalt visit thy fold, and shalt miss nothing.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Thou shalt know also that thy seed shall be great, And thine offspring as the grass of the earth.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Thou shalt come to thy grave in a full age, Like as a shock of grain cometh in in its season.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Lo this, we have searched it, so it is; Hear it, and know thou it for thy good.
