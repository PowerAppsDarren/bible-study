# Job 5 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Give now a cry for help; is there anyone who will give you an answer? and to which of the holy ones will you make your prayer?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For wrath is the cause of death to the foolish, and he who has no wisdom comes to his end through passion.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I have seen the foolish taking root, but suddenly the curse came on his house.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Now his children have no safe place, and they are crushed before the judges, for no one takes up their cause.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Their produce is taken by him who has no food, and their grain goes to the poor, and he who is in need of water gets it from their spring.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For evil does not come out of the dust, or trouble out of the earth;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** But trouble is man's fate from birth, as the flames go up from the fire.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But as for me, I would make my prayer to God, and I would put my cause before him:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Who does great things outside our knowledge, wonders without number:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Who gives rain on the earth, and sends water on the fields:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Lifting up those who are low, and putting the sad in a safe place;

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Who makes the designs of the wise go wrong, so that they are unable to give effect to their purposes.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He takes the wise in their secret designs, and the purposes of the twisted are cut off suddenly.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** In the daytime it becomes dark for them, and in the sunlight they go feeling about as if it was night.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** But he keeps safe from their sword those who have no father, and the poor from the power of the strong.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** So the poor man has hope, and the mouth of the evil-doer is stopped.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Truly, that man is happy who has training from the hand of God: so do not let your heart be shut to the teaching of the Ruler of all.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For after his punishment he gives comfort, and after wounding, his hands make you well.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He will keep you safe from six troubles, and in seven no evil will come near you.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** When there is need of food he will keep you from death, and in war from the power of the sword.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He will keep you safe from the evil tongue; and you will have no fear of wasting when it comes.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** You will make sport of destruction and need, and will have no fear of the beasts of the earth.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For you will be in agreement with the stones of the earth, and the beasts of the field will be at peace with you.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And you will be certain that your tent is at peace, and after looking over your property you will see that nothing is gone.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** You will be certain that your seed will be great, and your offspring like the plants of the earth.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** You will come to your last resting-place in full strength, as the grain is taken up to the crushing-floor in its time.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** See, we have made search with care, and it is so; it has come to our ears; see that you take note of it for yourself.
