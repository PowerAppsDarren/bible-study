# Job 5 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “Call now; is there any who will answer you? To which of the holy ones will you turn?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For resentment kills the foolish man, and jealousy kills the simple.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** I have seen the foolish taking root, but suddenly I cursed his habitation.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** His children are far from safety. They are crushed in the gate. Neither is there any to deliver them,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** whose harvest the hungry eats up, and take it even out of the thorns. The snare gapes for their substance.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For affliction doesn’t come out of the dust, neither does trouble spring out of the ground;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** but man is born to trouble, as the sparks fly upward.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** “But as for me, I would seek God. I would commit my cause to God,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** who does great things that can’t be fathomed, marvelous things without number;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** who gives rain on the earth, and sends waters on the fields;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** so that he sets up on high those who are low, those who mourn are exalted to safety.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** He frustrates the devices of the crafty, So that their hands can’t perform their enterprise.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** He takes the wise in their own craftiness; the counsel of the cunning is carried headlong.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** They meet with darkness in the day time, and grope at noonday as in the night.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** But he saves from the sword of their mouth, even the needy from the hand of the mighty.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** So the poor has hope, and injustice shuts her mouth.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** “Behold, happy is the man whom God corrects. Therefore do not despise the chastening of the Almighty.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For he wounds, and binds up. He injures, and his hands make whole.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He will deliver you in six troubles; yes, in seven no evil shall touch you.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** In famine he will redeem you from death; in war, from the power of the sword.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** You shall be hidden from the scourge of the tongue, neither shall you be afraid of destruction when it comes.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** At destruction and famine you shall laugh, neither shall you be afraid of the animals of the earth.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For you shall be allied with the stones of the field. The animals of the field shall be at peace with you.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** You shall know that your tent is in peace. You shall visit your fold, and shall miss nothing.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** You shall know also that your seed shall be great, Your offspring as the grass of the earth.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** You shall come to your grave in a full age, like a shock of grain comes in its season.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Look this, we have searched it, so it is. Hear it, and know it for your good.”
