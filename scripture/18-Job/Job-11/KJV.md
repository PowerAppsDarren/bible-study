# Job 11 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then answered Zophar the Naamathite, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Should not the multitude of words be answered? and should a man full of talk be justified?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Should thy lies make men hold their peace? and when thou mockest, shall no man make thee ashamed?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For thou hast said, My doctrine is pure, and I am clean in thine eyes.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But oh that God would speak, and open his lips against thee;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And that he would shew thee the secrets of wisdom, that they are double to that which is! Know therefore that God exacteth of thee less than thine iniquity deserveth.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Canst thou by searching find out God? canst thou find out the Almighty unto perfection?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** It is as high as heaven; what canst thou do? deeper than hell; what canst thou know?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The measure thereof is longer than the earth, and broader than the sea.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** If he cut off, and shut up, or gather together, then who can hinder him?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For he knoweth vain men: he seeth wickedness also; will he not then consider it?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For vain man would be wise, though man be born like a wild ass’s colt.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** If thou prepare thine heart, and stretch out thine hands toward him;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If iniquity be in thine hand, put it far away, and let not wickedness dwell in thy tabernacles.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** For then shalt thou lift up thy face without spot; yea, thou shalt be stedfast, and shalt not fear:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Because thou shalt forget thy misery, and remember it as waters that pass away:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And thine age shall be clearer than the noonday; thou shalt shine forth, thou shalt be as the morning.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And thou shalt be secure, because there is hope; yea, thou shalt dig about thee, and thou shalt take thy rest in safety.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Also thou shalt lie down, and none shall make thee afraid; yea, many shall make suit unto thee.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But the eyes of the wicked shall fail, and they shall not escape, and their hope shall be as the giving up of the ghost.
