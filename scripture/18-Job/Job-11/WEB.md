# Job 11 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Zophar, the Naamathite, answered,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Shouldn’t the multitude of words be answered? Should a man full of talk be justified?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Should your boastings make men hold their peace? When you mock, shall no man make you ashamed?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For you say, ‘My doctrine is pure. I am clean in your eyes.’

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But oh that God would speak, and open his lips against you,

<a id="v6"></a>**6** that he would show you the secrets of wisdom! For true wisdom has two sides. Know therefore that God exacts of you less than your iniquity deserves.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** “Can you fathom the mystery of God? Or can you probe the limits of the Almighty?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They are high as heaven. What can you do? They are deeper than Sheol. What can you know?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Its measure is longer than the earth, and broader than the sea.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** If he passes by, or confines, or convenes a court, then who can oppose him?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For he knows false men. He sees iniquity also, even though he doesn’t consider it.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** An empty-headed man becomes wise when a man is born as a wild donkey’s colt.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** “If you set your heart aright, stretch out your hands toward him.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If iniquity is in your hand, put it far away. Don’t let unrighteousness dwell in your tents.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Surely then you shall lift up your face without spot; Yes, you shall be steadfast, and shall not fear:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** for you shall forget your misery. You shall remember it like waters that have passed away.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Life shall be clearer than the noonday. Though there is darkness, it shall be as the morning.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** You shall be secure, because there is hope. Yes, you shall search, and shall take your rest in safety.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Also you shall lie down, and no one shall make you afraid. Yes, many shall court your favor.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But the eyes of the wicked shall fail. They shall have no way to flee. Their hope shall be the giving up of the spirit.”
