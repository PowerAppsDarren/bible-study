# Job 38 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Jehovah answered Job out of the whirlwind, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Who is this that darkeneth counsel By words without knowledge?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Gird up now thy loins like a man; For I will demand of thee, and declare thou unto me.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Where wast thou when I laid the foundations of the earth? Declare, if thou hast understanding.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Who determined the measures thereof, if thou knowest? Or who stretched the line upon it?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Whereupon were the foundations thereof fastened? Or who laid the corner-stone thereof,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** When the morning stars sang together, And all the sons of God shouted for joy?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Orwho shut up the sea with doors, When it brake forth, as if it had issued out of the womb;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** When I made clouds the garment thereof, And thick darkness a swaddling-band for it,

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And marked out for it my bound, And set bars and doors,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And said, Hitherto shalt thou come, but no further; And here shall thy proud waves be stayed?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Hast thou commanded the morning since thy days began, And caused the dayspring to know its place;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** That it might take hold of the ends of the earth, And the wicked be shaken out of it?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** It is changed as clay under the seal; Andall thingsstand forth as a garment:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And from the wicked their light is withholden, And the high arm is broken.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Hast thou entered into the springs of the sea? Or hast thou walked in the recesses of the deep?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Have the gates of death been revealed unto thee? Or hast thou seen the gates of the shadow of death?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Hast thou comprehended the earth in its breadth? Declare, if thou knowest it all.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Where is the way to the dwelling of light? And as for darkness, where is the place thereof,

<a id="v20"></a>**20** That thou shouldest take it to the bound thereof, And that thou shouldest discern the paths to the house thereof?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Doubtless, thou knowest, for thou wast then born, And the number of thy days is great!

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Hast thou entered the treasuries of the snow, Or hast thou seen the treasures of the hail,

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Which I have reserved against the time of trouble, Against the day of battle and war?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** By what way is the light parted, Or the east wind scattered upon the earth?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Who hath cleft a channel for the waterflood, Or the way for the lightning of the thunder;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** To cause it to rain on a land where no man is; On the wilderness, wherein there is no man;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** To satisfy the waste and desolate ground, And to cause the tender grass to spring forth?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Hath the rain a father? Or who hath begotten the drops of dew?

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Out of whose womb came the ice? And the hoary frost of heaven, who hath gendered it?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** The waters hide themselvesand becomelike stone, And the face of the deep is frozen.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Canst thou bind the cluster of the Pleiades, Or loose the bands of Orion?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Canst thou lead forth the Mazzaroth in their season? Or canst thou guide the Bear with her train?

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Knowest thou the ordinances of the heavens? Canst thou establish the dominion thereof in the earth?

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Canst thou lift up thy voice to the clouds, That abundance of waters may cover thee?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Canst thou send forth lightnings, that they may go, And say unto thee, Here we are?

<a id="v36"></a>**36** Who hath put wisdom in the inward parts? Or who hath given understanding to the mind?

<a id="v37"></a>**37** Who can number the clouds by wisdom? Or who can pour out the bottles of heaven,

<a id="v38"></a>**38** When the dust runneth into a mass, And the clods cleave fast together?

<a id="v39"></a>**39** Canst thou hunt the prey for the lioness, Or satisfy the appetite of the young lions,

<a id="v40"></a>**40** When they couch in their dens, Andabide in the covert to lie in wait?

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Who provideth for the raven his prey, When his young ones cry unto God, Andwander for lack of food?
