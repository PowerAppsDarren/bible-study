# Job 38 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then the Lord answered Job out of the whirlwind, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Who is this that darkeneth counsel by words without knowledge?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Gird up now thy loins like a man; for I will demand of thee, and answer thou me.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Where wast thou when I laid the foundations of the earth? declare, if thou hast understanding.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Who hath laid the measures thereof, if thou knowest? or who hath stretched the line upon it?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Whereupon are the foundations thereof fastened? or who laid the corner stone thereof;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** When the morning stars sang together, and all the sons of God shouted for joy?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Or who shut up the sea with doors, when it brake forth, as if it had issued out of the womb?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** When I made the cloud the garment thereof, and thick darkness a swaddlingband for it,

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And brake up for it my decreed place, and set bars and doors,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And said, Hitherto shalt thou come, but no further: and here shall thy proud waves be stayed?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Hast thou commanded the morning since thy days; and caused the dayspring to know his place;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** That it might take hold of the ends of the earth, that the wicked might be shaken out of it?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** It is turned as clay to the seal; and they stand as a garment.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And from the wicked their light is withholden, and the high arm shall be broken.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Hast thou entered into the springs of the sea? or hast thou walked in the search of the depth?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Have the gates of death been opened unto thee? or hast thou seen the doors of the shadow of death?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Hast thou perceived the breadth of the earth? declare if thou knowest it all.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Where is the way where light dwelleth? and as for darkness, where is the place thereof,

<a id="v20"></a>**20** That thou shouldest take it to the bound thereof, and that thou shouldest know the paths to the house thereof?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Knowest thou it, because thou wast then born? or because the number of thy days is great?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Hast thou entered into the treasures of the snow? or hast thou seen the treasures of the hail,

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Which I have reserved against the time of trouble, against the day of battle and war?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** By what way is the light parted, which scattereth the east wind upon the earth?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Who hath divided a watercourse for the overflowing of waters, or a way for the lightning of thunder;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** To cause it to rain on the earth, where no man is; on the wilderness, wherein there is no man;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** To satisfy the desolate and waste ground; and to cause the bud of the tender herb to spring forth?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Hath the rain a father? or who hath begotten the drops of dew?

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Out of whose womb came the ice? and the hoary frost of heaven, who hath gendered it?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** The waters are hid as with a stone, and the face of the deep is frozen.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Canst thou bind the sweet influences of Pleiades, or loose the bands of Orion?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Canst thou bring forth Mazzaroth in his season? or canst thou guide Arcturus with his sons?

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Knowest thou the ordinances of heaven? canst thou set the dominion thereof in the earth?

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Canst thou lift up thy voice to the clouds, that abundance of waters may cover thee?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Canst thou send lightnings, that they may go, and say unto thee, Here we are?

<a id="v36"></a>**36** Who hath put wisdom in the inward parts? or who hath given understanding to the heart?

<a id="v37"></a>**37** Who can number the clouds in wisdom? or who can stay the bottles of heaven,

<a id="v38"></a>**38** When the dust groweth into hardness, and the clods cleave fast together?

<a id="v39"></a>**39** Wilt thou hunt the prey for the lion? or fill the appetite of the young lions,

<a id="v40"></a>**40** When they couch in their dens, and abide in the covert to lie in wait?

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Who provideth for the raven his food? when his young ones cry unto God, they wander for lack of meat.
