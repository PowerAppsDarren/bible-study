# Job 38 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Yahweh answered Job out of the whirlwind,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Who is this who darkens counsel by words without knowledge?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Brace yourself like a man, for I will question you, then you answer me!

<a id="v4"></a>**4** “Where were you when I laid the foundations of the earth? Declare, if you have understanding.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Who determined its measures, if you know? Or who stretched the line on it?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Whereupon were its foundations fastened? Or who laid its cornerstone,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** when the morning stars sang together, and all the sons of God shouted for joy?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** “Or who shut up the sea with doors, when it broke out of the womb,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** when I made clouds its garment, and wrapped it in thick darkness,

<a id="v10"></a>**10** marked out for it my bound, set bars and doors,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** and said, ‘Here you may come, but no further. Here your proud waves shall be stayed?’

<a id="v12"></a>**12** “Have you commanded the morning in your days, and caused the dawn to know its place;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** that it might take hold of the ends of the earth, and shake the wicked out of it?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** It is changed as clay under the seal, and presented as a garment.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** From the wicked, their light is withheld. The high arm is broken.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** “Have you entered into the springs of the sea? Or have you walked in the recesses of the deep?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Have the gates of death been revealed to you? Or have you seen the gates of the shadow of death?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Have you comprehended the earth in its breadth? Declare, if you know it all.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** “What is the way to the dwelling of light? As for darkness, where is its place,

<a id="v20"></a>**20** that you should take it to its bound, that you should discern the paths to its house?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Surely you know, for you were born then, and the number of your days is great!

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Have you entered the treasuries of the snow, or have you seen the treasures of the hail,

<a id="v23"></a>**23** which I have reserved against the time of trouble, against the day of battle and war?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** By what way is the lightning distributed, or the east wind scattered on the earth?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Who has cut a channel for the flood water, or the path for the thunderstorm;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** To cause it to rain on a land where no man is; on the wilderness, in which there is no man;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** to satisfy the waste and desolate ground, to cause the tender grass to grow?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Does the rain have a father? Or who fathers the drops of dew?

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Out of whose womb came the ice? The gray frost of the sky, who has given birth to it?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** The waters become hard like stone, when the surface of the deep is frozen.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** “Can you bind the cluster of the Pleiades, or loosen the cords of Orion?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Can you lead the constellations out in their season? Or can you guide the Bear with her cubs?

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Do you know the laws of the heavens? Can you establish its dominion over the earth?

<a id="v34"></a>**34** “Can you lift up your voice to the clouds, That abundance of waters may cover you?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Can you send out lightnings, that they may go? Do they report to you, ‘Here we are?’

<a id="v36"></a>**36** Who has put wisdom in the inward parts? Or who has given understanding to the mind?

<a id="v37"></a>**37** Who can number the clouds by wisdom? Or who can pour out the bottles of the sky,

<a id="v38"></a>**38** when the dust runs into a mass, and the clods of earth stick together?

<a id="v39"></a>**39** “Can you hunt the prey for the lioness, or satisfy the appetite of the young lions,

<a id="v40"></a>**40** when they crouch in their dens, and lie in wait in the thicket?

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Who provides for the raven his prey, when his young ones cry to God, and wander for lack of food?
