# Job 13 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Lo, mine eye hath seen all this, mine ear hath heard and understood it.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** What ye know, the same do I know also: I am not inferior unto you.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Surely I would speak to the Almighty, and I desire to reason with God.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But ye are forgers of lies, ye are all physicians of no value.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** O that ye would altogether hold your peace! and it should be your wisdom.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Hear now my reasoning, and hearken to the pleadings of my lips.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Will ye speak wickedly for God? and talk deceitfully for him?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Will ye accept his person? will ye contend for God?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Is it good that he should search you out? or as one man mocketh another, do ye so mock him?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He will surely reprove you, if ye do secretly accept persons.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Shall not his excellency make you afraid? and his dread fall upon you?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Your remembrances are like unto ashes, your bodies to bodies of clay.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Hold your peace, let me alone, that I may speak, and let come on me what will.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Wherefore do I take my flesh in my teeth, and put my life in mine hand?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Though he slay me, yet will I trust in him: but I will maintain mine own ways before him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** He also shall be my salvation: for an hypocrite shall not come before him.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Hear diligently my speech, and my declaration with your ears.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Behold now, I have ordered my cause; I know that I shall be justified.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Who is he that will plead with me? for now, if I hold my tongue, I shall give up the ghost.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Only do not two things unto me: then will I not hide myself from thee.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Withdraw thine hand far from me: and let not thy dread make me afraid.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Then call thou, and I will answer: or let me speak, and answer thou me.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** How many are mine iniquities and sins? make me to know my transgression and my sin.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Wherefore hidest thou thy face, and holdest me for thine enemy?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Wilt thou break a leaf driven to and fro? and wilt thou pursue the dry stubble?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For thou writest bitter things against me, and makest me to possess the iniquities of my youth.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Thou puttest my feet also in the stocks, and lookest narrowly unto all my paths; thou settest a print upon the heels of my feet.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And he, as a rotten thing, consumeth, as a garment that is moth eaten.
