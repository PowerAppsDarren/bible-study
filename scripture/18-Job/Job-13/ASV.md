# Job 13 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Lo, mine eye hath seen all this, Mine ear hath heard and understood it.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** What ye know, the same do I know also: I am not inferior unto you.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Surely I would speak to the Almighty, And I desire to reason with God.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** But ye are forgers of lies; Ye are all physicians of no value.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Oh that ye would altogether hold your peace! And it would be your wisdom.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Hear now my reasoning, And hearken to the pleadings of my lips.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Will ye speak unrighteously for God, And talk deceitfully for him?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Will ye show partiality to him? Will ye contend for God?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Is it good that he should search you out? Or as one deceiveth a man, will ye deceive him?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He will surely reprove you, If ye do secretly show partiality.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Shall not his majesty make you afraid, And his dread fall upon you?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Your memorable sayings are proverbs of ashes, Your defences are defences of clay.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Hold your peace, let me alone, that I may speak; And let come on me what will.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Wherefore should I take my flesh in my teeth, And put my life in my hand?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Behold, he will slay me; I have no hope: Nevertheless I will maintain my ways before him.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** This also shall be my salvation, That a godless man shall not come before him.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Hear diligently my speech, And let my declaration be in your ears.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Behold now, I have set my cause in order; I know that I am righteous.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Who is he that will contend with me? For then would I hold my peace and give up the ghost.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Only do not two things unto me; Then will I not hide myself from thy face:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Withdraw thy hand far from me; And let not thy terror make me afraid.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Then call thou, and I will answer; Or let me speak, and answer thou me.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** How many are mine iniquities and sins? Make me to know my transgression and my sin.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Wherefore hidest thou thy face, And holdest me for thine enemy?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Wilt thou harass a driven leaf? And wilt thou pursue the dry stubble?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For thou writest bitter things against me, And makest me to inherit the iniquities of my youth:

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Thou puttest my feet also in the stocks, And markest all my paths; Thou settest a bound to the soles of my feet:

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Though I am like a rotten thing that consumeth, Like a garment that is moth-eaten.
