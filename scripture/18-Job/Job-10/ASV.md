# Job 10 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My soul is weary of my life; I will give free course to my complaint; I will speak in the bitterness of my soul.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** I will say unto God, Do not condemn me; Show me wherefore thou contendest with me.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Is it good unto thee that thou shouldest oppress, That thou shouldest despise the work of thy hands, And shine upon the counsel of the wicked?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Hast thou eyes of flesh? Or seest thou as man seeth?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Are thy days as the days of man, Or thy years as man’s days,

<a id="v6"></a>**6** That thou inquirest after mine iniquity, And searchest after my sin,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Although thou knowest that I am not wicked, And there is none that can deliver out of thy hand?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Thy hands have framed me and fashioned me Together round about; yet thou dost destroy me.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Remember, I beseech thee, that thou hast fashioned me as clay; And wilt thou bring me into dust again?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Hast thou not poured me out as milk, And curdled me like cheese?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Thou hast clothed me with skin and flesh, And knit me together with bones and sinews.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Thou hast granted me life and lovingkindness; And thy visitation hath preserved my spirit.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Yet these things thou didst hide in thy heart; I know that this is with thee:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If I sin, then thou markest me, And thou wilt not acquit me from mine iniquity.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** If I be wicked, woe unto me; And if I be righteous, yet shall I not lift up my head; Being filled with ignominy, And looking upon mine affliction.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And ifmy head exalt itself, thou huntest me as a lion; And again thou showest thyself marvellous upon me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Thou renewest thy witnesses against me, And increasest thine indignation upon me: Changes and warfare are with me.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Wherefore then hast thou brought me forth out of the womb? I had given up the ghost, and no eye had seen me.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** I should have been as though I had not been; I should have been carried from the womb to the grave.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Are not my days few? cease then, And let me alone, that I may take comfort a little,

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Before I go whence I shall not return, Evento the land of darkness and of the shadow of death;

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The land dark as midnight, The landof the shadow of death, without any order, And where the light is as midnight.
