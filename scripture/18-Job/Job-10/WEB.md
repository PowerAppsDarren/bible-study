# Job 10 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “My soul is weary of my life. I will give free course to my complaint. I will speak in the bitterness of my soul.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** I will tell God, ‘Do not condemn me. Show me why you contend with me.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Is it good to you that you should oppress, that you should despise the work of your hands, and smile on the counsel of the wicked?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Do you have eyes of flesh? Or do you see as man sees?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Are your days as the days of mortals, or your years as man’s years,

<a id="v6"></a>**6** that you inquire after my iniquity, and search after my sin?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Although you know that I am not wicked, there is no one who can deliver out of your hand.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** “‘Your hands have framed me and fashioned me altogether, yet you destroy me.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Remember, I beg you, that you have fashioned me as clay. Will you bring me into dust again?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Haven’t you poured me out like milk, and curdled me like cheese?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** You have clothed me with skin and flesh, and knit me together with bones and sinews.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** You have granted me life and loving kindness. Your visitation has preserved my spirit.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Yet you hid these things in your heart. I know that this is with you:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** if I sin, then you mark me. You will not acquit me from my iniquity.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** If I am wicked, woe to me. If I am righteous, I still shall not lift up my head, being filled with disgrace, and conscious of my affliction.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If my head is held high, you hunt me like a lion. Again you show yourself powerful to me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** You renew your witnesses against me, and increase your indignation on me. Changes and warfare are with me.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** “‘Why, then, have you brought me out of the womb? I wish I had given up the spirit, and no eye had seen me.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** I should have been as though I had not been. I should have been carried from the womb to the grave.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Aren’t my days few? Cease then. Leave me alone, that I may find a little comfort,

<a id="v21"></a>**21** before I go where I shall not return from, to the land of darkness and of the shadow of death;

<a id="v22"></a>**22** the land dark as midnight, of the shadow of death, without any order, where the light is as midnight.’”
