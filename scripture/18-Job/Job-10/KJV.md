# Job 10 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** My soul is weary of my life; I will leave my complaint upon myself; I will speak in the bitterness of my soul.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** I will say unto God, Do not condemn me; shew me wherefore thou contendest with me.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Is it good unto thee that thou shouldest oppress, that thou shouldest despise the work of thine hands, and shine upon the counsel of the wicked?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Hast thou eyes of flesh? or seest thou as man seeth?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Are thy days as the days of man? are thy years as man’s days,

<a id="v6"></a>**6** That thou enquirest after mine iniquity, and searchest after my sin?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Thou knowest that I am not wicked; and there is none that can deliver out of thine hand.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Thine hands have made me and fashioned me together round about; yet thou dost destroy me.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Remember, I beseech thee, that thou hast made me as the clay; and wilt thou bring me into dust again?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Hast thou not poured me out as milk, and curdled me like cheese?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Thou hast clothed me with skin and flesh, and hast fenced me with bones and sinews.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Thou hast granted me life and favour, and thy visitation hath preserved my spirit.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And these things hast thou hid in thine heart: I know that this is with thee.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If I sin, then thou markest me, and thou wilt not acquit me from mine iniquity.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** If I be wicked, woe unto me; and if I be righteous, yet will I not lift up my head. I am full of confusion; therefore see thou mine affliction;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For it increaseth. Thou huntest me as a fierce lion: and again thou shewest thyself marvellous upon me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Thou renewest thy witnesses against me, and increasest thine indignation upon me; changes and war are against me.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Wherefore then hast thou brought me forth out of the womb? Oh that I had given up the ghost, and no eye had seen me!

<a id="v19"></a>**19** I should have been as though I had not been; I should have been carried from the womb to the grave.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Are not my days few? cease then, and let me alone, that I may take comfort a little,

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Before I go whence I shall not return, even to the land of darkness and the shadow of death;

<a id="v22"></a>**22** A land of darkness, as darkness itself; and of the shadow of death, without any order, and where the light is as darkness.
