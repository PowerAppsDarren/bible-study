# Job 6 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Job made answer and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** If only my passion might be measured, and put into the scales against my trouble!

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For then its weight would be more than the sand of the seas: because of this my words have been uncontrolled.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For the arrows of the Ruler of all are present with me, and their poison goes deep into my spirit: his army of fears is put in order against me.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Does the ass of the fields give out his voice when he has grass? or does the ox make sounds over his food?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Will a man take food which has no taste without salt? or is there any taste in the soft substance of purslain?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** My soul has no desire for such things, they are as disease in my food.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** If only I might have an answer to my prayer, and God would give me my desire!

<a id="v9"></a>**9** If only he would be pleased to put an end to me; and would let loose his hand, so that I might be cut off!

<a id="v10"></a>**10** So I would still have comfort, and I would have joy in the pains of death, for I have not been false to the words of the Holy One.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Have I strength to go on waiting, or have I any end to be looking forward to?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Is my strength the strength of stones, or is my flesh brass?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** I have no help in myself, and wisdom is completely gone from me.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** He whose heart is shut against his friend has given up the fear of the Ruler of all.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** My friends have been false like a stream, like streams in the valleys which come to an end:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Which are dark because of the ice, and the snow falling into them;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Under the burning sun they are cut off, and come to nothing because of the heat.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The camel-trains go out of their way; they go up into the waste and come to destruction.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The camel-trains of Tema were searching with care, the bands of Sheba were waiting for them:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** They were put to shame because of their hope; they came and their hope was gone.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** So have you now become to me; you see my sad condition and are in fear.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Did I say, Give me something? or, Make a payment for me out of your wealth?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Or, Get me out of the power of my hater? or, Give money so that I may be free from the power of the cruel ones?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Give me teaching and I will be quiet; and make me see my error.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** How pleasing are upright words! but what force is there in your arguments?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** My words may seem wrong to you, but the words of him who has no hope are for the wind.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Truly, you are such as would give up the child of a dead man to his creditors, and would make a profit out of your friend.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Now then, let your eyes be turned to me, for truly I will not say what is false to your face.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Let your minds be changed, and do not have an evil opinion of me; yes, be changed, for my righteousness is still in me.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Is there evil in my tongue? is not the cause of my trouble clear to me?
