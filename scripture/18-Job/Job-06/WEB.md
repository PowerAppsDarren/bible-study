# Job 6 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Job answered,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Oh that my anguish were weighed, and all my calamity laid in the balances!

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For now it would be heavier than the sand of the seas, therefore have my words been rash.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For the arrows of the Almighty are within me. My spirit drinks up their poison. The terrors of God set themselves in array against me.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Does the wild donkey bray when he has grass? Or does the ox low over his fodder?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Can that which has no flavor be eaten without salt? Or is there any taste in the white of an egg?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** My soul refuses to touch them. They are as loathsome food to me.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** “Oh that I might have my request, that God would grant the thing that I long for,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** even that it would please God to crush me; that he would let loose his hand, and cut me off!

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Be it still my consolation, yes, let me exult in pain that doesn’t spare, that I have not denied the words of the Holy One.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** What is my strength, that I should wait? What is my end, that I should be patient?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Is my strength the strength of stones? Or is my flesh of brass?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Isn’t it that I have no help in me, That wisdom is driven quite from me?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** “To him who is ready to faint, kindness should be shown from his friend; even to him who forsakes the fear of the Almighty.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** My brothers have dealt deceitfully as a brook, as the channel of brooks that pass away;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Which are black by reason of the ice, in which the snow hides itself.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** In the dry season, they vanish. When it is hot, they are consumed out of their place.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The caravans that travel beside them turn aside. They go up into the waste, and perish.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The caravans of Tema looked. The companies of Sheba waited for them.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** They were distressed because they were confident. They came there, and were confounded.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For now you are nothing. You see a terror, and are afraid.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Did I say, ‘Give to me?’ or, ‘Offer a present for me from your substance?’

<a id="v23"></a>**23** or, ‘Deliver me from the adversary’s hand?’ or, ‘Redeem me from the hand of the oppressors?’

<a id="v24"></a>**24** “Teach me, and I will hold my peace. Cause me to understand wherein I have erred.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** How forcible are words of uprightness! But your reproof, what does it reprove?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Do you intend to reprove words, since the speeches of one who is desperate are as wind?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Yes, you would even cast lots for the fatherless, and make merchandise of your friend.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Now therefore be pleased to look at me, for surely I shall not lie to your face.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Please return. Let there be no injustice. Yes, return again. My cause is righteous.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Is there injustice on my tongue? Can’t my taste discern mischievous things?
