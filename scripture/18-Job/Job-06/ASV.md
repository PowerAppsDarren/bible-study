# Job 6 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Job answered and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Oh that my vexation were but weighed, And all my calamity laid in the balances!

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For now it would be heavier than the sand of the seas: Therefore have my words been rash.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For the arrows of the Almighty are within me, The poison whereof my spirit drinketh up: The terrors of God do set themselves in array against me.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Doth the wild ass bray when he hath grass? Or loweth the ox over his fodder?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Can that which hath no savor be eaten without salt? Or is there any taste in the white of an egg?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** My soul refuseth to touch them; They are as loathsome food to me.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Oh that I might have my request; And that God would grantmethe thing that I long for!

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Even that it would please God to crush me; That he would let loose his hand, and cut me off!

<a id="v10"></a>**10** And be it still my consolation, Yea, let me exult in pain that spareth not, That I have not denied the words of the Holy One.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** What is my strength, that I should wait? And what is mine end, that I should be patient?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Is my strength the strength of stones? Or is my flesh of brass?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Is it not that I have no help in me, And that wisdom is driven quite from me?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** To him that is ready to faint kindness should be showed from his friend; Even to him that forsaketh the fear of the Almighty.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** My brethren have dealt deceitfully as a brook, As the channel of brooks that pass away;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Which are black by reason of the ice, And wherein the snow hideth itself:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** What time they wax warm, they vanish; When it is hot, they are consumed out of their place.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The caravansthat travelby the way of them turn aside; They go up into the waste, and perish.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The caravans of Tema looked, The companies of Sheba waited for them.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** They were put to shame because they had hoped; They came thither, and were confounded.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For now ye are nothing; Ye see a terror, and are afraid.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Did I say, Give unto me? Or, Offer a present for me of your substance?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Or, Deliver me from the adversary’s hand? Or, Redeem me from the hand of the oppressors?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Teach me, and I will hold my peace; And cause me to understand wherein I have erred.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** How forcible are words of uprightness! But your reproof, what doth it reprove?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Do ye think to reprove words, Seeing that the speeches of one that is desperate are as wind?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Yea, ye would castlotsupon the fatherless, And make merchandise of your friend.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Now therefore be pleased to look upon me; For surely I shall not lie to your face.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Return, I pray you, let there be no injustice; Yea, return again, my cause is righteous.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Is there injustice on my tongue? Cannot my taste discern mischievous things?
