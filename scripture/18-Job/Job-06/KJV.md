# Job 6 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** But Job answered and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Oh that my grief were throughly weighed, and my calamity laid in the balances together!

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For now it would be heavier than the sand of the sea: therefore my words are swallowed up.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For the arrows of the Almighty are within me, the poison whereof drinketh up my spirit: the terrors of God do set themselves in array against me.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Doth the wild ass bray when he hath grass? or loweth the ox over his fodder?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Can that which is unsavoury be eaten without salt? or is there any taste in the white of an egg?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The things that my soul refused to touch are as my sorrowful meat.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Oh that I might have my request; and that God would grant me the thing that I long for!

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Even that it would please God to destroy me; that he would let loose his hand, and cut me off!

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Then should I yet have comfort; yea, I would harden myself in sorrow: let him not spare; for I have not concealed the words of the Holy One.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** What is my strength, that I should hope? and what is mine end, that I should prolong my life?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Is my strength the strength of stones? or is my flesh of brass?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Is not my help in me? and is wisdom driven quite from me?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** To him that is afflicted pity should be shewed from his friend; but he forsaketh the fear of the Almighty.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** My brethren have dealt deceitfully as a brook, and as the stream of brooks they pass away;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Which are blackish by reason of the ice, and wherein the snow is hid:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** What time they wax warm, they vanish: when it is hot, they are consumed out of their place.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The paths of their way are turned aside; they go to nothing, and perish.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The troops of Tema looked, the companies of Sheba waited for them.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** They were confounded because they had hoped; they came thither, and were ashamed.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For now ye are nothing; ye see my casting down, and are afraid.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Did I say, Bring unto me? or, Give a reward for me of your substance?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Or, Deliver me from the enemy’s hand? or, Redeem me from the hand of the mighty?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Teach me, and I will hold my tongue: and cause me to understand wherein I have erred.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** How forcible are right words! but what doth your arguing reprove?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Do ye imagine to reprove words, and the speeches of one that is desperate, which are as wind?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Yea, ye overwhelm the fatherless, and ye dig a pit for your friend.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Now therefore be content, look upon me; for it is evident unto you if I lie.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Return, I pray you, let it not be iniquity; yea, return again, my righteousness is in it.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Is there iniquity in my tongue? cannot my taste discern perverse things?
