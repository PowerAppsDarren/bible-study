# Job 31 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** I made a covenant with mine eyes; why then should I think upon a maid?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For what portion of God is there from above? and what inheritance of the Almighty from on high?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Is not destruction to the wicked? and a strange punishment to the workers of iniquity?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Doth not he see my ways, and count all my steps?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If I have walked with vanity, or if my foot hath hasted to deceit;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Let me be weighed in an even balance, that God may know mine integrity.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** If my step hath turned out of the way, and mine heart walked after mine eyes, and if any blot hath cleaved to mine hands;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Then let me sow, and let another eat; yea, let my offspring be rooted out.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** If mine heart have been deceived by a woman, or if I have laid wait at my neighbour’s door;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Then let my wife grind unto another, and let others bow down upon her.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For this is an heinous crime; yea, it is an iniquity to be punished by the judges.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For it is a fire that consumeth to destruction, and would root out all mine increase.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** If I did despise the cause of my manservant or of my maidservant, when they contended with me;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** What then shall I do when God riseth up? and when he visiteth, what shall I answer him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Did not he that made me in the womb make him? and did not one fashion us in the womb?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If I have withheld the poor from their desire, or have caused the eyes of the widow to fail;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Or have eaten my morsel myself alone, and the fatherless hath not eaten thereof;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** (For from my youth he was brought up with me, as with a father, and I have guided her from my mother’s womb;)

<a id="v19"></a>**19** If I have seen any perish for want of clothing, or any poor without covering;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** If his loins have not blessed me, and if he were not warmed with the fleece of my sheep;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** If I have lifted up my hand against the fatherless, when I saw my help in the gate:

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Then let mine arm fall from my shoulder blade, and mine arm be broken from the bone.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For destruction from God was a terror to me, and by reason of his highness I could not endure.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** If I have made gold my hope, or have said to the fine gold, Thou art my confidence;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** If I rejoiced because my wealth was great, and because mine hand had gotten much;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** If I beheld the sun when it shined, or the moon walking in brightness;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And my heart hath been secretly enticed, or my mouth hath kissed my hand:

<a id="v28"></a>**28** This also were an iniquity to be punished by the judge: for I should have denied the God that is above.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** If I rejoiced at the destruction of him that hated me, or lifted up myself when evil found him:

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Neither have I suffered my mouth to sin by wishing a curse to his soul.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** If the men of my tabernacle said not, Oh that we had of his flesh! we cannot be satisfied.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** The stranger did not lodge in the street: but I opened my doors to the traveller.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** If I covered my transgressions as Adam, by hiding mine iniquity in my bosom:

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Did I fear a great multitude, or did the contempt of families terrify me, that I kept silence, and went not out of the door?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Oh that one would hear me! behold, my desire is, that the Almighty would answer me, and that mine adversary had written a book.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** Surely I would take it upon my shoulder, and bind it as a crown to me.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** I would declare unto him the number of my steps; as a prince would I go near unto him.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** If my land cry against me, or that the furrows likewise thereof complain;

<a id="v39"></a>**39** If I have eaten the fruits thereof without money, or have caused the owners thereof to lose their life:

<a id="v40"></a>**40** Let thistles grow instead of wheat, and cockle instead of barley. The words of Job are ended.
