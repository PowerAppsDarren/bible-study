# Job 31 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** I made an agreement with my eyes; how then might my eyes be looking on a virgin?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For what is God's reward from on high, or the heritage given by the Ruler of all from heaven?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Is it not trouble for the sinner, and destruction for the evil-doers?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Does he not see my ways, and are not my steps all numbered?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If I have gone in false ways, or my foot has been quick in working deceit;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** (Let me be measured in upright scales, and let God see my righteousness:)

<a id="v7"></a>**7** If my steps have been turned out of the way, or if my heart went after my eyes, or if the property of another is in my hands;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Let me put seed in the earth for another to have the fruit of it, and let my produce be uprooted.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** If my heart went after another man's wife, or if I was waiting secretly at my neighbour's door;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Then let my wife give pleasure to another man and let others make use of her body.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For that would be a crime; it would be an act for which punishment would be measured out by the judges:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** It would be a fire burning even to destruction, and taking away all my produce.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** If I did wrong in the cause of my man-servant, or my woman-servant, when they went to law with me;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** What then will I do when God comes as my judge? and what answer may I give to his questions?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Did not God make him as well as me? did he not give us life in our mothers' bodies?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If I kept back the desire of the poor; if the widow's eye was looking for help to no purpose;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** If I kept my food for myself, and did not give some of it to the child with no father;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** (For I was cared for by God as by a father from my earliest days; he was my guide from the body of my mother;)

<a id="v19"></a>**19** If I saw one near to death for need of clothing, and that the poor had nothing covering him;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** If his back did not give me a blessing, and the wool of my sheep did not make him warm;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** If my hand had been lifted up against him who had done no wrong, when I saw that I was supported by the judges;

<a id="v22"></a>**22** May my arm be pulled from my body, and be broken from its base.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For the fear of God kept me back, and because of his power I might not do such things.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** If I made gold my hope, or if I ever said to the best gold, I have put my faith in you;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** If I was glad because my wealth was great, and because my hand had got together a great store;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** If, when I saw the sun shining, and the moon moving on its bright way,

<a id="v27"></a>**27** A secret feeling of worship came into my heart, and my hand gave kisses from my mouth;

<a id="v28"></a>**28** That would have been another sin to be rewarded with punishment by the judges; for I would have been false to God on high.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** If I was glad at the trouble of my hater, and gave cries of joy when evil overtook him;

<a id="v30"></a>**30** (For I did not let my mouth give way to sin, in putting a curse on his life;)

<a id="v31"></a>**31** If the men of my tent did not say, Who has not had full measure of his meat?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** The traveller did not take his night's rest in the street, and my doors were open to anyone on a journey;

<a id="v33"></a>**33** If I kept my evil doings covered, and my sin in the secret of my breast,

<a id="v34"></a>**34** For fear of the great body of people, or for fear that families might make sport of me, so that I kept quiet, and did not go out of my door;

<a id="v35"></a>**35** If only God would give ear to me, and the Ruler of all would give me an answer! or if what he has against me had been put in writing!

<a id="v36"></a>**36** Truly I would take up the book in my hands; it would be to me as a crown;

<a id="v37"></a>**37** I would make clear the number of my steps, I would put it before him like a prince! The words of Job are ended.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** If my land has made an outcry against me, or the ploughed earth has been in sorrow;

<a id="v39"></a>**39** If I have taken its produce without payment, causing the death of its owners;

<a id="v40"></a>**40** Then in place of grain let thorns come up, and in place of barley evil-smelling plants.
