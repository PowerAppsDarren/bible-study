# Job 31 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “I made a covenant with my eyes, how then should I look lustfully at a young woman?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For what is the portion from God above, and the heritage from the Almighty on high?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Is it not calamity to the unrighteous, and disaster to the workers of iniquity?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Doesn’t he see my ways, and number all my steps?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** “If I have walked with falsehood, and my foot has hurried to deceit

<a id="v6"></a>**6** (let me be weighed in an even balance, that God may know my integrity);

<a id="v7"></a>**7** if my step has turned out of the way, if my heart walked after my eyes, if any defilement has stuck to my hands,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** then let me sow, and let another eat. Yes, let the produce of my field be rooted out.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** “If my heart has been enticed to a woman, and I have laid wait at my neighbor’s door,

<a id="v10"></a>**10** then let my wife grind for another, and let others sleep with her.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For that would be a heinous crime. Yes, it would be an iniquity to be punished by the judges:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For it is a fire that consumes to destruction, and would root out all my increase.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** “If I have despised the cause of my male servant or of my female servant, when they contended with me;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** What then shall I do when God rises up? When he visits, what shall I answer him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Didn’t he who made me in the womb make him? Didn’t one fashion us in the womb?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** “If I have withheld the poor from their desire, or have caused the eyes of the widow to fail,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** or have eaten my morsel alone, and the fatherless has not eaten of it

<a id="v18"></a>**18** (no, from my youth he grew up with me as with a father, her have I guided from my mother’s womb);

<a id="v19"></a>**19** if I have seen any perish for want of clothing, or that the needy had no covering;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** if his heart hasn’t blessed me, if he hasn’t been warmed with my sheep’s fleece;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** if I have lifted up my hand against the fatherless, because I saw my help in the gate,

<a id="v22"></a>**22** then let my shoulder fall from the shoulder blade, and my arm be broken from the bone.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For calamity from God is a terror to me. Because his majesty, I can do nothing.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** “If I have made gold my hope, and have said to the fine gold, ‘You are my confidence;’

<a id="v25"></a>**25** If I have rejoiced because my wealth was great, and because my hand had gotten much;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** if I have seen the sun when it shined, or the moon moving in splendor,

<a id="v27"></a>**27** and my heart has been secretly enticed, and my hand threw a kiss from my mouth,

<a id="v28"></a>**28** this also would be an iniquity to be punished by the judges; for I should have denied the God who is above.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** “If I have rejoiced at the destruction of him who hated me, or lifted up myself when evil found him;

<a id="v30"></a>**30** (yes, I have not allowed my mouth to sin by asking his life with a curse);

<a id="v31"></a>**31** if the men of my tent have not said, ‘Who can find one who has not been filled with his meat?’

<a id="v32"></a>**32** (the foreigner has not lodged in the street, but I have opened my doors to the traveler);

<a id="v33"></a>**33** if like Adam I have covered my transgressions, by hiding my iniquity in my heart,

<a id="v34"></a>**34** because I feared the great multitude, and the contempt of families terrified me, so that I kept silence, and didn’t go out of the door—

<a id="v35"></a>**35** oh that I had one to hear me! (behold, here is my signature, let the Almighty answer me); let the accuser write my indictment!

<a id="v36"></a>**36** Surely I would carry it on my shoulder; and I would bind it to me as a crown.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** I would declare to him the number of my steps. as a prince would I go near to him.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** If my land cries out against me, and its furrows weep together;

<a id="v39"></a>**39** if I have eaten its fruits without money, or have caused its owners to lose their life,

<a id="v40"></a>**40** let briers grow instead of wheat, and stinkweed instead of barley.”   The words of Job are ended.
