# Job 31 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** I made a covenant with mine eyes; How then should I look upon a virgin?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For what is the portion from God above, And the heritage from the Almighty on high?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Is it not calamity to the unrighteous, And disaster to the workers of iniquity?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Doth not he see my ways, And number all my steps?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If I have walked with falsehood, And my foot hath hasted to deceit

<a id="v6"></a>**6** (Let me be weighed in an even balance, That God may know mine integrity);

<a id="v7"></a>**7** If my step hath turned out of the way, And my heart walked after mine eyes, And if any spot hath cleaved to my hands:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Then let me sow, and let another eat; Yea, let the produce of my field be rooted out.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** If my heart hath been enticed unto a woman, And I have laid wait at my neighbor’s door;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Then let my wife grind unto another, And let others bow down upon her.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For that were a heinous crime; Yea, it were an iniquity to be punished by the judges:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For it is a fire that consumeth unto Destruction, And would root out all mine increase.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** If I have despised the cause of my man-servant or of my maid-servant, When they contended with me;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** What then shall I do when God riseth up? And when he visiteth, what shall I answer him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Did not he that made me in the womb make him? And did not one fashion us in the womb?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If I have withheld the poor fromtheirdesire, Or have caused the eyes of the widow to fail,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Or have eaten my morsel alone, And the fatherless hath not eaten thereof

<a id="v18"></a>**18** (Nay, from my youth he grew up with me as with a father, And her have I guided from my mother’s womb);

<a id="v19"></a>**19** If I have seen any perish for want of clothing, Or that the needy had no covering;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** If his loins have not blessed me, And if he hath not been warmed with the fleece of my sheep;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** If I have lifted up my hand against the fatherless, Because I saw my help in the gate:

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Then let my shoulder fall from the shoulder-blade, And mine arm be broken from the bone.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For calamity from God is a terror to me, And by reason of his majesty I can do nothing.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** If I have made gold my hope, And have said to the fine gold, Thou artmy confidence;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** If I have rejoiced because my wealth was great, And because my hand had gotten much;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** If I have beheld the sun when it shined, Or the moon walking in brightness,

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And my heart hath been secretly enticed, And my mouth hath kissed my hand:

<a id="v28"></a>**28** This also were an iniquity to be punished by the judges; For I should have denied the God that is above.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** If I have rejoiced at the destruction of him that hated me, Or lifted up myself when evil found him

<a id="v30"></a>**30** (Yea, I have not suffered my mouth to sin By asking his life with a curse);

<a id="v31"></a>**31** If the men of my tent have not said, Who can find one that hath not been filled with his meat?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** (The sojourner hath not lodged in the street; But I have opened my doors to the traveller);

<a id="v33"></a>**33** If like Adam I have covered my transgressions, By hiding mine iniquity in my bosom,

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Because I feared the great multitude, And the contempt of families terrified me, So that I kept silence, and went not out of the door—

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Oh that I had one to hear me! (Lo, here is my signature, let the Almighty answer me) Andthat I hadthe indictment which mine adversary hath written!

<a id="v36"></a>**36** Surely I would carry it upon my shoulder; I would bind it unto me as a crown:

<a id="v37"></a>**37** I would declare unto him the number of my steps; As a prince would I go near unto him.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** If my land crieth out against me, And the furrows thereof weep together;

<a id="v39"></a>**39** If I have eaten the fruits thereof without money, Or have caused the owners thereof to lose their life:

<a id="v40"></a>**40** Let thistles grow instead of wheat, And cockle instead of barley.   The words of Job are ended.
