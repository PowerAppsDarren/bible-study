# Job 41 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Is it possible for Leviathan to be pulled out with a fish-hook, or for a hook to be put through the bone of his mouth?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Will you put a cord into his nose, or take him away with a cord round his tongue?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Will he make prayers to you, or say soft words to you?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Will he make an agreement with you, so that you may take him as a servant for ever?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Will you make sport with him, as with a bird? or put him in chains for your young women?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Will the fishermen make profit out of him? will they have him cut up for the traders?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Will you put sharp-pointed irons into his skin, or fish-spears into his head?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Only put your hand on him, and see what a fight you will have; you will not do it again!

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Truly, the hope of his attacker is false; he is overcome even on seeing him!

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He is so cruel that no one is ready to go against him. Who then is able to keep his place before me?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Who ever went against me, and got the better of me? There is no one under heaven!

<a id="v12"></a>**12** I will not keep quiet about the parts of his body, or about his power, and the strength of his frame.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Who has ever taken off his outer skin? who may come inside his inner coat of iron?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Who has made open the doors of his face? Fear is round about his teeth.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** His back is made of lines of plates, joined tight together, one against the other, like a stamp.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** One is so near to the other that no air may come between them.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** They take a grip of one another; they are joined together, so that they may not be parted.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** His sneezings give out flames, and his eyes are like the eyes of the dawn.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Out of his mouth go burning lights, and flames of fire are jumping up.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Smoke comes out of his nose, like a pot boiling on the fire.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His breath puts fire to coals, and a flame goes out of his mouth.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Strength is in his neck, and fear goes dancing before him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The plates of his flesh are joined together, fixed, and not to be moved.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** His heart is as strong as a stone, hard as the lower crushing-stone.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** When he gets ready for the fight, the strong are overcome with fear.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** The sword may come near him but is not able to go through him; the spear, or the arrow, or the sharp-pointed iron.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Iron is to him as dry grass, and brass as soft wood.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The arrow is not able to put him to flight: stones are no more to him than dry stems.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** A thick stick is no better than a leaf of grass, and he makes sport of the onrush of the spear.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Under him are sharp edges of broken pots: as if he was pulling a grain-crushing instrument over the wet earth.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The deep is boiling like a pot of spices, and the sea like a perfume-vessel.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** After him his way is shining, so that the deep seems white.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** On earth there is not another like him, who is made without fear.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Everything which is high goes in fear of him; he is king over all the sons of pride.
