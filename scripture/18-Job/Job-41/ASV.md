# Job 41 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Canst thou draw out leviathan with a fishhook? Or press down his tongue with a cord?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Canst thou put a rope into his nose? Or pierce his jaw through with a hook?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Will he make many supplications unto thee? Or will he speak soft words unto thee?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Will he make a covenant with thee, That thou shouldest take him for a servant for ever?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Wilt thou play with him as with a bird? Or wilt thou bind him for thy maidens?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Will the bands of fishermen make traffic of him? Will they part him among the merchants?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Canst thou fill his skin with barbed irons, Or his head with fish-spears?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Lay thy hand upon him; Remember the battle, and do so no more.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Behold, the hope of him is in vain: Will not one be cast down even at the sight of him?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** None is so fierce that he dare stir him up; Who then is he that can stand before me?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Who hath first given unto me, that I should repay him? Whatsoever is under the whole heaven is mine.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** I will not keep silence concerning his limbs, Nor his mighty strength, nor his goodly frame.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Who can strip off his outer garment? Who shall come within his jaws?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Who can open the doors of his face? Round about his teeth is terror.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Hisstrong scales are his pride, Shut up together as with a close seal.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** One is so near to another, That no air can come between them.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** They are joined one to another; They stick together, so that they cannot be sundered.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** His sneezings flash forth light, And his eyes are like the eyelids of the morning.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Out of his mouth go burning torches, And sparks of fire leap forth.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Out of his nostrils a smoke goeth, As of a boiling pot and burning rushes.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His breath kindleth coals, And a flame goeth forth from his mouth.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** In his neck abideth strength, And terror danceth before him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The flakes of his flesh are joined together: They are firm upon him; they cannot be moved.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** His heart is as firm as a stone; Yea, firm as the nether millstone.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** When he raiseth himself up, the mighty are afraid: By reason of consternation they are beside themselves.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** If one lay at him with the sword, it cannot avail; Nor the spear, the dart, nor the pointed shaft.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He counteth iron as straw, And brass as rotten wood.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The arrow cannot make him flee: Sling-stones are turned with him into stubble.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Clubs are counted as stubble: He laugheth at the rushing of the javelin.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** His underparts are like sharp potsherds: He spreadeth as it were a threshing-wain upon the mire.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** He maketh the deep to boil like a pot: He maketh the sea like a pot of ointment.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He maketh a path to shine after him; One would think the deep to be hoary.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Upon earth there is not his like, That is made without fear.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** He beholdeth everything that is high: He is king over all the sons of pride.
