# Job 41 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “Can you draw out Leviathan with a fish hook, or press down his tongue with a cord?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Can you put a rope into his nose, or pierce his jaw through with a hook?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Will he make many petitions to you, or will he speak soft words to you?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Will he make a covenant with you, that you should take him for a servant forever?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Will you play with him as with a bird? Or will you bind him for your girls?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Will traders barter for him? Will they part him among the merchants?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Can you fill his skin with barbed irons, or his head with fish spears?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Lay your hand on him. Remember the battle, and do so no more.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Behold, the hope of him is in vain. Won’t one be cast down even at the sight of him?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** None is so fierce that he dare stir him up. Who then is he who can stand before me?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Who has first given to me, that I should repay him? Everything under the heavens is mine.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** “I will not keep silence concerning his limbs, nor his mighty strength, nor his goodly frame.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Who can strip off his outer garment? Who shall come within his jaws?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Who can open the doors of his face? Around his teeth is terror.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Strong scales are his pride, shut up together with a close seal.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** One is so near to another, that no air can come between them.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** They are joined one to another. They stick together, so that they can’t be pulled apart.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** His sneezing flashes out light. His eyes are like the eyelids of the morning.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Out of his mouth go burning torches. Sparks of fire leap out.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Out of his nostrils a smoke goes, as of a boiling pot over a fire of reeds.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His breath kindles coals. A flame goes out of his mouth.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** There is strength in his neck. Terror dances before him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The flakes of his flesh are joined together. They are firm on him. They can’t be moved.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** His heart is as firm as a stone, yes, firm as the lower millstone.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** When he raises himself up, the mighty are afraid. They retreat before his thrashing.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** If one attacks him with the sword, it can’t prevail; nor the spear, the dart, nor the pointed shaft.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He counts iron as straw; and brass as rotten wood.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The arrow can’t make him flee. Sling stones are like chaff to him.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Clubs are counted as stubble. He laughs at the rushing of the javelin.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** His undersides are like sharp potsherds, leaving a trail in the mud like a threshing sledge.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** He makes the deep to boil like a pot. He makes the sea like a pot of ointment.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He makes a path shine after him. One would think the deep had white hair.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** On earth there is not his equal, that is made without fear.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** He sees everything that is high. He is king over all the sons of pride.”
