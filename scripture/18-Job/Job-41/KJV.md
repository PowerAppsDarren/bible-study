# Job 41 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Canst thou draw out leviathan with an hook? or his tongue with a cord which thou lettest down?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Canst thou put an hook into his nose? or bore his jaw through with a thorn?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Will he make many supplications unto thee? will he speak soft words unto thee?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Will he make a covenant with thee? wilt thou take him for a servant for ever?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Wilt thou play with him as with a bird? or wilt thou bind him for thy maidens?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Shall the companions make a banquet of him? shall they part him among the merchants?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Canst thou fill his skin with barbed irons? or his head with fish spears?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Lay thine hand upon him, remember the battle, do no more.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Behold, the hope of him is in vain: shall not one be cast down even at the sight of him?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** None is so fierce that dare stir him up: who then is able to stand before me?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Who hath prevented me, that I should repay him? whatsoever is under the whole heaven is mine.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** I will not conceal his parts, nor his power, nor his comely proportion.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Who can discover the face of his garment? or who can come to him with his double bridle?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Who can open the doors of his face? his teeth are terrible round about.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** His scales are his pride, shut up together as with a close seal.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** One is so near to another, that no air can come between them.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** They are joined one to another, they stick together, that they cannot be sundered.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** By his neesings a light doth shine, and his eyes are like the eyelids of the morning.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Out of his mouth go burning lamps, and sparks of fire leap out.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Out of his nostrils goeth smoke, as out of a seething pot or caldron.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His breath kindleth coals, and a flame goeth out of his mouth.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** In his neck remaineth strength, and sorrow is turned into joy before him.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The flakes of his flesh are joined together: they are firm in themselves; they cannot be moved.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** His heart is as firm as a stone; yea, as hard as a piece of the nether millstone.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** When he raiseth up himself, the mighty are afraid: by reason of breakings they purify themselves.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** The sword of him that layeth at him cannot hold: the spear, the dart, nor the habergeon.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He esteemeth iron as straw, and brass as rotten wood.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The arrow cannot make him flee: slingstones are turned with him into stubble.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Darts are counted as stubble: he laugheth at the shaking of a spear.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Sharp stones are under him: he spreadeth sharp pointed things upon the mire.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** He maketh the deep to boil like a pot: he maketh the sea like a pot of ointment.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He maketh a path to shine after him; one would think the deep to be hoary.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Upon earth there is not his like, who is made without fear.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** He beholdeth all high things: he is a king over all the children of pride.
