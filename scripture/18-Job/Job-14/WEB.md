# Job 14 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “Man, who is born of a woman, is of few days, and full of trouble.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** He grows up like a flower, and is cut down. He also flees like a shadow, and doesn’t continue.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Do you open your eyes on such a one, and bring me into judgment with you?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Who can bring a clean thing out of an unclean? Not one.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Seeing his days are determined, the number of his months is with you, and you have appointed his bounds that he can’t pass;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Look away from him, that he may rest, until he shall accomplish, as a hireling, his day.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** “For there is hope for a tree, If it is cut down, that it will sprout again, that the tender branch of it will not cease.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Though its root grows old in the earth, and its stock dies in the ground,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** yet through the scent of water it will bud, and sprout boughs like a plant.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** But man dies, and is laid low. Yes, man gives up the spirit, and where is he?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** As the waters fail from the sea, and the river wastes and dries up,

<a id="v12"></a>**12** so man lies down and doesn’t rise. Until the heavens are no more, they shall not awake, nor be roused out of their sleep.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** “Oh that you would hide me in Sheol, that you would keep me secret, until your wrath is past, that you would appoint me a set time, and remember me!

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If a man dies, shall he live again? All the days of my warfare would I wait, until my release should come.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** You would call, and I would answer you. You would have a desire to the work of your hands.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But now you number my steps. Don’t you watch over my sin?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** My disobedience is sealed up in a bag. You fasten up my iniquity.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** “But the mountain falling comes to nothing. The rock is removed out of its place;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The waters wear the stones. The torrents of it wash away the dust of the earth. So you destroy the hope of man.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** You forever prevail against him, and he departs. You change his face, and send him away.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His sons come to honor, and he doesn’t know it. They are brought low, but he doesn’t perceive it of them.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** But his flesh on him has pain, and his soul within him mourns.”
