# Job 14 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Man that is born of a woman is of few days, and full of trouble.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** He cometh forth like a flower, and is cut down: he fleeth also as a shadow, and continueth not.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And dost thou open thine eyes upon such an one, and bringest me into judgment with thee?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Who can bring a clean thing out of an unclean? not one.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Seeing his days are determined, the number of his months are with thee, thou hast appointed his bounds that he cannot pass;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Turn from him, that he may rest, till he shall accomplish, as an hireling, his day.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For there is hope of a tree, if it be cut down, that it will sprout again, and that the tender branch thereof will not cease.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Though the root thereof wax old in the earth, and the stock thereof die in the ground;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Yet through the scent of water it will bud, and bring forth boughs like a plant.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** But man dieth, and wasteth away: yea, man giveth up the ghost, and where is he?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** As the waters fail from the sea, and the flood decayeth and drieth up:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** So man lieth down, and riseth not: till the heavens be no more, they shall not awake, nor be raised out of their sleep.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** O that thou wouldest hide me in the grave, that thou wouldest keep me secret, until thy wrath be past, that thou wouldest appoint me a set time, and remember me!

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If a man die, shall he live again? all the days of my appointed time will I wait, till my change come.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Thou shalt call, and I will answer thee: thou wilt have a desire to the work of thine hands.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For now thou numberest my steps: dost thou not watch over my sin?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** My transgression is sealed up in a bag, and thou sewest up mine iniquity.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And surely the mountain falling cometh to nought, and the rock is removed out of his place.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The waters wear the stones: thou washest away the things which grow out of the dust of the earth; and thou destroyest the hope of man.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Thou prevailest for ever against him, and he passeth: thou changest his countenance, and sendest him away.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His sons come to honour, and he knoweth it not; and they are brought low, but he perceiveth it not of them.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** But his flesh upon him shall have pain, and his soul within him shall mourn.
