# Job 14 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Man, that is born of a woman, Is of few days, and full of trouble.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** He cometh forth like a flower, and is cut down: He fleeth also as a shadow, and continueth not.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And dost thou open thine eyes upon such a one, And bringest me into judgment with thee?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Who can bring a clean thing out of an unclean? not one.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Seeing his days are determined, The number of his months is with thee, And thou hast appointed his bounds that he cannot pass;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Look away from him, that he may rest, Till he shall accomplish, as a hireling, his day.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For there is hope of a tree, If it be cut down, that it will sprout again, And that the tender branch thereof will not cease.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Though the root thereof wax old in the earth, And the stock thereof die in the ground;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Yet through the scent of water it will bud, And put forth boughs like a plant.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** But man dieth, and is laid low: Yea, man giveth up the ghost, and where is he?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Asthe waters fail from the sea, And the river wasteth and drieth up;

<a id="v12"></a>**12** So man lieth down and riseth not: Till the heavens be no more, they shall not awake, Nor be roused out of their sleep.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Oh that thou wouldest hide me in Sheol, That thou wouldest keep me secret, until thy wrath be past, That thou wouldest appoint me a set time, and remember me!

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If a man die, shall he liveagain? All the days of my warfare would I wait, Till my release should come.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Thou wouldest call, and I would answer thee: Thou wouldest have a desire to the work of thy hands.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But now thou numberest my steps: Dost thou not watch over my sin?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** My transgression is sealed up in a bag, And thou fastenest up mine iniquity.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But the mountain falling cometh to nought; And the rock is removed out of its place;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The waters wear the stones; The overflowings thereof wash away the dust of the earth: So thou destroyest the hope of man.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Thou prevailest for ever against him, and he passeth; Thou changest his countenance, and sendest him away.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His sons come to honor, and he knoweth it not; And they are brought low, but he perceiveth it not of them.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** But his flesh upon him hath pain, And his soul within him mourneth.
