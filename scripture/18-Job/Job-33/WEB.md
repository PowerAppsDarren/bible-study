# Job 33 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “However, Job, please hear my speech, and listen to all my words.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** See now, I have opened my mouth. My tongue has spoken in my mouth.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** My words shall utter the uprightness of my heart. That which my lips know they shall speak sincerely.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The Spirit of God has made me, and the breath of the Almighty gives me life.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If you can, answer me. Set your words in order before me, and stand up.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Behold, I am toward God even as you are. I am also formed out of the clay.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Behold, my terror shall not make you afraid, neither shall my pressure be heavy on you.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** “Surely you have spoken in my hearing, I have heard the voice of your words, saying,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** ‘I am clean, without disobedience. I am innocent, neither is there iniquity in me.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Behold, he finds occasions against me. He counts me for his enemy.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He puts my feet in the stocks. He marks all my paths.’

<a id="v12"></a>**12** “Behold, I will answer you. In this you are not just, for God is greater than man.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Why do you strive against him, because he doesn’t give account of any of his matters?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For God speaks once, yes twice, though man pays no attention.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** In a dream, in a vision of the night, when deep sleep falls on men, in slumbering on the bed;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Then he opens the ears of men, and seals their instruction,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** That he may withdraw man from his purpose, and hide pride from man.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He keeps back his soul from the pit, and his life from perishing by the sword.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He is chastened also with pain on his bed, with continual strife in his bones;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** So that his life abhors bread, and his soul dainty food.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His flesh is so consumed away, that it can’t be seen. His bones that were not seen stick out.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Yes, his soul draws near to the pit, and his life to the destroyers.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** “If there is beside him an angel, an interpreter, one among a thousand, to show to man what is right for him;

<a id="v24"></a>**24** then God is gracious to him, and says, ‘Deliver him from going down to the pit, I have found a ransom.’

<a id="v25"></a>**25** His flesh shall be fresher than a child’s. He returns to the days of his youth.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He prays to God, and he is favorable to him, so that he sees his face with joy. He restores to man his righteousness.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He sings before men, and says, ‘I have sinned, and perverted that which was right, and it didn’t profit me.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** He has redeemed my soul from going into the pit. My life shall see the light.’

<a id="v29"></a>**29** “Behold, God works all these things, twice, yes three times, with a man,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** to bring back his soul from the pit, that he may be enlightened with the light of the living.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Mark well, Job, and listen to me. Hold your peace, and I will speak.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** If you have anything to say, answer me. Speak, for I desire to justify you.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** If not, listen to me. Hold your peace, and I will teach you wisdom.”
