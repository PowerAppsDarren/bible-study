# Job 33 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Wherefore, Job, I pray thee, hear my speeches, and hearken to all my words.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Behold, now I have opened my mouth, my tongue hath spoken in my mouth.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** My words shall be of the uprightness of my heart: and my lips shall utter knowledge clearly.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The Spirit of God hath made me, and the breath of the Almighty hath given me life.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If thou canst answer me, set thy words in order before me, stand up.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Behold, I am according to thy wish in God’s stead: I also am formed out of the clay.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Behold, my terror shall not make thee afraid, neither shall my hand be heavy upon thee.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Surely thou hast spoken in mine hearing, and I have heard the voice of thy words, saying,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** I am clean without transgression, I am innocent; neither is there iniquity in me.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Behold, he findeth occasions against me, he counteth me for his enemy,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He putteth my feet in the stocks, he marketh all my paths.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Behold, in this thou art not just: I will answer thee, that God is greater than man.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Why dost thou strive against him? for he giveth not account of any of his matters.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For God speaketh once, yea twice, yet man perceiveth it not.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** In a dream, in a vision of the night, when deep sleep falleth upon men, in slumberings upon the bed;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Then he openeth the ears of men, and sealeth their instruction,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** That he may withdraw man from his purpose, and hide pride from man.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He keepeth back his soul from the pit, and his life from perishing by the sword.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He is chastened also with pain upon his bed, and the multitude of his bones with strong pain:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** So that his life abhorreth bread, and his soul dainty meat.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His flesh is consumed away, that it cannot be seen; and his bones that were not seen stick out.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Yea, his soul draweth near unto the grave, and his life to the destroyers.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If there be a messenger with him, an interpreter, one among a thousand, to shew unto man his uprightness:

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Then he is gracious unto him, and saith, Deliver him from going down to the pit: I have found a ransom.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** His flesh shall be fresher than a child’s: he shall return to the days of his youth:

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He shall pray unto God, and he will be favourable unto him: and he shall see his face with joy: for he will render unto man his righteousness.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He looketh upon men, and if any say, I have sinned, and perverted that which was right, and it profited me not;

<a id="v28"></a>**28** He will deliver his soul from going into the pit, and his life shall see the light.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Lo, all these things worketh God oftentimes with man,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** To bring back his soul from the pit, to be enlightened with the light of the living.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Mark well, O Job, hearken unto me: hold thy peace, and I will speak.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** If thou hast any thing to say, answer me: speak, for I desire to justify thee.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** If not, hearken unto me: hold thy peace, and I shall teach thee wisdom.
