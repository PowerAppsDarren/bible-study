# Job 33 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Howbeit, Job, I pray thee, hear my speech, And hearken to all my words.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Behold now, I have opened my mouth; My tongue hath spoken in my mouth.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** My wordsshall utterthe uprightness of my heart; And that which my lips know they shall speak sincerely.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** The Spirit of God hath made me, And the breath of the Almighty giveth me life.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** If thou canst, answer thou me; Setthy wordsin order before me, stand forth.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Behold, I am toward God even as thou art: I also am formed out of the clay.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Behold, my terror shall not make thee afraid, Neither shall my pressure be heavy upon thee.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Surely thou hast spoken in my hearing, And I have heard the voice ofthywords, saying,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** I am clean, without transgression; I am innocent, neither is there iniquity in me:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Behold, he findeth occasions against me, He counteth me for his enemy;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He putteth my feet in the stocks, He marketh all my paths.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Behold, I will answer thee, in this thou art not just; For God is greater than man.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Why dost thou strive against him, For that he giveth not account of any of his matters?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For God speaketh once, Yea twice, though man regardeth it not.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** In a dream, in a vision of the night, When deep sleep falleth upon men, In slumberings upon the bed;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Then he openeth the ears of men, And sealeth their instruction,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** That he may withdraw man from his purpose, And hide pride from man;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He keepeth back his soul from the pit, And his life from perishing by the sword.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He is chastened also with pain upon his bed, And with continual strife in his bones;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** So that his life abhorreth bread, And his soul dainty food.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** His flesh is consumed away, that it cannot be seen; And his bones that were not seen stick out.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Yea, his soul draweth near unto the pit, And his life to the destroyers.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If there be with him an angel, An interpreter, one among a thousand, To show unto man what is right for him;

<a id="v24"></a>**24** ThenGodis gracious unto him, and saith, Deliver him from going down to the pit, I have found a ransom.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** His flesh shall be fresher than a child’s; He returneth to the days of his youth.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He prayeth unto God, and he is favorable unto him, So that he seeth his face with joy: And he restoreth unto man his righteousness.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He singeth before men, and saith, I have sinned, and perverted that which was right, And it profited me not:

<a id="v28"></a>**28** He hath redeemed my soul from going into the pit, And my life shall behold the light.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Lo, all these things doth God work, Twice, yea thrice, with a man,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** To bring back his soul from the pit, That he may be enlightened with the light of the living.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Mark well, O Job, hearken unto me: Hold thy peace, and I will speak.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** If thou hast anything to say, answer me: Speak, for I desire to justify thee.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** If not, hearken thou unto me: Hold thy peace, and I will teach thee wisdom.
