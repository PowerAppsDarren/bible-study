# Job 28 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Truly there is a mine for silver, and a place where gold is washed out.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Iron is taken out of the earth, and stone is changed into brass by the fire.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Man puts an end to the dark, searching out to the farthest limit the stones of the deep places of the dark.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** He makes a deep mine far away from those living in the light of day; when they go about on the earth, they have no knowledge of those who are under them, who are hanging far from men, twisting from side to side on a cord.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** As for the earth, bread comes out of it; but under its face it is turned up as if by fire.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Its stones are the place of sapphires, and it has dust of gold.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** No bird has knowledge of it, and the hawk's eye has never seen it.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The great beasts have not gone over it, and the cruel lion has not taken that way.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Man puts out his hand on the hard rock, overturning mountains by the roots.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He makes deep ways, cut through the rock, and his eye sees everything of value.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He keeps back the streams from flowing, and makes the secret things come out into the light.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** But where may wisdom be seen? and where is the resting-place of knowledge?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Man has not seen the way to it, and it is not in the land of the living.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The deep waters say, It is not in me: and the sea says, It is not with me.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Gold may not be given for it, or a weight of silver in payment for it.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** It may not be valued with the gold of Ophir, with the onyx of great price, or the sapphire.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Gold and glass are not equal to it in price, and it may not be exchanged for jewels of the best gold.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** There is no need to say anything about coral or crystal; and the value of wisdom is greater than that of pearls.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The topaz of Ethiopia is not equal to it, and it may not be valued with the best gold.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** From where then does wisdom come, and where is the resting-place of knowledge?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For it is kept secret from the eyes of all living, unseen by the birds of the air.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Destruction and Death say, We have only had word of it with our ears.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** God has knowledge of the way to it, and of its resting-place;

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For his eyes go to the ends of the earth, and he sees everything under heaven.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** When he made a weight for the wind, measuring out the waters;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** When he made a law for the rain, and a way for the thunder-flames;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Then he saw it, and put it on record; he gave it its fixed form, searching it out completely.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And he said to man, Truly the fear of the Lord is wisdom, and to keep from evil is the way to knowledge.
