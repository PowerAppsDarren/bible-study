# Job 28 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “Surely there is a mine for silver, and a place for gold which they refine.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Iron is taken out of the earth, and copper is smelted out of the ore.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Man sets an end to darkness, and searches out, to the furthest bound, the stones of obscurity and of thick darkness.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** He breaks open a shaft away from where people live. They are forgotten by the foot. They hang far from men, they swing back and forth.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** As for the earth, out of it comes bread; Underneath it is turned up as it were by fire.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Sapphires come from its rocks. It has dust of gold.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** That path no bird of prey knows, neither has the falcon’s eye seen it.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The proud animals have not trodden it, nor has the fierce lion passed by there.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He puts his hand on the flinty rock, and he overturns the mountains by the roots.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He cuts out channels among the rocks. His eye sees every precious thing.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He binds the streams that they don’t trickle. The thing that is hidden he brings out to light.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** “But where shall wisdom be found? Where is the place of understanding?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Man doesn’t know its price; Neither is it found in the land of the living.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The deep says, ‘It isn’t in me.’ The sea says, ‘It isn’t with me.’

<a id="v15"></a>**15** It can’t be gotten for gold, neither shall silver be weighed for its price.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** It can’t be valued with the gold of Ophir, with the precious onyx, or the sapphire.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Gold and glass can’t equal it, neither shall it be exchanged for jewels of fine gold.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** No mention shall be made of coral or of crystal. Yes, the price of wisdom is above rubies.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The topaz of Ethiopia shall not equal it, Neither shall it be valued with pure gold.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Where then does wisdom come from? Where is the place of understanding?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Seeing it is hidden from the eyes of all living, and kept close from the birds of the sky.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Destruction and Death say, ‘We have heard a rumor of it with our ears.’

<a id="v23"></a>**23** “God understands its way, and he knows its place.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For he looks to the ends of the earth, and sees under the whole sky.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** He establishes the force of the wind. Yes, he measures out the waters by measure.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** When he made a decree for the rain, and a way for the lightning of the thunder;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** then he saw it, and declared it. He established it, yes, and searched it out.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** To man he said, ‘Behold, the fear of the Lord, that is wisdom. To depart from evil is understanding.’”
