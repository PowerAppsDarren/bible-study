# Job 39 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Knowest thou the time when the wild goats of the rock bring forth? Or canst thou mark when the hinds do calve?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Canst thou number the months that they fulfil? Or knowest thou the time when they bring forth?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** They bow themselves, they bring forth their young, They cast out their pains.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Their young ones become strong, they grow up in the open field; They go forth, and return not again.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Who hath sent out the wild ass free? Or who hath loosed the bonds of the swift ass,

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Whose home I have made the wilderness, And the salt land his dwelling-place?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He scorneth the tumult of the city, Neither heareth he the shoutings of the driver.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The range of the mountains is his pasture, And he searcheth after every green thing.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Will the wild-ox be content to serve thee? Or will he abide by thy crib?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Canst thou bind the wild-ox with his band in the furrow? Or will he harrow the valleys after thee?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Wilt thou trust him, because his strength is great? Or wilt thou leave to him thy labor?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Wilt thou confide in him, that he will bring home thy seed, And gatherthe grainof thy threshing-floor?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The wings of the ostrich wave proudly; But are they the pinions and plumage of love?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For she leaveth her eggs on the earth, And warmeth them in the dust,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And forgetteth that the foot may crush them, Or that the wild beast may trample them.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** She dealeth hardly with her young ones, as if they were not hers: Though her labor be in vain, she is without fear;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Because God hath deprived her of wisdom, Neither hath he imparted to her understanding.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** What time she lifteth up herself on high, She scorneth the horse and his rider.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Hast thou given the horsehismight? Hast thou clothed his neck with the quivering mane?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Hast thou made him to leap as a locust? The glory of his snorting is terrible.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He paweth in the valley, and rejoiceth in his strength: He goeth out to meet the armed men.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He mocketh at fear, and is not dismayed; Neither turneth he back from the sword.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The quiver rattleth against him, The flashing spear and the javelin.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He swalloweth the ground with fierceness and rage; Neither believeth he that it is the voice of the trumpet.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** As oft as the trumpetsoundethhe saith, Aha! And he smelleth the battle afar off, The thunder of the captains, and the shouting.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Is it by thy wisdom that the hawk soareth, And stretcheth her wings toward the south?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Is it at thy command that the eagle mounteth up, And maketh her nest on high?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** On the cliff she dwelleth, and maketh her home, Upon the point of the cliff, and the stronghold.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** From thence she spieth out the prey; Her eyes behold it afar off.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Her young ones also suck up blood: And where the slain are, there is she.
