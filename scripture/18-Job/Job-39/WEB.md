# Job 39 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “Do you know the time when the mountain goats give birth? Do you watch when the doe bears fawns?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Can you number the months that they fulfill? Or do you know the time when they give birth?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** They bow themselves, they bear their young. They end their labor pains.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Their young ones become strong. They grow up in the open field. They go out, and don’t return again.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** “Who has set the wild donkey free? Or who has loosened the bonds of the swift donkey,

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Whose home I have made the wilderness, and the salt land his dwelling place?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He scorns the tumult of the city, neither does he hear the shouting of the driver.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The range of the mountains is his pasture, He searches after every green thing.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** “Will the wild ox be content to serve you? Or will he stay by your feeding trough?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Can you hold the wild ox in the furrow with his harness? Or will he till the valleys after you?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Will you trust him, because his strength is great? Or will you leave to him your labor?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Will you confide in him, that he will bring home your seed, and gather the grain of your threshing floor?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** “The wings of the ostrich wave proudly; but are they the feathers and plumage of love?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For she leaves her eggs on the earth, warms them in the dust,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** and forgets that the foot may crush them, or that the wild animal may trample them.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** She deals harshly with her young ones, as if they were not hers. Though her labor is in vain, she is without fear,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** because God has deprived her of wisdom, neither has he imparted to her understanding.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** When she lifts up herself on high, she scorns the horse and his rider.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** “Have you given the horse might? Have you clothed his neck with a quivering mane?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Have you made him to leap as a locust? The glory of his snorting is awesome.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He paws in the valley, and rejoices in his strength. He goes out to meet the armed men.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He mocks at fear, and is not dismayed, neither does he turn back from the sword.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The quiver rattles against him, the flashing spear and the javelin.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He eats up the ground with fierceness and rage, neither does he stand still at the sound of the trumpet.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** As often as the trumpet sounds he snorts, ‘Aha!’ He smells the battle afar off, the thunder of the captains, and the shouting.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** “Is it by your wisdom that the hawk soars, and stretches her wings toward the south?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Is it at your command that the eagle mounts up, and makes his nest on high?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** On the cliff he dwells, and makes his home, on the point of the cliff, and the stronghold.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** From there he spies out the prey. His eyes see it afar off.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** His young ones also suck up blood. Where the slain are, there he is.”
