# Job 39 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Knowest thou the time when the wild goats of the rock bring forth? or canst thou mark when the hinds do calve?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Canst thou number the months that they fulfil? or knowest thou the time when they bring forth?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** They bow themselves, they bring forth their young ones, they cast out their sorrows.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Their young ones are in good liking, they grow up with corn; they go forth, and return not unto them.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Who hath sent out the wild ass free? or who hath loosed the bands of the wild ass?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Whose house I have made the wilderness, and the barren land his dwellings.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He scorneth the multitude of the city, neither regardeth he the crying of the driver.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The range of the mountains is his pasture, and he searcheth after every green thing.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Will the unicorn be willing to serve thee, or abide by thy crib?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Canst thou bind the unicorn with his band in the furrow? or will he harrow the valleys after thee?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Wilt thou trust him, because his strength is great? or wilt thou leave thy labour to him?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Wilt thou believe him, that he will bring home thy seed, and gather it into thy barn?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Gavest thou the goodly wings unto the peacocks? or wings and feathers unto the ostrich?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Which leaveth her eggs in the earth, and warmeth them in dust,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And forgetteth that the foot may crush them, or that the wild beast may break them.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** She is hardened against her young ones, as though they were not hers: her labour is in vain without fear;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Because God hath deprived her of wisdom, neither hath he imparted to her understanding.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** What time she lifteth up herself on high, she scorneth the horse and his rider.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Hast thou given the horse strength? hast thou clothed his neck with thunder?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Canst thou make him afraid as a grasshopper? the glory of his nostrils is terrible.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He paweth in the valley, and rejoiceth in his strength: he goeth on to meet the armed men.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** He mocketh at fear, and is not affrighted; neither turneth he back from the sword.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The quiver rattleth against him, the glittering spear and the shield.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He swalloweth the ground with fierceness and rage: neither believeth he that it is the sound of the trumpet.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** He saith among the trumpets, Ha, ha; and he smelleth the battle afar off, the thunder of the captains, and the shouting.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Doth the hawk fly by thy wisdom, and stretch her wings toward the south?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Doth the eagle mount up at thy command, and make her nest on high?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** She dwelleth and abideth on the rock, upon the crag of the rock, and the strong place.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** From thence she seeketh the prey, and her eyes behold afar off.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Her young ones also suck up blood: and where the slain are, there is she.
