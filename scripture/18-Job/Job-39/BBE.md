# Job 39 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Have you knowledge of the rock-goats? or do you see the roes giving birth to their young?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Is the number of their months fixed by you? or is the time when they give birth ordered by you?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** They are bent down, they give birth to their young, they let loose the fruit of their body.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Their young ones are strong, living in the open country; they go out and do not come back again.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Who has let the ass of the fields go free? or made loose the bands of the loud-voiced beast?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** To whom I have given the waste land for a heritage, and the salt land as a living-place.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** He makes sport of the noise of the town; the voice of the driver does not come to his ears;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He goes looking for his grass-lands in the mountains, searching out every green thing.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Will the ox of the mountains be your servant? or is his night's resting-place by your food-store?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Will he be pulling your plough with cords, turning up the valleys after you?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Will you put your faith in him, because his strength is great? will you give the fruit of your work into his care?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Will you be looking for him to come back, and get in your seed to the crushing-floor?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Is the wing of the ostrich feeble, or is it because she has no feathers,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** That she puts her eggs on the earth, warming them in the dust,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Without a thought that they may be crushed by the foot, and broken by the beasts of the field?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** She is cruel to her young ones, as if they were not hers; her work is to no purpose; she has no fear.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For God has taken wisdom from her mind, and given her no measure of knowledge.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** When she is shaking her wings on high, she makes sport of the horse and of him who is seated on him.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Do you give strength to the horse? is it by your hand that his neck is clothed with power?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Is it through you that he is shaking like a locust, in the pride of his loud-sounding breath?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** He is stamping with joy in the valley; he makes sport of fear.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** In his strength he goes out against the arms of war, turning not away from the sword.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The bow is sounding against him; he sees the shining point of spear and arrow.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Shaking with passion, he is biting the earth; he is not able to keep quiet at the sound of the horn;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** When it comes to his ears he says, Aha! He is smelling the fight from far off, and hearing the thunder of the captains, and the war-cries.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Is it through your knowledge that the hawk takes his flight, stretching out his wings to the south?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Or is it by your orders that the eagle goes up, and makes his resting-place on high?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** On the rock is his house, and on the mountain-top his strong place.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** From there he is watching for food; his eye sees it far off.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** His young have blood for their drink, and where the dead bodies are, there is he to be seen.
