# Job 30 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** But now they that are younger than I have me in derision, whose fathers I would have disdained to have set with the dogs of my flock.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Yea, whereto might the strength of their hands profit me, in whom old age was perished?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For want and famine they were solitary; fleeing into the wilderness in former time desolate and waste.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Who cut up mallows by the bushes, and juniper roots for their meat.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** They were driven forth from among men, (they cried after them as after a thief;)

<a id="v6"></a>**6** To dwell in the clifts of the valleys, in caves of the earth, and in the rocks.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Among the bushes they brayed; under the nettles they were gathered together.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They were children of fools, yea, children of base men: they were viler than the earth.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And now am I their song, yea, I am their byword.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** They abhor me, they flee far from me, and spare not to spit in my face.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Because he hath loosed my cord, and afflicted me, they have also let loose the bridle before me.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Upon my right hand rise the youth; they push away my feet, and they raise up against me the ways of their destruction.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They mar my path, they set forward my calamity, they have no helper.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** They came upon me as a wide breaking in of waters: in the desolation they rolled themselves upon me.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Terrors are turned upon me: they pursue my soul as the wind: and my welfare passeth away as a cloud.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And now my soul is poured out upon me; the days of affliction have taken hold upon me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** My bones are pierced in me in the night season: and my sinews take no rest.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** By the great force of my disease is my garment changed: it bindeth me about as the collar of my coat.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He hath cast me into the mire, and I am become like dust and ashes.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** I cry unto thee, and thou dost not hear me: I stand up, and thou regardest me not.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Thou art become cruel to me: with thy strong hand thou opposest thyself against me.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Thou liftest me up to the wind; thou causest me to ride upon it, and dissolvest my substance.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For I know that thou wilt bring me to death, and to the house appointed for all living.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Howbeit he will not stretch out his hand to the grave, though they cry in his destruction.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Did not I weep for him that was in trouble? was not my soul grieved for the poor?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** When I looked for good, then evil came unto me: and when I waited for light, there came darkness.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** My bowels boiled, and rested not: the days of affliction prevented me.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** I went mourning without the sun: I stood up, and I cried in the congregation.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** I am a brother to dragons, and a companion to owls.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** My skin is black upon me, and my bones are burned with heat.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** My harp also is turned to mourning, and my organ into the voice of them that weep.
