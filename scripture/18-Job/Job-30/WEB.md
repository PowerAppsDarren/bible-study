# Job 30 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “But now those who are younger than I have me in derision, whose fathers I would have disdained to put with my sheep dogs.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Of what use is the strength of their hands to me, men in whom ripe age has perished?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** They are gaunt from lack and famine. They gnaw the dry ground, in the gloom of waste and desolation.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** They pluck salt herbs by the bushes. The roots of the broom are their food.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** They are driven out from the midst of men. They cry after them as after a thief;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** So that they dwell in frightful valleys, and in holes of the earth and of the rocks.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Among the bushes they bray; and under the nettles they are gathered together.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They are children of fools, yes, children of base men. They were flogged out of the land.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** “Now I have become their song. Yes, I am a byword to them.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** They abhor me, they stand aloof from me, and don’t hesitate to spit in my face.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For he has untied his cord, and afflicted me; and they have thrown off restraint before me.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** On my right hand rise the rabble. They thrust aside my feet, They cast up against me their ways of destruction.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They mar my path, They set forward my calamity, without anyone’s help.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** As through a wide breach they come, in the midst of the ruin they roll themselves in.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Terrors have turned on me. They chase my honor as the wind. My welfare has passed away as a cloud.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** “Now my soul is poured out within me. Days of affliction have taken hold on me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** In the night season my bones are pierced in me, and the pains that gnaw me take no rest.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** By great force is my garment disfigured. It binds me about as the collar of my coat.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He has cast me into the mire. I have become like dust and ashes.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** I cry to you, and you do not answer me. I stand up, and you gaze at me.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** You have turned to be cruel to me. With the might of your hand you persecute me.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** You lift me up to the wind, and drive me with it. You dissolve me in the storm.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For I know that you will bring me to death, To the house appointed for all living.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** “However doesn’t one stretch out a hand in his fall? Or in his calamity therefore cry for help?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Didn’t I weep for him who was in trouble? Wasn’t my soul grieved for the needy?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** When I looked for good, then evil came; When I waited for light, there came darkness.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** My heart is troubled, and doesn’t rest. Days of affliction have come on me.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** I go mourning without the sun. I stand up in the assembly, and cry for help.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** I am a brother to jackals, and a companion to ostriches.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** My skin grows black and peels from me. My bones are burned with heat.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Therefore my harp has turned to mourning, and my pipe into the voice of those who weep.
