# Job 30 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** But now those who are younger than I make sport of me; those whose fathers I would not have put with the dogs of my flocks.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Of what use is the strength of their hands to me? all force is gone from them.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** They are wasted for need of food, biting the dry earth; their only hope of life is in the waste land.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** They are pulling off the salt leaves from the brushwood, and making a meal of roots.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** They are sent out from among their townsmen, men are crying after them as thieves

<a id="v6"></a>**6** They have to get a resting-place in the hollows of the valleys, in holes of the earth and rocks.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** They make noises like asses among the brushwood; they get together under the thorns.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They are sons of shame, and of men without a name, who have been forced out of the land.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And now I have become their song, and I am a word of shame to them.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** I am disgusting to them; they keep away from me, and put marks of shame on me.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For he has made loose the cord of my bow, and put me to shame; he has sent down my flag to the earth before me.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The lines of his men of war put themselves in order, and make high their ways of destruction against me:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They have made waste my roads, with a view to my destruction; his bowmen come round about me;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** As through a wide broken place in the wall they come on, I am overturned by the shock of their attack.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Fears have come on me; my hope is gone like the wind, and my well-being like a cloud.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But now my soul is turned to water in me, days of trouble overtake me:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The flesh is gone from my bones, and they give me no rest; there is no end to my pains.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** With great force he takes a grip of my clothing, pulling me by the neck of my coat.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Truly God has made me low, even to the earth, and I have become like dust.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** You give no answer to my cry, and take no note of my prayer.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** You have become cruel to me; the strength of your hand is hard on me.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Lifting me up, you make me go on the wings of the wind; I am broken up by the storm.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For I am certain that you will send me back to death, and to the meeting-place ordered for all living.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Has not my hand been stretched out in help to the poor? have I not been a saviour to him in his trouble?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Have I not been weeping for the crushed? and was not my soul sad for him who was in need?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For I was looking for good, and evil came; I was waiting for light, and it became dark.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** My feelings are strongly moved, and give me no rest; days of trouble have overtaken me.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** I go about in dark clothing, uncomforted; I get up in the public place, crying out for help.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** I have become a brother to the jackals, and go about in the company of ostriches.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** My skin is black and dropping off me; and my bones are burning with the heat of my disease.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And my music has been turned to sorrow, and the sound of my pipe into the noise of weeping.
