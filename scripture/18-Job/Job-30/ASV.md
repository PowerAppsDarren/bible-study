# Job 30 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** But now they that are younger than I have me in derision, Whose fathers I disdained to set with the dogs of my flock.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Yea, the strength of their hands, whereto should it profit me? Men in whom ripe age is perished.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** They are gaunt with want and famine; They gnaw the dry ground, in the gloom of wasteness and desolation.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** They pluck salt-wort by the bushes; And the roots of the broom are their food.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** They are driven forth from the midst of men; They cry after them as after a thief;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** So that they dwell in frightful valleys, In holes of the earth and of the rocks.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Among the bushes they bray; Under the nettles they are gathered together.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** They arechildren of fools, yea, children of base men; They were scourged out of the land.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And now I am become their song, Yea, I am a byword unto them.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** They abhor me, they stand aloof from me, And spare not to spit in my face.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For he hath loosed his cord, and afflicted me; And they have cast off the bridle before me.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Upon my right hand rise the rabble; They thrust aside my feet, And they cast up against me their ways of destruction.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They mar my path, They set forward my calamity, Even men that have no helper.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** As through a wide breach they come: In the midst of the ruin they roll themselves upon me.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Terrors are turned upon me; They chase mine honor as the wind; And my welfare is passed away as a cloud.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And now my soul is poured out within me; Days of affliction have taken hold upon me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** In the night season my bones are pierced in me, And thepainsthat gnaw me take no rest.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** By God’sgreat force is my garment disfigured; It bindeth me about as the collar of my coat.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** He hath cast me into the mire, And I am become like dust and ashes.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** I cry unto thee, and thou dost not answer me: I stand up, and thou gazest at me.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Thou art turned to be cruel to me; With the might of thy hand thou persecutest me.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Thou liftest me up to the wind, thou causest me to rideupon it; And thou dissolvest me in the storm.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For I know that thou wilt bring me to death, And to the house appointed for all living.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Howbeit doth not one stretch out the hand in his fall? Or in his calamity therefore cry for help?

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Did not I weep for him that was in trouble? Was not my soul grieved for the needy?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** When I looked for good, then evil came; And when I waited for light, there came darkness.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** My heart is troubled, and resteth not; Days of affliction are come upon me.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** I go mourning without the sun: I stand up in the assembly, and cry for help.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** I am a brother to jackals, And a companion to ostriches.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** My skin is black, and fallethfrom me, And my bones are burned with heat.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Therefore is my harpturnedto mourning, And my pipe into the voice of them that weep.
