# Job 4 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Eliphaz the Temanite answered and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** If we assay to commune with thee, wilt thou be grieved? but who can withhold himself from speaking?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Behold, thou hast instructed many, and thou hast strengthened the weak hands.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Thy words have upholden him that was falling, and thou hast strengthened the feeble knees.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But now it is come upon thee, and thou faintest; it toucheth thee, and thou art troubled.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Is not this thy fear, thy confidence, thy hope, and the uprightness of thy ways?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Remember, I pray thee, who ever perished, being innocent? or where were the righteous cut off?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Even as I have seen, they that plow iniquity, and sow wickedness, reap the same.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** By the blast of God they perish, and by the breath of his nostrils are they consumed.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The roaring of the lion, and the voice of the fierce lion, and the teeth of the young lions, are broken.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The old lion perisheth for lack of prey, and the stout lion’s whelps are scattered abroad.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now a thing was secretly brought to me, and mine ear received a little thereof.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** In thoughts from the visions of the night, when deep sleep falleth on men,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Fear came upon me, and trembling, which made all my bones to shake.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Then a spirit passed before my face; the hair of my flesh stood up:

<a id="v16"></a>**16** It stood still, but I could not discern the form thereof: an image was before mine eyes, there was silence, and I heard a voice, saying,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Shall mortal man be more just than God? shall a man be more pure than his maker?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Behold, he put no trust in his servants; and his angels he charged with folly:

<a id="v19"></a>**19** How much less in them that dwell in houses of clay, whose foundation is in the dust, which are crushed before the moth?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** They are destroyed from morning to evening: they perish for ever without any regarding it.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Doth not their excellency which is in them go away? they die, even without wisdom.
