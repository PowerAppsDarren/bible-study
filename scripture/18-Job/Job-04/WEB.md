# Job 4 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then Eliphaz the Temanite answered,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “If someone ventures to talk with you, will you be grieved? But who can withhold himself from speaking?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Behold, you have instructed many, you have strengthened the weak hands.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Your words have supported him who was falling, You have made firm the feeble knees.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But now it has come to you, and you faint. It touches you, and you are troubled.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Isn’t your piety your confidence? Isn’t the integrity of your ways your hope?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** “Remember, now, whoever perished, being innocent? Or where were the upright cut off?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** According to what I have seen, those who plow iniquity, and sow trouble, reap the same.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** By the breath of God they perish. By the blast of his anger are they consumed.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The roaring of the lion, and the voice of the fierce lion, the teeth of the young lions, are broken.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The old lion perishes for lack of prey. The cubs of the lioness are scattered abroad.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** “Now a thing was secretly brought to me. My ear received a whisper of it.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** In thoughts from the visions of the night, when deep sleep falls on men,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** fear came on me, and trembling, which made all my bones shake.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Then a spirit passed before my face. The hair of my flesh stood up.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** It stood still, but I couldn’t discern its appearance. A form was before my eyes. Silence, then I heard a voice, saying,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** ‘Shall mortal man be more just than God? Shall a man be more pure than his Maker?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Behold, he puts no trust in his servants. He charges his angels with error.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** How much more, those who dwell in houses of clay, whose foundation is in the dust, who are crushed before the moth!

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Between morning and evening they are destroyed. They perish forever without any regarding it.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Isn’t their tent cord plucked up within them? They die, and that without wisdom.’
