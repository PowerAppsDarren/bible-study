# Job 4 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then answered Eliphaz the Temanite, and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** If one assay to commune with thee, wilt thou be grieved? But who can withhold himself from speaking?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Behold, thou hast instructed many, And thou hast strengthened the weak hands.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Thy words have upholden him that was falling, And thou hast made firm the feeble knees.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But now it is come unto thee, and thou faintest; It toucheth thee, and thou art troubled.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Is not thy fear of God thy confidence, Andthe integrity of thy ways thy hope?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Remember, I pray thee, who ever perished, being innocent? Or where were the upright cut off?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** According as I have seen, they that plow iniquity, And sow trouble, reap the same.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** By the breath of God they perish, And by the blast of his anger are they consumed.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The roaring of the lion, and the voice of the fierce lion, And the teeth of the young lions, are broken.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The old lion perisheth for lack of prey, And the whelps of the lioness are scattered abroad.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now a thing was secretly brought to me, And mine ear received a whisper thereof.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** In thoughts from the visions of the night, When deep sleep falleth on men,

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Fear came upon me, and trembling, Which made all my bones to shake.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Then a spirit passed before my face; The hair of my flesh stood up.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** It stood still, but I could not discern the appearance thereof; A form was before mine eyes: There wassilence, and I heard a voice, saying,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Shall mortal man be more just than God? Shall a man be more pure than his Maker?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Behold, he putteth no trust in his servants; And his angels he chargeth with folly:

<a id="v19"></a>**19** How much more them that dwell in houses of clay, Whose foundation is in the dust, Who are crushed before the moth!

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Betwixt morning and evening they are destroyed: They perish for ever without any regarding it.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Is not their tent-cord plucked up within them? They die, and that without wisdom.
