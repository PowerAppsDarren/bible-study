# Job 34 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And Elihu made answer and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Give ear, you wise, to my words; and you who have knowledge, give attention to me;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For words are tested by the ear, as food is tasted by the mouth.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Let us make the decision for ourselves as to what is right; let us have the knowledge among ourselves of what is good.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For Job has said, I am upright, and it is God who has taken away my right;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Though I am right, still I am in pain; my wound may not be made well, though I have done no wrong.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** What man is like Job, a man who freely makes sport of God,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And goes in the company of evil-doers, walking in the way of sinners?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For he has said, It is no profit to a man to take delight in God.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Now then, you wise, take note; you men of knowledge, give ear to me. Let it be far from God to do evil, and from the Ruler of all to do wrong.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For he gives to every man the reward of his work, and sees that he gets the fruit of his ways.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Truly, God does not do evil, and the Ruler of all is not a false judge.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Who put the earth into his care, or made him responsible for the world?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If he made his spirit come back to him, taking his breath into himself again,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** All flesh would come to an end together, and man would go back to the dust.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If you are wise, take note of this; give ear to the voice of my words.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** How may a hater of right be a ruler? and will you say that the upright Ruler of all is evil?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He who says to a king, You are an evil-doer; and to rulers, You are sinners;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Who has no respect for rulers, and who gives no more attention to those who have wealth than to the poor, for they are all the work of his hands.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Suddenly they come to an end, even in the middle of the night: the blow comes on the men of wealth, and they are gone, and the strong are taken away without the hand of man.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For his eyes are on the ways of a man, and he sees all his steps.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** There is no dark place, and no thick cloud, in which the workers of evil may take cover.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For he does not give man a fixed time to come before him to be judged.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He sends the strong to destruction without searching out their cause, and puts others in their place.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** For he has knowledge of their works, overturning them in the night, so that they are crushed.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** The evil-doers are broken by his wrath, he puts his hand on them with force before the eyes of all onlookers.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Because they did not go after him, and took no note of his ways,

<a id="v28"></a>**28** So that the cry of the poor might come up to him, and the prayer of those in need come to his ears.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Men of knowledge, and all wise men, hearing me, will say,

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Job's words do not come from knowledge; they are not the fruit of wisdom.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** May Job be tested to the end, because his answers have been like those of evil men.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** For in addition to his sin, he is uncontrolled in heart; before our eyes he makes sport of God, increasing his words against him.
