# Job 34 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Moreover Elihu answered,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Hear my words, you wise men. Give ear to me, you who have knowledge.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For the ear tries words, as the palate tastes food.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Let us choose for us that which is right. Let us know among ourselves what is good.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For Job has said, ‘I am righteous, God has taken away my right:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Notwithstanding my right I am considered a liar. My wound is incurable, though I am without disobedience.’

<a id="v7"></a>**7** What man is like Job, who drinks scorn like water,

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Who goes in company with the workers of iniquity, and walks with wicked men?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For he has said, ‘It profits a man nothing that he should delight himself with God.’

<a id="v10"></a>**10** “Therefore listen to me, you men of understanding: far be it from God, that he should do wickedness, from the Almighty, that he should commit iniquity.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For the work of a man he will render to him, and cause every man to find according to his ways.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Yes surely, God will not do wickedly, neither will the Almighty pervert justice.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Who put him in charge of the earth? or who has appointed him over the whole world?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If he set his heart on himself, If he gathered to himself his spirit and his breath,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** all flesh would perish together, and man would turn again to dust.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** “If now you have understanding, hear this. Listen to the voice of my words.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Shall even one who hates justice govern? Will you condemn him who is righteous and mighty?—

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Who says to a king, ‘Vile!’ or to nobles, ‘Wicked!’?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Who doesn’t respect the persons of princes, nor respects the rich more than the poor; for they all are the work of his hands.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** In a moment they die, even at midnight. The people are shaken and pass away. The mighty are taken away without a hand.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** “For his eyes are on the ways of a man. He sees all his goings.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** There is no darkness, nor thick gloom, where the workers of iniquity may hide themselves.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For he doesn’t need to consider a man further, that he should go before God in judgment.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He breaks in pieces mighty men in ways past finding out, and sets others in their place.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Therefore he takes knowledge of their works. He overturns them in the night, so that they are destroyed.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He strikes them as wicked men in the open sight of others;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** because they turned aside from following him, and wouldn’t pay attention to any of his ways,

<a id="v28"></a>**28** so that they caused the cry of the poor to come to him. He heard the cry of the afflicted.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** When he gives quietness, who then can condemn? When he hides his face, who then can see him? Alike whether to a nation, or to a man,

<a id="v30"></a>**30** that the godless man may not reign, that there be no one to ensnare the people.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** “For has any said to God, ‘I am guilty, but I will not offend any more.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Teach me that which I don’t see. If I have done iniquity, I will do it no more’?

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Shall his recompense be as you desire, that you refuse it? For you must choose, and not I. Therefore speak what you know.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Men of understanding will tell me, yes, every wise man who hears me:

<a id="v35"></a>**35** ‘Job speaks without knowledge. His words are without wisdom.’

<a id="v36"></a>**36** I wish that Job were tried to the end, because of his answering like wicked men.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** For he adds rebellion to his sin. He claps his hands among us, and multiplies his words against God.”
