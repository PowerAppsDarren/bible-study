# Job 34 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Furthermore Elihu answered and said,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Hear my words, O ye wise men; and give ear unto me, ye that have knowledge.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For the ear trieth words, as the mouth tasteth meat.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Let us choose to us judgment: let us know among ourselves what is good.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For Job hath said, I am righteous: and God hath taken away my judgment.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Should I lie against my right? my wound is incurable without transgression.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** What man is like Job, who drinketh up scorning like water?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Which goeth in company with the workers of iniquity, and walketh with wicked men.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For he hath said, It profiteth a man nothing that he should delight himself with God.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Therefore hearken unto me, ye men of understanding: far be it from God, that he should do wickedness; and from the Almighty, that he should commit iniquity.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** For the work of a man shall he render unto him, and cause every man to find according to his ways.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Yea, surely God will not do wickedly, neither will the Almighty pervert judgment.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Who hath given him a charge over the earth? or who hath disposed the whole world?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** If he set his heart upon man, if he gather unto himself his spirit and his breath;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** All flesh shall perish together, and man shall turn again unto dust.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If now thou hast understanding, hear this: hearken to the voice of my words.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Shall even he that hateth right govern? and wilt thou condemn him that is most just?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Is it fit to say to a king, Thou art wicked? and to princes, Ye are ungodly?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** How much less to him that accepteth not the persons of princes, nor regardeth the rich more than the poor? for they all are the work of his hands.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** In a moment shall they die, and the people shall be troubled at midnight, and pass away: and the mighty shall be taken away without hand.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For his eyes are upon the ways of man, and he seeth all his goings.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** There is no darkness, nor shadow of death, where the workers of iniquity may hide themselves.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For he will not lay upon man more than right; that he should enter into judgment with God.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** He shall break in pieces mighty men without number, and set others in their stead.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Therefore he knoweth their works, and he overturneth them in the night, so that they are destroyed.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** He striketh them as wicked men in the open sight of others;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Because they turned back from him, and would not consider any of his ways:

<a id="v28"></a>**28** So that they cause the cry of the poor to come unto him, and he heareth the cry of the afflicted.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** When he giveth quietness, who then can make trouble? and when he hideth his face, who then can behold him? whether it be done against a nation, or against a man only:

<a id="v30"></a>**30** That the hypocrite reign not, lest the people be ensnared.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Surely it is meet to be said unto God, I have borne chastisement, I will not offend any more:

<a id="v32"></a>**32** That which I see not teach thou me: if I have done iniquity, I will do no more.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Should it be according to thy mind? he will recompense it, whether thou refuse, or whether thou choose; and not I: therefore speak what thou knowest.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Let men of understanding tell me, and let a wise man hearken unto me.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Job hath spoken without knowledge, and his words were without wisdom.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** My desire is that Job may be tried unto the end because of his answers for wicked men.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** For he addeth rebellion unto his sin, he clappeth his hands among us, and multiplieth his words against God.
