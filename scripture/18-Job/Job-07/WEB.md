# Job 7 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “Isn’t a man forced to labor on earth? Aren’t his days like the days of a hired hand?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As a servant who earnestly desires the shadow, as a hireling who looks for his wages,

<a id="v3"></a>**3** so am I made to possess months of misery, wearisome nights are appointed to me.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** When I lie down, I say, ‘When shall I arise, and the night be gone?’ I toss and turn until the dawning of the day.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** My flesh is clothed with worms and clods of dust. My skin closes up, and breaks out afresh.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** My days are swifter than a weaver’s shuttle, and are spent without hope.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Oh remember that my life is a breath. My eye shall no more see good.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The eye of him who sees me shall see me no more. Your eyes shall be on me, but I shall not be.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** As the cloud is consumed and vanishes away, so he who goes down to Sheol shall come up no more.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He shall return no more to his house, neither shall his place know him any more.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** “Therefore I will not keep silent. I will speak in the anguish of my spirit. I will complain in the bitterness of my soul.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Am I a sea, or a sea monster, that you put a guard over me?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** When I say, ‘My bed shall comfort me. My couch shall ease my complaint;’

<a id="v14"></a>**14** then you scare me with dreams, and terrify me through visions:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** so that my soul chooses strangling, death rather than my bones.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I loathe my life. I don’t want to live forever. Leave me alone, for my days are but a breath.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** What is man, that you should magnify him, that you should set your mind on him,

<a id="v18"></a>**18** that you should visit him every morning, and test him every moment?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** How long will you not look away from me, nor leave me alone until I swallow down my spittle?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** If I have sinned, what do I do to you, you watcher of men? Why have you set me as a mark for you, so that I am a burden to myself?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Why do you not pardon my disobedience, and take away my iniquity? For now shall I lie down in the dust. You will seek me diligently, but I shall not be.”
