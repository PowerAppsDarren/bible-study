# Job 7 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Is there not an appointed time to man upon earth? are not his days also like the days of an hireling?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As a servant earnestly desireth the shadow, and as an hireling looketh for the reward of his work:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** So am I made to possess months of vanity, and wearisome nights are appointed to me.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** When I lie down, I say, When shall I arise, and the night be gone? and I am full of tossings to and fro unto the dawning of the day.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** My flesh is clothed with worms and clods of dust; my skin is broken, and become loathsome.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** My days are swifter than a weaver’s shuttle, and are spent without hope.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** O remember that my life is wind: mine eye shall no more see good.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The eye of him that hath seen me shall see me no more: thine eyes are upon me, and I am not.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** As the cloud is consumed and vanisheth away: so he that goeth down to the grave shall come up no more.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He shall return no more to his house, neither shall his place know him any more.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Therefore I will not refrain my mouth; I will speak in the anguish of my spirit; I will complain in the bitterness of my soul.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Am I a sea, or a whale, that thou settest a watch over me?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** When I say, My bed shall comfort me, my couch shall ease my complaint;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Then thou scarest me with dreams, and terrifiest me through visions:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** So that my soul chooseth strangling, and death rather than my life.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I loathe it; I would not live alway: let me alone; for my days are vanity.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** What is man, that thou shouldest magnify him? and that thou shouldest set thine heart upon him?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And that thou shouldest visit him every morning, and try him every moment?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** How long wilt thou not depart from me, nor let me alone till I swallow down my spittle?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** I have sinned; what shall I do unto thee, O thou preserver of men? why hast thou set me as a mark against thee, so that I am a burden to myself?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And why dost thou not pardon my transgression, and take away mine iniquity? for now shall I sleep in the dust; and thou shalt seek me in the morning, but I shall not be.
