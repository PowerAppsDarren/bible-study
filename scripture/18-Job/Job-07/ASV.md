# Job 7 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Is there not a warfare to man upon earth? And are not his days like the days of a hireling?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As a servant that earnestly desireth the shadow, And as a hireling that looketh for his wages:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** So am I made to possess months of misery, And wearisome nights are appointed to me.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** When I lie down, I say, When shall I arise, and the night be gone? And I am full of tossings to and fro unto the dawning of the day.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** My flesh is clothed with worms and clods of dust; My skin closeth up, and breaketh out afresh.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** My days are swifter than a weaver’s shuttle, And are spent without hope.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Oh remember that my life is a breath: Mine eye shall no more see good.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The eye of him that seeth me shall behold me no more; Thine eyes shall be upon me, but I shall not be.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** As the cloud is consumed and vanisheth away, So he that goeth down to Sheol shall come up no more.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He shall return no more to his house, Neither shall his place know him any more.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Therefore I will not refrain my mouth; I will speak in the anguish of my spirit; I will complain in the bitterness of my soul.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Am I a sea, or a sea-monster, That thou settest a watch over me?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** When I say, My bed shall comfort me, My couch shall ease my complaint;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Then thou scarest me with dreams, And terrifiest me through visions:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** So that my soul chooseth strangling, And death rather than these my bones.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I loathe my life; I would not live alway: Let me alone; for my days are vanity.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** What is man, that thou shouldest magnify him, And that thou shouldest set thy mind upon him,

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And that thou shouldest visit him every morning, And try him every moment?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** How long wilt thou not look away from me, Nor let me alone till I swallow down my spittle?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** If I have sinned, what do I unto thee, O thou watcher of men? Why hast thou set me as a mark for thee, So that I am a burden to myself?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And why dost thou not pardon my transgression, and take away mine iniquity? For now shall I lie down in the dust; And thou wilt seek me diligently, but I shall not be.
