# Job 7 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Has not man his ordered time of trouble on the earth? and are not his days like the days of a servant working for payment?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** As a servant desiring the shades of evening, and a workman looking for his payment:

<a id="v3"></a>**3** So I have for my heritage months of pain to no purpose, and nights of weariness are given to me.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** When I go to my bed, I say, When will it be time to get up? but the night is long, and I am turning from side to side till morning light.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** My flesh is covered with worms and dust; my skin gets hard and then is cracked again.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** My days go quicker than the cloth-worker's thread, and come to an end without hope.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** O, keep in mind that my life is wind: my eye will never again see good.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The eye of him who sees me will see me no longer: your eyes will be looking for me, but I will be gone.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** A cloud comes to an end and is gone; so he who goes down into the underworld comes not up again.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** He will not come back to his house, and his place will have no more knowledge of him.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** So I will not keep my mouth shut; I will let the words come from it in the pain of my spirit, my soul will make a bitter outcry.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Am I a sea, or a sea-beast, that you put a watch over me?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** When I say, In my bed I will have comfort, there I will get rest from my disease;

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Then you send dreams to me, and visions of fear;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** So that a hard death seems better to my soul than my pains.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I have no desire for life, I would not be living for ever! Keep away from me, for my days are as a breath.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** What is man, that you have made him great, and that your attention is fixed on him,

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And that your hand is on him every morning, and that you are testing him every minute?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** How long will it be before your eyes are turned away from me, so that I may have a minute's breathing-space?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** If I have done wrong, what have I done to you, O keeper of men? why have you made me a mark for your blows, so that I am a weariness to myself?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And why do you not take away my sin, and let my wrongdoing be ended? for now I go down to the dust, and you will be searching for me with care, but I will be gone.
