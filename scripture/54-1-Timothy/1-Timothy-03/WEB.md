# 1 Timothy 3 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** This is a faithful saying: if a man seeks the office of an overseer, he desires a good work.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The overseer therefore must be without reproach, the husband of one wife, temperate, sensible, modest, hospitable, good at teaching;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** not a drinker, not violent, not greedy for money, but gentle, not quarrelsome, not covetous;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** one who rules his own house well, having children in subjection with all reverence;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** (but if a man doesn’t know how to rule his own house, how will he take care of the assembly of God?)

<a id="v6"></a>**6** not a new convert, lest being puffed up he fall into the same condemnation as the devil.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Moreover he must have good testimony from those who are outside, to avoid falling into reproach and the snare of the devil.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Servants, in the same way, must be reverent, not double-tongued, not addicted to much wine, not greedy for money;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** holding the mystery of the faith in a pure conscience.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Let them also first be tested; then let them serve if they are blameless.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Their wives in the same way must be reverent, not slanderers, temperate, faithful in all things.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Let servants be husbands of one wife, ruling their children and their own houses well.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For those who have served well gain for themselves a good standing, and great boldness in the faith which is in Christ Jesus.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** These things I write to you, hoping to come to you shortly;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** but if I wait long, that you may know how men ought to behave themselves in God’s house, which is the assembly of the living God, the pillar and ground of the truth.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Without controversy, the mystery of godliness is great:  God was revealed in the flesh, justified in the spirit, seen by angels, preached among the nations, believed on in the world, and received up in glory.
