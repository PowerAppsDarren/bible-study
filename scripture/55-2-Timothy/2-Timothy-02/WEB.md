# 2 Timothy 2 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** You therefore, my child, be strengthened in the grace that is in Christ Jesus.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The things which you have heard from me among many witnesses, commit the same to faithful men, who will be able to teach others also.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** You therefore must endure hardship, as a good soldier of Christ Jesus.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** No soldier on duty entangles himself in the affairs of life, that he may please him who enrolled him as a soldier.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Also, if anyone competes in athletics, he isn’t crowned unless he has competed by the rules.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The farmers who labor must be the first to get a share of the crops.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Consider what I say, and may the Lord give you understanding in all things.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Remember Jesus Christ, risen from the dead, of the seed of David, according to my Good News,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** in which I suffer hardship to the point of chains as a criminal. But God’s word isn’t chained.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Therefore I endure all things for the chosen ones’ sake, that they also may obtain the salvation which is in Christ Jesus with eternal glory.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** This saying is faithful:  “For if we died with him, we will also live with him.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** If we endure, we will also reign with him. If we deny him, he also will deny us.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** If we are faithless, he remains faithful. He can’t deny himself.”

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Remind them of these things, charging them in the sight of the Lord, that they don’t argue about words, to no profit, to the subverting of those who hear.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Give diligence to present yourself approved by God, a workman who doesn’t need to be ashamed, properly handling the Word of Truth.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But shun empty chatter, for it will go further in ungodliness,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** and those words will consume like gangrene, of whom is Hymenaeus and Philetus;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** men who have erred concerning the truth, saying that the resurrection is already past, and overthrowing the faith of some.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** However God’s firm foundation stands, having this seal, “The Lord knows those who are his,” and, “Let every one who names the name of the Lord depart from unrighteousness.”

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Now in a large house there are not only vessels of gold and of silver, but also of wood and of clay. Some are for honor, and some for dishonor.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** If anyone therefore purges himself from these, he will be a vessel for honor, sanctified, and suitable for the master’s use, prepared for every good work.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Flee from youthful lusts; but pursue righteousness, faith, love, and peace with those who call on the Lord out of a pure heart.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But refuse foolish and ignorant questionings, knowing that they generate strife.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The Lord’s servant must not quarrel, but be gentle towards all, able to teach, patient,

<a id="v25"></a>**25** in gentleness correcting those who oppose him: perhaps God may give them repentance leading to a full knowledge of the truth,

<a id="v26"></a>**26** and they may recover themselves out of the devil’s snare, having been taken captive by him to his will.
