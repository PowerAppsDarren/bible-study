# 2 Timothy 2 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Thou therefore, my child, be strengthened in the grace that is in Christ Jesus.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And the things which thou hast heard from me among many witnesses, the same commit thou to faithful men, who shall be able to teach others also.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Suffer hardship with me, as a good soldier of Christ Jesus.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** No soldier on service entangleth himself in the affairs of this life; that he may please him who enrolled him as a soldier.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And if also a man contend in the games, he is not crowned, except he have contended lawfully.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The husbandman that laboreth must be the first to partake of the fruits.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Consider what I say; for the Lord shall give thee understanding in all things.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Remember Jesus Christ, risen from the dead, of the seed of David, according to my gospel:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** wherein I suffer hardship unto bonds, as a malefactor; but the word of God is not bound.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Therefore I endure all things for the elect’s sake, that they also may obtain the salvation which is in Christ Jesus with eternal glory.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Faithful is the saying: For if we died with him, we shall also live with him:

<a id="v12"></a>**12** if we endure, we shall also reign with him: if we shall deny him, he also will deny us:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** if we are faithless, he abideth faithful; for he cannot deny himself.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Of these things put them in remembrance, charging them in the sight of the Lord, that they strive not about words, to no profit, to the subverting of them that hear.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Give diligence to present thyself approved unto God, a workman that needeth not to be ashamed, handling aright the word of truth.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But shun profane babblings: for they will proceed further in ungodliness,

<a id="v17"></a>**17** and their word will eat as doth a gangrene: of whom is Hymenæus and Philetus;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** men who concerning the truth have erred, saying that the resurrection is past already, and overthrow the faith of some.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Howbeit the firm foundation of God standeth, having this seal, The Lord knoweth them that are his: and, Let every one that nameth the name of the Lord depart from unrighteousness.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Now in a great house there are not only vessels of gold and of silver, but also of wood and of earth; and some unto honor, and some unto dishonor.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** If a man therefore purge himself from these, he shall be a vessel unto honor, sanctified, meet for the master’s use, prepared unto every good work.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** But flee youthful lusts, and follow after righteousness, faith, love, peace, with them that call on the Lord out of a pure heart.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But foolish and ignorant questionings refuse, knowing that they gender strifes.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** And the Lord’s servant must not strive, but be gentle towards all, apt to teach, forbearing,

<a id="v25"></a>**25** in meekness correcting them that oppose themselves; if peradventure God may give them repentance unto the knowledge of the truth,

<a id="v26"></a>**26** and they may recover themselves out of the snare of the devil, having been taken captive by him unto his will.
