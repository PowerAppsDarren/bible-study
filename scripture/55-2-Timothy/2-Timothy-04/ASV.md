# 2 Timothy 4 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** I chargetheein the sight of God, and of Christ Jesus, who shall judge the living and the dead, and by his appearing and his kingdom:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** preach the word; be urgent in season, out of season; reprove, rebuke, exhort, with all longsuffering and teaching.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For the time will come when they will not endure the sound doctrine; but, having itching ears, will heap to themselves teachers after their own lusts;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** and will turn away their ears from the truth, and turn aside unto fables.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But be thou sober in all things, suffer hardship, do the work of an evangelist, fulfil thy ministry.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For I am already being offered, and the time of my departure is come.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** I have fought the good fight, I have finished the course, I have kept the faith:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** henceforth there is laid up for me the crown of righteousness, which the Lord, the righteous judge, shall give to me at that day; and not to me only, but also to all them that have loved his appearing.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Give diligence to come shortly unto me:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** for Demas forsook me, having loved this present world, and went to Thessalonica; Crescens to Galatia, Titus to Dalmatia.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Only Luke is with me. Take Mark, and bring him with thee; for he is useful to me for ministering.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** But Tychicus I sent to Ephesus.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The cloak that I left at Troas with Carpus, bring when thou comest, and the books, especially the parchments.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Alexander the coppersmith did me much evil: the Lord will render to him according to his works:

<a id="v15"></a>**15** of whom do thou also beware; for he greatly withstood our words.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** At my first defence no one took my part, but all forsook me: may it not be laid to their account.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** But the Lord stood by me, and strengthened me; that through me the message might be fully proclaimed, and that all the Gentiles might hear: and I was delivered out of the mouth of the lion.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The Lord will deliver me from every evil work, and will save me unto his heavenly kingdom: to whom be the glory for ever and ever. Amen.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Salute Prisca and Aquila, and the house of Onesiphorus.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Erastus remained at Corinth: but Trophimus I left at Miletus sick.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Give diligence to come before winter. Eubulus saluteth thee, and Pudens, and Linus, and Claudia, and all the brethren.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** The Lord be with thy spirit. Grace be with you.
