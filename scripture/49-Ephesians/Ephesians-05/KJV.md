# Ephesians 5 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Be ye therefore followers of God, as dear children;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And walk in love, as Christ also hath loved us, and hath given himself for us an offering and a sacrifice to God for a sweetsmelling savour.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But fornication, and all uncleanness, or covetousness, let it not be once named among you, as becometh saints;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Neither filthiness, nor foolish talking, nor jesting, which are not convenient: but rather giving of thanks.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For this ye know, that no whoremonger, nor unclean person, nor covetous man, who is an idolater, hath any inheritance in the kingdom of Christ and of God.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Let no man deceive you with vain words: for because of these things cometh the wrath of God upon the children of disobedience.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Be not ye therefore partakers with them.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For ye were sometimes darkness, but now are ye light in the Lord: walk as children of light:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** (For the fruit of the Spirit is in all goodness and righteousness and truth;)

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Proving what is acceptable unto the Lord.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And have no fellowship with the unfruitful works of darkness, but rather reprove them.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For it is a shame even to speak of those things which are done of them in secret.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But all things that are reproved are made manifest by the light: for whatsoever doth make manifest is light.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Wherefore he saith, Awake thou that sleepest, and arise from the dead, and Christ shall give thee light.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** See then that ye walk circumspectly, not as fools, but as wise,

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Redeeming the time, because the days are evil.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Wherefore be ye not unwise, but understanding what the will of the Lord is.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And be not drunk with wine, wherein is excess; but be filled with the Spirit;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Speaking to yourselves in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing and making melody in your heart to the Lord;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Giving thanks always for all things unto God and the Father in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Submitting yourselves one to another in the fear of God.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Wives, submit yourselves unto your own husbands, as unto the Lord.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For the husband is the head of the wife, even as Christ is the head of the church: and he is the saviour of the body.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Therefore as the church is subject unto Christ, so let the wives be to their own husbands in every thing.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave himself for it;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** That he might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the word,

<a id="v27"></a>**27** That he might present it to himself a glorious church, not having spot, or wrinkle, or any such thing; but that it should be holy and without blemish.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** So ought men to love their wives as their own bodies. He that loveth his wife loveth himself.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For no man ever yet hated his own flesh; but nourisheth and cherisheth it, even as the Lord the church:

<a id="v30"></a>**30** For we are members of his body, of his flesh, and of his bones.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** For this cause shall a man leave his father and mother, and shall be joined unto his wife, and they two shall be one flesh.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** This is a great mystery: but I speak concerning Christ and the church.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Nevertheless let every one of you in particular so love his wife even as himself; and the wife see that she reverence her husband.
