# Ephesians 5 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Be ye therefore imitators of God, as beloved children;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** and walk in love, even as Christ also loved you, and gave himself up for us, an offering and a sacrifice to God for an odor of a sweet smell.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But fornication, and all uncleanness, or covetousness, let it not even be named among you, as becometh saints;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** nor filthiness, nor foolish talking, or jesting, which are not befitting: but rather giving of thanks.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For this ye know of a surety, that no fornicator, nor unclean person, nor covetous man, who is an idolater, hath any inheritance in the kingdom of Christ and God.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Let no man deceive you with empty words: for because of these things cometh the wrath of God upon the sons of disobedience.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Be not ye therefore partakers with them;

<a id="v8"></a>**8** for ye were once darkness, but are now light in the Lord: walk as children of light

<a id="v9"></a>**9** (for the fruit of the light is in all goodness and righteousness and truth),

<a id="v10"></a>**10** proving what is well-pleasing unto the Lord;

<a id="v11"></a>**11** and have no fellowship with the unfruitful works of darkness, but rather even reprove them;

<a id="v12"></a>**12** for the things which are done by them in secret it is a shame even to speak of.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But all things when they are reproved are made manifest by the light: for everything that is made manifest is light.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Wherefore he saith, Awake, thou that sleepest, and arise from the dead, and Christ shall shine upon thee.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Look therefore carefully how ye walk, not as unwise, but as wise;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** redeeming the time, because the days are evil.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Wherefore be ye not foolish, but understand what the will of the Lord is.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** And be not drunken with wine, wherein is riot, but be filled with the Spirit;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** speaking one to another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing and making melody with your heart to the Lord;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** giving thanks always for all things in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ to God, even the Father;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** subjecting yourselves one to another in the fear of Christ.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Wives, be in subjection unto your own husbands, as unto the Lord.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For the husband is the head of the wife, as Christ also is the head of the church, being himself the saviour of the body.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** But as the church is subject to Christ, so let the wives also be to their husbands in everything.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave himself up for it;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** that he might sanctify it, having cleansed it by the washing of water with the word,

<a id="v27"></a>**27** that he might present the church to himself a glorious church, not having spot or wrinkle or any such thing; but that it should be holy and without blemish.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Even so ought husbands also to love their own wives as their own bodies. He that loveth his own wife loveth himself:

<a id="v29"></a>**29** for no man ever hated his own flesh; but nourisheth and cherisheth it, even as Christ also the church;

<a id="v30"></a>**30** because we are members of his body.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** For this cause shall a man leave his father and mother, and shall cleave to his wife; and the two shall become one flesh.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** This mystery is great: but I speak in regard of Christ and of the church.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Nevertheless do ye also severally love each one his own wife even as himself; and let the wife see that she fear her husband.
