# Ephesians 5 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Be therefore imitators of God, as beloved children.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Walk in love, even as Christ also loved you, and gave himself up for us, an offering and a sacrifice to God for a sweet-smelling fragrance.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But sexual immorality, and all uncleanness, or covetousness, let it not even be mentioned among you, as becomes saints;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** nor filthiness, nor foolish talking, nor jesting, which are not appropriate; but rather giving of thanks.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Know this for sure, that no sexually immoral person, nor unclean person, nor covetous man, who is an idolater, has any inheritance in the Kingdom of Christ and God.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Let no one deceive you with empty words. For because of these things, the wrath of God comes on the children of disobedience.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Therefore don’t be partakers with them.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For you were once darkness, but are now light in the Lord. Walk as children of light,

<a id="v9"></a>**9** for the fruit of the Spirit is in all goodness and righteousness and truth,

<a id="v10"></a>**10** proving what is well pleasing to the Lord.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Have no fellowship with the unfruitful works of darkness, but rather even reprove them.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For the things which are done by them in secret, it is a shame even to speak of.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But all things, when they are reproved, are revealed by the light, for everything that reveals is light.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Therefore he says, “Awake, you who sleep, and arise from the dead, and Christ will shine on you.”

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Therefore watch carefully how you walk, not as unwise, but as wise;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** redeeming the time, because the days are evil.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Therefore don’t be foolish, but understand what the will of the Lord is.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Don’t be drunken with wine, in which is dissipation, but be filled with the Spirit,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** speaking to one another in psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs; singing, and making melody in your heart to the Lord;

<a id="v20"></a>**20** giving thanks always concerning all things in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, to God, even the Father;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** subjecting yourselves one to another in the fear of Christ.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Wives, be subject to your own husbands, as to the Lord.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For the husband is the head of the wife, and Christ also is the head of the assembly, being himself the savior of the body.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** But as the assembly is subject to Christ, so let the wives also be to their own husbands in everything.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the assembly, and gave himself up for it;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** that he might sanctify it, having cleansed it by the washing of water with the word,

<a id="v27"></a>**27** that he might present the assembly to himself gloriously, not having spot or wrinkle or any such thing; but that it should be holy and without defect.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Even so husbands also ought to love their own wives as their own bodies. He who loves his own wife loves himself.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For no man ever hated his own flesh; but nourishes and cherishes it, even as the Lord also does the assembly;

<a id="v30"></a>**30** because we are members of his body, of his flesh and bones.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** “For this cause a man will leave his father and mother, and will be joined to his wife. The two will become one flesh.”

<a id="v32"></a>**32** This mystery is great, but I speak concerning Christ and of the assembly.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Nevertheless each of you must also love his own wife even as himself; and let the wife see that she respects her husband.
