# Ecclesiastes 4 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Then I returned and saw all the oppressions that are done under the sun: and, behold, the tears of such as were oppressed, and they had no comforter; and on the side of their oppressors there was power; but they had no comforter.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Wherefore I praised the dead that have been long dead more than the living that are yet alive;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** yea, better than them both did I esteem him that hath not yet been, who hath not seen the evil work that is done under the sun.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Then I saw all labor and every skilful work, that for this a man is envied of his neighbor. This also is vanity and a striving after wind.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The fool foldeth his hands together, and eateth his own flesh.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Better is a handful, with quietness, than two handfuls with labor and striving after wind.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Then I returned and saw vanity under the sun.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** There is one that is alone, and he hath not a second; yea, he hath neither son nor brother; yet is there no end of all his labor, neither are his eyes satisfied with riches. For whom then, saith he, do I labor, and deprive my soul of good? This also is vanity, yea, it is a sore travail.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Two are better than one, because they have a good reward for their labor.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** For if they fall, the one will lift up his fellow; but woe to him that is alone when he falleth, and hath not another to lift him up.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Again, if two lie together, then they have warmth; but how can one be warm alone?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And if a man prevail against him that is alone, two shall withstand him; and a threefold cord is not quickly broken.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Better is a poor and wise youth than an old and foolish king, who knoweth not how to receive admonition any more.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For out of prison he came forth to be king; yea, even in his kingdom he was born poor.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** I saw all the living that walk under the sun, that they were with the youth, the second, that stood up in his stead.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** There was no end of all the people, even of all them over whom he was: yet they that come after shall not rejoice in him. Surely this also is vanity and a striving after wind.
