# Ecclesiastes 4 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** So I returned, and considered all the oppressions that are done under the sun: and behold the tears of such as were oppressed, and they had no comforter; and on the side of their oppressors there was power; but they had no comforter.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Wherefore I praised the dead which are already dead more than the living which are yet alive.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Yea, better is he than both they, which hath not yet been, who hath not seen the evil work that is done under the sun.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Again, I considered all travail, and every right work, that for this a man is envied of his neighbour. This is also vanity and vexation of spirit.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The fool foldeth his hands together, and eateth his own flesh.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Better is an handful with quietness, than both the hands full with travail and vexation of spirit.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Then I returned, and I saw vanity under the sun.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** There is one alone, and there is not a second; yea, he hath neither child nor brother: yet is there no end of all his labour; neither is his eye satisfied with riches; neither saith he, For whom do I labour, and bereave my soul of good? This is also vanity, yea, it is a sore travail.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Two are better than one; because they have a good reward for their labour.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** For if they fall, the one will lift up his fellow: but woe to him that is alone when he falleth; for he hath not another to help him up.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Again, if two lie together, then they have heat: but how can one be warm alone?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And if one prevail against him, two shall withstand him; and a threefold cord is not quickly broken.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Better is a poor and a wise child than an old and foolish king, who will no more be admonished.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For out of prison he cometh to reign; whereas also he that is born in his kingdom becometh poor.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** I considered all the living which walk under the sun, with the second child that shall stand up in his stead.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** There is no end of all the people, even of all that have been before them: they also that come after shall not rejoice in him. Surely this also is vanity and vexation of spirit.
