# Ecclesiastes 10 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Dead flies cause the ointment of the apothecary to send forth a stinking savour: so doth a little folly him that is in reputation for wisdom and honour.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** A wise man’s heart is at his right hand; but a fool’s heart at his left.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Yea also, when he that is a fool walketh by the way, his wisdom faileth him, and he saith to every one that he is a fool.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** If the spirit of the ruler rise up against thee, leave not thy place; for yielding pacifieth great offences.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** There is an evil which I have seen under the sun, as an error which proceedeth from the ruler:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Folly is set in great dignity, and the rich sit in low place.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** I have seen servants upon horses, and princes walking as servants upon the earth.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He that diggeth a pit shall fall into it; and whoso breaketh an hedge, a serpent shall bite him.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Whoso removeth stones shall be hurt therewith; and he that cleaveth wood shall be endangered thereby.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** If the iron be blunt, and he do not whet the edge, then must he put to more strength: but wisdom is profitable to direct.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Surely the serpent will bite without enchantment; and a babbler is no better.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The words of a wise man’s mouth are gracious; but the lips of a fool will swallow up himself.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The beginning of the words of his mouth is foolishness: and the end of his talk is mischievous madness.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** A fool also is full of words: a man cannot tell what shall be; and what shall be after him, who can tell him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The labour of the foolish wearieth every one of them, because he knoweth not how to go to the city.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Woe to thee, O land, when thy king is a child, and thy princes eat in the morning!

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Blessed art thou, O land, when thy king is the son of nobles, and thy princes eat in due season, for strength, and not for drunkenness!

<a id="v18"></a>**18** By much slothfulness the building decayeth; and through idleness of the hands the house droppeth through.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A feast is made for laughter, and wine maketh merry: but money answereth all things.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Curse not the king, no not in thy thought; and curse not the rich in thy bedchamber: for a bird of the air shall carry the voice, and that which hath wings shall tell the matter.
