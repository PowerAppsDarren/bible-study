# Ecclesiastes 10 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Dead flies cause the oil of the perfumer to produce an evil odor; so does a little folly outweigh wisdom and honor.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** A wise man’s heart is at his right hand, but a fool’s heart at his left.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Yes also, when the fool walks by the way, his understanding fails him, and he says to everyone that he is a fool.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** If the spirit of the ruler rises up against you, don’t leave your place; for gentleness lays great offenses to rest.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** There is an evil which I have seen under the sun, the sort of error which proceeds from the ruler.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Folly is set in great dignity, and the rich sit in a low place.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** I have seen servants on horses, and princes walking like servants on the earth.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He who digs a pit may fall into it; and whoever breaks through a wall may be bitten by a snake.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Whoever carves out stones may be injured by them. Whoever splits wood may be endangered thereby.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** If the ax is blunt, and one doesn’t sharpen the edge, then he must use more strength; but skill brings success.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** If the snake bites before it is charmed, then is there no profit for the charmer’s tongue.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The words of a wise man’s mouth are gracious; but a fool is swallowed by his own lips.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The beginning of the words of his mouth is foolishness; and the end of his talk is mischievous madness.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** A fool also multiplies words.   Man doesn’t know what will be; and that which will be after him, who can tell him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The labor of fools wearies every one of them; for he doesn’t know how to go to the city.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Woe to you, land, when your king is a child, and your princes eat in the morning!

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Happy are you, land, when your king is the son of nobles,  and your princes eat in due season, for strength, and not for drunkenness!

<a id="v18"></a>**18** By slothfulness the roof sinks in; and through idleness of the hands the house leaks.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A feast is made for laughter, and wine makes the life glad; and money is the answer for all things.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Don’t curse the king, no, not in your thoughts; and don’t curse the rich in your bedroom: for a bird of the sky may carry your voice, and that which has wings may tell the matter.
