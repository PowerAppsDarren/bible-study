# Ecclesiastes 10 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Dead flies make the oil of the perfumer give out an evil smell; more valued is a little wisdom than the great glory of the foolish.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** The heart of the wise man goes in the right direction; but the heart of a foolish man in the wrong.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And when the foolish man is walking in the way, he has no sense and lets everyone see that he is foolish.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** If the wrath of the ruler is against you, keep in your place; in him who keeps quiet even great sins may be overlooked.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** There is an evil which I have seen under the sun, like an error which comes by chance from a ruler:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The foolish are placed in high positions, but men of wealth are kept low.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** I have seen servants on horses, and rulers walking on the earth as servants.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** He who makes a hole for others will himself go into it, and for him who makes a hole through a wall the bite of a snake will be a punishment.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** He who gets out stones from the earth will be damaged by them, and in the cutting of wood there is danger.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** If the iron has no edge, and he does not make it sharp, then he has to put out more strength; but wisdom makes things go well.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** If a snake gives a bite before the word of power is said, then there is no longer any use in the word of power.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The words of a wise man's mouth are sweet to all, but the lips of a foolish man are his destruction.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The first words of his mouth are foolish, and the end of his talk is evil crime.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** The foolish are full of words; man has no knowledge of what will be; and who is able to say what will be after him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The work of the foolish will be a weariness to him, because he has no knowledge of the way to the town.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Unhappy is the land whose king is a boy, and whose rulers are feasting in the morning.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Happy is the land whose ruler is of noble birth, and whose chiefs take food at the right time, for strength and not for feasting.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** When no work is done the roof goes in, and when the hands do nothing water comes into the house.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** A feast is for laughing, and wine makes glad the heart; but by the one and the other money is wasted.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Say not a curse against the king, even in your thoughts; and even secretly say not a curse against the man of wealth; because a bird of the air will take the voice, and that which has wings will give news of it.
