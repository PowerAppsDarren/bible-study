# Ecclesiastes 1 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The words of the Preacher, the son of David, king in Jerusalem.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Vanity of vanities, saith the Preacher; vanity of vanities, all is vanity.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** What profit hath man of all his labor wherein he laboreth under the sun?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** One generation goeth, and another generation cometh; but the earth abideth for ever.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The sun also ariseth, and the sun goeth down, and hasteth to its place where it ariseth.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The wind goeth toward the south, and turneth about unto the north; it turneth about continually in its course, and the wind returneth again to its circuits.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** All the rivers run into the sea, yet the sea is not full; unto the place whither the rivers go, thither they go again.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** All things are full of weariness; man cannot utter it: the eye is not satisfied with seeing, nor the ear filled with hearing.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** That which hath been is that which shall be; and that which hath been done is that which shall be done: and there is no new thing under the sun.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Is there a thing whereof it may be said, See, this is new? it hath been long ago, in the ages which were before us.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** There is no remembrance of the former generations; neither shall there be any remembrance of the latter generations that are to come, among those that shall come after.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** I the Preacher was king over Israel in Jerusalem.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And I applied my heart to seek and to search out by wisdom concerning all that is done under heaven: it is a sore travail that God hath given to the sons of men to be exercised therewith.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I have seen all the works that are done under the sun; and, behold, all is vanity and a striving after wind.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** That which is crooked cannot be made straight; and that which is wanting cannot be numbered.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I communed with mine own heart, saying, Lo, I have gotten me great wisdom above all that were before me in Jerusalem; yea, my heart hath had great experience of wisdom and knowledge.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And I applied my heart to know wisdom, and to know madness and folly: I perceived that this also was a striving after wind.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For in much wisdom is much grief; and he that increaseth knowledge increaseth sorrow.
