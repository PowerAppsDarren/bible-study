# Ecclesiastes 1 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The words of the Preacher, the son of David, king in Jerusalem.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Vanity of vanities, saith the Preacher, vanity of vanities; all is vanity.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** What profit hath a man of all his labour which he taketh under the sun?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** One generation passeth away, and another generation cometh: but the earth abideth for ever.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The sun also ariseth, and the sun goeth down, and hasteth to his place where he arose.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The wind goeth toward the south, and turneth about unto the north; it whirleth about continually, and the wind returneth again according to his circuits.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** All the rivers run into the sea; yet the sea is not full; unto the place from whence the rivers come, thither they return again.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** All things are full of labour; man cannot utter it: the eye is not satisfied with seeing, nor the ear filled with hearing.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The thing that hath been, it is that which shall be; and that which is done is that which shall be done: and there is no new thing under the sun.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Is there any thing whereof it may be said, See, this is new? it hath been already of old time, which was before us.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** There is no remembrance of former things; neither shall there be any remembrance of things that are to come with those that shall come after.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** I the Preacher was king over Israel in Jerusalem.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And I gave my heart to seek and search out by wisdom concerning all things that are done under heaven: this sore travail hath God given to the sons of man to be exercised therewith.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I have seen all the works that are done under the sun; and, behold, all is vanity and vexation of spirit.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** That which is crooked cannot be made straight: and that which is wanting cannot be numbered.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I communed with mine own heart, saying, Lo, I am come to great estate, and have gotten more wisdom than all they that have been before me in Jerusalem: yea, my heart had great experience of wisdom and knowledge.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And I gave my heart to know wisdom, and to know madness and folly: I perceived that this also is vexation of spirit.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For in much wisdom is much grief: and he that increaseth knowledge increaseth sorrow.
