# Ecclesiastes 1 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The words of the Preacher, the son of David, king in Jerusalem:

<a id="v2"></a>**2** “Vanity of vanities,” says the Preacher; “Vanity of vanities, all is vanity.”

<a id="v3"></a>**3** What does man gain from all his labor in which he labors under the sun?

<a id="v4"></a>**4** One generation goes, and another generation comes; but the earth remains forever.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** The sun also rises, and the sun goes down, and hurries to its place where it rises.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** The wind goes toward the south, and turns around to the north. It turns around continually as it goes, and the wind returns again to its courses.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** All the rivers run into the sea, yet the sea is not full. To the place where the rivers flow, there they flow again.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** All things are full of weariness beyond uttering. The eye is not satisfied with seeing, nor the ear filled with hearing.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** That which has been is that which shall be; and that which has been done is that which shall be done: and there is no new thing under the sun.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Is there a thing of which it may be said, “Behold, this is new?” It has been long ago, in the ages which were before us.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** There is no memory of the former; neither shall there be any memory of the latter that are to come, among those that shall come after.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** I, the Preacher, was king over Israel in Jerusalem.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** I applied my heart to seek and to search out by wisdom concerning all that is done under the sky. It is a heavy burden that God has given to the sons of men to be afflicted with.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I have seen all the works that are done under the sun; and behold, all is vanity and a chasing after wind.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** That which is crooked can’t be made straight; and that which is lacking can’t be counted.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I said to myself, “Behold, I have obtained for myself great wisdom above all who were before me in Jerusalem. Yes, my heart has had great experience of wisdom and knowledge.”

<a id="v17"></a>**17** I applied my heart to know wisdom, and to know madness and folly. I perceived that this also was a chasing after wind.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For in much wisdom is much grief; and he who increases knowledge increases sorrow.
