# John 5 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** After this there was a feast of the Jews; and Jesus went up to Jerusalem.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Now there is at Jerusalem by the sheep market a pool, which is called in the Hebrew tongue Bethesda, having five porches.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** In these lay a great multitude of impotent folk, of blind, halt, withered, waiting for the moving of the water.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For an angel went down at a certain season into the pool, and troubled the water: whosoever then first after the troubling of the water stepped in was made whole of whatsoever disease he had.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And a certain man was there, which had an infirmity thirty and eight years.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** When Jesus saw him lie, and knew that he had been now a long time in that case, he saith unto him, Wilt thou be made whole?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The impotent man answered him, Sir, I have no man, when the water is troubled, to put me into the pool: but while I am coming, another steppeth down before me.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Jesus saith unto him, Rise, take up thy bed, and walk.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And immediately the man was made whole, and took up his bed, and walked: and on the same day was the sabbath.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The Jews therefore said unto him that was cured, It is the sabbath day: it is not lawful for thee to carry thy bed.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He answered them, He that made me whole, the same said unto me, Take up thy bed, and walk.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Then asked they him, What man is that which said unto thee, Take up thy bed, and walk?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And he that was healed wist not who it was: for Jesus had conveyed himself away, a multitude being in that place.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Afterward Jesus findeth him in the temple, and said unto him, Behold, thou art made whole: sin no more, lest a worse thing come unto thee.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The man departed, and told the Jews that it was Jesus, which had made him whole.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And therefore did the Jews persecute Jesus, and sought to slay him, because he had done these things on the sabbath day.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** But Jesus answered them, My Father worketh hitherto, and I work.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Therefore the Jews sought the more to kill him, because he not only had broken the sabbath, but said also that God was his Father, making himself equal with God.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Then answered Jesus and said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, The Son can do nothing of himself, but what he seeth the Father do: for what things soever he doeth, these also doeth the Son likewise.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** For the Father loveth the Son, and sheweth him all things that himself doeth: and he will shew him greater works than these, that ye may marvel.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** For as the Father raiseth up the dead, and quickeneth them; even so the Son quickeneth whom he will.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** For the Father judgeth no man, but hath committed all judgment unto the Son:

<a id="v23"></a>**23** That all men should honour the Son, even as they honour the Father. He that honoureth not the Son honoureth not the Father which hath sent him.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Verily, verily, I say unto you, He that heareth my word, and believeth on him that sent me, hath everlasting life, and shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Verily, verily, I say unto you, The hour is coming, and now is, when the dead shall hear the voice of the Son of God: and they that hear shall live.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For as the Father hath life in himself; so hath he given to the Son to have life in himself;

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And hath given him authority to execute judgment also, because he is the Son of man.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Marvel not at this: for the hour is coming, in the which all that are in the graves shall hear his voice,

<a id="v29"></a>**29** And shall come forth; they that have done good, unto the resurrection of life; and they that have done evil, unto the resurrection of damnation.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** I can of mine own self do nothing: as I hear, I judge: and my judgment is just; because I seek not mine own will, but the will of the Father which hath sent me.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** If I bear witness of myself, my witness is not true.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** There is another that beareth witness of me; and I know that the witness which he witnesseth of me is true.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Ye sent unto John, and he bare witness unto the truth.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** But I receive not testimony from man: but these things I say, that ye might be saved.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** He was a burning and a shining light: and ye were willing for a season to rejoice in his light.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** But I have greater witness than that of John: for the works which the Father hath given me to finish, the same works that I do, bear witness of me, that the Father hath sent me.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And the Father himself, which hath sent me, hath borne witness of me. Ye have neither heard his voice at any time, nor seen his shape.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** And ye have not his word abiding in you: for whom he hath sent, him ye believe not.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** Search the scriptures; for in them ye think ye have eternal life: and they are they which testify of me.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** And ye will not come to me, that ye might have life.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** I receive not honour from men.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** But I know you, that ye have not the love of God in you.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** I am come in my Father’s name, and ye receive me not: if another shall come in his own name, him ye will receive.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** How can ye believe, which receive honour one of another, and seek not the honour that cometh from God only?

<a id="v45"></a>**45** Do not think that I will accuse you to the Father: there is one that accuseth you, even Moses, in whom ye trust.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** For had ye believed Moses, ye would have believed me: for he wrote of me.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** But if ye believe not his writings, how shall ye believe my words?
