# John 4 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** When therefore the Lord knew how the Pharisees had heard that Jesus made and baptized more disciples than John,

<a id="v2"></a>**2** (Though Jesus himself baptized not, but his disciples,)

<a id="v3"></a>**3** He left Judea, and departed again into Galilee.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And he must needs go through Samaria.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Then cometh he to a city of Samaria, which is called Sychar, near to the parcel of ground that Jacob gave to his son Joseph.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Now Jacob’s well was there. Jesus therefore, being wearied with his journey, sat thus on the well: and it was about the sixth hour.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** There cometh a woman of Samaria to draw water: Jesus saith unto her, Give me to drink.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** (For his disciples were gone away unto the city to buy meat.)

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Then saith the woman of Samaria unto him, How is it that thou, being a Jew, askest drink of me, which am a woman of Samaria? for the Jews have no dealings with the Samaritans.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Jesus answered and said unto her, If thou knewest the gift of God, and who it is that saith to thee, Give me to drink; thou wouldest have asked of him, and he would have given thee living water.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The woman saith unto him, Sir, thou hast nothing to draw with, and the well is deep: from whence then hast thou that living water?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Art thou greater than our father Jacob, which gave us the well, and drank thereof himself, and his children, and his cattle?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Jesus answered and said unto her, Whosoever drinketh of this water shall thirst again:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But whosoever drinketh of the water that I shall give him shall never thirst; but the water that I shall give him shall be in him a well of water springing up into everlasting life.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The woman saith unto him, Sir, give me this water, that I thirst not, neither come hither to draw.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Jesus saith unto her, Go, call thy husband, and come hither.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The woman answered and said, I have no husband. Jesus said unto her, Thou hast well said, I have no husband:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For thou hast had five husbands; and he whom thou now hast is not thy husband: in that saidst thou truly.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The woman saith unto him, Sir, I perceive that thou art a prophet.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Our fathers worshipped in this mountain; and ye say, that in Jerusalem is the place where men ought to worship.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Jesus saith unto her, Woman, believe me, the hour cometh, when ye shall neither in this mountain, nor yet at Jerusalem, worship the Father.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Ye worship ye know not what: we know what we worship: for salvation is of the Jews.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But the hour cometh, and now is, when the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth: for the Father seeketh such to worship him.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** God is a Spirit: and they that worship him must worship him in spirit and in truth.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The woman saith unto him, I know that Messias cometh, which is called Christ: when he is come, he will tell us all things.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Jesus saith unto her, I that speak unto thee am he.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And upon this came his disciples, and marvelled that he talked with the woman: yet no man said, What seekest thou? or, Why talkest thou with her?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** The woman then left her waterpot, and went her way into the city, and saith to the men,

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Come, see a man, which told me all things that ever I did: is not this the Christ?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Then they went out of the city, and came unto him.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** In the mean while his disciples prayed him, saying, Master, eat.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** But he said unto them, I have meat to eat that ye know not of.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Therefore said the disciples one to another, Hath any man brought him ought to eat?

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Jesus saith unto them, My meat is to do the will of him that sent me, and to finish his work.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Say not ye, There are yet four months, and then cometh harvest? behold, I say unto you, Lift up your eyes, and look on the fields; for they are white already to harvest.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** And he that reapeth receiveth wages, and gathereth fruit unto life eternal: that both he that soweth and he that reapeth may rejoice together.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** And herein is that saying true, One soweth, and another reapeth.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** I sent you to reap that whereon ye bestowed no labour: other men laboured, and ye are entered into their labours.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And many of the Samaritans of that city believed on him for the saying of the woman, which testified, He told me all that ever I did.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** So when the Samaritans were come unto him, they besought him that he would tarry with them: and he abode there two days.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** And many more believed because of his own word;

<a id="v42"></a>**42** And said unto the woman, Now we believe, not because of thy saying: for we have heard him ourselves, and know that this is indeed the Christ, the Saviour of the world.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** Now after two days he departed thence, and went into Galilee.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** For Jesus himself testified, that a prophet hath no honour in his own country.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** Then when he was come into Galilee, the Galileans received him, having seen all the things that he did at Jerusalem at the feast: for they also went unto the feast.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** So Jesus came again into Cana of Galilee, where he made the water wine. And there was a certain nobleman, whose son was sick at Capernaum.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** When he heard that Jesus was come out of Judea into Galilee, he went unto him, and besought him that he would come down, and heal his son: for he was at the point of death.

<a id="v48"></a>**48** Then said Jesus unto him, Except ye see signs and wonders, ye will not believe.

<a id="v49"></a>**49** The nobleman saith unto him, Sir, come down ere my child die.

<a id="v50"></a>**50** Jesus saith unto him, Go thy way; thy son liveth. And the man believed the word that Jesus had spoken unto him, and he went his way.

<a id="v51"></a>**51** And as he was now going down, his servants met him, and told him, saying, Thy son liveth.

<a id="v52"></a>**52** Then enquired he of them the hour when he began to amend. And they said unto him, Yesterday at the seventh hour the fever left him.

<a id="v53"></a>**53** So the father knew that it was at the same hour, in the which Jesus said unto him, Thy son liveth: and himself believed, and his whole house.

<a id="v54"></a>**54** This is again the second miracle that Jesus did, when he was come out of Judea into Galilee.
