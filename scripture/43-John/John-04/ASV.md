# John 4 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** When therefore the Lord knew that the Pharisees had heard that Jesus was making and baptizing more disciples than John

<a id="v2"></a>**2** (although Jesus himself baptized not, but his disciples),

<a id="v3"></a>**3** he left Judæa, and departed again into Galilee.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And he must needs pass through Samaria.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** So he cometh to a city of Samaria, called Sychar, near to the parcel of ground that Jacob gave to his son Joseph:

<a id="v6"></a>**6** and Jacob’s well was there. Jesus therefore, being wearied with his journey, sat thus by the well. It was about the sixth hour.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** There cometh a woman of Samaria to draw water: Jesus saith unto her, Give me to drink.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For his disciples were gone away into the city to buy food.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** The Samaritan woman therefore saith unto him, How is it that thou, being a Jew, askest drink of me, who am a Samaritan woman? (For Jews have no dealings with Samaritans.)

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Jesus answered and said unto her, If thou knewest the gift of God, and who it is that saith to thee, Give me to drink; thou wouldest have asked of him, and he would have given thee living water.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The woman saith unto him, Sir, thou hast nothing to draw with, and the well is deep: whence then hast thou that living water?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Art thou greater than our father Jacob, who gave us the well, and drank thereof himself, and his sons, and his cattle?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Jesus answered and said unto her, Every one that drinketh of this water shall thirst again:

<a id="v14"></a>**14** but whosoever drinketh of the water that I shall give him shall never thirst; but the water that I shall give him shall become in him a well of water springing up unto eternal life.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The woman saith unto him, Sir, give me this water, that I thirst not, neither come all the way hither to draw.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Jesus saith unto her, Go, call thy husband, and come hither.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** The woman answered and said unto him, I have no husband. Jesus saith unto her, Thou saidst well, I have no husband:

<a id="v18"></a>**18** for thou hast had five husbands; and he whom thou now hast is not thy husband: this hast thou said truly.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The woman saith unto him, Sir, I perceive that thou art a prophet.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Our fathers worshipped in this mountain; and ye say, that in Jerusalem is the place where men ought to worship.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Jesus saith unto her, Woman, believe me, the hour cometh, when neither in this mountain, nor in Jerusalem, shall ye worship the Father.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Ye worship that which ye know not: we worship that which we know; for salvation is from the Jews.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** But the hour cometh, and now is, when the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and truth: for such doth the Father seek to be his worshippers.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** God is a Spirit: and they that worship him must worship in spirit and truth.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** The woman saith unto him, I know that Messiah cometh (he that is called Christ): when he is come, he will declare unto us all things.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Jesus saith unto her, I that speak unto thee am he.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** And upon this came his disciples; and they marvelled that he was speaking with a woman; yet no man said, What seekest thou? or, Why speakest thou with her?

<a id="v28"></a>**28** So the woman left her waterpot, and went away into the city, and saith to the people,

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Come, see a man, who told me all things that ever I did: can this be the Christ?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** They went out of the city, and were coming to him.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** In the mean while the disciples prayed him, saying, Rabbi, eat.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** But he said unto them, I have meat to eat that ye know not.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The disciples therefore said one to another, Hath any man brought him aught to eat?

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Jesus saith unto them, My meat is to do the will of him that sent me, and to accomplish his work.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Say not ye, There are yet four months, and then cometh the harvest? behold, I say unto you, Lift up your eyes, and look on the fields, that they are white already unto harvest.

<a id="v36"></a>**36** He that reapeth receiveth wages, and gathereth fruit unto life eternal; that he that soweth and he that reapeth may rejoice together.

<a id="v37"></a>**37** For herein is the saying true, One soweth, and another reapeth.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** I sent you to reap that whereon ye have not labored: others have labored, and ye are entered into their labor.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** And from that city many of the Samaritans believed on him because of the word of the woman, who testified, He told me all things thateverI did.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** So when the Samaritans came unto him, they besought him to abide with them: and he abode there two days.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** And many more believed because of his word;

<a id="v42"></a>**42** and they said to the woman, Now we believe, not because of thy speaking: for we have heard for ourselves, and know that this is indeed the Saviour of the world.

<a id="v43"></a>**43** And after the two days he went forth from thence into Galilee.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** For Jesus himself testified, that a prophet hath no honor in his own country.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** So when he came into Galilee, the Galilæans received him, having seen all the things that he did in Jerusalem at the feast: for they also went unto the feast.

<a id="v46"></a>**46** He came therefore again unto Cana of Galilee, where he made the water wine. And there was a certain nobleman, whose son was sick at Capernaum.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** When he heard that Jesus was come out of Judæa into Galilee, he went unto him, and besought him that he would come down, and heal his son; for he was at the point of death.

<a id="v48"></a>**48** Jesus therefore said unto him, Except ye see signs and wonders, ye will in no wise believe.

<a id="v49"></a>**49** The nobleman saith unto him, Sir, come down ere my child die.

<a id="v50"></a>**50** Jesus saith unto him, Go thy way; thy son liveth. The man believed the word that Jesus spake unto him, and he went his way.

<a id="v51"></a>**51** And as he was now going down, his servants met him, saying, that his son lived.

<a id="v52"></a>**52** So he inquired of them the hour when he began to amend. They said therefore unto him, Yesterday at the seventh hour the fever left him.

<a id="v53"></a>**53** So the father knew that it was at that hour in which Jesus said unto him, Thy son liveth: and himself believed, and his whole house.

<a id="v54"></a>**54** This is again the second sign that Jesus did, having come out of Judæa into Galilee.
