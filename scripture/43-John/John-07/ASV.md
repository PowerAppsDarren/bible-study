# John 7 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** And after these things Jesus walked in Galilee: for he would not walk in Judæa, because the Jews sought to kill him.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Now the feast of the Jews, the feast of tabernacles, was at hand.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** His brethren therefore said unto him, Depart hence, and go into Judæa, that thy disciples also may behold thy works which thou doest.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For no man doeth anything in secret, and himself seeketh to be known openly. If thou doest these things, manifest thyself to the world.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For even his brethren did not believe on him.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Jesus therefore saith unto them, My time is not yet come; but your time is always ready.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The world cannot hate you; but me it hateth, because I testify of it, that its works are evil.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Go ye up unto the feast: I go not up unto this feast; because my time is not yet fulfilled.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And having said these things unto them, he abode still in Galilee.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** But when his brethren were gone up unto the feast, then went he also up, not publicly, but as it were in secret.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The Jews therefore sought him at the feast, and said, Where is he?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** And there was much murmuring among the multitudes concerning him: some said, He is a good man; others said, Not so, but he leadeth the multitude astray.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Yet no man spake openly of him for fear of the Jews.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But when it was now the midst of the feast Jesus went up into the temple, and taught.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The Jews therefore marvelled, saying, How knoweth this man letters, having never learned?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Jesus therefore answered them, and said, My teaching is not mine, but his that sent me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** If any man willeth to do his will, he shall know of the teaching, whether it is of God, or whether I speak from myself.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He that speaketh from himself seeketh his own glory: but he that seeketh the glory of him that sent him, the same is true, and no unrighteousness is in him.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Did not Moses give you the law, and yet none of you doeth the law? Why seek ye to kill me?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The multitude answered, Thou hast a demon: who seeketh to kill thee?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Jesus answered and said unto them, I did one work, and ye all marvel because thereof.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Moses hath given you circumcision (not that it is of Moses, but of the fathers); and on the sabbath ye circumcise a man.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If a man receiveth circumcision on the sabbath, that the law of Moses may not be broken; are ye wroth with me, because I made a man every whit whole on the sabbath?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Judge not according to appearance, but judge righteous judgment.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Some therefore of them of Jerusalem said, Is not this he whom they seek to kill?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And lo, he speaketh openly, and they say nothing unto him. Can it be that the rulers indeed know that this is the Christ?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Howbeit we know this man whence he is: but when the Christ cometh, no one knoweth whence he is.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Jesus therefore cried in the temple, teaching and saying, Ye both know me, and know whence I am; and I am not come of myself, but he that sent me is true, whom ye know not.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** I know him; because I am from him, and he sent me.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** They sought therefore to take him: and no man laid his hand on him, because his hour was not yet come.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** But of the multitude many believed on him; and they said, When the Christ shall come, will he do more signs than those which this man hath done?

<a id="v32"></a>**32** The Pharisees heard the multitude murmuring these things concerning him; and the chief priests and the Pharisees sent officers to take him.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Jesus therefore said, Yet a little while am I with you, and I go unto him that sent me.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Ye shall seek me, and shall not find me: and where I am, ye cannot come.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** The Jews therefore said among themselves, Whither will this man go that we shall not find him? will he go unto the Dispersion among the Greeks, and teach the Greeks?

<a id="v36"></a>**36** What is this word that he said, Ye shall seek me, and shall not find me; and where I am, ye cannot come?

<a id="v37"></a>**37** Now on the last day, the greatdayof the feast, Jesus stood and cried, saying, If any man thirst, let him come unto me and drink.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** He that believeth on me, as the scripture hath said, from within him shall flow rivers of living water.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** But this spake he of the Spirit, which they that believed on him were to receive: for the Spirit was not yet given; because Jesus was not yet glorified.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** Some of the multitude therefore, when they heard these words, said, This is of a truth the prophet.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Others said, This is the Christ. But some said, What, doth the Christ come out of Galilee?

<a id="v42"></a>**42** Hath not the scripture said that the Christ cometh of the seed of David, and from Bethlehem, the village where David was?

<a id="v43"></a>**43** So there arose a division in the multitude because of him.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** And some of them would have taken him; but no man laid hands on him.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** The officers therefore came to the chief priests and Pharisees; and they said unto them, Why did ye not bring him?

<a id="v46"></a>**46** The officers answered, Never man so spake.

<a id="v47"></a>**47** The Pharisees therefore answered them, Are ye also led astray?

<a id="v48"></a>**48** Hath any of the rulers believed on him, or of the Pharisees?

<a id="v49"></a>**49** But this multitude that knoweth not the law are accursed.

<a id="v50"></a>**50** Nicodemus saith unto them (he that came to him before, being one of them),

<a id="v51"></a>**51** Doth our law judge a man, except it first hear from himself and know what he doeth?

<a id="v52"></a>**52** They answered and said unto him, Art thou also of Galilee? Search, and see that out of Galilee ariseth no prophet.
