# John 7 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** After these things, Jesus was walking in Galilee, for he wouldn’t walk in Judea, because the Jews sought to kill him.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Now the feast of the Jews, the Feast of Booths, was at hand.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** His brothers therefore said to him, “Depart from here, and go into Judea, that your disciples also may see your works which you do.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For no one does anything in secret, and himself seeks to be known openly. If you do these things, reveal yourself to the world.”

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For even his brothers didn’t believe in him.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Jesus therefore said to them, “My time has not yet come, but your time is always ready.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** The world can’t hate you, but it hates me, because I testify about it, that its works are evil.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** You go up to the feast. I am not yet going up to this feast, because my time is not yet fulfilled.”

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Having said these things to them, he stayed in Galilee.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** But when his brothers had gone up to the feast, then he also went up, not publicly, but as it were in secret.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The Jews therefore sought him at the feast, and said, “Where is he?”

<a id="v12"></a>**12** There was much murmuring among the multitudes concerning him. Some said, “He is a good man.” Others said, “Not so, but he leads the multitude astray.”

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Yet no one spoke openly of him for fear of the Jews.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** But when it was now the midst of the feast, Jesus went up into the temple and taught.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** The Jews therefore marveled, saying, “How does this man know letters, having never been educated?”

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Jesus therefore answered them, “My teaching is not mine, but his who sent me.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** If anyone desires to do his will, he will know about the teaching, whether it is from God, or if I am speaking from myself.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** He who speaks from himself seeks his own glory, but he who seeks the glory of him who sent him is true, and no unrighteousness is in him.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Didn’t Moses give you the law, and yet none of you keeps the law? Why do you seek to kill me?”

<a id="v20"></a>**20** The multitude answered, “You have a demon! Who seeks to kill you?”

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Jesus answered them, “I did one work, and you all marvel because of it.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Moses has given you circumcision (not that it is of Moses, but of the fathers), and on the Sabbath you circumcise a boy.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** If a boy receives circumcision on the Sabbath, that the law of Moses may not be broken, are you angry with me, because I made a man completely healthy on the Sabbath?

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Don’t judge according to appearance, but judge righteous judgment.”

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Therefore some of them of Jerusalem said, “Isn’t this he whom they seek to kill?

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Behold, he speaks openly, and they say nothing to him. Can it be that the rulers indeed know that this is truly the Christ?

<a id="v27"></a>**27** However we know where this man comes from, but when the Christ comes, no one will know where he comes from.”

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Jesus therefore cried out in the temple, teaching and saying, “You both know me, and know where I am from. I have not come of myself, but he who sent me is true, whom you don’t know.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** I know him, because I am from him, and he sent me.”

<a id="v30"></a>**30** They sought therefore to take him; but no one laid a hand on him, because his hour had not yet come.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** But of the multitude, many believed in him. They said, “When the Christ comes, he won’t do more signs than those which this man has done, will he?”

<a id="v32"></a>**32** The Pharisees heard the multitude murmuring these things concerning him, and the chief priests and the Pharisees sent officers to arrest him.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Then Jesus said, “I will be with you a little while longer, then I go to him who sent me.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** You will seek me, and won’t find me; and where I am, you can’t come.”

<a id="v35"></a>**35** The Jews therefore said among themselves, “Where will this man go that we won’t find him? Will he go to the Dispersion among the Greeks, and teach the Greeks?

<a id="v36"></a>**36** What is this word that he said, ‘You will seek me, and won’t find me; and where I am, you can’t come’?”

<a id="v37"></a>**37** Now on the last and greatest day of the feast, Jesus stood and cried out, “If anyone is thirsty, let him come to me and drink!

<a id="v38"></a>**38** He who believes in me, as the Scripture has said, from within him will flow rivers of living water.”

<a id="v39"></a>**39** But he said this about the Spirit, which those believing in him were to receive. For the Holy Spirit was not yet given, because Jesus wasn’t yet glorified.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** Many of the multitude therefore, when they heard these words, said, “This is truly the prophet.”

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Others said, “This is the Christ.” But some said, “What, does the Christ come out of Galilee?

<a id="v42"></a>**42** Hasn’t the Scripture said that the Christ comes of the seed of David,  and from Bethlehem, the village where David was?”

<a id="v43"></a>**43** So there arose a division in the multitude because of him.

<a id="v44"></a>**44** Some of them would have arrested him, but no one laid hands on him.

<a id="v45"></a>**45** The officers therefore came to the chief priests and Pharisees, and they said to them, “Why didn’t you bring him?”

<a id="v46"></a>**46** The officers answered, “No man ever spoke like this man!”

<a id="v47"></a>**47** The Pharisees therefore answered them, “You aren’t also led astray, are you?

<a id="v48"></a>**48** Have any of the rulers believed in him, or of the Pharisees?

<a id="v49"></a>**49** But this multitude that doesn’t know the law is accursed.”

<a id="v50"></a>**50** Nicodemus (he who came to him by night, being one of them) said to them,

<a id="v51"></a>**51** “Does our law judge a man, unless it first hears from him personally and knows what he does?”

<a id="v52"></a>**52** They answered him, “Are you also from Galilee? Search, and see that no prophet has arisen out of Galilee.”
