# John 10 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Verily, verily, I say unto you, He that entereth not by the door into the fold of the sheep, but climbeth up some other way, the same is a thief and a robber.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** But he that entereth in by the door is the shepherd of the sheep.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** To him the porter openeth; and the sheep hear his voice: and he calleth his own sheep by name, and leadeth them out.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** When he hath put forth all his own, he goeth before them, and the sheep follow him: for they know his voice.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And a stranger will they not follow, but will flee from him: for they know not the voice of strangers.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** This parable spake Jesus unto them: but they understood not what things they were which he spake unto them.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Jesus therefore said unto them again, Verily, verily, I say unto you, I am the door of the sheep.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** All that came before me are thieves and robbers: but the sheep did not hear them.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** I am the door; by me if any man enter in, he shall be saved, and shall go in and go out, and shall find pasture.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The thief cometh not, but that he may steal, and kill, and destroy: I came that they may have life, and may have it abundantly.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I am the good shepherd: the good shepherd layeth down his life for the sheep.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** He that is a hireling, and not a shepherd, whose own the sheep are not, beholdeth the wolf coming, and leaveth the sheep, and fleeth, and the wolf snatcheth them, and scattereth them:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** he fleeth because he is a hireling, and careth not for the sheep.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I am the good shepherd; and I know mine own, and mine own know me,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** even as the Father knoweth me, and I know the Father; and I lay down my life for the sheep.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And other sheep I have, which are not of this fold: them also I must bring, and they shall hear my voice; and they shall become one flock, one shepherd.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Therefore doth the Father love me, because I lay down my life, that I may take it again.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** No one taketh it away from me, but I lay it down of myself. I have power to lay it down, and I have power to take it again. This commandment received I from my Father.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** There arose a division again among the Jews because of these words.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And many of them said, He hath a demon, and is mad; why hear ye him?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Others said, These are not the sayings of one possessed with a demon. Can a demon open the eyes of the blind?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** And it was the feast of the dedication at Jerusalem:

<a id="v23"></a>**23** it was winter; and Jesus was walking in the temple in Solomon’s porch.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The Jews therefore came round about him, and said unto him, How long dost thou hold us in suspense? If thou art the Christ, tell us plainly.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Jesus answered them, I told you, and ye believe not: the works that I do in my Father’s name, these bear witness of me.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** But ye believe not, because ye are not of my sheep.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me:

<a id="v28"></a>**28** and I give unto them eternal life; and they shall never perish, and no one shall snatch them out of my hand.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** My Father, who hath given them unto me, is greater than all; and no one is able to snatch them out of the Father’s hand.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** I and the Father are one.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** The Jews took up stones again to stone him.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Jesus answered them, Many good works have I showed you from the Father; for which of those works do ye stone me?

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The Jews answered him, For a good work we stone thee not, but for blasphemy; and because that thou, being a man, makest thyself God.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Jesus answered them, Is it not written in your law, I said, Ye are gods?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** If he called them gods, unto whom the word of God came (and the scripture cannot be broken),

<a id="v36"></a>**36** say ye of him, whom the Father sanctified and sent into the world, Thou blasphemest; because I said, I am the Son of God?

<a id="v37"></a>**37** If I do not the works of my Father, believe me not.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** But if I do them, though ye believe not me, believe the works: that ye may know and understand that the Father is in me, and I in the Father.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** They sought again to take him: and he went forth out of their hand.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** And he went away again beyond the Jordan into the place where John was at the first baptizing; and there he abode.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** And many came unto him; and they said, John indeed did no sign: but all things whatsoever John spake of this man were true.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** And many believed on him there.
