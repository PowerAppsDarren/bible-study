# John 10 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Truly I say to you, He who does not go through the door into the place where the sheep are kept, but gets in by some other way, is a thief and an outlaw.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** He who goes in by the door is the keeper of the sheep.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The porter lets him in; and the sheep give ear to his voice; he says over the names of the sheep, and takes them out.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** When he has got them all out, he goes before them, and the sheep go after him, for they have knowledge of his voice.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** They will not go after another who is not their keeper, but will go from him in flight, because his voice is strange to them.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** In this Jesus was teaching them in the form of a story: but what he said was not clear to them.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** So Jesus said again, Truly I say to you, I am the door of the sheep.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** All who came before me are thieves and outlaws: but the sheep did not give ear to them.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** I am the door: if any man goes in through me he will have salvation, and will go in and go out, and will get food.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The thief comes only to take the sheep and to put them to death: he comes for their destruction: I have come so that they may have life and have it in greater measure.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I am the good keeper of sheep: the good keeper gives his life for the sheep.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** He who is a servant, and not the keeper or the owner of the sheep, sees the wolf coming and goes in flight, away from the sheep; and the wolf comes down on them and sends them in all directions:

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Because he is a servant he has no interest in the sheep.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I am the good keeper; I have knowledge of my sheep, and they have knowledge of me,

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Even as the Father has knowledge of me and I of the Father; and I am giving my life for the sheep.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And I have other sheep which are not of this field: I will be their guide in the same way, and they will give ear to my voice, so there will be one flock and one keeper.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For this reason am I loved by the Father, because I give up my life so that I may take it again.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** No one takes it away from me; I give it up of myself. I have power to give it up, and I have power to take it again. These orders I have from my Father.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** There was a division again among the Jews because of these words.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And a number of them said, He has an evil spirit and is out of his mind; why do you give ear to him?

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Others said, These are not the words of one who has an evil spirit. Is it possible for an evil spirit to make blind people see?

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Then came the feast of the opening of the Temple in Jerusalem: it was winter;

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And Jesus was walking in the Temple, in Solomon's covered way.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Then the Jews came round him, saying, how long are you going to keep us in doubt? If you are the Christ, say so clearly.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Jesus said in answer, I have said it and you have no belief: the works which I do in my Father's name, these give witness about me.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** But you have no belief because you are not of my sheep.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** My sheep give ear to my voice, and I have knowledge of them, and they come after me:

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And I give them eternal life; they will never come to destruction, and no one will ever take them out of my hand.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** That which my Father has given to me has more value than all; and no one is able to take anything out of the Father's hand.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** I and my Father are one.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Then the Jews took up stones again to send at him.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Jesus said to them in answer, I have let you see a number of good works from the Father; for which of those works are you stoning me?

<a id="v33"></a>**33** This was their answer: We are not stoning you for a good work but for evil words; because being a man you make yourself God.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** In answer, Jesus said, Is there not a saying in your law, I said, You are gods?

<a id="v35"></a>**35** If he said they were gods, to whom the word of God came (and the Writings may not be broken),

<a id="v36"></a>**36** Do you say of him whom the Father made holy and sent into the world, Your words are evil; because I said, I am God's Son?

<a id="v37"></a>**37** If I am not doing the works of my Father, do not have belief in me;

<a id="v38"></a>**38** But if I am doing them, then have belief in the works even if you have no belief in me; so that you may see clearly and be certain that the Father is in me and I am in the Father.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** Then again they made an attempt to take him; but he got away from them.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** And he went again to the other side of the Jordan, to the place where John first gave baptism; and he was there for a time.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** And a great number of people came to him, saying, John did no sign: but everything John said of this man was true.

<a id="v42"></a>**42** And a number came to have faith in him there.
