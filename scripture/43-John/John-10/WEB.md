# John 10 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** “Most certainly, I tell you, one who doesn’t enter by the door into the sheep fold, but climbs up some other way, the same is a thief and a robber.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** But one who enters in by the door is the shepherd of the sheep.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The gatekeeper opens the gate for him, and the sheep listen to his voice. He calls his own sheep by name, and leads them out.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Whenever he brings out his own sheep, he goes before them, and the sheep follow him, for they know his voice.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** They will by no means follow a stranger, but will flee from him; for they don’t know the voice of strangers.”

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Jesus spoke this parable to them, but they didn’t understand what he was telling them.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Jesus therefore said to them again, “Most certainly, I tell you, I am the sheep’s door.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** All who came before me are thieves and robbers, but the sheep didn’t listen to them.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** I am the door. If anyone enters in by me, he will be saved, and will go in and go out, and will find pasture.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** The thief only comes to steal, kill, and destroy. I came that they may have life, and may have it abundantly.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** I am the good shepherd.The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** He who is a hired hand, and not a shepherd, who doesn’t own the sheep, sees the wolf coming, leaves the sheep, and flees. The wolf snatches the sheep, and scatters them.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The hired hand flees because he is a hired hand, and doesn’t care for the sheep.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** I am the good shepherd. I know my own, and I’m known by my own;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** even as the Father knows me, and I know the Father. I lay down my life for the sheep.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** I have other sheep, which are not of this fold.I must bring them also, and they will hear my voice. They will become one flock with one shepherd.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Therefore the Father loves me, because I lay down my life, that I may take it again.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** No one takes it away from me, but I lay it down by myself. I have power to lay it down, and I have power to take it again. I received this commandment from my Father.”

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Therefore a division arose again among the Jews because of these words.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Many of them said, “He has a demon, and is insane! Why do you listen to him?”

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Others said, “These are not the sayings of one possessed by a demon. It isn’t possible for a demon to open the eyes of the blind, is it?”

<a id="v22"></a>**22** It was the Feast of the Dedication at Jerusalem.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** It was winter, and Jesus was walking in the temple, in Solomon’s porch.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** The Jews therefore came around him and said to him, “How long will you hold us in suspense? If you are the Christ, tell us plainly.”

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Jesus answered them, “I told you, and you don’t believe. The works that I do in my Father’s name, these testify about me.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** But you don’t believe, because you are not of my sheep, as I told you.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** I give eternal life to them. They will never perish, and no one will snatch them out of my hand.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** My Father, who has given them to me, is greater than all. No one is able to snatch them out of my Father’s hand.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** I and the Father are one.”

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Therefore Jews took up stones again to stone him.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Jesus answered them, “I have shown you many good works from my Father. For which of those works do you stone me?”

<a id="v33"></a>**33** The Jews answered him, “We don’t stone you for a good work, but for blasphemy: because you, being a man, make yourself God.”

<a id="v34"></a>**34** Jesus answered them, “Isn’t it written in your law, ‘I said, you are gods?’

<a id="v35"></a>**35** If he called them gods, to whom the word of God came (and the Scripture can’t be broken),

<a id="v36"></a>**36** do you say of him whom the Father sanctified and sent into the world, ‘You blaspheme,’ because I said, ‘I am the Son of God?’

<a id="v37"></a>**37** If I don’t do the works of my Father, don’t believe me.

<a id="v38"></a>**38** But if I do them, though you don’t believe me, believe the works; that you may know and believe that the Father is in me, and I in the Father.”

<a id="v39"></a>**39** They sought again to seize him, and he went out of their hand.

<a id="v40"></a>**40** He went away again beyond the Jordan into the place where John was baptizing at first, and there he stayed.

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Many came to him. They said, “John indeed did no sign, but everything that John said about this man is true.”

<a id="v42"></a>**42** Many believed in him there.
