# John 9 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** As he passed by, he saw a man blind from birth.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** His disciples asked him, “Rabbi, who sinned, this man or his parents, that he was born blind?”

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Jesus answered, “Neither did this man sin, nor his parents; but, that the works of God might be revealed in him.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** I must work the works of him who sent me, while it is day. The night is coming, when no one can work.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** While I am in the world, I am the light of the world.”

<a id="v6"></a>**6** When he had said this, he spat on the ground, made mud with the saliva, anointed the blind man’s eyes with the mud,

<a id="v7"></a>**7** and said to him, “Go, wash in the pool of Siloam” (which means “Sent”). So he went away, washed, and came back seeing.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** The neighbors therefore, and those who saw that he was blind before, said, “Isn’t this he who sat and begged?”

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Others were saying, “It is he.” Still others were saying, “He looks like him.”   He said, “I am he.”

<a id="v10"></a>**10** They therefore were asking him, “How were your eyes opened?”

<a id="v11"></a>**11** He answered, “A man called Jesus made mud, anointed my eyes, and said to me, ‘Go to the pool of Siloam, and wash.’ So I went away and washed, and I received sight.”

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Then they asked him, “Where is he?”   He said, “I don’t know.”

<a id="v13"></a>**13** They brought him who had been blind to the Pharisees.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** It was a Sabbath when Jesus made the mud and opened his eyes.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Again therefore the Pharisees also asked him how he received his sight. He said to them, “He put mud on my eyes, I washed, and I see.”

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Some therefore of the Pharisees said, “This man is not from God, because he doesn’t keep the Sabbath.” Others said, “How can a man who is a sinner do such signs?” There was division among them.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Therefore they asked the blind man again, “What do you say about him, because he opened your eyes?”   He said, “He is a prophet.”

<a id="v18"></a>**18** The Jews therefore did not believe concerning him, that he had been blind, and had received his sight, until they called the parents of him who had received his sight,

<a id="v19"></a>**19** and asked them, “Is this your son, whom you say was born blind? How then does he now see?”

<a id="v20"></a>**20** His parents answered them, “We know that this is our son, and that he was born blind;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** but how he now sees, we don’t know; or who opened his eyes, we don’t know. He is of age. Ask him. He will speak for himself.”

<a id="v22"></a>**22** His parents said these things because they feared the Jews; for the Jews had already agreed that if any man would confess him as Christ, he would be put out of the synagogue.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Therefore his parents said, “He is of age. Ask him.”

<a id="v24"></a>**24** So they called the man who was blind a second time, and said to him, “Give glory to God. We know that this man is a sinner.”

<a id="v25"></a>**25** He therefore answered, “I don’t know if he is a sinner. One thing I do know: that though I was blind, now I see.”

<a id="v26"></a>**26** They said to him again, “What did he do to you? How did he open your eyes?”

<a id="v27"></a>**27** He answered them, “I told you already, and you didn’t listen. Why do you want to hear it again? You don’t also want to become his disciples, do you?”

<a id="v28"></a>**28** They insulted him and said, “You are his disciple, but we are disciples of Moses.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** We know that God has spoken to Moses. But as for this man, we don’t know where he comes from.”

<a id="v30"></a>**30** The man answered them, “How amazing! You don’t know where he comes from, yet he opened my eyes.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** We know that God doesn’t listen to sinners, but if anyone is a worshiper of God, and does his will, he listens to him.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Since the world began it has never been heard of that anyone opened the eyes of someone born blind.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** If this man were not from God, he could do nothing.”

<a id="v34"></a>**34** They answered him, “You were altogether born in sins, and do you teach us?” They threw him out.

<a id="v35"></a>**35** Jesus heard that they had thrown him out, and finding him, he said, “Do you believe in the Son of God?”

<a id="v36"></a>**36** He answered, “Who is he, Lord, that I may believe in him?”

<a id="v37"></a>**37** Jesus said to him, “You have both seen him, and it is he who speaks with you.”

<a id="v38"></a>**38** He said, “Lord, I believe!” and he worshiped him.

<a id="v39"></a>**39** Jesus said, “I came into this world for judgment, that those who don’t see may see; and that those who see may become blind.”

<a id="v40"></a>**40** Those of the Pharisees who were with him heard these things, and said to him, “Are we also blind?”

<a id="v41"></a>**41** Jesus said to them, “If you were blind, you would have no sin; but now you say, ‘We see.’ Therefore your sin remains.
