# 1 Corinthians 5 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** It is actually reported that there is fornication among you, and such fornication as is not even among the Gentiles, that oneof you hath his father’s wife.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** And ye are puffed up, and did not rather mourn, that he that had done this deed might be taken away from among you.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For I verily, being absent in body but present in spirit, have already as though I were present judged him that hath so wrought this thing,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** in the name of our Lord Jesus, ye being gathered together, and my spirit, with the power of our Lord Jesus,

<a id="v5"></a>**5** to deliver such a one unto Satan for the destruction of the flesh, that the spirit may be saved in the day of the Lord Jesus.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Your glorying is not good. Know ye not that a little leaven leaveneth the whole lump?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Purge out the old leaven, that ye may be a new lump, even as ye are unleavened. For our passover also hath been sacrificed, even Christ:

<a id="v8"></a>**8** wherefore let us keep the feast, not with old leaven, neither with the leaven of malice and wickedness, but with the unleavened bread of sincerity and truth.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** I wrote unto you in my epistle to have no company with fornicators;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** not at all meaning with the fornicators of this world, or with the covetous and extortioners, or with idolaters; for then must ye needs go out of the world:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** but as it is, I wrote unto you not to keep company, if any man that is named a brother be a fornicator, or covetous, or an idolater, or a reviler, or a drunkard, or an extortioner; with such a one no, not to eat.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For what have I to do with judging them that are without? Do not ye judge them that are within?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** But them that are without God judgeth. Put away the wicked man from among yourselves.
