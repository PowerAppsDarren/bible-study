# 1 Corinthians 12 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Now concerning spiritual things, brothers, I don’t want you to be ignorant.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** You know that when you were heathen, you were led away to those mute idols, however you might be led.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Therefore I make known to you that no man speaking by God’s Spirit says, “Jesus is accursed.” No one can say, “Jesus is Lord,” but by the Holy Spirit.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Now there are various kinds of gifts, but the same Spirit.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** There are various kinds of service, and the same Lord.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** There are various kinds of workings, but the same God, who works all things in all.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** But to each one is given the manifestation of the Spirit for the profit of all.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For to one is given through the Spirit the word of wisdom, and to another the word of knowledge, according to the same Spirit;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** to another faith, by the same Spirit; and to another gifts of healings, by the same Spirit;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** and to another workings of miracles; and to another prophecy; and to another discerning of spirits; to another different kinds of languages; and to another the interpretation of languages.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** But the one and the same Spirit works all of these, distributing to each one separately as he desires.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For as the body is one, and has many members, and all the members of the body, being many, are one body; so also is Christ.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For in one Spirit we were all baptized into one body, whether Jews or Greeks, whether bond or free; and were all given to drink into one Spirit.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For the body is not one member, but many.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** If the foot would say, “Because I’m not the hand, I’m not part of the body,” it is not therefore not part of the body.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** If the ear would say, “Because I’m not the eye, I’m not part of the body,” it’s not therefore not part of the body.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** If the whole body were an eye, where would the hearing be? If the whole were hearing, where would the smelling be?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But now God has set the members, each one of them, in the body, just as he desired.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** If they were all one member, where would the body be?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But now they are many members, but one body.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The eye can’t tell the hand, “I have no need for you,” or again the head to the feet, “I have no need for you.”

<a id="v22"></a>**22** No, much rather, those members of the body which seem to be weaker are necessary.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Those parts of the body which we think to be less honorable, on those we bestow more abundant honor; and our unpresentable parts have more abundant propriety;

<a id="v24"></a>**24** whereas our presentable parts have no such need. But God composed the body together, giving more abundant honor to the inferior part,

<a id="v25"></a>**25** that there should be no division in the body, but that the members should have the same care for one another.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** When one member suffers, all the members suffer with it. Or when one member is honored, all the members rejoice with it.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Now you are the body of Christ, and members individually.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** God has set some in the assembly: first apostles, second prophets, third teachers, then miracle workers, then gifts of healings, helps, governments, and various kinds of languages.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Are all apostles? Are all prophets? Are all teachers? Are all miracle workers?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Do all have gifts of healings? Do all speak with various languages? Do all interpret?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** But earnestly desire the best gifts. Moreover, I show a most excellent way to you.
