# 1 Corinthians 12 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Now concerning spiritual gifts, brethren, I would not have you ignorant.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Ye know that ye were Gentiles, carried away unto these dumb idols, even as ye were led.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Wherefore I give you to understand, that no man speaking by the Spirit of God calleth Jesus accursed: and that no man can say that Jesus is the Lord, but by the Holy Ghost.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Now there are diversities of gifts, but the same Spirit.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** And there are differences of administrations, but the same Lord.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And there are diversities of operations, but it is the same God which worketh all in all.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** But the manifestation of the Spirit is given to every man to profit withal.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For to one is given by the Spirit the word of wisdom; to another the word of knowledge by the same Spirit;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** To another faith by the same Spirit; to another the gifts of healing by the same Spirit;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** To another the working of miracles; to another prophecy; to another discerning of spirits; to another divers kinds of tongues; to another the interpretation of tongues:

<a id="v11"></a>**11** But all these worketh that one and the selfsame Spirit, dividing to every man severally as he will.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For as the body is one, and hath many members, and all the members of that one body, being many, are one body: so also is Christ.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** For by one Spirit are we all baptized into one body, whether we be Jews or Gentiles, whether we be bond or free; and have been all made to drink into one Spirit.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** For the body is not one member, but many.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** If the foot shall say, Because I am not the hand, I am not of the body; is it therefore not of the body?

<a id="v16"></a>**16** And if the ear shall say, Because I am not the eye, I am not of the body; is it therefore not of the body?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** If the whole body were an eye, where were the hearing? If the whole were hearing, where were the smelling?

<a id="v18"></a>**18** But now hath God set the members every one of them in the body, as it hath pleased him.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** And if they were all one member, where were the body?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But now are they many members, yet but one body.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** And the eye cannot say unto the hand, I have no need of thee: nor again the head to the feet, I have no need of you.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Nay, much more those members of the body, which seem to be more feeble, are necessary:

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And those members of the body, which we think to be less honourable, upon these we bestow more abundant honour; and our uncomely parts have more abundant comeliness.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For our comely parts have no need: but God hath tempered the body together, having given more abundant honour to that part which lacked:

<a id="v25"></a>**25** That there should be no schism in the body; but that the members should have the same care one for another.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** And whether one member suffer, all the members suffer with it; or one member be honoured, all the members rejoice with it.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Now ye are the body of Christ, and members in particular.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** And God hath set some in the church, first apostles, secondarily prophets, thirdly teachers, after that miracles, then gifts of healings, helps, governments, diversities of tongues.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** Are all apostles? are all prophets? are all teachers? are all workers of miracles?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Have all the gifts of healing? do all speak with tongues? do all interpret?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** But covet earnestly the best gifts: and yet shew I unto you a more excellent way.
