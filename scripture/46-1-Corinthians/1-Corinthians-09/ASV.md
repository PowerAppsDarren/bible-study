# 1 Corinthians 9 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Am I not free? am I not an apostle? have I not seen Jesus our Lord? are not ye my work in the Lord?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** If to others I am not an apostle, yet at least I am to you; for the seal of mine apostleship are ye in the Lord.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** My defence to them that examine me is this.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Have we no right to eat and to drink?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Have we no right to lead about a wife that is a believer, even as the rest of the apostles, and the brethren of the Lord, and Cephas?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Or I only and Barnabas, have we not a right to forbear working?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** What soldier ever serveth at his own charges? who planteth a vineyard, and eateth not the fruit thereof? or who feedeth a flock, and eateth not of the milk of the flock?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Do I speak these things after the manner of men? or saith not the law also the same?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For it is written in the law of Moses, Thou shalt not muzzle the ox when he treadeth out the corn. Is it for the oxen that God careth,

<a id="v10"></a>**10** or saith he it assuredly for our sake? Yea, for our sake it was written: because he that ploweth ought to plow in hope, and he that thresheth, to thresh in hope of partaking.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** If we sowed unto you spiritual things, is it a great matter if we shall reap your carnal things?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** If others partake of this right over you, do not we yet more? Nevertheless we did not use this right; but we bear all things, that we may cause no hindrance to the gospel of Christ.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Know ye not that they that minister about sacred things eat of the things of the temple, and they that wait upon the altar have their portion with the altar?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Even so did the Lord ordain that they that proclaim the gospel should live of the gospel.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** But I have used none of these things: and I write not these things that it may be so done in my case; for it were good for me rather to die, than that any man should make my glorying void.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For if I preach the gospel, I have nothing to glory of; for necessity is laid upon me; for woe is unto me, if I preach not the gospel.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For if I do this of mine own will, I have a reward: but if not of mine own will, I have a stewardship intrusted to me.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** What then is my reward? That, when I preach the gospel, I may make the gospel without charge, so as not to use to the full my right in the gospel.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For though I was free from all men, I brought myself under bondage to all, that I might gain the more.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And to the Jews I became as a Jew, that I might gain Jews; to them that are under the law, as under the law, not being myself under the law, that I might gain them that are under the law;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** to them that are without law, as without law, not being without law to God, but under law to Christ, that I might gain them that are without law.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** To the weak I became weak, that I might gain the weak: I am become all things to all men, that I may by all means save some.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And I do all things for the gospel’s sake, that I may be a joint partaker thereof.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Know ye not that they that run in a race run all, but one receiveth the prize? Even so run; that ye may attain.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And every man that striveth in the games exerciseth self-control in all things. Now they do it to receive a corruptible crown; but we an incorruptible.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** I therefore so run, as not uncertainly; so fight I, as not beating the air:

<a id="v27"></a>**27** but I buffet my body, and bring it into bondage: lest by any means, after that I have preached to others, I myself should be rejected.
