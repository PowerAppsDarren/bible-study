# 1 Corinthians 9 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Am I not an apostle? am I not free? have I not seen Jesus Christ our Lord? are not ye my work in the Lord?

<a id="v2"></a>**2** If I be not an apostle unto others, yet doubtless I am to you: for the seal of mine apostleship are ye in the Lord.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Mine answer to them that do examine me is this,

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Have we not power to eat and to drink?

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Have we not power to lead about a sister, a wife, as well as other apostles, and as the brethren of the Lord, and Cephas?

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Or I only and Barnabas, have not we power to forbear working?

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Who goeth a warfare any time at his own charges? who planteth a vineyard, and eateth not of the fruit thereof? or who feedeth a flock, and eateth not of the milk of the flock?

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Say I these things as a man? or saith not the law the same also?

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For it is written in the law of Moses, Thou shalt not muzzle the mouth of the ox that treadeth out the corn. Doth God take care for oxen?

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Or saith he it altogether for our sakes? For our sakes, no doubt, this is written: that he that ploweth should plow in hope; and that he that thresheth in hope should be partaker of his hope.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** If we have sown unto you spiritual things, is it a great thing if we shall reap your carnal things?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** If others be partakers of this power over you, are not we rather? Nevertheless we have not used this power; but suffer all things, lest we should hinder the gospel of Christ.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Do ye not know that they which minister about holy things live of the things of the temple? and they which wait at the altar are partakers with the altar?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Even so hath the Lord ordained that they which preach the gospel should live of the gospel.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** But I have used none of these things: neither have I written these things, that it should be so done unto me: for it were better for me to die, than that any man should make my glorying void.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For though I preach the gospel, I have nothing to glory of: for necessity is laid upon me; yea, woe is unto me, if I preach not the gospel!

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For if I do this thing willingly, I have a reward: but if against my will, a dispensation of the gospel is committed unto me.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** What is my reward then? Verily that, when I preach the gospel, I may make the gospel of Christ without charge, that I abuse not my power in the gospel.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For though I be free from all men, yet have I made myself servant unto all, that I might gain the more.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** And unto the Jews I became as a Jew, that I might gain the Jews; to them that are under the law, as under the law, that I might gain them that are under the law;

<a id="v21"></a>**21** To them that are without law, as without law, (being not without law to God, but under the law to Christ,) that I might gain them that are without law.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** To the weak became I as weak, that I might gain the weak: I am made all things to all men, that I might by all means save some.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** And this I do for the gospel’s sake, that I might be partaker thereof with you.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Know ye not that they which run in a race run all, but one receiveth the prize? So run, that ye may obtain.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** And every man that striveth for the mastery is temperate in all things. Now they do it to obtain a corruptible crown; but we an incorruptible.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** I therefore so run, not as uncertainly; so fight I, not as one that beateth the air:

<a id="v27"></a>**27** But I keep under my body, and bring it into subjection: lest that by any means, when I have preached to others, I myself should be a castaway.
