# 1 Corinthians 8 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Now concerning things sacrificed to idols: We know that we all have knowledge. Knowledge puffs up, but love builds up.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** But if anyone thinks that he knows anything, he doesn’t yet know as he ought to know.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But if anyone loves God, the same is known by him.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Therefore concerning the eating of things sacrificed to idols, we know that no idol is anything in the world, and that there is no other God but one.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** For though there are things that are called “gods”, whether in the heavens or on earth; as there are many “gods” and many “lords”;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** yet to us there is one God, the Father, of whom are all things, and we for him; and one Lord, Jesus Christ, through whom are all things, and we live through him.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** However, that knowledge isn’t in all men. But some, with consciousness of the idol until now, eat as of a thing sacrificed to an idol, and their conscience, being weak, is defiled.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But food will not commend us to God. For neither, if we don’t eat, are we the worse; nor, if we eat, are we the better.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** But be careful that by no means does this liberty of yours become a stumbling block to the weak.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** For if a man sees you who have knowledge sitting in an idol’s temple, won’t his conscience, if he is weak, be emboldened to eat things sacrificed to idols?

<a id="v11"></a>**11** And through your knowledge, he who is weak perishes, the brother for whose sake Christ died.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Thus, sinning against the brothers, and wounding their conscience when it is weak, you sin against Christ.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Therefore if food causes my brother to stumble, I will eat no meat forever more, that I don’t cause my brother to stumble.
