# 1 Corinthians 10 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** For I would not, brethren, have you ignorant, that our fathers were all under the cloud, and all passed through the sea;

<a id="v2"></a>**2** and were all baptized unto Moses in the cloud and in the sea;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** and did all eat the same spiritual food;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** and did all drink the same spiritual drink: for they drank of a spiritual rock that followed them: and the rock was Christ.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Howbeit with most of them God was not well pleased: for they were overthrown in the wilderness.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Now these things were our examples, to the intent we should not lust after evil things, as they also lusted.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Neither be ye idolaters, as were some of them; as it is written, The people sat down to eat and drink, and rose up to play.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Neither let us commit fornication, as some of them committed, and fell in one day three and twenty thousand.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Neither let us make trial of the Lord, as some of them made trial, and perished by the serpents.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Neither murmur ye, as some of them murmured, and perished by the destroyer.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Now these things happened unto them by way of example; and they were written for our admonition, upon whom the ends of the ages are come.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Wherefore let him that thinketh he standeth take heed lest he fall.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** There hath no temptation taken you but such as man can bear: but God is faithful, who will not suffer you to be tempted above that ye are able; but will with the temptation make also the way of escape, that ye may be able to endure it.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Wherefore, my beloved, flee from idolatry.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** I speak as to wise men; judge ye what I say.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** The cup of blessing which we bless, is it not a communion of the blood of Christ? The bread which we break, is it not a communion of the body of Christ?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** seeing that we, who are many, are one bread, one body: for we all partake of the one bread.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Behold Israel after the flesh: have not they that eat the sacrifices communion with the altar?

<a id="v19"></a>**19** What say I then? that a thing sacrificed to idols is anything, or that an idol is anything?

<a id="v20"></a>**20** But I say, that the things which the Gentiles sacrifice, they sacrifice to demons, and not to God: and I would not that ye should have communion with demons.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Ye cannot drink the cup of the Lord, and the cup of demons: ye cannot partake of the table of the Lord, and of the table of demons.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Or do we provoke the Lord to jealousy? are we stronger than he?

<a id="v23"></a>**23** All things are lawful; but not all things are expedient. All things are lawful; but not all things edify.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Let no man seek his own, but each his neighbor’s good.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Whatsoever is sold in the shambles, eat, asking no question for conscience’ sake;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** for the earth is the Lord’s, and the fulness thereof.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** If one of them that believe not biddeth you to a feast, and ye are disposed to go; whatsoever is set before you, eat, asking no question for conscience’ sake.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** But if any man say unto you, This hath been offered in sacrifice, eat not, for his sake that showed it, and for conscience’ sake:

<a id="v29"></a>**29** conscience, I say, not thine own, but the other’s; for why is my liberty judged by another conscience?

<a id="v30"></a>**30** If I partake with thankfulness, why am I evil spoken of for that for which I give thanks?

<a id="v31"></a>**31** Whether therefore ye eat, or drink, or whatsoever ye do, do all to the glory of God.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** Give no occasion of stumbling, either to Jews, or to Greeks, or to the church of God:

<a id="v33"></a>**33** even as I also please all men in all things, not seeking mine own profit, but the profit of the many, that they may be saved.
