# 1 Corinthians 11 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Be ye imitators of me, even as I also am of Christ.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Now I praise you that ye remember me in all things, and hold fast the traditions, even as I delivered them to you.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** But I would have you know, that the head of every man is Christ; and the head of the woman is the man; and the head of Christ is God.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Every man praying or prophesying, having his head covered, dishonoreth his head.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But every woman praying or prophesying with her head unveiled dishonoreth her head; for it is one and the same thing as if she were shaven.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For if a woman is not veiled, let her also be shorn: but if it is a shame to a woman to be shorn or shaven, let her be veiled.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For a man indeed ought not to have his head veiled, forasmuch as he is the image and glory of God: but the woman is the glory of the man.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** For the man is not of the woman; but the woman of the man:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** for neither was the man created for the woman; but the woman for the man:

<a id="v10"></a>**10** for this cause ought the woman to have a sign of authority on her head, because of the angels.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Nevertheless, neither is the woman without the man, nor the man without the woman, in the Lord.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** For as the woman is of the man, so is the man also by the woman; but all things are of God.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Judge ye in yourselves: is it seemly that a woman pray unto God unveiled?

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Doth not even nature itself teach you, that, if a man have long hair, it is a dishonor to him?

<a id="v15"></a>**15** But if a woman have long hair, it is a glory to her: for her hair is given her for a covering.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** But if any man seemeth to be contentious, we have no such custom, neither the churches of God.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** But in giving you this charge, I praise you not, that ye come together not for the better but for the worse.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For first of all, when ye come together in the church, I hear that divisions exist among you; and I partly believe it.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** For there must be also factions among you, that they that are approved may be made manifest among you.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** When therefore ye assemble yourselves together, it is not possible to eat the Lord’s supper:

<a id="v21"></a>**21** for in your eating each one taketh before other his own supper; and one is hungry, and another is drunken.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** What, have ye not houses to eat and to drink in? or despise ye the church of God, and put them to shame that have not? What shall I say to you? shall I praise you? In this I praise you not.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** For I received of the Lord that which also I delivered unto you, that the Lord Jesus in the night in which he was betrayed took bread;

<a id="v24"></a>**24** and when he had given thanks, he brake it, and said, This is my body, which is for you: this do in remembrance of me.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** In like manner also the cup, after supper, saying, This cup is the new covenant in my blood: this do, as often as ye drink it, in remembrance of me.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** For as often as ye eat this bread, and drink the cup, ye proclaim the Lord’s death till he come.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Wherefore whosoever shall eat the bread or drink the cup of the Lord in an unworthy manner, shall be guilty of the body and the blood of the Lord.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** But let a man prove himself, and so let him eat of the bread, and drink of the cup.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For he that eateth and drinketh, eateth and drinketh judgment unto himself, if he discern not the body.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** For this cause many among you are weak and sickly, and not a few sleep.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** But if we discerned ourselves, we should not be judged.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** But when we are judged, we are chastened of the Lord, that we may not be condemned with the world.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** Wherefore, my brethren, when ye come together to eat, wait one for another.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** If any man is hungry, let him eat at home; that your coming together be not unto judgment. And the rest will I set in order whensoever I come.
