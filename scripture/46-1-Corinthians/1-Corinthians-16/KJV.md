# 1 Corinthians 16 — King James Version (KJV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** Now concerning the collection for the saints, as I have given order to the churches of Galatia, even so do ye.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Upon the first day of the week let every one of you lay by him in store, as God hath prospered him, that there be no gatherings when I come.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And when I come, whomsoever ye shall approve by your letters, them will I send to bring your liberality unto Jerusalem.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And if it be meet that I go also, they shall go with me.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Now I will come unto you, when I shall pass through Macedonia: for I do pass through Macedonia.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** And it may be that I will abide, yea, and winter with you, that ye may bring me on my journey whithersoever I go.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** For I will not see you now by the way; but I trust to tarry a while with you, if the Lord permit.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** But I will tarry at Ephesus until Pentecost.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For a great door and effectual is opened unto me, and there are many adversaries.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Now if Timotheus come, see that he may be with you without fear: for he worketh the work of the Lord, as I also do.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Let no man therefore despise him: but conduct him forth in peace, that he may come unto me: for I look for him with the brethren.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** As touching our brother Apollos, I greatly desired him to come unto you with the brethren: but his will was not at all to come at this time; but he will come when he shall have convenient time.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Watch ye, stand fast in the faith, quit you like men, be strong.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Let all your things be done with charity.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** I beseech you, brethren, (ye know the house of Stephanas, that it is the firstfruits of Achaia, and that they have addicted themselves to the ministry of the saints,)

<a id="v16"></a>**16** That ye submit yourselves unto such, and to every one that helpeth with us, and laboureth.

<a id="v17"></a>**17** I am glad of the coming of Stephanas and Fortunatus and Achaicus: for that which was lacking on your part they have supplied.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** For they have refreshed my spirit and yours: therefore acknowledge ye them that are such.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** The churches of Asia salute you. Aquila and Priscilla salute you much in the Lord, with the church that is in their house.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** All the brethren greet you. Greet ye one another with an holy kiss.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** The salutation of me Paul with mine own hand.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** If any man love not the Lord Jesus Christ, let him be Anathema Maran-atha.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** My love be with you all in Christ Jesus. Amen.  The first epistle to the Corinthians was written from Philippi by Stephanas, and Fortunatus, and Achaicus, and Timotheus.
