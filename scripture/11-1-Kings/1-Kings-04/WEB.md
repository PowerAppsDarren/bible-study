# 1 Kings 4 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** King Solomon was king over all Israel.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** These were the princes whom he had: Azariah the son of Zadok, the priest;

<a id="v3"></a>**3** Elihoreph and Ahijah, the sons of Shisha, scribes; Jehoshaphat the son of Ahilud, the recorder;

<a id="v4"></a>**4** and Benaiah the son of Jehoiada was over the army; and Zadok and Abiathar were priests;

<a id="v5"></a>**5** and Azariah the son of Nathan was over the officers; and Zabud the son of Nathan was chief minister, the king’s friend;

<a id="v6"></a>**6** and Ahishar was over the household; and Adoniram the son of Abda was over the men subject to forced labor.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Solomon had twelve officers over all Israel, who provided food for the king and his household: each man had to make provision for a month in the year.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** These are their names: Ben Hur, in the hill country of Ephraim;

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Ben Deker, in Makaz, and in Shaalbim, and Beth Shemesh, and Elon Beth Hanan;

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Ben Hesed, in Arubboth (to him belonged Socoh, and all the land of Hepher);

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Ben Abinadab, in all the height of Dor (he had Taphath the daughter of Solomon as wife);

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Baana the son of Ahilud, in Taanach and Megiddo, and all Beth Shean which is beside Zarethan, beneath Jezreel, from Beth Shean to Abel Meholah, as far as beyond Jokmeam;

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Ben Geber, in Ramoth Gilead (to him belonged the towns of Jair the son of Manasseh, which are in Gilead; to him belonged the region of Argob, which is in Bashan, sixty great cities with walls and bronze bars);

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Ahinadab the son of Iddo, in Mahanaim;

<a id="v15"></a>**15** Ahimaaz, in Naphtali (he also took Basemath the daughter of Solomon as wife);

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Baana the son of Hushai, in Asher and Bealoth;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** Jehoshaphat the son of Paruah, in Issachar;

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Shimei the son of Ela, in Benjamin;

<a id="v19"></a>**19** Geber the son of Uri, in the land of Gilead, the country of Sihon king of the Amorites and of Og king of Bashan; and he was the only officer who was in the land.

<a id="v20"></a>**20** Judah and Israel were many as the sand which is by the sea in multitude, eating and drinking and making merry.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** Solomon ruled over all the kingdoms from the River to the land of the Philistines, and to the border of Egypt: they brought tribute, and served Solomon all the days of his life.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Solomon’s provision for one day was thirty measures of fine flour, and sixty measures of meal,

<a id="v23"></a>**23** ten head of fat cattle, and twenty head of cattle out of the pastures, and one hundred sheep, besides deer, and gazelles, and roebucks, and fattened fowl.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** For he had dominion over all on this side the River, from Tiphsah even to Gaza, over all the kings on this side the River: and he had peace on all sides around him.

<a id="v25"></a>**25** Judah and Israel lived safely, every man under his vine and under his fig tree, from Dan even to Beersheba, all the days of Solomon.

<a id="v26"></a>**26** Solomon had forty thousand stalls of horses for his chariots, and twelve thousand horsemen.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Those officers provided food for king Solomon, and for all who came to king Solomon’s table, every man in his month; they let nothing be lacking.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** Barley also and straw for the horses and swift steeds brought they to the place where the officers were, every man according to his duty.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** God gave Solomon wisdom and understanding exceeding much, and very great understanding, even as the sand that is on the seashore.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** Solomon’s wisdom excelled the wisdom of all the children of the east, and all the wisdom of Egypt.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** For he was wiser than all men; than Ethan the Ezrahite, and Heman, and Calcol, and Darda, the sons of Mahol: and his fame was in all the nations all around.

<a id="v32"></a>**32** He spoke three thousand proverbs; and his songs were one thousand five.

<a id="v33"></a>**33** He spoke of trees, from the cedar that is in Lebanon even to the hyssop that springs out of the wall; he spoke also of animals, and of birds, and of creeping things, and of fish.

<a id="v34"></a>**34** There came of all peoples to hear the wisdom of Solomon, from all kings of the earth, who had heard of his wisdom.
