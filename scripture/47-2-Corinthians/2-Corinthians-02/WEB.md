# 2 Corinthians 2 — World English Bible (WEB)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** But I determined this for myself, that I would not come to you again in sorrow.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** For if I make you sorry, then who will make me glad but he who is made sorry by me?

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And I wrote this very thing to you, so that, when I came, I wouldn’t have sorrow from them of whom I ought to rejoice; having confidence in you all, that my joy would be shared by all of you.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** For out of much affliction and anguish of heart I wrote to you with many tears, not that you should be made sorry, but that you might know the love that I have so abundantly for you.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** But if any has caused sorrow, he has caused sorrow, not to me, but in part (that I not press too heavily) to you all.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Sufficient to such a one is this punishment which was inflicted by the many;

<a id="v7"></a>**7** so that on the contrary you should rather forgive him and comfort him, lest by any means such a one should be swallowed up with his excessive sorrow.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** Therefore I beg you to confirm your love toward him.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** For to this end I also wrote, that I might know the proof of you, whether you are obedient in all things.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Now I also forgive whomever you forgive anything. For if indeed I have forgiven anything, I have forgiven that one for your sakes in the presence of Christ,

<a id="v11"></a>**11** that no advantage may be gained over us by Satan; for we are not ignorant of his schemes.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** Now when I came to Troas for the Good News of Christ, and when a door was opened to me in the Lord,

<a id="v13"></a>**13** I had no relief for my spirit, because I didn’t find Titus, my brother, but taking my leave of them, I went out into Macedonia.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Now thanks be to God, who always leads us in triumph in Christ, and reveals through us the sweet aroma of his knowledge in every place.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** For we are a sweet aroma of Christ to God, in those who are saved, and in those who perish;

<a id="v16"></a>**16** to the one a stench from death to death; to the other a sweet aroma from life to life. Who is sufficient for these things?

<a id="v17"></a>**17** For we are not as so many, peddling the word of God. But as of sincerity, but as of God, in the sight of God, we speak in Christ.
