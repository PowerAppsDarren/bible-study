# Isaiah 1 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The vision of Isaiah the son of Amoz, which he saw concerning Judah and Jerusalem, in the days of Uzziah, Jotham, Ahaz, and Hezekiah, kings of Judah.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Hear, O heavens, and give ear, O earth; for Jehovah hath spoken: I have nourished and brought up children, and they have rebelled against me.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** The ox knoweth his owner, and the ass his master’s crib; but Israel doth not know, my people doth not consider.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** Ah sinful nation, a people laden with iniquity, a seed of evil-doers, children that deal corruptly! they have forsaken Jehovah, they have despised the Holy One of Israel, they are estranged and gone backward.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Why will ye be still stricken, that ye revolt more and more? the whole head is sick, and the whole heart faint.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** From the sole of the foot even unto the head there is no soundness in it; but wounds, and bruises, and fresh stripes: they have not been closed, neither bound up, neither mollified with oil.

<a id="v7"></a>**7** Your country is desolate; your cities are burned with fire; your land, strangers devour it in your presence, and it is desolate, as overthrown by strangers.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And the daughter of Zion is left as a booth in a vineyard, as a lodge in a garden of cucumbers, as a besieged city.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** Except Jehovah of hosts had left unto us a very small remnant, we should have been as Sodom, we should have been like unto Gomorrah.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Hear the word of Jehovah, ye rulers of Sodom; give ear unto the law of our God, ye people of Gomorrah.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** What unto me is the multitude of your sacrifices? saith Jehovah: I have had enough of the burnt-offerings of rams, and the fat of fed beasts; and I delight not in the blood of bullocks, or of lambs, or of he-goats.

<a id="v12"></a>**12** When ye come to appear before me, who hath required this at your hand, to trample my courts?

<a id="v13"></a>**13** Bring no more vain oblations; incense is an abomination unto me; new moon and sabbath, the calling of assemblies,—I cannot away with iniquity and the solemn meeting.

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Your new moons and your appointed feasts my soul hateth; they are a trouble unto me; I am weary of bearing them.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** And when ye spread forth your hands, I will hide mine eyes from you; yea, when ye make many prayers, I will not hear: your hands are full of blood.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** Wash you, make you clean; put away the evil of your doings from before mine eyes; cease to do evil;

<a id="v17"></a>**17** learn to do well; seek justice, relieve the oppressed, judge the fatherless, plead for the widow.

<a id="v18"></a>**18** Come now, and let us reason together, saith Jehovah: though your sins be as scarlet, they shall be as white as snow; though they be red like crimson, they shall be as wool.

<a id="v19"></a>**19** If ye be willing and obedient, ye shall eat the good of the land:

<a id="v20"></a>**20** but if ye refuse and rebel, ye shall be devoured with the sword; for the mouth of Jehovah hath spoken it.

<a id="v21"></a>**21** How is the faithful city become a harlot! she that was full of justice! righteousness lodged in her, but now murderers.

<a id="v22"></a>**22** Thy silver is become dross, thy wine mixed with water.

<a id="v23"></a>**23** Thy princes are rebellious, and companions of thieves; every one loveth bribes, and followeth after rewards: they judge not the fatherless, neither doth the cause of the widow come unto them.

<a id="v24"></a>**24** Therefore saith the Lord, Jehovah of hosts, the Mighty One of Israel, Ah, I will ease me of mine adversaries, and avenge me of mine enemies;

<a id="v25"></a>**25** and I will turn my hand upon thee, and thoroughly purge away thy dross, and will take away all thy tin;

<a id="v26"></a>**26** and I will restore thy judges as at the first, and thy counsellors as at the beginning: afterward thou shalt be called The city of righteousness, a faithful town.

<a id="v27"></a>**27** Zion shall be redeemed with justice, and her converts with righteousness.

<a id="v28"></a>**28** But the destruction of transgressors and sinners shall be together, and they that forsake Jehovah shall be consumed.

<a id="v29"></a>**29** For they shall be ashamed of the oaks which ye have desired, and ye shall be confounded for the gardens that ye have chosen.

<a id="v30"></a>**30** For ye shall be as an oak whose leaf fadeth, and as a garden that hath no water.

<a id="v31"></a>**31** And the strong shall be as tow, and his work as a spark; and they shall both burn together, and none shall quench them.
