# Isaiah 21 — Basic English Bible (BBE)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** The word about the waste land. As storm-winds in the South go rushing through, it comes from the waste land, from the land greatly to be feared.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** A vision of fear comes before my eyes; the worker of deceit goes on in his false way, and the waster goes on making waste. Up! Elam; to the attack! Media; I have put an end to her sorrow.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** For this cause I am full of bitter grief; pains like the pains of a woman in childbirth have come on me: I am bent down with sorrow at what comes to my ears; I am shocked by what I see.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** My mind is wandering, fear has overcome me: the evening of my desire has been turned into shaking for me.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** They make ready the table, they put down the covers, they take food and drink. Up! you captains; put oil on your breastplates.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** For so has the Lord said to me, Go, let a watchman be placed; let him give word of what he sees:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** And when he sees war-carriages, horsemen by twos, war-carriages with asses, war-carriages with camels, let him give special attention.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And the watchman gave a loud cry, O my lord, I am on the watchtower all day, and am placed in my watch every night:

<a id="v9"></a>**9** See, here come war-carriages with men, horsemen by twos: and in answer he said, Babylon is made low, is made low, and all her images are broken on the earth.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** O my crushed ones, the grain of my floor! I have given you the word which came to me from the Lord of armies, the God of Israel.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** The word about Edom. A voice comes to me from Seir, Watchman, how far gone is the night? how far gone is the night?

<a id="v12"></a>**12** The watchman says, The morning has come, but night is still to come: if you have questions to put, put them, and come back again.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** The word about Arabia. In the thick woods of Arabia will be your night's resting-place, O travelling bands of Dedanites!

<a id="v14"></a>**14** Give water to him who is in need of water; give bread, O men of the land of Tema, to those in flight.

<a id="v15"></a>**15** For they are in flight from the sharp sword, and the bent bow, and from the trouble of war.

<a id="v16"></a>**16** For so has the Lord said to me, In a year, by the years of a servant working for payment, all the glory of Kedar will come to an end:

<a id="v17"></a>**17** And the rest of the bowmen, the men of war of the children of Kedar, will be small in number: for the Lord, the God of Israel, has said it.
