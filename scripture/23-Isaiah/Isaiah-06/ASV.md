# Isaiah 6 — American Standard Version (ASV)

*Public domain. Source: getbible.net.*

---

<a id="v1"></a>**1** In the year that king Uzziah died I saw the Lord sitting upon a throne, high and lifted up; and his train filled the temple.

<a id="v2"></a>**2** Above him stood the seraphim: each one had six wings; with twain he covered his face, and with twain he covered his feet, and with twain he did fly.

<a id="v3"></a>**3** And one cried unto another, and said, Holy, holy, holy, is Jehovah of hosts: the whole earth is full of his glory.

<a id="v4"></a>**4** And the foundations of the thresholds shook at the voice of him that cried, and the house was filled with smoke.

<a id="v5"></a>**5** Then said I, Woe is me! for I am undone; because I am a man of unclean lips, and I dwell in the midst of a people of unclean lips: for mine eyes have seen the King, Jehovah of hosts.

<a id="v6"></a>**6** Then flew one of the seraphim unto me, having a live coal in his hand, which he had taken with the tongs from off the altar:

<a id="v7"></a>**7** and he touched my mouth with it, and said, Lo, this hath touched thy lips; and thine iniquity is taken away, and thy sin forgiven.

<a id="v8"></a>**8** And I heard the voice of the Lord, saying, Whom shall I send, and who will go for us? Then I said, Here am I; send me.

<a id="v9"></a>**9** And he said, Go, and tell this people, Hear ye indeed, but understand not; and see ye indeed, but perceive not.

<a id="v10"></a>**10** Make the heart of this people fat, and make their ears heavy, and shut their eyes; lest they see with their eyes, and hear with their ears, and understand with their heart, and turn again, and be healed.

<a id="v11"></a>**11** Then said I, Lord, how long? And he answered, Until cities be waste without inhabitant, and houses without man, and the land become utterly waste,

<a id="v12"></a>**12** and Jehovah have removed men far away, and the forsaken places be many in the midst of the land.

<a id="v13"></a>**13** And if there be yet a tenth in it, it also shall in turn be eaten up: as a terebinth, and as an oak, whose stock remaineth, when they are felled; so the holy seed is the stock thereof.
