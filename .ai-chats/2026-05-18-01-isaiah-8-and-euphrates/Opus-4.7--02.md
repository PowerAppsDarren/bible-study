# 📖 Isaiah 8 — "Should the Living Consult the Dead?"
### A Deep Devotional Study

---

## 🔥 Setting the Stage — A Baby Named "Hurry-Plunder-Speed-Spoil"

Isaiah 8 opens with one of the strangest naming ceremonies in Scripture. God tells Isaiah to take a "great scroll" and write on it in *common script* — meaning legible to anyone passing by — the name:

> **מַהֵר שָׁלָל חָשׁ בַּז** — *Maher-Shalal-Hash-Baz* — "Speed the spoil, hasten the plunder" (Isaiah 8:1, 3)

Then Isaiah's wife conceives and bears a son, and God says: *that's his name*. Imagine the kid at school roll call.

But this is prophetic theater. Before the boy can say "my father" or "my mother" (Isaiah 8:4), Assyria will sweep down and gut Damascus and Samaria. The name is a walking, breathing, diaper-wearing prophecy. **God dates His prophecies by the milestones of a toddler.** That's how confident He is.

Chuck Missler used to point out that Isaiah's two sons — *Shear-Jashub* ("a remnant shall return," 7:3) and *Maher-Shalal-Hash-Baz* — together preach the whole message of the chapter: *judgment is coming fast, but a remnant survives*. The prophet's family is the sermon.

---

## 🌊 The Gentle Waters vs. the Mighty River

> "Because this people refused the waters of **Shiloah** that flow gently... behold, the Lord is bringing up against them the waters of the River, mighty and many, the king of Assyria..." (Isaiah 8:6–7)

Two waters. Two kingdoms. Two ways to live.

- **Shiloah** (שִׁלֹחַ, *Shiloach*) — the small, quiet stream feeding Jerusalem from the Gihon Spring. Connected to the root *shalach*, "sent." Quiet. Faithful. Sufficient. Sent by God. This same pool shows up in John 9 when Jesus tells the blind man, "Go, wash in the pool of *Siloam* (which means **Sent**)." John even translates the Hebrew for us — because the whole gospel is hiding in that word.
- **The River** — capital R — the Euphrates. Assyria's lifeblood. Loud, impressive, overwhelming. The river of empire.

Judah looked at Shiloah and said *that's not enough*. They wanted the Euphrates' muscle. So God says: *fine. You want the big river? It'll flow up over your neck.* (8:8)

Dr. Bill Creasy reads this geographically and literarily — God uses the very thing they coveted as the instrument of their discipline. **The idol becomes the rod.** What we trust instead of God will eventually be what God uses to break us free *from* it.

---

## 🛡️ "God With Us" — Right in the Middle of the Storm

In the same breath where the Euphrates floods Judah "up to the neck," God says:

> "...and its outspread wings will fill the breadth of your land, **O Immanuel**." (Isaiah 8:8)

There it is again. *Immanuel.* עִמָּנוּ אֵל — *"With us, God."* The same name from Isaiah 7:14 that Matthew 1:23 pins on Jesus. Even when the flood is at neck-height, the land still belongs to Immanuel. **The judgment doesn't cancel the promise — it sits inside it.**

Then comes one of my favorite defiant verses in the whole prophets:

> "Take counsel together, but it will come to nothing; speak a word, but it will not stand, **for God is with us**." (Isaiah 8:10)

*Ki immanu El.* The nations can plot. The empires can scheme. The committees can meet. *God is with us.* That phrase doesn't just describe Jesus' incarnation — it's a war cry.

---

## 👁️ Don't Call It a Conspiracy — Fear God Instead

> "Do not call conspiracy all that this people calls conspiracy, and do not fear what they fear, nor be in dread. But the LORD of hosts, him you shall **honor as holy**. Let him be your fear, let him be your dread." (Isaiah 8:12–13)

The Hebrew word for "conspiracy" here is **קֶשֶׁר** (*qesher*) — a binding, a plot, a tying-together. Judah was wrapped up in geopolitical panic about Syria and Ephraim teaming up. Isaiah says: *stop. You're afraid of the wrong things.*

This is the verse Peter quotes directly in **1 Peter 3:14–15** to a persecuted church: *"Have no fear of them, nor be troubled, but in your hearts honor Christ the Lord as holy."* Peter takes Isaiah's "LORD of hosts" and reads it as *Christ.* Casual high Christology in one of the most cited OT prophecies.

John Bevere has built whole books on this principle — **the fear of the Lord displaces every other fear.** You will fear something. The only question is what. Fear the algorithm, fear the election, fear the market, fear the diagnosis — *or* fear the One who holds all of them. Isaiah says pick one. Choose well.

---

## 🪨 The Stone — Sanctuary or Stumbling

> "And he will become a sanctuary and a stone of offense and a rock of stumbling to both houses of Israel..." (Isaiah 8:14)

Same stone. Two reactions.

- To those who fear the LORD → **sanctuary** (*miqdash* — holy place, refuge)
- To those who refuse → **stone of stumbling** (*even negeph*) and **rock of offense** (*tsur mikshol*)

Paul grabs this in **Romans 9:33** and welds it to Isaiah 28:16 — "Behold, I am laying in Zion a stone of stumbling, and a rock of offense." Peter does the same in **1 Peter 2:6–8.** Both apostles see Jesus standing right here in Isaiah 8. The Messiah is the stone. You either build your life on Him or trip over Him. **There is no third option.** Neutrality toward Christ is one of the great lies of our age.

## ❄️ THE VERSE — Isaiah 8:19

> "And when they say to you, 'Inquire of **the mediums and the necromancers** who chirp and mutter,' should not a people inquire of their God? **Should they inquire of the dead on behalf of the living?**" (Isaiah 8:19)

Let's slow way down here. This is one of the most surgically relevant verses in the entire prophets for 2026.

The Hebrew is loaded. Three words to know:

- **אוֹב** (*ʼov*) — translated "mediums." Originally referred to a leather wineskin (think of the bloated, hollow sound), and came to mean both the spirit consulted *and* the person who channels it. The witch of Endor who summoned Samuel for Saul in **1 Samuel 28** is called a *baʼalat-ʼov* — "mistress of an *ov*." Same word.
- **יִדְּעֹנִי** (*yiddeʻoni*) — translated "necromancers" or "spiritists." From the root *yada* — "to know." A *yiddeʻoni* is literally a "knower" — someone trafficking in hidden knowledge from the dead. Modern translation: anyone selling you secret knowledge from the other side.
- **צָפַף... הָגָה** (*tsaphaph... hagah*) — "chirp and mutter." It's onomatopoeia. The sound of a bird peeping and a low growl. Isaiah is *mocking* the mediums. *Listen to them — they sound like pigeons.* Compare this to **Isaiah 29:4**, where the same verbs describe a defeated voice "from the dust." The dead don't have authority. They have *static.*

Now the gut-punch question:

> **"Should the living consult the dead?"**

The Torah had already banned this in **Leviticus 19:31** and **Deuteronomy 18:10–12** — calling it *toʻevah* (תּוֹעֵבָה), an abomination, something so revolting to God it nauseates Him. Saul lost his kingdom over it (1 Chronicles 10:13). And here in Isaiah, the prophet asks the question with absolute scorn: **Why would the alive go beg wisdom from the dead?**

But notice what God offers *instead* in the very next verse:

> **"To the teaching and to the testimony!** If they will not speak according to this word, it is because they have no dawn." (Isaiah 8:20)

**Torah** (תּוֹרָה — instruction) and **Teʻudah** (תְּעוּדָה — testimony, the bound-up witness of God). The contrast is brutal: *chirping mediums* on one side, *the written, sealed, breathed Word of God* on the other. *Pick.*

### Why this lands in 2026

Walk down any aisle. Tarot is mainstream. Astrology apps push notifications at sunrise. "Manifesting" replaced praying. Mediums have Netflix specials. *Yiddeʻoni* — "knowers" — sell courses on accessing your "higher self." Spiritualism dressed as wellness. Necromancy rebranded as "ancestor work."

Perry Stone has been pounding this drum for decades — what the Torah called *toʻevah* the culture now calls *self-care*. Jonathan Cahn names this pattern as Israel's *exact* spiral before judgment: when a covenant nation trades the Word for the witch.

And the most chilling part — Isaiah's audience would have called these mediums sophisticated. Cutting-edge. *Spiritually open.* God's verdict: **they have no dawn.** *Ein-lo shachar* — no morning. No light is coming for them. They're chirping in the dark.

If you're reading Scripture today and listening to the Holy Spirit — you have dawn. They don't. **Don't trade your sunrise for a séance.**

---

## 🐍 The Spiritual Warfare Layer

Ephesians 6:12 says we wrestle "against the rulers, against the authorities, against the cosmic powers over this present darkness, against the spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly places." That's not metaphor.

Mediumship isn't fraud — at least not always. Sometimes it's contact. But the entity on the other end of an *ov* is *never* your dead grandmother. **The dead in Christ rest** (1 Thessalonians 4:14); the dead apart from Christ are not available to chat. What answers is a familiar spirit — a deceiver wearing the voice you miss most. Saul thought he got Samuel. He got a message of death from a being playing a part.

The Ethiopian canon's **Book of Enoch** fills in the backstory: the Watchers in Genesis 6 taught humanity *charms, enchantments, divination, and the cutting of roots* — i.e., this exact toolkit. Necromancy isn't ancient ignorance. It's fallen-angel curriculum that never got pulled from the schedule.

## ☁️ Where We Stand

Isaiah closes the chapter looking around at a darkening land:

> "They will pass through the land, greatly distressed and hungry. And when they are hungry, they will be enraged and will speak contemptuously against their king and their God, and turn their faces upward. And they will look to the earth, but behold, distress and darkness, the gloom of anguish. And they will be thrust into thick darkness." (Isaiah 8:21–22)

A hungry, angry, cursing-God people, looking *up* and finding no answer, looking *down* and finding only more darkness. That's where you end up when you ditch Shiloah for the Euphrates and Scripture for séance.

**But Isaiah 9 is coming.** And Isaiah 9 begins: *"But there will be no gloom for her who was in anguish... The people who walked in darkness have seen a great light."* Dr. John Barnett often points out that chapter divisions weren't original — Isaiah 8:22 and 9:1 are one breath. **God never ends the sentence with darkness.**

---

## 🕊️ Closing Reflection

Isaiah 8 is a chapter about *where you go for answers.* When the news scares you, when the diagnosis lands, when you don't know what's next — who do you consult?

The chirpers will always have a booth open. The river will always look bigger than the stream. The conspiracy will always feel more urgent than the holiness of God.

But there is a stone in Zion. He is sanctuary or stumbling. He is *Immanuel.* And the only book that doesn't chirp from the dust is the one in your lap.

**To the law and to the testimony.** That's the dawn.
