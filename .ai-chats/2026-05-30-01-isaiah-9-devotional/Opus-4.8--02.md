## Isaiah 1–9: How We Got Here

- **Ch. 1 — The Courtroom Opens.** God puts Judah on trial: a nation so sick it's like a body covered in sores, worshipping with bloody hands. The shocker — *"Come now, let us reason together... though your sins are like scarlet, they shall be as white as snow"* (1:18). Mercy offered before judgment falls.

- **Ch. 2 — The Mountain & the Pride.** A vision of the *"last days"* when the LORD's mountain is exalted and nations stream to it (2:2-4, "swords into plowshares"). Then the flip side: the **Day of the LORD** against all human pride — *"the loftiness of man shall be bowed down"* (2:17).

- **Ch. 3 — Leadership Collapse.** God removes the props — the leaders, the wise, the supply. Society falls apart; *"children are their oppressors"* (3:12). A long, stinging rebuke of the proud "daughters of Zion" and their finery. Anarchy from the top down.

- **Ch. 4 — A Small, Bright Remnant.** Short chapter, big turn: after the purge, *"the Branch of the LORD"* (4:2 — a messianic title) is beautiful, and God's presence returns as cloud-and-fire over Zion, like a new Exodus. Hope in the ashes.

- **Ch. 5 — The Vineyard & the Six Woes.** God's love song to His vineyard (Judah) that yielded only *wild grapes* (5:1-7). Then six **"Woe!"** verdicts — greed, drunkenness, calling evil good and good evil (5:20). Ends with a foreign army summoned, *"none weary... none stumbling"* — judgment is coming.

- **Ch. 6 — The Throne Room.** Isaiah's call. He sees the LORD high and lifted up, seraphim crying *"Holy, holy, holy"* (6:3). He's undone — *"Woe is me!"* — a coal touches his lips, and he answers, *"Here am I, send me"* (6:8). His commission: preach to a people who won't listen, until the land is emptied — but a *"holy seed"* survives in the stump (6:13).

- **Ch. 7 — Immanuel.** Now history kicks in: Syria + Israel (Ephraim) gang up on little Judah. King Ahaz is terrified. Isaiah says *"be still, don't fear these two smoldering stubs."* Ahaz refuses a sign, so God gives one anyway: *"the virgin shall conceive and bear a Son, and shall call His name **Immanuel**"* — God with us (7:14).

- **Ch. 8 — The Flood & the Darkness.** Isaiah's own son gets a prophetic name (*Maher-Shalal-Hash-Baz*, "swift to plunder"). Assyria will flood in *"up to the neck"* (8:8). God warns Isaiah not to fear what the people fear — *sanctify the LORD as your dread* (8:13). The chapter ends in pitch black: people consulting mediums instead of God, *"thrust into thick darkness"* (8:22).

- **Ch. 9 — The Great Light.** **"Nevertheless"** — into that exact darkness (8:22 → 9:2), the light dawns first in *Galilee*. The Child is born, the Son is given, the government on His shoulder: *Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace* (9:6-7). Then the sobering back half: a proud nation that won't repent, and four times the bell tolls — *"His hand is stretched out still."*

---

**The throughline:** Isaiah keeps alternating **judgment → hope, judgment → hope**, and each hope-flash gets more *personal* — from a Branch (ch. 4), to a holy seed (ch. 6), to Immanuel (ch. 7), to the full throne-name Child-King (ch. 9). He's zooming the camera in on **one Person** the whole time.
