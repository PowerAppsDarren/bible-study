## Conversation Summary

The user opened a theological vibe-session exploring whether God instructed the angels to worship Adam (man made in God's image) and whether Satan's rebellion was rooted in refusal to honor the imago Dei. The conversation traced the tradition through:

- **Life of Adam and Eve** (Vita Adae et Evae) -- Satan narrates his own refusal to bow before Adam at Michael's command
- **Quran** (Surah 2:34, 7:11-12, 15:28-33) -- Iblis refuses to prostrate before Adam ("I am made of fire, he is made of clay")
- **2 Enoch 31:3-6** -- connects Satan's fall to envy of Adam
- **Church Fathers** (Irenaeus, Tertullian, Augustine) -- angelic jealousy toward humanity as a factor in the fall

Canonical touchpoints discussed:
- **Hebrews 1:6** ("Let all God's angels worship Him") and **Hebrews 2:5-8** (the world to come subjected to man, not angels; Psalm 8 quotation)
- **Ezekiel 28 / Isaiah 14** -- pride as the root of Satan's fall, but pride over *what*?
- **Genesis 1:26-27** -- imago Dei given to humanity alone; no angel is ever described as bearing God's image
- The logic connecting Genesis 3 -- Satan's first move attacks the image-relationship, tempting humans to distrust the God whose image they carry

Key insight surfaced: the canonical picture provides the ingredients (angels as servants, man as image-bearer and heir, Satan falling through pride) but the extracanonical sources connect the dots into the specific narrative. The tradition may capture a genuine theological logic even if not in the 66 books.
